## Nature and Our Lives

Nature and culture are deeply connected in India. From the food we eat to the clothes we wear, from the festivals we celebrate to the medicines we use -- nature plays a role in everything. In this chapter, we explore how people in villages live closely with nature and take care of it.

---

## The Flame of the Forest

The **Palash tree** is one of the most beautiful trees in India. When its bright **orange-red flowers** bloom, the entire forest looks reddish-orange from a distance. That is why the Palash is called the **"Flame of the Forest"**.

In Gujarat, the Palash tree is known as **Kesuda**. Different regions of India have different local names for the same trees and flowers.

![The Palash tree with bright orange-red flowers, also known as the Flame of the Forest](palash-flame-of-forest.png)

> India has thousands of colourful flowers like marigold, jasmine, sunflower, lotus and hibiscus. Each region has its own favourites.

## Houses Made from Nature

In many Indian villages, houses are built using **natural materials** like clay, hay, cow dung and wood. These houses stay cool in summer and warm in winter.

Village houses are often decorated with beautiful **wall paintings**. The paints used are made from natural materials like:

- **Rice flour** mixed with water
- Coloured powdered stones
- Natural extracts from flowers and leaves

> **Gond art** is a famous form of painting from central India. Gond artists paint pictures of trees, animals, birds and people on walls using **natural colours**. It is a beautiful tradition that has been passed down for generations.

![A Gond art painting showing trees, animals and birds in colourful natural patterns](gond-art-painting.png)

## Making Natural Dyes

You can make your own natural dye at home! Here is how:

1. **Collect** flowers, leaves or roots with colour -- like beetroot, hibiscus or marigold.
2. **Boil** them in 1-2 litres of water for 30 minutes to an hour.
3. **Strain** the liquid to remove the plant parts. The coloured liquid is your natural dye!
4. **Soak** a light-coloured cloth in the dye overnight.
5. **Squeeze and dry** the cloth the next morning.

Natural dyes are safe and have been used in India for centuries to colour clothes and pottery.

## Storing Grains the Traditional Way

In Indian villages, grains are stored in **earthen pots** and **bamboo baskets**. To protect the grains from insects and pests:

- Earthen pots are lined inside with **neem leaves** (neem keeps insects away).
- Bamboo baskets are coated on the outside with **cow dung**.

| Traditional Container | Used In | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Tumri** | Uttarakhand | Made from a round, dried bottle gourd |
| **Earthen pot** | Many states | Clay pot lined with neem leaves |
| **Bamboo basket** | Tribal areas | Coated with cow dung on the outside |

These simple methods have been used for hundreds of years and work very well without any chemicals.

## The Jenu Kuruba Tribe

The **Jenu Kurubas** are a tribe from **Karnataka**. Their name comes from the word **"Jenu"**, which means **honey**. They collect honey from beehives in the forest.

> What makes them special? When they collect honey, they **sing songs to apologise to the bees**. This shows their deep respect for nature and all living creatures.

## Medicinal Plants

Many plants around us have **healing properties**. In India, people have used plants as medicine for thousands of years.

| Plant | Part Used | Health Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| **Tulsi** (Indian basil) | Leaves | Helps with cough and cold |
| **Neem** | Leaves, oil | Keeps mosquitoes away, heals skin problems |
| **Ajwain** (carom seeds) | Seeds | Helps with stomach ache |
| **Turmeric** (haldi) | Root powder | Heals wounds, reduces swelling |
| **Aloe vera** | Leaf gel | Good for skin and hair |

Neem oil, for example, can be rubbed on skin to keep mosquitoes away -- a natural mosquito repellent!

## First-Aid Box

A **first-aid box** is a small kit that should be kept at home and in school. It contains things needed to treat small injuries immediately.

**What to keep in a first-aid box:** bandages, cotton, antiseptic cream, scissors, pain relief medicine, mosquito repellent and a small torch.

## Solar Energy in Villages

Some villages in India are becoming **fully solar-powered**. Solar panels use sunlight to produce electricity for fans, lights, TVs and other appliances. This is clean energy that does not pollute the environment.

## Festivals That Honour Nature

In India, many festivals celebrate our connection with nature:

| Festival | What It Honours |
|---|---|
| **Vat Purnima** | Worshipping the Banyan tree |
| **Hari Jiroti** | Planting fruit-bearing trees |
| **Kaziranga Elephant Festival** | Awareness about elephants |
| **Van Mahotsav** | Planting trees across India |
| **Pongal** | Thanking nature for the harvest |

> **Sacred Groves** are small patches of forests that local communities protect. The trees and animals within them are preserved. People gather here to worship and celebrate nature.

## How to Care for Nature

Here are some simple things we can all do:

- **Plant trees** in your neighbourhood.
- **Keep food and water** outside for birds.
- **Say no to plastic** bags and bottles.
- **Reduce paper waste** to save trees.
- **Organise clean-up drives** with friends and family.
- **Spread awareness** about protecting plants and animals.

---

## Key Points to Remember

- Nature and culture are deeply connected in Indian life.
- The **Palash** tree is called the "Flame of the Forest" for its bright red-orange flowers.
- Village houses are made from **natural materials** and decorated with traditional art like **Gond art**.
- Grains can be stored safely using **neem leaves** and **cow dung** coatings.
- Many Indian plants like **tulsi, neem and turmeric** have medicinal value.
- **Solar energy** is a clean way to produce electricity.
- Indian festivals show deep **respect for nature** -- trees, animals and the harvest.
