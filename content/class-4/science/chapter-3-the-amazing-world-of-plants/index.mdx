## Nature is Full of Wonders

Have you ever watched a spider weaving its web? Or a butterfly flying around flowers? Nature is full of amazing animals and plants, each with special features. In this chapter, we go on a **nature trail** to explore forests, animals, birds, insects and plants.

---

## A Nature Trail at Pachmarhi

**Pachmarhi** is a beautiful hill station in **Madhya Pradesh**. It is surrounded by thick forests filled with tall trees and chirping birds. A nature trail is a walk through a forest or natural area where you observe plants and animals carefully.

**Safety rules to follow in a forest or zoo:**

- Do not tease or feed animals.
- Do not damage trees, flowers or public property.
- Do not bring polythene bags or throw garbage.
- Do not bring pets or weapons.
- Be careful and stay alert in a new place.

> Following these rules keeps **you** safe and protects the **animals and plants** too.

---

## Animals on Land

Forests are home to many different animals. Each animal has **special features** that help it survive:

| Animal | Special Feature |
|---|---|
| **Elephant** | Long trunk to pick up food and drink water |
| **Deer** | Fast legs for running away from danger |
| **Tiger** | Sharp teeth and claws for hunting |
| **Gaur** (Indian bison) | Strong body with short horns, looks like a buffalo |
| **Indian Giant Squirrel** | Big, red-coloured squirrel found in Pachmarhi |

> **Did you know?** The **Spectacled Monkey** is the state animal of Tripura. It has white patches around its eyes that make it look like it is wearing spectacles!

---

## Birds and Their Beaks

Different birds have **different types of beaks and claws** based on what they eat:

| Bird | Beak Type | What It Eats |
|---|---|---|
| **Sparrow** | Short and strong | Cracks seeds and grains |
| **Eagle** | Sharp, curved beak with sharp claws | Catches and tears prey |
| **Sunbird** | Long, thin beak | Drinks nectar from flowers |
| **Hornbill** | Large, curved beak with a headgear | Fruits and small animals |
| **Peacock** | Short beak | Grains, insects and small creatures |
| **Owl** | Hooked beak, sharp eyesight for night | Mice and small animals |

We can guess what a bird eats by looking at the **shape of its beak**.

---

## Animals Near Water

Many animals live in or near **ponds, rivers and lakes**. They have special features to help them survive in water:

| Animal | Movement | Special Feature |
|---|---|---|
| **Fish** | Swims | Has fins and a streamlined body |
| **Frog** | Jumps | Lives both on land and in water |
| **Turtle** | Walks and swims | Strong shell for protection, webbed feet |
| **Crocodile** | Crawls and swims | Powerful jaws and a long tail |
| **Crab** | Walks sideways | Hard outer shell, strong pincers |

> The **gharial**, a type of crocodile found in India, has a long, narrow snout specially shaped for catching fish.

---

## Tiny Creatures -- Insects

**Insects** are tiny animals with some common features:

- They have **three pairs of legs** (6 legs in total).
- They have **one pair of antennae** to sense their surroundings.
- Many insects have **wings** (like butterflies, flies and grasshoppers).

**Common insects you may see around you:** ants, beetles, bees, flies, butterflies, grasshoppers, praying mantises and ladybugs.

---

## Leaves and Their Features

Leaves come in many **shapes, sizes, colours and textures**. When you look at a leaf closely, you can notice:

- **Colour** -- Most leaves are green, but some can be red, yellow or brown.
- **Shape** -- Oval, heart-shaped, long and narrow, or round.
- **Texture** -- Smooth, rough, waxy or hairy.
- **Smell** -- Some leaves have a strong smell (like lemon or neem leaves).
- **Veins** -- Lines on the leaf that carry water and food. Leaves have different patterns of veins.

> You can make a beautiful **leaf print** by placing a leaf (vein-side up) under a plain sheet of paper and rubbing a crayon over it. The leaf pattern appears like magic!

## The Web of Life

All living things in nature are **connected** to each other. This is called the **web of life**.

- Trees give **fruits and shade** to animals and birds.
- Birds eat **insects** and spread **seeds**.
- Rivers provide **water** to fish, frogs and many other creatures.
- **Earthworms** make the soil healthy for plants to grow.

If one part of this web is removed -- say, all the trees are cut down -- it affects every other living thing. That is why we must **protect nature**.

## State Symbols

Every state in India has its own **state animal, state bird and state tree**. For example:

| State | State Animal | State Bird |
|---|---|---|
| Madhya Pradesh | Barasingha (swamp deer) | Indian paradise flycatcher |
| Tripura | Spectacled Monkey (Phayre's langur) | Green imperial pigeon |
| Rajasthan | Chinkara | Great Indian bustard |

---

## Key Points to Remember

- A **nature trail** helps us observe and learn about plants and animals in their natural habitat.
- Animals have **special features** like trunks, sharp claws, fins and shells to help them survive.
- A bird's **beak and claws** tell us what it eats.
- **Insects** have six legs and antennae. Many have wings.
- **Leaves** come in different shapes, sizes, textures and vein patterns.
- All living things are connected through the **web of life** -- removing one part affects everything else.
- We must follow **safety rules** in forests and zoos to protect nature and ourselves.
