## The Sky Changes Through the Day

Have you ever looked up at the sky at different times of the day? The sky looks very different in the morning, afternoon and at night.

| Time of Day | What the Sky Looks Like |
|---|---|
| **Morning** | Orange and pink colours, the Sun rises in the east |
| **Afternoon** | Bright blue sky, strong sunlight, the Sun is overhead |
| **Evening** | Orange and red colours again, the Sun sets in the west |
| **Night** | Dark sky filled with the Moon and twinkling stars |

The activities of birds, animals and people also change during the day. Birds chirp and fly out in the morning to find food. At sunset, they return to their nests. People wake up and start work during the day, and rest at night.

---

## The Sun -- The Brightest Object in the Sky

The **Sun** is the brightest object we can see in the sky. It is so bright that we cannot see other stars during the daytime. The Sun gives us **light and heat**, which are essential for all life on Earth.

**The Sun's journey across the sky:**

1. The Sun appears to **rise in the East** in the morning.
2. It moves **upward** and reaches **overhead** around noon.
3. It then moves **downward** towards the **West** in the afternoon.
4. Finally, it **sets in the West** in the evening.

> The Sun does not actually move around the Earth. It is the **Earth** that rotates (spins), which makes the Sun appear to move across the sky.

## Shadows

A **shadow** is a dark shape formed when an object blocks light. When the Sun shines on you, your body blocks the light and creates a shadow on the ground.

### How Shadows Change During the Day

| Time of Day | Position of Sun | Shadow |
|---|---|---|
| **Morning** | Low in the East | Long shadow, pointing West |
| **Noon** | Directly overhead | Very short shadow |
| **Evening** | Low in the West | Long shadow, pointing East |

**Why do shadows change?**

- When the Sun is **low** in the sky (morning and evening), shadows are **long**.
- When the Sun is **high** in the sky (noon), shadows are **short**.
- The **direction** of the shadow is always **opposite** to the position of the Sun.

---

## Experimenting with Shadows

You can explore shadows using a **torch** and a **stick** in a dark room:

- If the object is **close** to the light, the shadow is **bigger**.
- If the object is **far** from the light, the shadow is **smaller**.
- If you change the **direction** of the light, the direction of the shadow also changes.

> **Shadow puppetry** has been practised in India for a very long time. Artists use cut-out figures to create shadow shapes on a screen, telling stories through the movement of shadows.

---

## The Sundial -- Telling Time with Shadows

Long ago, people noticed that shadows change throughout the day. They used this observation to invent the **sundial** -- a device that tells time based on the shadow cast by the Sun.

> **Raja Jai Singh** of Jaipur built the famous **Jantar Mantar** observatories in five cities: Jaipur, Ujjain, Delhi, Varanasi and Mathura. The largest sundial there is called the **Samrat Yantra**, and it can tell the time accurately using the position of the Sun's shadow.

## The Night Sky -- Stars

After sunset, the sky slowly fills with thousands of **twinkling stars**. Stars are actually very far away from us. They look tiny because of the great distance.

When you look at the night sky carefully, you can see **patterns** formed by groups of stars. One famous pattern is **Saptarshi** (the Seven Sages), which looks like a large spoon or ladle in the sky.

> Next time you are in an open area at night, look up and try to find star patterns. You might spot some interesting shapes!

## The Moon

The **Moon** is the biggest and brightest object in the night sky. It does not produce its own light -- it reflects the **light of the Sun**.

### Changing Shapes of the Moon

The Moon's shape appears to change over a period of about **one month**. These changing shapes are called **phases of the Moon**:

| Phase | What It Looks Like |
|---|---|
| **Poornima** (Full Moon) | A complete, round circle |
| **Half Moon** | Looks like it has been cut in half |
| **Crescent Moon** | A thin, curved shape like the letter "C" |
| **Amavasya** (New Moon) | Moon is not visible at all |

After the New Moon, the Moon slowly grows bigger each night until it becomes a Full Moon. Then it slowly gets smaller again until the next New Moon. This cycle repeats every month.

> Ancient people used the **shape of the Moon** to count days. This is how the earliest **calendars** were created!

## Festivals Based on the Sun and Moon

Many Indian festivals are celebrated based on the position of the Sun or the shape of the Moon:

| Festival | Connection to the Sky |
|---|---|
| **Diwali** | Celebrated on Amavasya (New Moon night -- no Moon in the sky) |
| **Rakshabandhan** | Celebrated on Poornima (Full Moon night) |
| **Guru Poornima** | Celebrated on a Full Moon night |
| **Buddha Poornima** | Celebrated on a Full Moon night |
| **Eid** | Celebrated when the crescent Moon is first seen after a New Moon |
| **Chhath Puja** | Dedicated to the Sun god |
| **Karva Chauth** | Women observe fast and wait for the Moon to rise |
| **Pongal** | Celebrates the Sun and the harvest |

---

## India's Chandrayaan Mission

India has sent spacecraft to explore the Moon! On **23 August 2023**, India's **Vikram Lander** (part of the Chandrayaan-3 mission) successfully landed on the Moon. It collected important data about the Moon's surface. India became the **fourth country** in the world to land on the Moon, and the **first** to land near the Moon's south pole.

---

## Key Points to Remember

- The **sky** looks different at different times of the day -- morning, afternoon and night.
- The **Sun** appears to rise in the **East** and set in the **West**.
- **Shadows** are long in the morning and evening, and short at noon.
- The direction of a shadow is always **opposite** to the position of the Sun.
- A **sundial** tells time using the Sun's shadow. Jantar Mantar has the famous Samrat Yantra.
- **Stars** form patterns in the night sky, like the Saptarshi constellation.
- The **Moon** changes shape over a month -- from Full Moon (Poornima) to New Moon (Amavasya).
- Many Indian festivals like Diwali, Eid and Chhath Puja are connected to the **Sun and Moon**.
- India's **Chandrayaan-3** mission landed on the Moon on 23 August 2023.
