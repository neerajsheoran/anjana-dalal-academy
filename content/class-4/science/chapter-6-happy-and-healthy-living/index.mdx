## What Makes Us Happy?

We all feel happy for different reasons -- playing games with friends, eating our favourite food, spending time with family, or learning something new. Being happy is an important part of staying healthy.

But have you ever thought about where our food comes from? The rice, wheat and vegetables on our plate go through a very long journey before they reach us.

---

## The Journey of a Grain

Every grain of rice or wheat goes through many steps before it reaches our plate. Let us follow this journey:

| Step | What Happens |
|---|---|
| 1. **Ploughing** | The farmer prepares the soil using a plough |
| 2. **Sowing** | Seeds are planted in the soil |
| 3. **Watering** | The crops need water to grow |
| 4. **Growing** | Sunlight, soil and water help the plant grow |
| 5. **Protecting** | The farmer keeps away birds and pests |
| 6. **Harvesting** | The ripe crop is cut and collected |
| 7. **Threshing** | Grains are separated from the plant |
| 8. **Storing** | Grains are packed and stored |
| 9. **Transporting** | Grains are sent to shops and markets |
| 10. **Selling** | Shopkeepers sell the grains to us |
| 11. **Cooking** | Family members cook the food at home |
| 12. **Eating** | The food finally reaches our plate! |

Many **elements of nature** help in this journey:

- **Soil** gives the plant a place to grow and provides nutrients.
- **Water** keeps the plant alive and helps it grow.
- **Sunlight** gives the plant energy.
- **Air** is needed for the plant to breathe.
- **Earthworms** make the soil soft and fertile.
- **Cattle** (bullocks) help in ploughing the fields.

> The journey of a grain is long and involves the hard work of many people and animals. That is why every grain is **precious** and we should never waste food.

## Gratitude for Food

In India, we have many festivals where we thank **Mother Nature** for the food we get:

| Festival | What We Celebrate |
|---|---|
| **Makar Sankranti** | The harvest and the Sun god |
| **Pongal** | Gratitude for a good harvest (Tamil Nadu) |
| **Onam** | The harvest season (Kerala) |
| **Lohri** | The harvest of rabi crops (Punjab) |
| **Akshay Tritya** | New beginnings and agriculture |
| **Raja Utsav** | Honouring the earth (Odisha) |

When we remember all the people and elements that brought food to our plate, we feel **grateful** and enjoy our meals even more.

## Mindful Eating

**Mindful eating** means paying full attention to what we eat and how we feel while eating. Here is how to practice it:

1. **Sit calmly** and focus on your food.
2. **Put the food in your mouth slowly.**
3. **Feel the texture** using your tongue and mouth.
4. **Chew gently** and notice the taste and flavour.
5. **Swallow** only after chewing well.
6. **Enjoy every bite** without rushing.

When we eat mindfully, we enjoy our food more, make healthier choices and **waste less food**.

> Your **tongue** helps you taste the food. Your **teeth** break it into smaller pieces. Inside your mouth, the food gets softer and easier to swallow.

---

## Do Not Waste Food

Every grain is precious because so many people and elements of nature worked hard to bring it to your plate. Here are some tips to avoid wasting food:

- Take only as much food as you can eat.
- If you do not like something on your plate, try a small amount instead of leaving it all.
- If someone offers you more food than you need, **politely say no**.
- Finish everything on your plate before asking for more.

## Exercise and Physical Activity

Along with good food, our body also needs **regular exercise** to stay healthy. Physical activities make us strong, energetic and happy.

**Fun ways to exercise:**

- Playing outdoor games like kabaddi, kho-kho, cricket and football
- Running, jumping and cycling
- Skipping rope
- Swimming
- Practising **yoga** every day

**Some easy yoga poses for children:**

| Yoga Pose | Benefit |
|---|---|
| **Tadasana** (Mountain pose) | Improves posture and balance |
| **Vrikshasana** (Tree pose) | Builds concentration and leg strength |
| **Bhujangasana** (Cobra pose) | Strengthens the back |
| **Balasana** (Child's pose) | Relaxes the body and mind |

> Regular physical activity keeps us **strong, energetic and happy**. It also improves our posture and helps us focus better in studies.

---

## Sleep Well

After a busy day of playing and studying, our body needs **rest**. Sleep is very important for our health.

**What happens when we sleep well:**
- We feel **fresh and active** the next day.
- Our body repairs itself during sleep.
- Our brain organises what we learned during the day.

**What happens when we do not sleep enough:**
- We feel **tired, sleepy and irritable**.
- It becomes hard to focus on work or play.
- We may fall sick more easily.

> Getting enough sleep is just as important as eating healthy food and exercising. Children need about **9-11 hours of sleep** every night.

---

## Feeling Safe

Staying happy and healthy also means feeling **safe**. It is important to know the difference between a **safe touch** and an **unsafe touch**.

- A **safe touch** makes you feel comfortable and happy -- like a hug from your mother or a pat on the back from your teacher.
- An **unsafe touch** makes you feel uneasy, scared or uncomfortable.

**What to do if someone's touch makes you uncomfortable:**

1. Say **"No"** firmly.
2. Move away from that person.
3. Tell a **trusted adult** immediately -- your parents, teacher or a family member.

> You should **never** feel guilty about saying "No" to anyone who makes you uncomfortable. Your safety is most important.

---

## Key Points to Remember

- The **journey of a grain** from farm to plate involves many people, animals and elements of nature.
- We should feel **grateful** for everyone who helps bring food to our table.
- **Mindful eating** means paying attention to our food and enjoying every bite.
- **Never waste food** -- every grain is precious.
- **Exercise and yoga** keep our body strong and our mind happy.
- **Sleep** is essential -- children need 9-11 hours every night.
- Know the difference between **safe and unsafe touch** -- always speak up if you feel uncomfortable.
