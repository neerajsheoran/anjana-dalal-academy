## Things Around Us Are Made of Materials

Look around you -- books, newspapers, notebooks, greeting cards, tissue paper and cardboard boxes. All of these are made from **paper**. But have you ever wondered where paper comes from? Paper is made from **trees**!

---

## How Paper is Made

Paper is made from the **wood** of trees. Here is the basic process:

1. Trees are cut down and the wood is collected.
2. The wood is broken into tiny pieces called **wood pulp**.
3. The pulp is mixed with water and chemicals.
4. The wet mixture is spread out into thin sheets.
5. The sheets are dried to become paper.

Making paper requires **many trees** and a lot of **water and energy**. That is why it is important to use paper carefully and not waste it.

![Steps of paper making: from trees to wood pulp to wet sheets to dried paper](paper-making-process.png)

> **Did you know?** In earlier times, people did not have paper. They wrote on **cloth**, **leaves** and **wooden boards**. In India, people used **palm leaves** called **talapatra** to write on. Today, environment-friendly materials like **banana fibre** can also be used to make paper!

## Making Recycled Paper at Home

You can make your own **recycled paper** from old newspapers! Here is how:

**Materials needed:** Old newspapers, fenugreek seeds (methi) and water.

**Steps:**

1. **Tear** the newspapers into small pieces.
2. **Soak** the pieces in water with some fenugreek seeds and leave it overnight. The methi seeds act as a **binding material** (like glue).
3. **Mash** the soaked paper and seeds into a wet mixture (paper pulp). You can use a grinder or mash with your hands.
4. **Spread** the paste on a flat surface and roll it into a thin sheet.
5. **Dry** the sheet completely.

Your recycled paper is ready! You can use it to make **greeting cards**, **decorative items** or even **paper masks**.

## Colouring Paper with Natural Dyes

You can colour your recycled paper using **natural ingredients** from your kitchen:

| Natural Ingredient | Colour It Gives |
|---|---|
| **Turmeric** (haldi) | Yellow |
| **Spinach** (palak) | Green |
| **Beetroot** (chukandar) | Pink/Red |
| **Henna** (mehndi) | Brown/Orange |

Natural dyes are made from different parts of plants -- leaves, flowers, roots and seeds. They are **safe to use** and do not harm the environment.

## Different Types of Paper

We use many different types of paper in our daily lives. Each type has its own **special quality** that makes it useful:

| Type of Paper | Special Quality | Common Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Writing paper** | Smooth surface | Notebooks, letters |
| **Newspaper** | Thin, light and cheap | Daily news printing |
| **Art paper** | Glossy and thick | Magazines, posters |
| **Cardboard** | Thick and hard | Boxes, packaging |
| **Tissue paper** | Very thin, soft, absorbs water | Cleaning, wiping |
| **Chart paper** | Colourful, firm | School projects, displays |

## Braille Paper

**Braille** is a special system that helps **visually challenged people** (people who cannot see) to read and write. Braille paper has tiny **raised bumps** that represent letters, numbers and punctuation marks. By touching these bumps with their fingers, visually challenged people can read.

## The 5Rs of Waste Management

We produce a lot of waste every day. To **reduce waste** and protect our environment, we can follow the **5Rs**:

![The 5Rs of waste management: Refuse, Reduce, Reuse, Repurpose, Recycle shown in a circle](five-rs-waste-management.png)

### 1. Refuse

**Say "No"** to things that create waste.

*Example:* Refuse single-use paper cups. Use your own reusable cup instead.

### 2. Reduce

Use things **carefully** so less waste is produced.

*Example:* Write on **both sides** of a paper before throwing it away.

### 3. Reuse

Use something **again and again** before throwing it away.

*Example:* Use a gift bag again for another occasion instead of buying a new one.

### 4. Repurpose

Use something in a **new and creative way**.

*Example:* Turn old newspapers into decorative items or toys.

### 5. Recycle

Turn old things into **something new** through a special process.

*Example:* Old newspapers can be recycled into handmade paper.

## Why Should We Save Paper?

- Saving paper means **fewer trees** need to be cut down.
- It saves the **water and energy** used in making paper.
- It **reduces waste** going into landfills.
- It helps protect **forests** and the animals that live in them.

> By making small changes in our daily life -- like writing on both sides of a page, using cloth bags and recycling old paper -- we can make a big difference for our planet.

---

## Key Points to Remember

- Paper is made from **trees**, so wasting paper harms forests.
- We can make **recycled paper** at home using old newspapers, water and fenugreek seeds.
- Paper can be coloured with **natural dyes** from turmeric, beetroot, spinach and henna.
- Different types of paper (writing paper, cardboard, tissue paper) have different uses.
- **Braille** paper helps visually challenged people read using raised bumps.
- The **5Rs** -- Refuse, Reduce, Reuse, Repurpose, Recycle -- help us manage waste and protect the environment.
- Every small step we take to **save paper** helps save trees, water and energy.
