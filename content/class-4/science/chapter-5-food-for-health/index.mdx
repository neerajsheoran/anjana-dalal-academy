## Why Do We Need Food?

Our body needs food to stay **healthy, energetic and strong**. Food helps us grow, gives us energy to play and study, and protects us from diseases. Since no single food item has everything our body needs, we must eat a **variety of foods** every day.

---

## The Six Tastes (Shad Rasa)

In **Ayurveda**, the ancient Indian science of health, there are **six tastes** called **Shad Rasa**. A balanced meal should ideally include all six tastes:

| Taste | Hindi Name | Example Foods |
|---|---|---|
| **Sweet** | Meetha | Jaggery, banana, honey, rice |
| **Sour** | Khatta | Tamarind, lemon, raw mango, curd |
| **Salty** | Namkeen | Salt, pickles |
| **Pungent** | Teekha | Green chilli, ginger, garlic, pepper |
| **Bitter** | Karva | Neem, karela (bitter gourd), methi |
| **Astringent** | Kasaila | Raw banana, turmeric, raw mango |

> **Ugadi Pachadi** is a traditional dish from Andhra Pradesh, Karnataka and Telangana that includes all six tastes! It is made during the Ugadi festival and each taste represents a different part of life.

---

## Three Groups of Food

All the food we eat can be placed into three main groups based on what they do for our body:

### 1. Energy-Giving Foods

These foods give our body the **energy** it needs to walk, run, play and work.

**Examples:** Rice, wheat, potato, maize, banana, apple, sweet potato, honey, millets, butter, ghee and oil.

### 2. Body-Building Foods

These foods help our body **grow**, build muscles and become **strong**.

**Examples:** Pulses (dal), soybeans, chickpeas (chana), peanuts, tofu, milk, cheese, beans, almonds, walnuts, eggs, meat and fish.

### 3. Protective Foods

These foods help us **fight diseases** and keep our body healthy.

**Examples:** Carrot, orange, lemon, papaya, spinach, cabbage, guava, amla, tomato and other fruits and vegetables.

> If we eat food from only **one group** every day, our body will not get everything it needs. That is why eating from **all three groups** is important.

## Millets -- The Super Food

**Millets** are grains like **jowar** (sorghum), **bajra** (pearl millet), **ragi** (finger millet) and **jau** (barley). They are rich in **nutrients** and very good for health.

In many Indian homes, grandparents used to eat jowar and bajra roti in winters and jau roti in summers. Today, people are rediscovering these healthy grains.

---

## A Balanced Food Plate

A **balanced plate** includes food from all three food groups in the right quantities:

- **Most of the plate:** Fruits and vegetables (protective foods)
- **A good portion:** Pulses, milk, nuts (body-building foods)
- **Some portion:** Rice, roti, bread (energy-giving foods)

In India, every region has a special **thali** which includes different types of food items with a variety of tastes and health benefits.

## Say No to Junk Food

**Junk food** is food that is high in oil, salt and sugar but low in nutrition. Eating junk food regularly is harmful to our health.

| Junk Food Examples | Why They Are Unhealthy |
|---|---|
| Chips, samosa | Too much oil and salt |
| Soft drinks (cold drinks) | Too much sugar |
| Burgers, pizza | Over-processed, low nutrition |
| Bread pakoda | Deep fried in oil |
| Packaged snacks | Contain artificial ingredients |

**Fresh, home-cooked food** is always better than packaged or processed food.

---

## Water for Health

**Water** is a very important part of our diet. Here is why:

- Water helps in **digesting food**.
- It keeps our **skin and body healthy**.
- It removes **waste** from our body through urine, sweat and stool.
- Fruits like **watermelon** and **cucumber** also contain a lot of water.

Drinking enough water every day should be part of our **healthy habits**.

## Different Methods of Cooking

Food can be prepared in many different ways:

| Cooking Method | Example Dishes |
|---|---|
| **Steaming** | Idli, idiyappam |
| **Boiling** | Rice, dal, kheer |
| **Frying** | Samosa, gulab jamun |
| **Roasting** | Papad, corn |
| **Grilling** | Paneer tikka |
| **Baking** | Cake, bread |
| **No cooking needed** | Fruit chaat, vegetable salad |

> **Did you know?** A **solar cooker** uses sunlight to cook food instead of gas or electricity. It does not produce smoke or ash, so it is very good for the environment.

---

## Reading Food Labels

Whenever you buy **packaged food**, always check the label. Look for:

- **Expiry date** -- Do not eat food that has passed its expiry date.
- **Ingredients** -- Know what is inside the packet.
- **Net weight** -- How much food is inside.

This habit helps us make **safe and healthy** food choices.

---

## Key Points to Remember

- Our body needs food for **energy, growth and protection** from diseases.
- **Shad Rasa** (six tastes) in Ayurveda tells us to eat balanced food with all tastes.
- Food is divided into **three groups**: energy-giving, body-building and protective.
- **Millets** like jowar, bajra and ragi are super foods rich in nutrients.
- A **balanced plate** has food from all three groups in the right amounts.
- **Junk food** is unhealthy -- fresh home-cooked food is the best choice.
- **Water** is essential for digestion, healthy skin and removing waste from the body.
- Always **check food labels** before buying packaged food.
