## Our Neighbourhood

Our neighbourhood is full of interesting places and people. There are **bus stops**, **post offices**, **hospitals**, **banks**, **markets** and much more. Each of these places serves an important purpose and helps people in their daily lives.

---

## Communication -- Then and Now

**Communication** means sharing information with others. The way people communicate has changed a lot over time.

| Time Period | How People Communicated |
|---|---|
| Very long ago | Drumbeats and carvings on rocks |
| Long ago | Letters carried by messengers and pigeons |
| Later | Postal service for letters and parcels |
| After that | Telephone, radio and television |
| Today | Mobile phones, computers and the internet |

In the past, sending a letter could take **weeks** to reach someone. Today, we can send a message or make a video call in just **seconds**!

![Evolution of communication: drumbeats, letters by pigeon, telephone, and mobile phone](communication-evolution.png)

> **Did you know?** India has one of the largest postal networks in the world. Even today, post offices help people send letters, parcels and important documents.

## The Post Office

A **post office** is a public place where letters and parcels are sent and received. Here is how the postal system works:

1. You write a letter and drop it in a **red postbox**.
2. The **postman** collects the letters from the postbox.
3. The letters go to the **post office**, where they are sorted by region.
4. The sorted letters are sent to their destinations.

Post offices also offer other services like **money orders**, **savings accounts** and **speed post**.

## Modes of Transport

People use different **modes of transport** to travel from one place to another.

| Type | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Road transport** | Bus, car, auto-rickshaw, e-rickshaw, bicycle |
| **Rail transport** | Train, metro |
| **Water transport** | Boat, ship, ferry |
| **Air transport** | Aeroplane, helicopter |

Modern facilities like **metro trains** and **flyovers** have made travel much faster and easier. Metro trains run at high speed and flyovers help vehicles move above busy roads, saving time for travellers.

> However, building new roads, bridges and buildings can affect animals, birds and trees. We need to **balance development** with protecting our environment -- for example, by planting more trees.

## The Hospital

A **hospital** is one of the most important places in any neighbourhood. It is where people go when they are sick or injured. Hospitals also organise **health camps** like eye check-up camps to help the community.

| Person | Role in the Hospital |
|---|---|
| **Doctor** | Treats patients and prescribes medicines |
| **Nurse** | Takes care of patients and assists doctors |
| **Pharmacist** | Gives medicines to patients |
| **Lab technician** | Runs tests to help doctors find out what is wrong |
| **Ambulance driver** | Brings patients to the hospital in emergencies |

## Banks and Saving Money

A **bank** is a place where people keep their money safe. Banks also help people save money for the future.

**Important things about banks:**

- An **ATM** (Automated Teller Machine) is a machine that lets you take out money anytime.
- A **bank passbook** records all the money that goes in and comes out of your account. It has details like your account number, name, address and IFSC code.
- In earlier times, people had to visit the bank and wait in long queues. Now, we can send money using **mobile phones** in seconds!

> **Did you know?** Long ago, people used the **barter system** -- exchanging goods directly. A farmer might give wheat to get milk. But finding the right trade was hard, so **money** was invented to make exchanges simpler.

**Why should we save money?**

- Savings help us in times of need.
- We can use saved money to buy things we need in the future.
- It is a good habit to save a part of whatever money we get.

## Directions and Maps

When you look at a **map**, the four main directions are shown like this:

| Direction | Position on the Map |
|---|---|
| **North** | Top |
| **South** | Bottom |
| **East** | Right |
| **West** | Left |

![A compass rose showing North, South, East, and West directions](compass-directions.png)

**How to find East:** The direction where the Sun rises is **East**. Once you know East, you can figure out all the other directions.

**Landmarks** are well-known places or structures that help us find our way. Examples include a big temple, a park, a hospital, a tall building or a famous shop.

When giving directions to someone, you can use words like **left**, **right**, **forward**, **backward** along with landmark names to help them find the way.

## Key Points to Remember

- **Communication** has evolved from drumbeats and letters to mobile phones and the internet.
- **Post offices** help people send and receive letters, parcels and money.
- **Modes of transport** include road, rail, water and air travel.
- **Hospitals** provide treatment, and many workers help run them.
- **Banks** keep our money safe and help us save for the future.
- **Maps** use directions (North, South, East, West) and **landmarks** to help us find places.
- We should appreciate and support all the **community services** that make our lives easier.
