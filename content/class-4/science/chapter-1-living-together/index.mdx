## What is a Community?

We all live in a **community**. A community is a group of people who live near each other and share places, resources and experiences. In every community, you will find important places like **schools**, **hospitals**, **markets**, **parks**, **police stations** and **bus stops**. These places help everyone live a better life.

Think about your own neighbourhood. You see shopkeepers selling daily items, roads connecting different areas, doctors treating sick people, and teachers helping children learn. All these people and places are **connected** to each other.

> If even one important place is missing from your area -- like a hospital or a school -- life becomes very difficult for everyone.

---

## Public Places and Why They Matter

**Public places** are spaces that everyone can use. Parks, playgrounds, libraries, bus stops and post offices are all public places. People use them for many things -- playing, relaxing, reading, travelling and meeting others.

It is our responsibility to keep public places **clean and safe**. We should not throw garbage, break benches or damage walls. When we take care of these places, everyone benefits.

---

## Van Mahotsav -- A Festival for Planting Trees

**Van Mahotsav** is celebrated every year in India from **1st July to 7th July**, during the rainy season. It is also called the **Tree Planting Festival**. People across the country plant trees to protect the environment and make the earth greener.

Here is what children in a village did for Van Mahotsav:

| Activity | Who Helped |
|---|---|
| Cleaned the park | Children and parents |
| Fixed broken benches | A villager with wood and nails |
| Repaired the street light | An electrician from the village |
| Brought saplings | A person who runs a plant nursery |
| Planted trees along the boundary | Everyone together |

When people **work together**, big things can be done quickly and happily.

## Helping Hands in the Community

Many people work hard to keep our community running smoothly. Let us look at some of them:

| Person | What They Do | Tools They Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Mason** | Builds and repairs walls using bricks and cement | Trowel |
| **Carpenter** | Makes and fixes wooden furniture and structures | Hammer, saw |
| **Electrician** | Fixes lights and electrical equipment | Wires, pliers |
| **Gardener** | Takes care of plants and gardens | Spade, watering can |
| **Sweeper** | Keeps streets and public places clean | Broom |

Every person in the community has an important role. Without any one of them, things would not work well.

## Working Together as a Team

Just like people in a village come together during Van Mahotsav, people in our **schools** also work as a team. Teachers, students, bus drivers, caretakers, cooks and office staff all play different roles.

> An **ant colony** is a great example of teamwork in nature! Some ants collect food, some take care of baby ants, and some protect the nest. Bees also work the same way -- each bee has a special job in the hive.

---

## Community Celebrations and Togetherness

Festivals bring people together. When we celebrate together, we share food, laughter and joy. This creates beautiful memories and strong bonds.

**Indian examples of community celebrations:**

- **Uruka** (Magh Bihu) in Assam -- People build a hut called **Bhela Ghar** using bamboo and hay. They cook, eat and dance together on 14th January.
- **Khetala** in Sikkim -- During harvest time, people from the community come together to help each other cut crops. This creates a strong bond among them.
- **Van Mahotsav** -- After planting trees, villagers share a **community feast**, sitting together and eating on banana leaves.

---

## Sharing a Meal

After hard work, sharing a meal together makes everyone feel happy. In many Indian villages, after events like tree planting, people cook food together using vegetables and grains brought by everyone. They serve food on **banana leaves** and eat together.

> **Did you know?** When banana leaves are buried in a pit, they turn into **manure** that makes the soil richer and healthier for plants.

---

## A Real-Life Example of Teamwork

In **Kanker, Chhattisgarh**, the Chinar river often overflows during the rainy season. Roads get blocked and people cannot reach schools or hospitals. In 2024, the entire community came together and built a **bamboo bridge** in just **two days** using bamboo, stones and other materials. Now, students and villagers can cross the river safely.

This shows us that when a community works together, even the biggest problems can be solved.

---

## Key Points to Remember

- A **community** is a group of people living together and sharing resources.
- **Public places** like parks, schools and hospitals are important for everyone.
- **Van Mahotsav** is celebrated from 1-7 July to plant trees across India.
- People with different **skills and occupations** help keep the community running.
- **Teamwork** makes everything easier -- whether in a village, school or family.
- Festivals and celebrations bring people **closer** and create strong bonds.
- We should always **take care of nature** and our neighbourhood.
