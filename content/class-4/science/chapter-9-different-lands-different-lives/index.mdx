## India -- A Land of Many Landscapes

India is a large and beautiful country with many different types of **landforms** -- flat plains, hot deserts, long coastlines and tall mountains. The way people live, the food they eat, the clothes they wear and the festivals they celebrate all depend on the **land** around them.

---

## Life in the Plains -- Amritsar, Punjab

The plains are large, **flat areas** of land with rich and fertile soil. Because the soil is so good, many people here are **farmers**. Punjab is one of India's most important farming states.

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Land** | Flat plains with fertile soil |
| **Main occupation** | Farming (wheat, rice, corn, mustard) |
| **Famous food** | Makki di roti (corn flour roti) and sarson da saag (mustard leaves dish), lassi |
| **Animals** | Cows, buffaloes with shorter fur |
| **Clothing** | Colourful turbans for men, salwar kameez |

> **The Golden Temple** (Sri Harmandir Sahib) in Amritsar is one of the most important Gurudwaras. It is gold-plated and surrounded by a beautiful water tank called the **sarovar**. The temple runs a **langar** (community kitchen) that serves free meals to everyone -- it is one of the largest community kitchens in the world!

---

## Life in the Desert -- Rajasthan

A **desert** is a dry place with very **little rainfall**. The desert in Rajasthan is called the **Thar Desert**. Life here is very different from life in the plains.

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Land** | Sandy, dry with very little rain |
| **Plants** | Cactus, khejri, babool (survive on very little water) |
| **Animals** | Camels, lizards, wild cats, pythons, eagles |
| **Famous food** | Dal-bati-churma, ker sangri |
| **Clothing** | Colourful ghaghras for women, turbans for men |
| **Music** | Khartal, sarangi (traditional instruments) |
| **Houses** | Made of mud with thatched roofs |

**Special features of desert life:**
- The **camel** is called the "Ship of the Desert" because it can travel long distances on sand without needing much water.
- **Cactus** plants store water in their thick stems to survive the dry weather.
- Many houses have **tankas** (water tanks) built underground to store rainwater.
- Women wear colourful **bandhini** dupattas and glass bangles.

> **Jaisalmer Fort** is a famous desert fort made of golden-coloured **sandstone**. It is one of the few forts in the world where people still live inside! The famous filmmaker **Satyajit Ray** used this fort in his movie "Sonar Kella".

---

## Life on the Coast -- Puri, Odisha

**Coastal regions** are areas near the sea. Puri is a beautiful coastal city in **Odisha**, known for its beaches, temples and delicious food.

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Land** | Sandy beaches along the sea |
| **Main occupation** | Fishing |
| **Plants** | Coconut trees, palm trees |
| **Famous food** | Dalma, pakhala, chhena poda, rasagola |
| **Art** | Sand art on beaches, Sambalpuri saree weaving |
| **Sea creatures** | Turtles, crabs, dolphins, starfish, corals |

**Life near the sea:**
- Many people depend on **fishing** for their livelihood. Fishermen go out in boats early in the morning to catch fish.
- You can find beautiful **sea shells** on the beach, including the **shankh** (conch shell) which is blown during prayers in temples.
- Artists create stunning **sand art** on the beaches of Puri.

> **Shree Jagannath Temple** in Puri is one of the most famous temples in India. The city is known for the grand **Rath Yatra**, where thousands of people pull huge, colourful chariots of Lord Jagannath, Subhadra and Balabhadra through the streets.

### Challenges in Coastal Regions

Coastal areas sometimes face **cyclones** -- strong, dangerous storms that come from the sea. Cyclones bring heavy rain, strong winds and flooding, which can damage homes, trees and crops.

> The Government of **Odisha** has won national and international awards for saving people during cyclones through **early warnings**, advance preparations and effective management.

## Life in the Mountains -- Sikkim

**Mountains** are rocky landforms that rise high above the ground. They are usually **cold**, and many Himalayan mountains are covered in snow. Sikkim is a beautiful mountain state in north-east India.

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Land** | High mountains with steep slopes and valleys |
| **Climate** | Cold, clean and refreshing air |
| **Famous peak** | Mount Kangchenjunga (third highest in the world) |
| **Trees** | Oak, pine, walnut, chestnut |
| **Animals** | Yak (used for mountain travel) |
| **Famous food** | Thukpa (hot noodle soup), sel roti (sweet fried bread) |
| **Traditional clothes** | Bakhu, dumdyam, gunyo cholo |
| **Houses** | Wooden houses with slanting roofs |

**Why do mountain houses have slanting roofs?** So that rain and snow can slide off easily and not collect on top of the house.

> **Loosong and Namsoong** is one of the most important festivals in Sikkim. It marks the start of the **Sikkimese New Year** and celebrates the hard work of farmers. People enjoy local food, traditional games and a mask dance called **chaam**.

### Challenges in Mountain Regions

Mountains often face **landslides** during the rainy season, especially when heavy rain loosens the soil and rocks. Communities work together to keep people safe during such times.

## Comparing Life Across Landforms

| Category | Plains | Desert | Coastal | Mountains |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Climate** | Moderate | Hot and dry | Warm and humid | Cold |
| **Trees** | Mango, neem, peepal | Cactus, khejri, babool | Coconut, palm | Oak, pine, walnut |
| **Animals** | Cows, buffaloes | Camels, lizards | Fish, turtles, crabs | Yak |
| **Houses** | Brick and cement | Mud and thatch | Concrete, near shore | Wood with slanting roofs |
| **Food** | Roti, rice, dal | Dal-bati-churma | Fish, rice, dalma | Thukpa, sel roti |

---

## Key Points to Remember

- India has four major landforms: **plains, deserts, coastal areas and mountains**.
- The **land and climate** of a place affects the food, clothing, houses, plants and animals found there.
- **Plains** like Punjab have fertile soil and are great for farming.
- **Deserts** like Rajasthan are dry, and plants like cactus store water to survive.
- **Coastal regions** like Puri depend on fishing, and face cyclones from time to time.
- **Mountains** like Sikkim are cold, and houses have slanting roofs to handle snow and rain.
- Every region of India has its own unique **culture, festivals, food and traditions**.
