## Times-10 — Multiplying by 10

When we multiply any number by 10, the digits move one place to the left and we put a **0** in the ones place.

| Multiplication | Think of it as | Answer |
|---------------|----------------|--------|
| 2 x 10 | 2 Tens | 20 |
| 5 x 10 | 5 Tens | 50 |
| 8 x 10 | 8 Tens | 80 |
| 10 x 10 | 10 Tens | 100 |

> **Key rule:** Multiplying by 10 is the same as writing the number followed by a zero! 15 x 10 = 150, 26 x 10 = 260.

## Building Bigger Times Tables

You already know times tables from 1 to 10. Now we can build times tables for bigger numbers like 11, 12, 13, 14, 15, and beyond!

### Example: The Times-15 Table

To find any number times 15, split 15 into **10 + 5**:

| Problem | Split into 10 and 5 | Answer |
|---------|---------------------|--------|
| 1 x 15 | 1x10 + 1x5 = 10 + 5 | 15 |
| 2 x 15 | 2x10 + 2x5 = 20 + 10 | 30 |
| 3 x 15 | 3x10 + 3x5 = 30 + 15 | 45 |
| 4 x 15 | 4x10 + 4x5 = 40 + 20 | 60 |
| 5 x 15 | 5x10 + 5x5 = 50 + 25 | 75 |
| 6 x 15 | 6x10 + 6x5 = 60 + 30 | 90 |
| 10 x 15 | 10x10 + 10x5 = 100 + 50 | 150 |

### Comparing Times-5 and Times-15

| Times-5 | Times-15 | Difference |
|---------|----------|-----------|
| 1 x 5 = 5 | 1 x 15 = 15 | 15 - 5 = 10 |
| 2 x 5 = 10 | 2 x 15 = 30 | 30 - 10 = 20 |
| 3 x 5 = 15 | 3 x 15 = 45 | 45 - 15 = 30 |

The difference column gives us the **times-10 table**! This is because 15 = 5 + 10.

## Splitting into Equal Groups and Doubling

For even numbers like 14, we can split them into two equal halves:

### Example: Times-14 Table by Doubling

Since 14 = 7 + 7, we can find any number times 14 by **doubling** the answer from the times-7 table:

| Problem | Times-7 | Double it | Answer |
|---------|---------|-----------|--------|
| 3 x 14 | 3 x 7 = 21 | 21 + 21 | **42** |
| 6 x 14 | 6 x 7 = 42 | 42 + 42 | **84** |
| 8 x 14 | 8 x 7 = 56 | 56 + 56 | **112** |

> **Tip:** This works for any even number. Times-12 = double of times-6. Times-16 = double of times-8.

## Multiplying by Multiples of 10

When the number of groups is a multiple of 10:

| Problem | Think of it as | Answer |
|---------|----------------|--------|
| 26 x 10 | 26 Tens = 20 Tens + 6 Tens | 260 |
| 38 x 10 | 38 Tens | 380 |
| 53 x 10 | 53 Tens | 530 |

### Multiplying by 20, 30, 40...

| Problem | Strategy | Answer |
|---------|----------|--------|
| 12 x 20 | 12 x 2 Tens = 24 Tens | **240** |
| 24 x 40 | 24 x 4 Tens = 96 Tens | **960** |
| 13 x 30 | 13 x 3 Tens = 39 Tens | **390** |
| 50 x 60 | 50 x 6 Tens = 300 Tens | **3000** |

> **Key idea:** To multiply by 20, multiply by 2 first, then add a zero. To multiply by 30, multiply by 3 first, then add a zero. And so on.

## 2-Digit x 2-Digit Multiplication

To multiply two 2-digit numbers, we can use a **grid method** (also called the box method).

### Example: 15 x 14 = ?

Split both numbers: 15 = 10 + 5 and 14 = 10 + 4

|  x  | 10 | 4 |
|-----|-----|-----|
| **10** | 100 | 40 |
| **5** | 50 | 20 |

Add all the parts: 100 + 40 + 50 + 20 = **210**

![A grid method box showing 15 x 14 split into tens and ones for multiplication](grid-method-multiplication.png)

### Example: 64 x 152 = ? (Vande Bharat Mission)

During the COVID-19 pandemic, India's Vande Bharat Mission used 64 flights carrying 152 people each in the first week.

Split: 64 = 60 + 4 and 152 = 100 + 50 + 2

|  x  | 100 | 50 | 2 |
|-----|------|-----|-----|
| **60** | 6000 | 3000 | 120 |
| **4** | 400 | 200 | 8 |

Add all parts: 6000 + 3000 + 120 + 400 + 200 + 8 = **9728 people**

## Division with 2-Digit Divisors

When dividing by a 2-digit number, we use the **partial quotient method** — taking away large groups first.

### Example: 324 / 14 = ?

How many coaches (seating 14 children each) are needed for 324 children?

| Step | Children taken | Coaches | Remaining |
|------|---------------|---------|-----------|
| 1 | 140 (10 coaches x 14) | 10 | 324 - 140 = 184 |
| 2 | 140 (10 coaches x 14) | 10 | 184 - 140 = 44 |
| 3 | 14 (1 coach x 14) | 1 | 44 - 14 = 30 |
| 4 | 28 (2 coaches x 14) | 2 | 30 - 28 = 2 |

Total coaches: 10 + 10 + 1 + 2 = **23 coaches**, with **2 children remaining**

The remaining number is called the **remainder**. Since there are 2 children left, we would need **24 coaches** in total (one extra for the remaining children).

### Example: 960 / 64 = ?

The snake-boat race in Kerala needs boats with 64 paddlers each for 960 participants.

| Step | People placed | Boats | Remaining |
|------|--------------|-------|-----------|
| 1 | 640 (10 boats x 64) | 10 | 960 - 640 = 320 |
| 2 | 320 (5 boats x 64) | 5 | 320 - 320 = 0 |

Total boats: 10 + 5 = **15 boats** (no remainder)

## Multiplying by 100

When we multiply by 100, every digit moves **two places** to the left:

| Multiplication | Think of it as | Answer |
|---------------|----------------|--------|
| 2 x 100 | 2 Hundreds | 200 |
| 5 x 100 | 5 Hundreds | 500 |
| 10 x 100 | 10 Hundreds | 1000 |
| 11 x 100 | 11 Hundreds | 1100 |
| 20 x 100 | 20 Hundreds | 2000 |
| 53 x 100 | 53 Hundreds | 5300 |

> **Pattern:** Multiplying by 100 adds **two zeros** to the number.

### Multiplying by 200, 300, 500...

| Problem | Strategy | Answer |
|---------|----------|--------|
| 11 x 200 | 11 x 2 Hundreds = 22 Hundreds | **2200** |
| 15 x 200 | 15 x 2 Hundreds = 30 Hundreds | **3000** |
| 5 x 500 | 5 x 5 Hundreds = 25 Hundreds | **2500** |
| 7 x 800 | 7 x 8 Hundreds = 56 Hundreds | **5600** |

## Dividing by 10 and 100

Division by 10 and 100 is the reverse of multiplication:

| Division | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| 60 / 10 | 6 |
| 400 / 10 | 40 |
| 600 / 10 | 60 |
| 600 / 100 | 6 |
| 400 / 100 | 4 |
| 1000 / 10 | 100 |

> **Pattern:** Dividing by 10 removes one zero from the end. Dividing by 100 removes two zeros.

## Useful Relationships

Looking at patterns helps us solve problems faster:

| Set of problems | Pattern |
|----------------|---------|
| 14 x 1 = 14, 14 x 10 = 140, 14 x 100 = 1400 | Each time we multiply by 10 more, the answer also gets 10 times bigger |
| 7 x 5 = 35, 7 x 50 = 350, 7 x 500 = 3500 | Same pattern with multiples of 5 |
| 44 x 10 = 440, 22 x 20 = 440 | Doubling one factor and halving the other gives the same product |
| 16 x 100 = 1600, 4 x 400 = 1600 | Same idea — the product stays the same |

## Real-Life Problems from the Transport Museum

### The Toy Train

A toy train has **15 coaches** with **14 children** per coach.
Total capacity = 15 x 14 = **210 children**

### India's Oldest Long-Distance Train

The **Punjab Mail** first ran on 12 October 1912. Today it runs between Mumbai and Ferozepur with **24 coaches**, each carrying **72 passengers**.
Total capacity = 24 x 72 = **1728 passengers**

### The Snake Boat of Kerala

The famous **Vallam Kali** (snake-boat race) features boats that are **30 to 35 metres long** and can be paddled by **64 to 128 people**. This tradition is over **800 years old**!

## Key Words to Remember

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Multiply by 10** | Move digits one place left, add a zero (e.g., 26 x 10 = 260) |
| **Multiply by 100** | Move digits two places left, add two zeros (e.g., 26 x 100 = 2600) |
| **Grid method** | A way to multiply by splitting numbers and using a grid/box |
| **Partial quotient** | A division method where we subtract large groups step by step |
| **Remainder** | The amount left over after division |
| **Double** | Multiply by 2 |
| **Half** | Divide by 2 |
| **Times table** | A list showing the products of a number multiplied by 1 through 10 |
| **Factor** | A number that is multiplied (in 6 x 14, both 6 and 14 are factors) |
| **Product** | The result of multiplying two numbers together |
