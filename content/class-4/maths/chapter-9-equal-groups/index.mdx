## Multiples — Skip Counting on the Number Line

When an animal jumps equal steps on a number line, it lands on special numbers called **multiples**.

| Animal | Jump size | Numbers it lands on (multiples) |
|--------|----------|-------------------------------|
| Frog | 3 steps | 3, 6, 9, 12, 15, 18, 21, 24... |
| Squirrel | 4 steps | 4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24, 28, 32... |
| Rabbit | 6 steps | 6, 12, 18, 24, 30, 36, 42, 48... |
| Kangaroo | 8 steps | 8, 16, 24, 32, 40, 48, 56, 64... |

The numbers that the frog lands on (3, 6, 9, 12...) are called **multiples of 3**.
The numbers that the squirrel lands on (4, 8, 12, 16...) are called **multiples of 4**.

### Common Multiples

Sometimes two animals land on the **same number**. These are called **common multiples**.

- Common multiples of 3 and 4: **12, 24, 36, 48...**
- Common multiples of 6 and 8: **24, 48, 72...**

> **Example:** To reach 48, the rabbit (jumping 6) takes 8 jumps, and the kangaroo (jumping 8) takes 6 jumps. Both reach the same number!

## Multiplication — Equal Groups

**Multiplication** means finding the total when you have **equal groups**.

### The Parts of Multiplication

In 12 x 3 = 36:
- **12** is the **multiplier** (number of groups)
- **3** is the **multiplicand** (size of each group)
- **36** is the **product** (the answer)

### Example: Gulabo's Lily Flowers

Each lily has **3 petals**. If there are 12 flowers:
- Petals in 10 lilies = 10 x 3 = 30
- Petals in 2 lilies = 2 x 3 = 6
- Petals in 12 lilies = 30 + 6 = **36 petals**

> **Tip:** Break the number of groups into tens and ones to make multiplication easier!

## The Multiplication Table

The multiplication table (times table) shows the product of any two numbers from 1 to 10.

### Patterns in the Multiplication Table

1. **Row 2** has all even numbers: 2, 4, 6, 8, 10...
2. **Row 5** ends in 0 or 5: 5, 10, 15, 20, 25...
3. Numbers in any **row** are the same as numbers in the matching **column** (e.g., row 7 = column 7). This is because 3 x 7 = 7 x 3.
4. Rows with **even** multipliers have all even products.
5. Rows with **odd** multipliers have alternating odd and even products.
6. **Row 8** ones digits repeat: 8, 6, 4, 2, 0, 8, 6, 4, 2, 0

## The Doubling Magic

**Doubling** means multiplying by 2. It is a very useful trick!

| Number | Double |
|--------|--------|
| 23 | 46 |
| 32 | 64 |
| 45 | 90 |
| 95 | 190 |
| 150 | 300 |
| 199 | 398 |
| 500 | 1000 |

> **Key fact:** When you double any number, the result is always **even**. You can never get an odd number by doubling!

## Multiplying by Multiples of 10

When the number of groups is a multiple of 10, there is a simple pattern:

| Single digit | Times 10 | Times 20 | Times 30 |
|-------------|----------|----------|----------|
| 3 x 4 = 12 | 10 x 4 = 40 | 20 x 4 = 80 | 30 x 4 = 120 |
| 6 x 8 = 48 | 10 x 8 = 80 | 60 x 8 = 480 | |

> **Pattern:** To multiply by 20, first multiply by 2, then add a zero. To multiply by 30, first multiply by 3, then add a zero.

## Multiplying 2-Digit Numbers by 1-Digit Numbers

Break the 2-digit number into **tens and ones**, multiply each part, then add.

### Example: 18 x 4 = ?

| Part | Calculation | Result |
|------|-----------|--------|
| 10 x 4 | Ten groups of 4 | 40 |
| 8 x 4 | Eight groups of 4 | 32 |
| **Total** | 40 + 32 | **72** |

### Example: 23 x 8 = ?

| Part | Calculation | Result |
|------|-----------|--------|
| 20 x 8 | Twenty groups of 8 | 160 |
| 3 x 8 | Three groups of 8 | 24 |
| **Total** | 160 + 24 | **184** |

## Multiplying by Multiples of 100

The same pattern works with hundreds:

| Single digit | Times 10 | Times 100 |
|-------------|----------|-----------|
| 3 x 4 = 12 | 30 x 4 = 120 | 300 x 4 = 1200 |
| 2 x 3 = 6 | 20 x 3 = 60 | 200 x 3 = 600 |
| 3 x 5 = 15 | 30 x 5 = 150 | 300 x 5 = 1500 |

> **Pattern:** To multiply by 100s, multiply the single digits first, then add two zeros.

## Division — Sharing Equally and Making Groups

**Division** is the opposite of multiplication. It means splitting a number into equal groups.

### Example: 58 wheels for tempos (3 wheels each)

How many tempos can be made? 58 / 3 = ?

We take away groups step by step:
- 10 tempos need 30 wheels. Remaining: 58 - 30 = 28
- 5 tempos need 15 wheels. Remaining: 28 - 15 = 13
- 3 tempos need 9 wheels. Remaining: 13 - 9 = 4
- 1 tempo needs 3 wheels. Remaining: 4 - 3 = 1

Total tempos: 10 + 5 + 3 + 1 = **19 tempos**, with **1 wheel left over**

> **Key idea:** In division, the amount left over is called the **remainder**. It must always be less than the number you are dividing by.

---

## Patterns in Division

Just like multiplication, division has patterns with 10s and 100s:

| Simple | Divide by 10s | Divide by 100s |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| 9 / 3 = 3 | 90 / 3 = 30 | 900 / 3 = 300 |
| 15 / 3 = 5 | 150 / 3 = 50 | 1500 / 3 = 500 |
| 8 / 4 = 2 | 80 / 4 = 20 | 800 / 4 = 200 |

---

## Two Types of Division Problems

### 1. Sharing Problems (How many in each group?)

"108 people need to cross a river in 9 boats equally. How many people per boat?"
108 / 9 = **12 people per boat**

### 2. Grouping Problems (How many groups?)

"A factory has 58 wheels. Each tempo needs 3 wheels. How many tempos can be made?"
58 / 3 = **19 tempos** (with 1 wheel remaining)

## Always True, Sometimes True, or Never True?

| Statement | Answer |
|-----------|--------|
| Multiplying by 10 gives 0 in the ones digit | **Always true** |
| Multiplying a number by 2 gives an odd number | **Never true** |
| Multiplying a number by 5 gives 5 in the ones digit | **Sometimes true** (only when multiplied by an odd number) |
| The number after an odd number is even | **Always true** |
| Halving any number gives an even number | **Sometimes true** |
| Adding 0 to a number increases it by 1 | **Never true** (adding 0 does not change the number) |

## Key Words to Remember

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Multiple** | A number you get by multiplying a number by 1, 2, 3, 4... (e.g., multiples of 3 are 3, 6, 9, 12...) |
| **Common multiple** | A number that is a multiple of two or more numbers |
| **Multiplication** | Repeated addition of equal groups |
| **Multiplier** | The number of groups |
| **Multiplicand** | The size of each group |
| **Product** | The result of multiplication |
| **Division** | Splitting a number into equal groups |
| **Remainder** | The amount left over after division |
| **Doubling** | Multiplying a number by 2 |
| **Halving** | Dividing a number by 2 |
| **Array** | Objects arranged in equal rows and columns |
