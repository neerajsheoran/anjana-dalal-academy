## What Do We Measure?

When we talk about how long, how tall, how wide, or how deep something is, we are talking about **length**. In everyday life, we use different words:

| Word | What it means | Example |
|------|--------------|---------|
| **Height** | How tall something is | Height of a person, a tree |
| **Length** | How long something is | Length of a table, a road |
| **Breadth / Width** | How wide something is | Width of a door, a book |
| **Depth** | How deep something is | Depth of a well, a swimming pool |

All of these are measured using the same units of length!

---

## The Metre

The **metre (m)** is the main unit for measuring length. Here are some useful facts:

- The height of most Class 4 students is **more than 1 metre**
- The length of your arm is **less than 1 metre**
- The height of a classroom door is **more than 1 metre** (usually about 2 metres)

### Half Metre and Quarter Metre

We can divide a metre into smaller parts:

| Part | Fraction | Length |
|------|----------|--------|
| Half metre | 1/2 m | Half of 1 metre |
| Quarter metre | 1/4 m | One-fourth of 1 metre |

Important relationships:
- **1 m = 1/2 m + 1/2 m** (two halves make a whole)
- **1 m = 1/4 m + 1/4 m + 1/4 m + 1/4 m** (four quarters make a whole)
- **1/2 m = 1/4 m + 1/4 m** (two quarters make a half)

---

## Estimating Lengths

Before you measure something, it helps to **estimate** (make a good guess). Here are some real-world lengths to help you estimate:

| Object | Approximate Length |
|--------|-------------------|
| A blue whale | About 30 metres |
| A giraffe's height | About 4 metres |
| A crocodile | About 6 metres |
| A bus | About 3 metres long |
| A cricket bat | About 1 metre |

> **Tip:** When estimating, compare the object to something you already know. For example, "Is this table longer or shorter than my outstretched arms?"

## The Centimetre — Measuring Small Things

For small objects like an eraser, a pencil, or a grain of rice, the metre is too big. We need a smaller unit called the **centimetre (cm)**.

If you look at a ruler (scale), you can see it is divided into small equal parts. Each small part is **1 centimetre**.

### The Key Relationship

**1 metre (m) = 100 centimetres (cm)**

From this, we can work out:
- **1/2 m = 50 cm**
- **1/4 m = 25 cm**

### How Big is 1 cm?

- The width of your **fingernail** is about 1 cm
- A **grain of wheat** is less than 1 cm
- An **eraser** is a few centimetres long

![A ruler showing centimetre markings with a finger placed on it to show 1 cm width](ruler-centimetre-scale.png)

> **Tip:** Place your finger on a ruler. The width of your finger (near the nail) is roughly 1 cm. You can use this to estimate small lengths!

---

## Using a Ruler (Scale)

To measure an object using a ruler:

1. Place the object at the **0 mark** (not at the edge of the ruler)
2. Read the number at the other end of the object
3. That number is the length in **centimetres**

### Measuring with a Broken Ruler

If your ruler is broken and does not start at 0, you can still measure! Just subtract the starting number from the ending number.

For example, if the object goes from the 3 cm mark to the 10 cm mark:
Length = 10 - 3 = **7 cm**

---

## Converting Between Metres and Centimetres

Since **1 m = 100 cm**, we can convert between the two:

| Metres | Centimetres |
|--------|-------------|
| 1 m | 100 cm |
| 2 m | 200 cm |
| 4 m | 400 cm |
| 6 m | 600 cm |

### Mixed Measurements

Sometimes a measurement uses both metres and centimetres:

| Height | Same as |
|--------|---------|
| 1 m 20 cm | 120 cm |
| 1 m 40 cm | 140 cm |
| 2 m 30 cm | 230 cm |
| 5 m 50 cm | 550 cm |

> **Example:** Ramu says his height is 120 cm. Shamu says his height is 1 m 20 cm. Both are saying the same thing! 100 cm + 20 cm = 120 cm = 1 m 20 cm.

## Perimeter — The Length of the Boundary

The **perimeter** is the total length around the outside (boundary) of a shape. To find the perimeter, add up the lengths of all the sides.

### Example: A Rectangle

If a rectangle has a length of 5 cm and a breadth of 3 cm:

```
       5 cm
    ┌─────────┐
    │         │ 3 cm
    │         │
    └─────────┘
       5 cm
```

Perimeter = 5 + 3 + 5 + 3 = **16 cm**

![A rectangle with sides labeled 5 cm and 3 cm, and arrows showing perimeter around the boundary](rectangle-perimeter.png)

### Example: A Square

If a square has sides of 4 cm each:

Perimeter = 4 + 4 + 4 + 4 = **16 cm**

### Important Points About Perimeter

- Different shapes can have the **same perimeter** even if they look very different
- To find the perimeter of any shape, walk around its boundary and add up all the side lengths
- Perimeter is measured in the same units as length (cm or m)

> **Real-life example:** If Bhola wants to build a brick boundary around his garden, he needs to know the perimeter to find out how many bricks he needs!

## Measuring in Real Life

Here are some practical measuring activities:

| Activity | What to measure | Unit to use |
|----------|----------------|-------------|
| Your height | Stand against a wall, mark, then measure | cm or m and cm |
| Length of your hand | From wrist to fingertip | cm |
| Length of your textbook | One end to the other | cm |
| Height of a chair | From the floor to the seat | cm |
| Length of a classroom | One wall to the other | m |
| Perimeter of your desk | Around all four sides | cm |

## Key Words to Remember

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Length** | The measurement of how long something is |
| **Height** | The measurement of how tall something is |
| **Breadth / Width** | The measurement of how wide something is |
| **Depth** | The measurement of how deep something is |
| **Metre (m)** | A standard unit of length; about the length of a big step |
| **Centimetre (cm)** | A smaller unit of length; 100 cm = 1 m |
| **Estimate** | A good guess about a measurement before actually measuring |
| **Ruler / Scale** | A tool used to measure length in centimetres |
| **Measuring tape** | A flexible tape used to measure longer lengths |
| **Perimeter** | The total length of the boundary of a shape |
| **Boundary** | The outer edge of a shape or area |
