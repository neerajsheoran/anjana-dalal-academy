## Measuring Weight

Everything around us has **weight**. Some things are light (like a feather) and some things are heavy (like a bag of rice). We measure weight to know exactly how heavy something is.

### The Kilogram (kg)

The **kilogram (kg)** is the standard unit for measuring weight of everyday objects.

- A 1 kg salt packet is a good reference for what 1 kg feels like
- Your school bag probably weighs a few kilograms
- You can carry objects that are a few kilograms, but very heavy objects need machines

### The Gram (g)

For lighter objects, we use **grams (g)**. Think of small things like a pen, a leaf, or a biscuit.

### The Key Relationship

**1 kilogram (kg) = 1000 grams (g)**

| Weight | In grams |
|--------|----------|
| 1 kg | 1000 g |
| 1/2 kg | 500 g |
| 1/4 kg | 250 g |

From this we can see:
- **2 packets of 500 g = 1000 g = 1 kg**
- **4 packets of 250 g = 1000 g = 1 kg**
- **10 packets of 100 g = 1000 g = 1 kg**

> **Tip:** 500 g = 1/2 kg (half a kilogram) and 250 g = 1/4 kg (quarter of a kilogram).

## Estimating Weight

Before weighing, it helps to estimate by **lifting** the object and comparing it to something you know.

| Object | Estimated Weight |
|--------|-----------------|
| A pen | 10 g to 15 g |
| A leaf | 1 g to 5 g |
| A cat | 3 kg to 5 kg |
| A wooden chair | 6 kg to 10 kg |
| An empty gas cylinder | About 15 kg |
| A tiger | 150 kg to 300 kg |
| An elephant | More than 1000 kg |
| A 1 litre filled water bottle | About 1 kg |

### When to Use kg and When to Use g

| Use **kilograms (kg)** for | Use **grams (g)** for |
|---------------------------|---------------------|
| Rice, flour, sugar (bags) | Spices, turmeric, medicines |
| Fruits and vegetables | Jewellery, coins |
| Animals, people | Small food items, biscuits |
| Furniture, suitcases | Letters, pens, pencils |

## Fun at the Vegetable Market

At Indian vegetable markets, a **weighing balance** (tarazu) is used to weigh fruits and vegetables.

**Example:** If half a pumpkin weighs 1 kg, the full pumpkin weighs about **2 kg**.

### Comparing Weights

To compare weights on a balance:
- The **heavier** side goes **down**
- The **lighter** side goes **up**
- If both sides are **equal**, the balance stays level

> **Example:** 250 g is half of 500 g. So you need 2 packets of 250 g to balance 1 packet of 500 g.

## Measuring Capacity

**Capacity** is how much liquid a container can hold. We measure it in **litres** and **millilitres**.

### The Litre (l)

The **litre (l)** is the standard unit for measuring capacity.
- A large water bottle holds about 1 litre
- A bucket holds many litres
- A glass holds less than 1 litre

### The Millilitre (ml)

For smaller amounts of liquid, we use **millilitres (ml)**.
- An eye dropper holds about 1 ml
- A teaspoon holds about 5 ml
- A dosing cup for medicine holds about 10 ml

### The Key Relationship

**1 litre (l) = 1000 millilitres (ml)**

| Capacity | In millilitres |
|----------|---------------|
| 1 l | 1000 ml |
| 1/2 l | 500 ml |
| 1/4 l | 250 ml |

From this:
- **2 bottles of 500 ml = 1000 ml = 1 l**
- **4 bottles of 250 ml = 1000 ml = 1 l**
- **10 bottles of 100 ml = 1000 ml = 1 l**

## Water Conservation

Even a small drip from a leaking tap wastes a lot of water over time!

- A slow drip can waste **several litres in a day**
- In a week, that becomes many more litres
- In a year, it could be **thousands of litres** wasted

> **Think about it:** Always turn off taps tightly. Fix leaking taps quickly. Every drop counts!

## Key Words to Remember

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Weight** | How heavy an object is |
| **Kilogram (kg)** | A unit of weight; 1 kg = 1000 g |
| **Gram (g)** | A smaller unit of weight; used for light objects |
| **Capacity** | How much liquid a container can hold |
| **Litre (l)** | A unit of capacity; 1 l = 1000 ml |
| **Millilitre (ml)** | A smaller unit of capacity; used for small amounts of liquid |
| **Weighing balance** | A tool used to measure weight by comparing with known weights |
| **Estimate** | A good guess about a measurement before actually measuring |
| **Regroup** | Exchange between units (e.g., 1000 g = 1 kg, 1000 ml = 1 l) |
