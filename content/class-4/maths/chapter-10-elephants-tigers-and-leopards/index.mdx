## India's Amazing Wildlife

India is home to some of the most magnificent animals in the world. Did you know?

- **3/4 of the world's tiger population** lives in India
- **3/5 of the world's Asiatic elephant population** is in India
- India also has thousands of leopards across its forests

In this chapter, we will use real data about Indian wildlife to learn **addition and subtraction of 4-digit numbers**!

## Addition of 4-Digit Numbers

Adding 4-digit numbers works just like adding 3-digit numbers — start from the **Ones** column and move left, **regrouping** whenever a column adds up to 10 or more.

### Example: Elephants in Karnataka and Kerala

Karnataka has **6049** elephants. Kerala has **3054** elephants. How many elephants in total?

```
    Th   H   T   O
     6   0   4   9
  +  3   0   5   4
  ─────────────────
     9   1   0   3
```

**Step by step:**
1. Ones: 9 + 4 = 13. Write 3, carry 1.
2. Tens: 4 + 5 + 1 = 10. Write 0, carry 1.
3. Hundreds: 0 + 0 + 1 = 1. Write 1.
4. Thousands: 6 + 3 = 9. Write 9.

**Answer: 6049 + 3054 = 9103 elephants**

## Adding Three 4-Digit Numbers

### Example: Leopards in Three States

Gujarat has **1355** leopards, Karnataka has **1131**, and Madhya Pradesh has **1817**. What is the total?

```
    Th   H   T   O
     1   3   5   5
     1   1   3   1
  +  1   8   1   7
  ─────────────────
     4   3   0   3
```

**Step by step:**
1. Ones: 5 + 1 + 7 = 13. Write 3, carry 1.
2. Tens: 5 + 3 + 1 + 1 = 10. Write 0, carry 1.
3. Hundreds: 3 + 1 + 8 + 1 = 13. Write 3, carry 1.
4. Thousands: 1 + 1 + 1 + 1 = 4. Write 4.

**Answer: 1355 + 1131 + 1817 = 4303 leopards**

> **Remember the regrouping rules:**
> - 10 Ones = 1 Ten
> - 10 Tens = 1 Hundred
> - 10 Hundreds = 1 Thousand

## Subtraction of 4-Digit Numbers

Subtraction also works the same way — start from the **Ones** column and move left, **borrowing** when needed.

### Example: Elephants in Assam and Meghalaya

Assam has **5719** elephants. It has **3965 more** elephants than Meghalaya. How many elephants are in Meghalaya?

Meghalaya's elephants = 5719 - 3965

```
    Th   H   T   O
     4  16  11
     5   7   1   9
  -  3   9   6   5
  ─────────────────
     1   7   5   4
```

**Step by step:**
1. Ones: 9 - 5 = 4. Write 4.
2. Tens: 1 - 6? Cannot do! Borrow 1 Hundred (= 10 Tens). Now 11 - 6 = 5. Write 5.
3. Hundreds: 6 (after borrowing) - 9? Cannot do! Borrow 1 Thousand (= 10 Hundreds). Now 16 - 9 = 7. Write 7.
4. Thousands: 4 (after borrowing) - 3 = 1. Write 1.

**Answer: 5719 - 3965 = 1754 elephants in Meghalaya**

## The Addition Chart

An addition chart shows what happens when you add numbers from 0 to 12.

### Patterns in the Addition Chart

1. Every number in the chart appears **in a diagonal line** from top-right to bottom-left
2. Numbers along each diagonal are the **same**
3. If you look at a 2x2 window (box) anywhere in the chart:
   - The sum of numbers in each row is the **same**
   - The sum of numbers in each column is the **same**
   - The sum of the two diagonal numbers is also the **same**
4. Rows with **even** starting numbers have even sums when added to even numbers
5. The chart is **symmetric** — the numbers in row 5 are the same as column 5

## Reverse and Add

Here is a fun pattern with 2-digit numbers:

1. Take any 2-digit number, say **27**
2. Reverse its digits: **72**
3. Add them: 27 + 72 = **99**

| Number | Reverse | Sum |
|--------|---------|-----|
| 27 | 72 | 99 |
| 36 | 63 | 99 |
| 18 | 81 | 99 |
| 45 | 54 | 99 |
| 23 | 32 | 55 |
| 14 | 41 | 55 |

> **Pattern:** Numbers whose digits add up to the same total will give the same "reverse and add" sum! For example, 2+7 = 9, 3+6 = 9, 1+8 = 9 — and all give a sum of 99.

## Mental Maths Strategies

You can solve many problems **in your head** using clever tricks:

| Problem | Strategy | Answer |
|---------|----------|--------|
| 8787 - 99 | Subtract 100, then add 1 | **8688** |
| 4596 + 104 | Add 100, then add 4 | **4700** |
| 3459 + 21 | Add 20, then add 1 | **3480** |
| 4990 + 310 | Add 10 to get 5000, then add 300 | **5300** |
| 7844 - 15 | Subtract 20, then add 5; or subtract 10 then 5 | **7829** |
| 3999 + 290 | Add 1 to get 4000, then add 289 | **4289** |

## Comparing Without Calculating

You can sometimes compare sums and differences **without doing the full calculation**:

- **54 + 97 vs 54 + 90**: The first has a larger number added, so it is bigger
- **84 - 68 vs 90 - 68**: Subtracting the same number from a bigger starting number gives more
- **73 - 54 vs 73 - 56**: Subtracting more leaves less, so the first is bigger

> **Tip:** If one number stays the same and the other changes, just look at how the changed number affects the result!

## Handling Money — Deposit Slips

When depositing money at a bank, you fill out a **deposit slip** listing how many notes and coins of each type you have:

| Note/Coin | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| ₹500 note | ₹500 |
| ₹100 note | ₹100 |
| ₹50 note | ₹50 |
| ₹10 note | ₹10 |
| ₹5 coin | ₹5 |
| ₹2 coin | ₹2 |
| ₹1 coin | ₹1 |

**Example:** To deposit ₹2045:
- 3 notes of ₹500 = ₹1500
- 3 notes of ₹100 = ₹300
- 2 notes of ₹50 = ₹100
- 14 notes of ₹10 = ₹140
- 1 coin of ₹5 = ₹5
- **Total = ₹2045**

> The same amount can be made with **different combinations** of notes and coins!

## Key Words to Remember

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **4-digit number** | A number from 1000 to 9999, with Thousands, Hundreds, Tens, and Ones places |
| **Regrouping (carry)** | In addition, when a column totals 10 or more, carry 1 to the next column |
| **Borrowing** | In subtraction, taking 1 from the next column when the top digit is smaller |
| **Estimate** | A rough guess to check if your answer makes sense |
| **Mental maths** | Solving problems in your head using clever strategies |
| **Deposit slip** | A form used to record money being put into a bank account |
| **Reverse and add** | A number pattern where you reverse a number's digits and add them together |
| **Common sum** | Numbers that are always the same when looking at patterns in addition charts |
