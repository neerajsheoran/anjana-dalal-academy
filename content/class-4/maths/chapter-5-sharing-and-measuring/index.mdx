## Parts and Wholes

When we share something equally, we divide the whole into **equal parts**. Each equal part is called a **fraction**.

### What is a Half?

When you divide something into **2 equal parts**, each part is called a **half**.

We write half as **1/2**.

Two halves together make one whole: **1/2 + 1/2 = 1 whole**

### What is a Quarter?

When you divide something into **4 equal parts**, each part is called a **quarter** (or one-fourth).

We write quarter as **1/4**.

```
Whole:        [████████████████]
Four quarters: [████] [████] [████] [████]
                1/4    1/4    1/4    1/4
```

Four quarters together make one whole: **1/4 + 1/4 + 1/4 + 1/4 = 1 whole**

> **Ikra's Clever Trick:** Ikra asked her sister Samina, "Do you want half of the paper or two quarters?" Samina chose two quarters thinking it was more. But 1/2 = 2/4 — they are the same amount! Two quarters is the same as one half.

## Sharing — The Dhokla Story

Imagine a yummy dhokla being shared equally among friends:

| Number of people | Each person's share | Fraction |
|-----------------|-------------------|----------|
| 2 people | Half the dhokla | **1/2** |
| 3 people | One-third of the dhokla | **1/3** |
| 4 people | One-quarter of the dhokla | **1/4** |
| 5 people | One-fifth of the dhokla | **1/5** |
| 6 people | One-sixth of the dhokla | **1/6** |
| 8 people | One-eighth of the dhokla | **1/8** |

> **Important Rule:** When you share among **more** people, each person gets a **smaller** piece. So 1/8 is smaller than 1/4, and 1/4 is smaller than 1/2.

## Understanding Fractions

A fraction tells us **how many equal parts** we are talking about out of the total.

Every fraction has two numbers:

- The **bottom number** (denominator) tells us how many **equal parts** the whole is divided into
- The **top number** (numerator) tells us **how many parts** we are talking about

### Examples

| Fraction | Top number | Bottom number | Meaning |
|----------|-----------|---------------|---------|
| **1/3** | 1 | 3 | 1 part out of 3 equal parts |
| **2/5** | 2 | 5 | 2 parts out of 5 equal parts |
| **3/4** | 3 | 4 | 3 parts out of 4 equal parts |
| **4/5** | 4 | 5 | 4 parts out of 5 equal parts |
| **5/5** | 5 | 5 | 5 parts out of 5 = the whole! |

## Idha's Flower Garden

Idha has a garden divided into **5 equal parts** for different flowers.

If she plants Rose in 2 parts out of 5, that is **2/5** (two-fifths) of the garden.

| Flower | Parts used | Fraction |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| Mogra | 1 part | 1/5 (one-fifth) |
| Rose | 2 parts | 2/5 (two-fifths) |
| Lily | 1 part | 1/5 (one-fifth) |
| Marigold | 1 part | 1/5 (one-fifth) |

All the parts together: 1/5 + 2/5 + 1/5 + 1/5 = **5/5 = 1 whole garden**

## Karan's Dream Dosas

In India, dosa is a favourite food! Imagine a dosa divided into parts with different toppings:

A dosa split into **4 equal parts** could have:
- 1/4 Classic Potato
- 1/4 Spicy Onion
- 1/4 Chilly Paneer
- 1/4 Tangy Tomato

A dosa split into **2 equal parts** could have:
- 1/2 Spicy Onion
- 1/2 Tangy Tomato

A dosa split into **3 equal parts** could have:
- 2/3 Spicy Onion (2 parts out of 3)
- 1/3 Classic Potato (1 part out of 3)

> **Key idea:** The parts must always add up to the whole! If the dosa is divided into 3 parts, then 1/3 + 1/3 + 1/3 = 3/3 = 1 whole dosa.

## Comparing Fractions

### When the Top Number is the Same (like 1)

When comparing fractions that all have **1 on top** (like 1/2, 1/3, 1/4, 1/5):

**The bigger the bottom number, the smaller the fraction.**

| Comparison | Which is bigger? | Why? |
|-----------|-----------------|------|
| 1/4 vs 1/5 | **1/4** is bigger | Dividing into 4 parts gives bigger pieces than dividing into 5 parts |
| 1/6 vs 1/9 | **1/6** is bigger | Dividing into 6 parts gives bigger pieces than dividing into 9 parts |
| 1/2 vs 1/8 | **1/2** is bigger | Half is much bigger than one-eighth |

> **Think of it this way:** If you share a roti among 4 friends, each gets more than if you share it among 8 friends!

## Fractions of a Group

Fractions work with groups of objects too, not just single things.

### Example: 12 Cookies Shared Equally

| Shared among | Each person gets | Fraction |
|-------------|-----------------|----------|
| 2 children | 6 cookies | 1/2 of 12 = 6 |
| 3 children | 4 cookies | 1/3 of 12 = 4 |
| 4 children | 3 cookies | 1/4 of 12 = 3 |
| 6 children | 2 cookies | 1/6 of 12 = 2 |

To find a fraction of a group, **divide** the total by the bottom number.

### Example: Finding 1/4 of 8 Diyas

Divide 8 diyas into 4 equal groups: each group has **2 diyas**.
So, 1/4 of 8 = **2 diyas**.

## Equal Fractions (Equivalent Fractions)

Sometimes different fractions show the **same amount**. These are called **equivalent fractions** or **equal fractions**.

Try this with paper folding:
1. Fold a paper into **3 equal parts** and colour 1 part → that is **1/3**
2. Now fold the paper **in half** again → you now have **6 parts**, with **2 parts coloured** → that is **2/6**
3. Fold again → you have **12 parts**, with **4 coloured** → that is **4/12**

So: **1/3 = 2/6 = 4/12** — they are all the same amount!

Similarly:

| Fraction | Equivalent Fraction |
|----------|-------------------|
| 1/2 | 2/4 = 3/6 = 4/8 = 5/10 |
| 1/3 | 2/6 = 3/9 = 4/12 |
| 1/4 | 2/8 = 3/12 |

## Fractions Must Have Equal Parts

For a fraction to be correct, the parts **must be equal in size**.

If you divide a square into 4 parts but the parts are **not the same size**, you cannot call each part 1/4.

> **Be careful!** Always check that the parts are truly equal before writing a fraction. Drawing extra lines can help you see if the parts are the same size.

## Key Words to Remember

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Fraction** | A number that shows a part of a whole (e.g., 1/2, 3/4) |
| **Half (1/2)** | One of two equal parts of a whole |
| **Quarter (1/4)** | One of four equal parts of a whole |
| **Third (1/3)** | One of three equal parts of a whole |
| **Numerator** | The top number of a fraction — tells how many parts we have |
| **Denominator** | The bottom number of a fraction — tells how many equal parts the whole is divided into |
| **Whole** | The complete object or group before dividing |
| **Equal parts** | Parts that are exactly the same size |
| **Equivalent fractions** | Different fractions that show the same amount (e.g., 1/2 = 2/4) |
| **Fraction chart** | A diagram that compares different fractions side by side |
