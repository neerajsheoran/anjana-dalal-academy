## A Trip to Mawlynnong

Mawlynnong is a small village in Meghalaya, in the East Khasi Hills. It is famous as **Asia's Cleanest Village** and is also called **God's Own Garden**. The village has about 77 families and 414 people, all from the Khasi tribe. It is 79 km from Shillong.

In this chapter, we will learn **addition and subtraction** through the story of a school trip to this beautiful village!

## Addition — Adding Numbers Together

### Adding 2-Digit Numbers with Regrouping

When we add two numbers and the ones column adds up to **10 or more**, we need to **regroup** (carry over) to the tens column.

**Example:** 24 + 28 = ?

| Step | What we do |
|------|-----------|
| 1. Add the **Ones**: 4 + 8 = 12 | 12 Ones = **1 Ten and 2 Ones** |
| 2. Write 2 in the Ones column | Carry 1 Ten to the Tens column |
| 3. Add the **Tens**: 2 + 2 + 1 (carry) = 5 | Write 5 in the Tens column |

```
    T  O
    2  4
  + 2  8
  ------
    5  2
```

**Answer: 24 + 28 = 52**

### Adding 3-Digit Numbers with Regrouping

**Example:** 438 + 476 = ?

| Step | What we do |
|------|-----------|
| 1. Add the **Ones**: 8 + 6 = 14 | 14 Ones = 1 Ten and 4 Ones. Write 4, carry 1. |
| 2. Add the **Tens**: 3 + 7 + 1 = 11 | 11 Tens = 1 Hundred and 1 Ten. Write 1, carry 1. |
| 3. Add the **Hundreds**: 4 + 4 + 1 = 9 | Write 9 in the Hundreds column |

```
    H  T  O
    4  3  8
  + 4  7  6
  ---------
    9  1  4
```

**Answer: 438 + 476 = 914**

> **Key Rule:** When any column adds up to 10 or more, **regroup** by carrying 1 to the next column on the left. 10 Ones = 1 Ten. 10 Tens = 1 Hundred. 10 Hundreds = 1 Thousand.

## Estimating Before You Calculate

Before adding or subtracting, it is a good idea to **estimate** (make a rough guess) so you know if your answer makes sense.

**How to estimate:**
- Round each number to the nearest ten or hundred
- Add or subtract the rounded numbers

**Example:** 438 + 476
- 438 is close to 440, and 476 is close to 480
- 440 + 480 = 920
- So the answer should be close to 920 (actual answer is 914)

## Subtraction — Taking Away

### Subtracting 2-Digit Numbers with Regrouping

When the ones digit on top is **smaller** than the ones digit below, we need to **regroup** (borrow) from the tens.

**Example:** 83 - 46 = ?

| Step | What we do |
|------|-----------|
| 1. Ones: 3 is less than 6, so regroup | Borrow 1 Ten from 8 Tens. Now we have 7 Tens and 13 Ones |
| 2. Subtract Ones: 13 - 6 = 7 | Write 7 |
| 3. Subtract Tens: 7 - 4 = 3 | Write 3 |

```
    T  O
    7  13
    8  3
  - 4  6
  ------
    3  7
```

**Answer: 83 - 46 = 37**

### Subtracting 3-Digit Numbers with Regrouping

**Example:** 310 - 179 = ?

| Step | What we do |
|------|-----------|
| 1. Ones: 0 is less than 9. Borrow from Tens. | But Tens is 1, so borrow from Hundreds first. |
| 2. Regroup: 3 H becomes 2 H + 10 T. Then 10 T becomes 9 T + 10 O. | Now: 2 H, 10 T, 10 O |
| 3. Subtract Ones: 10 - 9 = 1 | |
| 4. Subtract Tens: 10 - 7 = 3 | (we used 1 Ten, so 9 remaining, but we gave 1 to Ones, so actually 0 T becomes 10 T after borrow) |
| 5. Subtract Hundreds: 2 - 1 = 1 | |

```
    H  T  O
    2  10 10
    3  1  0
  - 1  7  9
  ---------
    1  3  1
```

**Answer: 310 - 179 = 131**

> **Key Rule:** When a digit on top is smaller than the digit below, **regroup** by borrowing 1 from the column on the left. 1 Ten = 10 Ones. 1 Hundred = 10 Tens.

## Returning Change — A Real-Life Skill

When you buy something and pay more than the cost, you get **balance** (change) back. The balance is found by **subtracting** the cost from the amount paid.

**Balance = Amount Paid - Cost**

| Cost | Paid | Balance |
|------|------|---------|
| ₹113 | ₹150 | ₹37 |
| ₹435 | ₹500 | ₹65 |
| ₹185 | ₹200 | ₹15 |
| ₹149 | ₹500 | ₹351 |

> **Example from the market:** Daisy and Lou buy items from Sapan Dada's vegetable cart. If 1 kg of beans costs ₹53 and 1 kg of onion costs ₹32, the total is 53 + 32 = ₹85. If they pay ₹100, the balance is 100 - 85 = ₹15.

## Quick Mental Strategies

Sometimes you can add or subtract quickly in your head:

| Problem | Strategy | Answer |
|---------|----------|--------|
| 326 + 25 | Add 20, then add 5: 346 + 5 | **351** |
| 675 + 5 | Just add 5 to ones | **680** |
| 410 - 12 | 410 - 10 = 400, then 400 - 2 | **398** |
| 204 - 10 | Just subtract 10 from tens | **194** |
| 811 + 99 | Add 100, then subtract 1: 911 - 1 | **910** |
| 945 - 19 | Subtract 20, then add 1: 925 + 1 | **926** |

> **Tip:** Adding 99 is the same as adding 100 and then subtracting 1. Subtracting 19 is the same as subtracting 20 and then adding 1. These tricks make mental maths faster!

## Word Problems — Think Before You Calculate

When solving word problems, follow these steps:

1. **Read** the problem carefully
2. **Understand** what is being asked — do you need to add or subtract?
3. **Estimate** the answer first
4. **Calculate** using addition or subtraction
5. **Check** — does your answer make sense?

### When to Add
- Finding the **total** of two or more groups
- Finding **how many altogether**

### When to Subtract
- Finding **how many more** or **how many less**
- Finding **how many are left**
- Finding the **difference** between two numbers

> **Example:** Ram Chacha got 264 mangoes last year and 527 this year. How many **more** mangoes did he get this year? This is a subtraction problem: 527 - 264 = **263 more mangoes**.

## Key Words to Remember

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Addition** | Combining two or more numbers to find the total (sum) |
| **Subtraction** | Taking one number away from another to find the difference |
| **Regrouping** | Exchanging units from one place to another (e.g., 10 Ones = 1 Ten) |
| **Estimate** | A rough calculation or guess made before finding the exact answer |
| **Sum** | The result of adding two or more numbers |
| **Difference** | The result of subtracting one number from another |
| **Balance** | The money returned when you pay more than the cost |
| **Carry over** | Moving extra units to the next place when adding (same as regrouping) |
| **Borrow** | Taking units from the next place when subtracting (same as regrouping) |
| **Word problem** | A maths question written as a story or real-life situation |
