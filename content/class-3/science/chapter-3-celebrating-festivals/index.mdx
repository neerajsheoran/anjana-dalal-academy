## A Trip to the Mountains

**Rishi** lives in Jammu. He is travelling by bus to the Himalayas with his parents to visit his **Mama** (mother's brother), **Mami** (Mama's wife), and cousins **Chiya** and **Nonikaa**. They live in a small village near the mountains.

The bus ride is beautiful! The roads have **hairpin turns**, and the hills are covered with colourful flowers. Far away, Rishi can see tall trees of many kinds.

![A bus travelling on a winding mountain road with the Himalayas in the background](himalayan-road-trip.png)

> The **Himalayas** are the most majestic mountains in the world. They lie in the northern part of India. The word "Himalaya" means **"home of snow"**. The highest peak in the world, **Mount Everest** (about 8,848 metres), lies in our neighbouring country, Nepal.

---

## Road Signs and Safety

When we travel, we see many **signboards** on the road. These signs help keep us safe.

| Sign | What It Means |
|---|---|
| **No Parking** | You cannot park your vehicle here |
| **No Horn Please** | Do not honk your horn in this area |
| **Speed Breaker** | Slow down, there is a bump ahead |
| **No U-Turn** | You cannot turn back from here |
| **Men at Work** | Workers are working on the road -- drive carefully |
| **School Ahead** | A school is nearby -- slow down |

**Safety tips while travelling:**

- Always wear a seatbelt in a car
- Wear a helmet when riding a bicycle
- Walk on the footpath, not on the road
- Look both ways before crossing the road

![Common road safety signs including no parking, speed breaker, and school ahead](road-safety-signs.png)

## The Festival of Flowers

When Rishi reaches his Mama's village, he sees a garden full of colourful flowers. He recognises **rose**, **marigold**, and **hibiscus**, but there are many new flowers too.

Nonikaa points to a beautiful flower and says, "This is a **tulip**." She tells Rishi about the **Kashmir Tulip Festival** that happens every spring.

Rishi shares that in Jammu, they celebrate a spring festival too. They place **mustard** and **buransh** (Rhododendron) flowers at the doorstep of each home. The elders bless the children and give them toffees and sweets.

---

## Spring -- A Season of Celebration

**Spring** is a very special season in India. After the cold winter, the sun shines brightly, flowers bloom, grass grows, and new leaves appear on trees.

Many festivals are celebrated during spring across India:

| Festival | Where It Is Celebrated | Special Feature |
|---|---|---|
| **Kashmir Tulip Festival** | Kashmir | Beautiful tulip gardens |
| **Buransh Festival** | Jammu / Uttarakhand | Red rhododendron flowers |
| **Vishu** | Kerala | Vishu Kani display with yellow amaltas flowers, fruits and vegetables |
| **Sarhul** | Jharkhand | Sakhua flowers and germinated grains offered to the Earth and Sun |
| **Holi** | All over India | Festival of colours |

> **Vishu Kani** is a special ritual in Kerala. On the first day of the Malayali new year, people arrange auspicious items at sunrise. Seeing these items is believed to bring a happy year ahead.

---

## Sarhul -- A Spring Festival of Jharkhand

The **Sarhul** festival is celebrated in the spring season in Jharkhand. People wear their **traditional clothes** and walk in a procession to the **sakhua tree**. They carry freshly grown grains and sakhua flowers.

Offerings are made to the **Earth** and the **Sun** with respect and thankfulness. People celebrate by **singing and dancing** together to the beat of drums like **dhol** and **nagada**.

## Festivals and Food

Every festival in India has its own **special food**! Here are some examples:

| Festival | Special Food |
|---|---|
| **Chhath Puja** | Thekua |
| **Bihu** | Pitha |
| **Christmas** | Plum Cake |
| **Holi** | Gujiya |
| **Onam** | Sadya (grand feast) |
| **Ugadi** | Holige |
| **Eid-ul-Fitr** | Seviyan (sweet vermicelli) |

People also wear **special new clothes** during festivals. Celebrations bring everyone together -- family, friends, and neighbours.

## Festivals in Every Season

India celebrates festivals in every season of the year:

| Season | Some Festivals |
|---|---|
| **Spring** | Holi, Vishu, Sarhul, Ugadi |
| **Summer** | Rath Yatra, Buddha Purnima |
| **Monsoon** | Teej, Onam, Janmashtami |
| **Winter** | Diwali, Christmas, Lohri, Makar Sankranti |

## Spending Time with Relatives

Visiting relatives is a wonderful part of growing up. Rishi had a great time with his cousins Chiya and Nonikaa. They shared stories, ate delicious **haakh** (a Kashmiri leafy vegetable) with hot rice, and learned about each other's festivals.

> Festivals and celebrations bring people of all ages together. They help us learn about different cultures, foods, and traditions from across India.

---

## Key Points to Remember

- India has many **festivals** celebrated in every season of the year.
- **Spring** is a special season when flowers bloom and many festivals are celebrated.
- Each festival has its own **special food, clothes, and traditions**.
- **Road signs** help keep us safe while travelling.
- The **Himalayas** are the highest mountains in the world.
- Visiting **relatives** and spending time with them is a joyful experience.
- Festivals bring people **together** and teach us to respect nature, the Earth, and the Sun.
