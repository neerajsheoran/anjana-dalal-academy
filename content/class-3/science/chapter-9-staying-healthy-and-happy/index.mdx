## Being Clean and Bright

To stay healthy, we need to keep our body **clean** every day. Here are some important questions to ask yourself each morning:

- Did I **brush my teeth** today?
- Did I **take a shower**?
- Did I **comb my hair**?
- Did I **wash my hands** with soap?
- Are my **nails trimmed** and clean?

These simple habits keep our body **clean and free from diseases**. When we are clean and healthy, our body and mind work properly, and we feel happy.

---

## A Healthy Daily Routine

Every day, we should follow a healthy routine. Here are the important activities:

| Activity | How Often |
|---|---|
| **Brush your teeth** | Twice a day -- after waking up and before bed |
| **Take a shower** | Every day |
| **Wash hands with soap** | Before eating, after using the toilet, after coming home from outside |
| **Rinse your mouth** | Every time after eating food |
| **Drink water** | 6 to 8 glasses every day |
| **Eat healthy food** | Three meals a day with healthy snacks |
| **Sleep well** | At least 8 hours every night |
| **Play outside** | Every day for at least 1 hour |

> Following a good routine every day keeps both our **body and mind** healthy and happy.

---

## How Do We Brush Our Teeth?

**Moyna's Dadu** (grandfather) uses twigs from a **neem** or **babool** tree to clean his teeth! He chews the twig to make a brush and uses it to clean his teeth and massage his gums.

This old Indian practice is called **datun**. Long ago, people in India used twigs of certain plants like neem, karanj, and babool to clean their teeth. This tradition is so important that the **modern idea of toothbrushes** was actually inspired by it!

Today, we use toothbrushes and toothpaste to clean our teeth. We should brush our teeth **twice a day** to keep them strong and healthy.

> **Did you know?** The neem twig tastes bitter, but the babool twig tastes better!

## Traditional Cleaning Methods

In the old days, people used many natural things for cleaning:

| What They Cleaned | What They Used |
|---|---|
| **Teeth** | Neem and babool twigs (datun) |
| **Hair** | Reetha (soapnut) and Shikakai |
| **Body** | Besan (gram flour) and haldi (turmeric) |
| **Clothes** | Soapnut water |
| **Home** | Cow dung paste for floors |

You can even make an **eco-friendly home cleaner** using orange or lemon peels soaked in vinegar for two weeks! This is safe and natural.

## Animals Keep Themselves Clean Too!

Animals also like to be clean:

- **Monkeys** sit and groom each other, picking off insects from their fur.
- **Birds** use their beaks to clean and arrange their feathers.
- **Cats** lick their fur to keep it clean.
- **Elephants** spray water on themselves with their trunk.
- **Dogs** shake their body to remove water and dirt.

## Playing Outdoors and Exercise

Have you noticed that after running fast, you **breathe faster**, your **cheeks turn red**, and you start **sweating**? This happens because exercise makes our heart work harder and better. Exercise is very good for the body!

**Ways to exercise and stay fit:**

- Running and jumping
- Skipping rope
- Playing outdoor games
- Climbing stairs
- Walking and cycling
- Doing yoga

Harpreet's grandmother cannot move much because of pain in her knees. But she still walks around the house and loves to play **chess** with Harpreet. She says that both the **mind and body** should stay active!

> Exercise keeps our heart strong, our muscles healthy, and our mind sharp.

## Playing Safely

While having fun, it is very important to **stay safe**. Here is how to know if a place is safe to play:

| Place | Safe or Unsafe? | Why? |
|---|---|---|
| **A park** | Safe | Open space with no traffic |
| **A school ground** | Safe | Meant for children to play |
| **A busy road** | Unsafe | Moving vehicles can hurt you |
| **A roof without railings** | Unsafe | You could fall off |
| **A construction site** | Unsafe | Heavy materials and machines |

---

## Staying Safe from Strangers

When we are in public places, we may meet **strangers** (people we do not know). We should be careful and follow these rules:

- **Never take sweets or gifts** from strangers.
- **Never go with a stranger**, even if they say your parents sent them.
- **Never get into a stranger's car or vehicle**.
- Always stay with your **parents or a trusted adult** in public places.
- If a stranger tries to talk to you, say **"No, thank you"** and walk away.

> Your parents can decide a **secret password** with you. Only go with someone who knows this password.

## Key Points to Remember

- **Cleanliness** is very important -- brush your teeth twice, bathe every day, wash your hands often, and keep your nails trimmed.
- Indians have used **natural cleaning methods** for thousands of years, like neem twigs for teeth and reetha for hair.
- **Exercise** keeps our heart, muscles, and mind healthy -- play outdoor games every day.
- Always play in **safe places** like parks and playgrounds, not on roads or rooftops.
- **Never trust strangers** -- do not accept gifts from them or go with them.
- Follow a **healthy daily routine** with enough sleep, good food, exercise, and cleanliness.
- Both the **body and mind** need to stay active to be truly healthy.
