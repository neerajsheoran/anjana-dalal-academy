## Looking at Things Around Us

**Khushi** reached school early one morning. Sunlight shone through the windows and her classroom looked bright and beautiful. She looked around and noticed so many things -- tables, chairs, doors, windows, lights, bags, and books.

Khushi was curious: "Where have all these things come from? Who made them? What are they all made of?"

Let us find out!

---

## What Are Things Made Of?

Everything around us is made of some **material**. Here are some common materials and the things made from them:

| Material | Things Made From It |
|---|---|
| **Wood** | Tables, chairs, doors, pencils, shelves |
| **Metal** | Door hinges, nails, keys, spoons, coins |
| **Glass** | Window panes, light bulbs, mirrors, spectacles |
| **Plastic** | Bottles, bags, switches, rulers, toys |
| **Cloth (Fabric)** | Clothes, curtains, bags, towels |
| **Clay** | Pots, cups (kulhad), bricks, statues |
| **Rubber** | Erasers, tyres, balls, bands |

---

## Where Do Materials Come From?

Every material has a **source** -- a place where it comes from:

| Material | Source |
|---|---|
| **Wood** | Trees |
| **Metal** | Rocks deep inside the Earth (called **ore**) |
| **Glass** | Mostly made from **sand** |
| **Cloth** | Cotton comes from plants; wool from sheep; silk from silkworms |
| **Clay** | Soil and mud |
| **Rubber** | Rubber trees (and also made from petroleum) |
| **Plastic** | Made from petroleum (a liquid found deep underground) |

> **Did you know?** Glass is mostly made of **sand**! Sand is heated to a very high temperature until it melts and becomes glass.

---

## Transparent, Translucent, and Opaque

Have you noticed that you can see through some things but not through others?

| Type | What It Means | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Transparent** | You can see through it clearly | Glass, clean water, clear plastic |
| **Translucent** | You can see through it only partly (things look blurry) | Oiled paper, frosted glass, thin cloth, lampshade |
| **Opaque** | You cannot see through it at all | Wood, metal, stone, thick cloth, a plate |

Khushi could see the trees and flowers outside through the **glass window** because glass is **transparent**. But she could not see through the **wooden door** because wood is **opaque**.

> **Be careful!** Never look at the sun directly or through any object. It will harm your eyes.

---

## Coloured Transparent Materials

If you look through a **coloured transparent material** (like coloured glass or thin coloured plastic), the world around you looks different!

- Looking through **blue plastic** makes everything look blue.
- Looking through **yellow plastic** makes everything look yellow.
- A **blue object** seen through **yellow plastic** may look greenish!

This is a fun experiment you can try at home with coloured wrappers or cellophane paper.

---

## Properties of Materials

Different materials have different **properties** -- special features that make them useful for certain things:

| Property | What It Means | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Smooth** | Feels even and soft to touch | Glass, polished wood |
| **Rough** | Feels uneven and bumpy | Sandpaper, brick |
| **Shiny** | Reflects light and looks bright | Metals like steel and copper |
| **Dull** | Does not reflect much light | Cloth, rubber |
| **Hard** | Difficult to bend or break | Metal, stone, wood |
| **Soft** | Easy to press or bend | Cloth, sponge, cotton |
| **Flexible** | Can be bent without breaking | Rubber, fabric, thin plastic |
| **Rigid** | Cannot be bent easily | Wood, metal, glass |

---

## Sounds of Materials

When you tap a **metal spoon** on objects made of different materials, each one makes a **different sound**:

| Material | Sound |
|---|---|
| **Metal** | Ting-ting (sharp, ringing sound) |
| **Wood** | Thak-thak (dull, solid sound) |
| **Glass** | Ting (clear, high sound) |
| **Plastic** | Dub-dub (soft, flat sound) |
| **Cloth** | Almost no sound (very soft) |

You can compose your own beats using different materials -- like a musical orchestra!

---

## Does It Bend?

Some materials are **flexible** (they can bend), and some are **rigid** (they stay stiff).

- **Wood** is hard and rigid -- good for making tables and chairs.
- **Fabric** is soft and flexible -- good for making clothes and curtains.
- **Rubber** is flexible -- good for making balls and erasers.

Imagine if clothes were made of wood -- they would be so stiff and uncomfortable! That is why we use the **right material** for the right purpose.

---

## Solids, Liquids, and Gases

Everything around us can be put into one of three groups:

| Type | What It Is Like | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Solid** | Has a fixed shape, does not flow | Stone, spoon, ice, bottle, book |
| **Liquid** | Flows and takes the shape of its container | Water, milk, ink, honey, juice |
| **Gas** | Spreads out everywhere, has no fixed shape | Air, steam, smoke |

- A **solid** like a stone keeps its shape wherever you put it.
- A **liquid** like water takes the shape of whatever container you pour it into.
- A **gas** like air floats freely and fills up any space.

> Some things can be confusing! **Sand** looks like it flows, but each grain is actually a tiny solid. **Sponge** is soft and squishy, but it is a solid too.

---

## Natural and Artificial Things

Things around us can be divided into two types:

| Type | What It Means | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Natural** | Found in nature, not made by humans | Trees, birds, rocks, water, air, fruits |
| **Artificial** | Made by humans | Clothes, shoes, tables, buildings, plastic |

Natural things can be **alive** (like plants and animals) or **not alive** (like rocks, water, and air).

---

## Key Points to Remember

- Everything around us is made of some **material** -- wood, metal, glass, plastic, cloth, clay, or rubber.
- Materials come from different **sources** -- trees give wood, rocks give metal, sand gives glass.
- **Transparent** materials let us see through them, **translucent** materials let some light through, and **opaque** materials block light completely.
- Materials have different **properties** -- smooth or rough, shiny or dull, hard or soft, flexible or rigid.
- Things can be classified as **solids** (fixed shape), **liquids** (flow), or **gases** (spread freely).
- Things can also be **natural** (from nature) or **artificial** (made by people).
- We choose the **right material** for making different objects based on its properties.
