## Animals Love Living Near Plants

Have you noticed that wherever there are plants, there are animals too? If you look carefully, you will find many animals living **on, around, and even underneath** the plants around you.

Here are some animals and what they do near plants:

| Animal | What It Does Near Plants |
|---|---|
| **Elephants** | Feed on grass and leaves |
| **Moth** | Rests on a leaf |
| **Caterpillar** | Chews and eats leaves |
| **Earthworms** | Live in the soil near plant roots |
| **Tailorbird** | Sings sitting on a tree |
| **Woodpecker** | Pecks at tree trunks to find insects |
| **Ants** | Build their nests using leaves |
| **Frog** | Rests on leaves near water |
| **Butterfly** | Sits on leaves and flowers |
| **Squirrels** | Hide and rest in tree hollows |

> Where there are plants, there is always life! Plants provide food and shelter to many animals, birds, and insects.

---

## Life in the Soil

The **soil** under our feet is full of life! Soil is the topmost layer of the Earth's surface. It is made from:

- **Tiny pieces of broken rocks**
- **Old leaves, roots, and stems**
- **Living and dead insects and tiny creatures**

If you pick up a handful of soil near a plant, you might find:

- **Ants** and **termites** moving around
- **Small beetles** and **grasshoppers** hopping
- **Earthworms** and **millipedes** (especially after rain)

The soil can feel **rough, smooth, hard, dry, damp, or grainy**. It might contain **small stones, leaves, and insects**. After it rains, the same soil feels different -- it becomes wet and softer, and you might see more creatures in it.

---

## What Happens When It Rains?

During the **monsoon** season, you can see many more plants and animals appearing. Where do they come from?

- Some **insects** wait underground for the rains to come out.
- **Seeds** of plants that were lying in the soil for a long time start to **sprout** when they get water.
- More **grasses and small plants** start growing in wet soil.

> That is why everything looks green and full of life during the rainy season!

## How Animals Use Plants

Animals, birds, and insects use different parts of plants for **food, shelter, and resting**:

| Animal | How It Uses Plants |
|---|---|
| **Camels** | Eat leaves and thorny plants in the desert |
| **Owls** | Use tree hollows to lay eggs and raise their young |
| **Squirrels and Crows** | Use twigs to build nests |
| **Monkeys** | Love eating fruits from trees |
| **Sunbirds and Butterflies** | Drink sweet nectar from flowers |
| **Tailorbirds** | Stitch leaves together to build their nests |
| **Bats and Leopards** | Rest and shelter on tree branches |
| **Caterpillars** | Munch on leaves for food |

---

## Animals Use Plants for Food

Many animals depend on plants for their food:

- **Elephants** eat grass, leaves, and bamboo.
- **Cows and buffaloes** eat grass and fodder.
- **Monkeys** eat fruits like bananas and mangoes.
- **Butterflies and bees** drink nectar from flowers.
- **Caterpillars** eat soft, tender leaves.
- **Parrots** eat seeds and fruits.

---

## Animals Use Plants for Shelter

Plants are like **homes** for many animals:

- **Birds** build nests in the branches of trees using twigs, leaves, and grass.
- **Squirrels** live inside tree hollows.
- **Owls** make their homes in holes in old trees.
- **Insects** like ants and beetles live on tree bark and under leaves.
- **Leopards** rest on thick branches of tall trees.

## Sounds of Birds

If you close your eyes and listen, you can hear many **bird sounds** around you. Different birds make different sounds:

| Bird | Sound It Makes |
|---|---|
| **Pigeon** | Gutru gu, gutru gu |
| **Crow** | Caw, caw |
| **Sparrow** | Chirp, chirp |
| **Cuckoo (Koel)** | Ku-hoo, ku-hoo |

**When do you hear the most bird sounds?**

- **Early morning** -- birds are most active and sing a lot
- **Evening** -- birds return to their nests and call out

If you cup your ears with your hands and point towards the direction of a bird sound, you can hear it more clearly!

> If you do not hear bird sounds in your area, it might be because there are not enough trees and plants for them to live in.

## The Life Cycle of a Butterfly

One day, a girl named Suma found a **caterpillar** on a tagar plant. She watched it eat tender leaves every day. Here is what happened:

1. The caterpillar ate leaves continuously.
2. One day, it **stopped eating**.
3. The caterpillar formed a **cocoon** around itself.
4. After some time, it **came out of the cocoon**.
5. It had become a beautiful **butterfly**!
6. The butterfly **flew away** gently.

> This amazing change from caterpillar to butterfly is one of nature's most wonderful secrets!

---

## Key Points to Remember

- **Plants and animals live together** and help each other.
- **Soil** is full of life -- it contains insects, worms, roots, and tiny creatures.
- Animals use plants for **food, shelter, and resting**.
- After the **monsoon rains**, many new plants and animals appear.
- Different birds make **different sounds**, and we can hear them best in the early morning.
- A **caterpillar** changes into a **butterfly** through a cocoon -- this is a wonderful process of nature.
- Where there are **more plants**, there are **more animals, birds, and insects**.
