## So Many Kinds of Plants!

**Gopu**, **Simmi**, and **Raj** walk to school every day. On the way, they see beautiful flowers, mountains, and streams. They notice that plants come in many sizes and shapes -- very tiny, small, large, bushy, thin, curvy, straight, and tall.

Some leaves are rough and some are smooth. Some smell nice, like **tulsi**, **coriander**, **curry leaves**, **mint**, and **lemon grass**. Even mango leaves have a lovely smell when you rub them!

> Nature is amazing! Plants come in so many types. Let us learn about the different kinds of plants around us.

---

## Trees

**Trees** are the tallest plants. They have a big **trunk** made of wood and many **branches** that spread out with leaves on them. Trees have **roots** that go deep down into the soil.

Here are some common trees in India:

| Tree | Where You Can Find It |
|---|---|
| **Mango** | All over India -- gives us delicious fruits |
| **Neem** | Very common -- its leaves have medicinal value |
| **Peepal** | Found near temples and roadsides |
| **Banyan** | Has aerial roots that hang down from branches |
| **Coconut** | Coastal areas of India |
| **Jackfruit** | South and East India |
| **Khejri** | Rajasthan (desert areas) |
| **Chinar** | Kashmir |
| **Amaltas** | Known for its bright yellow flowers |

---

## Shrubs

**Shrubs** are medium-sized plants. They do not have one big trunk like a tree. Instead, they have **many brown, woody stems** growing close to the ground. Shrubs look bushy.

Common shrubs you may have seen:

- **Hibiscus** -- has pretty red flowers
- **Rose** -- beautiful flowers in many colours
- **Tulsi** (Holy Basil) -- found in many Indian homes, used in prayers and medicine
- **Curry leaf** -- used in South Indian cooking

**Did you know?** The pulses (dal) we eat come from shrubs!

| Pulse | Also Called |
|---|---|
| **Toor** | Pigeon peas (Arhar dal) |
| **Masoor** | Red lentils |
| **Moong** | Green gram |
| **Urad** | Black gram |

---

## Herbs and Grasses

**Herbs** are smaller plants with **soft, green stems** that do not become woody. They are much shorter than trees and shrubs.

Common herbs:

- **Mint** (pudina)
- **Tomato**
- **Coriander** (dhaniya)
- **Mustard** (sarson)

**Grasses** are a special type of herb. Their leaves are **thin and flat**, and their stems are **hollow**.

> Did you know? The grains we eat every day -- **rice (paddy)**, **wheat**, **bajra**, **jowar**, and **ragi** -- are all seeds of large grasses!

**Sugarcane** and **bamboo** are also types of grasses. Bamboo is the **tallest grass** in the world!

---

## Climbers and Creepers

Some plants have **thin and flexible stems** that cannot stand up on their own.

**Climbers** grow by climbing up walls, trees, or poles for support:

- **Money plant** -- climbs up walls and poles
- **Jasmine** -- climbs fences and walls with sweet-smelling flowers

**Creepers** grow by spreading along the ground:

- **Pumpkin** -- spreads its branches on the ground
- **Watermelon** -- creeps along the soil
- **Bottle gourd** (lauki) -- can creep or climb

| Type | How It Grows | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Climber** | Grows upwards using support | Money plant, Jasmine, Snake gourd |
| **Creeper** | Spreads along the ground | Pumpkin, Watermelon, Bottle gourd |

> Some plants can behave as both climbers and creepers depending on whether they find something to climb on!

---

## Parts of a Plant

Every plant has important parts that help it grow and stay alive:

| Part | What It Does |
|---|---|
| **Roots** | Hold the plant in the soil and absorb water and nutrients |
| **Stem** | Carries water from roots to leaves; holds the plant upright |
| **Leaves** | Make food for the plant using sunlight |
| **Flowers** | Help the plant reproduce (make new plants) |
| **Fruits** | Contain and protect the seeds |
| **Seeds** | Grow into new plants |

---

## Leaves -- All Shapes and Sizes

Leaves of different plants have different **colours, shapes, sizes, textures, and smells**.

| Leaf | Special Feature |
|---|---|
| **Peepal** | Heart-shaped with a pointed tip |
| **Mango** | Long and smooth, nice smell when rubbed |
| **Lotus** | Round and large, water rolls off it |
| **Khejri** | Tiny and feathery |

Raj's brother cannot see, but he can immediately **smell** fruits like mango, pineapple, jackfruit, guava, or jamun when they are nearby. Our nose is very good at recognising plants!

---

## Bark of Trees

**Bark** is the hard outer covering of a tree trunk. Different trees have different bark patterns. You can press a sheet of paper on the bark and gently rub a crayon over it to see the bark pattern -- this is called a **bark rubbing**.

If you look closely at tree bark, you may find **ants, insects, small plants, and even lizards** living on it!

---

## Fun Facts about Plants

- **Sugar** is produced from the sugarcane stem.
- **Bamboo** is the tallest grass in the world.
- **Rafflesia**, found in Mizoram, is the **biggest flower** in the world -- it is as big as an umbrella!

---

## Key Points to Remember

- Plants can be grouped into **trees, shrubs, herbs, grasses, climbers, and creepers**.
- **Trees** have a thick trunk and deep roots. **Shrubs** have many woody stems.
- **Herbs** have soft green stems. **Grasses** are a type of herb.
- **Climbers** grow upward using support. **Creepers** spread along the ground.
- Every plant has **roots, stem, leaves, flowers, fruits, and seeds**.
- Leaves come in different **shapes, sizes, colours, and smells**.
- Plants give us **food, shade, fresh air, and many other useful things**.
