## How Is Waste Created?

Every day, through our activities, we create a lot of **waste**. When we cook food, peel vegetables, unwrap packets, sharpen pencils, or throw away old things -- all of this creates waste.

Some common types of waste we create:

- **Vegetable and fruit peels**
- **Old newspapers and paper**
- **Plastic wrappers and bottles**
- **Old clothes and toys**
- **Empty tins and bottles**
- **Pencil shavings**
- **Old batteries**

If we do not manage our waste properly, it ends up in big **garbage piles** near our homes, schools, and on roadsides. This makes our surroundings dirty and unhealthy.

> Have you ever seen piles of waste lying around? How did it make you feel? We can all do something about it!

---

## Why Is Waste Harmful?

When waste is not managed properly, it causes many problems:

| Problem | How It Happens |
|---|---|
| **Animals get sick** | Cows and other animals can die by eating plastic mixed with food |
| **Air pollution** | When people burn plastic, it releases harmful gases |
| **Water pollution** | Wastewater flowing on roads carries germs |
| **Mosquitoes increase** | Dirty puddles and wastewater become breeding grounds for mosquitoes |
| **Diseases spread** | Germs in waste cause diseases in people living nearby |

---

## Three Golden Rules of Waste Management

People who manage waste well follow three important rules: **Reduce, Reuse, and Recycle**.

---

## Rule 1 -- Reduce

**REDUCE** means creating less waste in the first place. This is the most important rule!

**How to reduce waste:**

- Avoid food and drinks packed in **plastic wrappers** or bottles
- Use **cloth bags** instead of plastic bags for shopping
- Buy only what you **truly need**
- Use things until they are **completely worn out** before throwing them away
- Share old **toys and books** with those who need them

> The more things we buy, the more things we throw away. Buy less, waste less!

---

## Rule 2 -- Reuse

**REUSE** means using things again instead of throwing them away.

| Instead of Throwing Away | Reuse It Like This |
|---|---|
| **Old clothes** | Make quilts, bags, or dusters from them |
| **Old newspapers** | Use for gift wrapping or making paper bags |
| **Paper used on one side** | Use the blank side for rough work |
| **Plastic bottles** | Replace with a steel or copper bottle that you can refill |
| **Old calendars and boxes** | Make toys or decorative items |
| **Old sarees** | Make into beautiful quilts or bags |

> Our grandparents were experts at reusing things! They made quilts from old sarees and bags from old clothes. They gave old clothes to those in need.

![The three Rs: Reduce, Reuse, Recycle shown as a cycle with examples](reduce-reuse-recycle.png)

You can make many useful things from waste materials. For example, you can fold an old **newspaper** into a **paper bag** -- no scissors or glue needed, just folding!

---

## Rule 3 -- Recycle

**RECYCLE** means taking old, used materials and turning them into new things.

Materials that can be recycled:

| Material | What It Becomes |
|---|---|
| **Old paper** | New paper and cardboard |
| **Metal** | New metal objects |
| **Glass** | New glass bottles and jars |
| **Plastic** | New plastic items (though this is limited) |

To make recycling easier, we need to **separate our waste** into different types.

---

## Separating Waste -- Green and Blue Dustbins

You may have noticed **two coloured dustbins** at your school, park, bus stop, or market. They are there for a reason!

| Dustbin Colour | What Goes In | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Green Dustbin** | Dried leaves, fruit peels, vegetable waste, egg shells, rotten food | These materials **rot easily** and mix with the soil. They become **compost** (food for plants) |
| **Blue Dustbin** | Metal items, glass, plastic, paper | These materials **do not rot** but can be **recycled** into new things |

> Waste in the **green dustbin** becomes compost that helps plants grow. Waste in the **blue dustbin** is sent for recycling to make new objects.

## Clean Villages and Cities of India

Many places in India have become models of cleanliness by managing their waste well:

| Place | What Makes It Special |
|---|---|
| **Silluk, Arunachal Pradesh** | A "zero waste village" -- no trash on roads or corners. Streets lined with trees feel like walking in a garden! |
| **Chhota Narena** | One of the first villages to become waste-free |
| **Indore, Madhya Pradesh** | Known as one of India's cleanest cities |
| **Mysuru, Karnataka** | Famous for being a very clean city |

> The **Swachh Bharat Mission** (Clean India Mission) encourages all of us to keep our surroundings clean and manage waste properly.

---

## National Cleanliness Day

**30 January** is celebrated as **National Cleanliness Day** in India. On this day, we can take a pledge to:

- Always keep ourselves and our surroundings clean
- Not create too much waste
- Separate waste into green and blue dustbins
- Reduce, reuse, and recycle
- Build a **cleaner and greener** world

---

## Things You Can Do Every Day

Here are simple steps to keep your home, school, and neighbourhood clean:

- **Keep your room tidy** -- put things back in their place
- **Put waste in the correct dustbin** -- green for wet waste, blue for dry waste
- **Do not throw garbage on the road** -- always use a dustbin
- **Wrap sharp objects** (like broken glass) in cloth or paper so that sanitation workers are not hurt
- **Say thank you** to the people who clean your school and neighbourhood

> **Want not, Waste not.** Our aim should be to create as little waste as possible and manage it wisely.

---

## Be Grateful to Sanitation Workers

The people who keep our cities and villages clean -- **sanitation workers** -- deserve our respect and gratitude. They work hard every day, often in difficult conditions, to keep our surroundings clean and healthy.

You can show your gratitude by:

- Saying **"Thank you"** when you see them
- Not littering so their work is easier
- Making a small gift from waste material and giving it to them

---

## Key Points to Remember

- Our daily activities create a lot of **waste** -- peels, wrappers, old things, and more.
- If waste is not managed, it causes **pollution, diseases, and harm to animals**.
- The three golden rules are **Reduce, Reuse, and Recycle**.
- **Reduce** means creating less waste. **Reuse** means using things again. **Recycle** means making new things from old materials.
- **Green dustbins** are for wet waste (food scraps, leaves). **Blue dustbins** are for dry waste (metal, glass, plastic, paper).
- Many places in India like **Silluk, Indore, and Mysuru** are great examples of cleanliness.
- **30 January** is National Cleanliness Day.
- Always be **kind and grateful** to sanitation workers who keep our surroundings clean.
