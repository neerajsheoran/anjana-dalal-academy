## Animals and Plants in Our Homes

Many animals, birds, insects, and plants live with us in our homes. Some we **bring into our homes** on purpose, and some just **find their way in** on their own.

**Animals we welcome at home:**

- **Dogs** and **cats** as pets
- **Cows** and **goats** for milk
- **Parrots** and **fish** as pets
- **Tulsi** and other plants in our garden

**Uninvited visitors that come to our homes:**

- **Lizards** on walls
- **Ants** on the kitchen counter
- **Spiders** in corners
- **Squirrels** on window ledges
- **Pigeons** on balconies
- **Mosquitoes** and **flies**

> We should **never hurt animals**, even if we do not like them. Animals generally do not hurt us unless they feel scared or threatened.

## The Story of the Mango Tree

One day, a boy named **Shambhu** was walking through a forest with his father. They sat under the shade of a huge **mango tree**. The tree was home to **birds, squirrels, monkeys**, and many other animals.

Shambhu saw a monkey eating a mango and **throwing out the seed**. His father explained, "Do you know that a **new plant will grow** from this seed? This is how animals and plants help each other."

Shambhu picked up a mango that had dropped from the tree. His father told him, "We get so much from plants -- not just fruits and vegetables, but also **medicines, fibre for clothes, and wood for houses**."

## Uses of Plants

Plants give us many important things:

| What We Get | How We Use It |
|---|---|
| **Fruits and Vegetables** | We eat them for food |
| **Shade** | Trees give us cool shade on hot days |
| **Wood** | We use it to build houses and furniture |
| **Medicine** | Many plants are used to make medicines (like neem and tulsi) |
| **Fibre** | Cotton from plants is used to make clothes |
| **Fresh Air** | Plants clean the air we breathe |
| **Shelter for animals** | Birds, insects, and other animals live in trees |

---

## How Animals Help Plants

Animals also help plants in many important ways:

- **Squirrels** bury nuts in the soil and forget where they hid them. These nuts grow into new trees!
- **Monkeys and birds** eat fruits and throw away seeds far from the tree. New plants grow from these seeds. This is called **seed dispersal**.
- **Earthworms and insects** make the soil **fertile** (rich and healthy) through their waste, which helps plants grow better.
- **Bees and butterflies** carry **pollen** from one flower to another, helping plants make fruits and seeds.

> Thousands of trees are accidentally planted by squirrels! They bury nuts and then forget where they hid them.

## We Need Each Other

Plants, animals, and humans -- we all **need each other** to survive.

| Who Helps Whom | How |
|---|---|
| **Humans help animals** | By giving them food, water, and shelter |
| **Humans help plants** | By watering them and protecting them |
| **Animals help plants** | By spreading seeds and making soil fertile |
| **Plants help animals** | By giving food, shelter, and clean air |
| **Plants help humans** | By giving food, wood, medicine, and fresh air |
| **Animals help humans** | By giving milk, companionship, and more |

We get **milk** and loving company from cows, buffaloes, and goats. That is why we **respect** these animals and treat them with kindness and care.

## Make Friends with a Plant

You can choose a plant near your home or school and make it your **plant friend**! Here is how:

1. **Choose** a tree or shrub near you.
2. **Name** your plant, just like you would name a pet.
3. **Water** it every day and protect it.
4. **Observe** it closely -- look at its leaves, flowers, and fruits.
5. **Talk** to your plant when you feel happy or sad -- it can make you feel better!

> When you are feeling sad, upset, or angry, you can go to your plant friend and talk to it about how you are feeling. You can also talk to it when you are happy!

## Busy, Busy Ants!

Ants are one of the most hardworking creatures. You can do a fun activity to learn about them:

1. Place three types of food on the floor -- something **sweet** (sugar), something **fried** (papad), and something **cooked** (chapati or rice).
2. Watch what happens! Ants will soon come.
3. Notice which food they go to first.
4. See how many ants come and what they do with the food.

Ants usually love **sweet things** the most!

## The Grey Wagtails of Valparai

Here is a true story from a small town called **Valparai** in **Tamil Nadu**. Every year, children and teachers in the schools there watch for a special bird called the **Grey Wagtail** (known as **Vaalatti Kuruvi** in Tamil).

During the cold winter months, these birds fly from the mountains to the warmer parts of India. When the first Grey Wagtails arrive in Valparai, the children and teachers **celebrate** by putting up welcome posters and **distributing sweets** to everyone!

> These birds are called "Wagtails" because they keep **wagging their tails** up and down!

## Key Points to Remember

- **Plants, animals, and humans** all depend on each other to live.
- Plants give us **food, shelter, fresh air, medicine, wood, and fibre**.
- Animals help plants by **spreading seeds** and making the **soil fertile**.
- We should be **kind and caring** towards all animals and plants.
- We should **never hurt animals**, even the ones we do not like in our homes.
- Making a **plant friend** is a wonderful way to feel connected to nature.
- Living in **harmony** with nature means caring for plants and animals and letting them care for us too.
