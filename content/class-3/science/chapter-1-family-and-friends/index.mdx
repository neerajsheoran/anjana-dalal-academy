## Meet Bela and Her Family

Bela is a young girl who lives with her family. She has two brothers -- **Banku Bhaiya** (elder brother) and little **Bishu**. They also have a pet dog named **Shiru**. Bela's **Dadiji** (grandmother) and **Dadaji** (grandfather) live with them too.

Bela's family loves to spend time together. They play **chhupan-chhupai** (hide and seek) outside. They also play **peek-a-boo** with little Bishu. Their garden is a favourite spot where everyone sits, talks, shares jokes, and laughs together.

> A family is a group of people who love, care for, and support each other.

---

## Playing and Singing Together

On rainy days, the family sits under a small shelter in their garden. They play **antakshari** and **snakes and ladders**. Dadiji makes hot **pakodas** and tea while singing songs about the rain.

Dadaji has taught Banku and Bela many games that he used to play when he was young. He even joins in and plays with them! Shiru runs around barking and wagging his tail with joy.

**Bela and Banku never hurt Shiru. They share their food with him.**

---

## Types of Families

Families come in many shapes and sizes:

- A **small family** may have just parents and children.
- A **big family** may also include grandparents, uncles, aunts, and cousins.

| Family Member | What We Call Them |
|---|---|
| Father's father | Dadaji |
| Father's mother | Dadiji |
| Mother's sister | Mausi |
| Father's brother | Chacha |
| Mother's brother | Mama |

Every family is special. Some families have a few members, and some have many. Families also love and care for animals, trees, and plants. Animals like **cows, dogs, parrots, cats, and goats** can also be part of a family.

---

## Helping Each Other at Home

In a family, everyone helps each other with daily work:

- **Dadiji** oils and braids Bela's hair in the morning.
- **Parents** help each other with cooking, cleaning, and shopping.
- **Dadaji** loves gardening.
- **Banku** helps with cleaning and chopping vegetables.
- **Bela** helps Dadaji in the garden.

Their family friend **Kusum Mausi** is an excellent cook. She makes delicious sweets during festivals. Her daughter **Munni** is very good at making **rangoli** using dried flower colours, leaves, and fresh flowers.

> We all learn many things from our family members and neighbours.

---

## Things We Learn from Family

Our family teaches us many important things:

- **Grandparents** teach us old songs, games, and stories.
- **Parents** teach us how to cook, clean, and take care of the home.
- **Neighbours and friends** teach us new skills like making rangoli.

We also teach things to our family members! For example, children often help elders learn to use new things.

---

## Fun with Family and Friends

Bela's family loves going to the park together. They play **pitthoo** (a game where you pile up stones and hit them with a ball) with their friends. After playing, Dadaji buys everyone ice cream.

Some popular games children play with family and friends:

| Game | How to Play |
|---|---|
| **Chhupan-chhupai** | One person counts while others hide |
| **Pitthoo** | Stack stones and hit them with a ball |
| **Antakshari** | Sing songs starting with the last letter of the previous song |

---

## Caring for Others

Family members who need special care include:

- **Grandparents** who may be old and need help
- **Small children** who cannot do things on their own
- **Someone who is sick** who needs rest and medicine

We should also be **kind and caring towards animals**. Just like Bela and Banku are gentle with Shiru, we should never hurt or tease any animal.

---

## Key Points to Remember

- A **family** includes parents, grandparents, brothers, sisters, and sometimes pets too.
- Families can be **big or small** -- every family is special.
- Family members **love, care for, and help** each other.
- We learn many useful things from our **elders and neighbours**.
- We should be **kind to animals** and never hurt them.
- Playing, singing, and eating together makes a family **happy**.
- **Relatives** like uncles, aunts, and cousins may not live with us, but they are still part of our family.
