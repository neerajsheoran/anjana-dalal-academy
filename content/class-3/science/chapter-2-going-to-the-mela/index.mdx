## Getting Ready for the Mela

A **mela** (fair) has come to town! **Nita** and **Radha** are very excited. Their family is getting ready to go. The mela is a little far from home, so they need to take a bus.

Their **Dadiji** (grandmother) was not sure about going because her legs hurt. But Nita's father said, "Don't worry! We will take care of you." Everyone was happy when Dadiji agreed to come.

Their neighbours **Sneha** and **Rohit** are coming along too. **Mohan Chacha** (father's brother) and his family will meet them at the mela -- they are arriving by train and will take a city bus and an **autorickshaw** to reach the mela ground.

> Before a trip, we should always prepare well -- pack water bottles, carry a bag with essentials, and be ready on time.

---

## Travelling to the Mela

On the way to the mela, the group walked carefully on the road, keeping an eye on vehicles moving in both directions. Nita held Dadiji's hand and led her slowly to the bus stop.

**Bus number 401** arrived. The conductor and Nita's father helped Dadiji get on the bus. There was a **reserved seat for older people**, and Dadiji sat comfortably in it.

Rohit's father bought the tickets:

| Ticket Type | Number | Who |
|---|---|---|
| **Full tickets** | 5 | Adults |
| **Half tickets** | 4 | Children |

**Safety rules while travelling by bus:**

- Sit properly on your seat
- Do not jump around inside the bus
- Do not put your hands or head out of the window

---

## Different Ways to Travel

People use many types of transport to travel:

| Transport | Used For |
|---|---|
| **Bus** | Travelling within or between cities and towns |
| **Train** | Travelling long distances |
| **Autorickshaw** | Short trips within a city |
| **Bicycle** | Short distances near home |
| **Car** | Family trips |
| **Boat** | Crossing rivers and lakes |

---

## At the Mela

When they reached the mela ground, they saw many interesting things at the entrance:

- A **map** showing all the stalls and their locations
- An **ambulance**, **police jeep**, and **fire engine** parked nearby for safety
- A **Lost and Found booth** with volunteers to help find lost children or belongings

**Mohan Chacha** and Rohit quickly got a **wheelchair** for Dadiji so she could move around comfortably.

---

## Fun Things at the Mela

The children had a wonderful time at the mela! Here are some things they enjoyed:

| Activity | What They Did |
|---|---|
| **Toy stall** | Bought spinning tops, puppets, phirkis, and dolls |
| **Rides** | Rode the merry-go-round and the giant wheel |
| **Shopping** | Sneha and Radha bought bangles and trinkets |
| **Magic show** | Watched an exciting magic performance |

---

## Eating at the Mela

After all the fun, Mohan Chacha called the children to eat. But first, they **washed their hands properly** at the water point.

They enjoyed many delicious foods:

- **Gol gappas** (crispy shells filled with spicy water)
- **Chaat** (tangy snack)
- **Chhole kulche** (chickpea curry with bread)
- **Hot jalebis with rabri** (sweet fried spirals with thickened milk)
- **Kulfi** (Indian ice cream)

After eating, they made sure to **put all the waste in the dustbin**. Keeping the mela clean is everyone's responsibility.

---

## Safety at the Mela

Melas have many helpers to keep everyone safe:

| Helper | Their Job |
|---|---|
| **Police officers** | Keep order and help people |
| **Police dogs** | Help the police sniff out danger |
| **Firefighters** | Put out fires with fire engines |
| **Medical attendants** | Help injured or sick people with ambulances |
| **Volunteers** | Help find lost children at the Lost and Found booth |

---

## The Kumbh Mela

> The **Kumbh Mela** is the world's biggest festival! It is the largest gathering of people on Earth. It is held on the banks of holy rivers like the **Ganga**, **Yamuna**, **Godavari**, and **Shipra**. It takes place once every 12 years in **Haridwar**, **Prayagraj**, **Nashik**, and **Ujjain**.

---

## Key Points to Remember

- A **mela** is a fair where people enjoy games, food, rides, and shopping.
- We should always **care for our elders** -- help them walk, get them wheelchairs if needed, and give them comfortable seats.
- Always follow **safety rules while travelling** -- sit properly, do not lean out of windows.
- **Ambulances, fire engines, and police** are present at melas for everyone's safety.
- The **Lost and Found booth** helps reunite lost children with their families.
- **Wash your hands** before eating and **throw waste in the dustbin**.
- Many people work at a mela -- **toy sellers, halwais (sweet makers), puppeteers, bangle sellers, mehendi artists**, and more.
