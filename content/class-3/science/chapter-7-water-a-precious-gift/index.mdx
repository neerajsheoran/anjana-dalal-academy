## Rain, Rain, Come Again!

Water is one of the most precious gifts of nature. Most of the water we use comes from **rain**. But how do we know when it will rain?

Here are some clues that tell us rain is coming:

- **Dark clouds** appear in the sky
- The **wind** starts blowing strongly
- The air feels **humid** and heavy
- **Birds** start flying back to their nests

When it rains, we can observe many things:

- Is it a **heavy rain** or a **light drizzle**?
- Are the raindrops **big or small**?
- Is the rain falling **straight down** or at a **slant**?
- Sometimes the rain is so heavy that we see **sheets of water** instead of drops!

---

## Where Does Rainwater Go?

When it rains, a lot of water falls on the ground. But where does it all go?

| Where Rain Falls | What Happens |
|---|---|
| **On soil** | Water gets **absorbed** (soaked) into the ground |
| **On roads and concrete** | Water collects in **puddles** or flows away |
| **On slopes** | Water flows downhill and may join a **stream** |
| **Into rivers** | Streams join rivers and rivers flow to the **sea** |

When rainwater soaks into the soil, it slowly moves underground. Some of this water gets stored between rocks and soil. This is called **groundwater**, and it is the water we get from **wells and borewells**.

Water flowing in streams joins **rivers**. Some water gathers in **ponds** and **lakes**. Eventually, much of it reaches the **seas and oceans**.

> After the rain stops and the sun comes out, some of the water in puddles **dries up** and goes back into the air.

## How Water Reaches Our Homes

On most days, we get water from **taps** at home and school. But have you ever wondered how water reaches the tap?

Here is the journey of water to our homes:

1. Water comes from **rivers, lakes, wells, or borewells**.
2. It is stored in **big tanks** (sometimes on the roof of buildings).
3. **Pipes** carry the water from the tanks to the taps.
4. When we open the tap, the water flows out!

**Different ways people get water:**

| Method | How It Works |
|---|---|
| **Tap water** | Water comes through pipes from a tank |
| **Hand pump** | Water is pumped up from underground |
| **Well** | Water is drawn using a pulley or pump |
| **Water tanker** | Trucks bring water and deliver it to homes |
| **Carrying from a river** | People carry water in pots from a river or lake |

> In some places, people have to **walk long distances** to get water. We should always be **thankful** to those who bring us water.

## Water in Our Daily Lives

We use water for many activities every day:

- **Drinking** and **cooking**
- **Bathing** and **brushing teeth**
- **Washing clothes** and **utensils**
- **Cleaning** the house
- **Watering plants**
- **Flushing toilets**

Water is so important that **we cannot live more than 3 days without drinking water**.

---

## Storing Water

Sometimes, water supply can stop suddenly. That is why it is important to **store water**. People use many types of **vessels** to store water:

| Vessel | Material |
|---|---|
| **Clay pot** (matka) | Clay |
| **Clay surahi** | Clay |
| **Copper vessel** | Copper |
| **Brass pot** | Brass |
| **Steel pot** | Steel |
| **Aluminium pot** | Aluminium |
| **Glass bottle** | Glass |
| **Plastic bottle** | Plastic |
| **Plastic bucket** | Plastic |

> In the old days, people mostly used **clay pots and brass vessels** to store water. Clay pots keep water naturally cool!

---

## Water is Precious -- Save Every Drop!

Water is considered **sacred** because it is absolutely essential for life. But many places do not get enough water. We must learn to **save water**.

**Ways to save water:**

- Turn off the tap while brushing your teeth
- Take shorter showers
- Fix leaking taps quickly
- Reuse water from washing vegetables to water plants
- Do not waste water while playing

**Reusing water:** After we use water, it becomes dirty. We cannot use dirty water for drinking or cooking. But we **can reuse** it for:

- Watering plants
- Flushing toilets
- Cleaning floors

> We should not add too much soap and chemicals to water. Let us take care of this precious gift from the sky!

---

## Sharing Water with Others

In India, there is a beautiful tradition of keeping **pots of water** outside homes so that anyone feeling thirsty can drink. This is especially done during the **hot summer months**.

We should also share water with:

- **Postal workers and sanitation workers** who come to our door
- **Birds** -- by making a **birdbath** (a shallow dish of water kept on the terrace or balcony)
- **Stray animals** -- by keeping a bowl of water outside

**How to make a birdbath:**

1. Take a shallow, wide container (like an old bowl)
2. Place some small stones inside for birds to sit on
3. Fill it with fresh water
4. Keep it on your balcony, terrace, or yard
5. Change the water every day or two

---

## Key Points to Remember

- **Rain** is the main source of water on Earth.
- Rainwater flows into **streams, rivers, ponds, and lakes**, and also soaks into the ground.
- Water stored underground is called **groundwater**, and it comes out through **wells and borewells**.
- Water reaches our homes through **pipes, tankers, or by carrying** from wells and rivers.
- We use water for **drinking, cooking, bathing, cleaning**, and many more activities.
- Water is **precious** -- we must **save it** and not waste even a single drop.
- We can **reuse** water for watering plants and flushing toilets.
- Sharing water with **birds, animals, and people in need** is a kind and thoughtful act.
