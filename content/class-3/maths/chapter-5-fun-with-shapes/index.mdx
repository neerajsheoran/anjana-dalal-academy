## Flat Shapes (2D Shapes)

In Chapter 2, we learned about 3D shapes like cubes and cylinders. Now let us learn about **flat shapes** -- shapes you can draw on paper. These are called **2D shapes**.

When you draw a **rangoli** on the ground, you use flat shapes like circles, triangles, and rectangles!

---

## Rectangle

A **rectangle** is a shape with **4 sides** and **4 corners**.

```
+----------+
|          |
|          |
+----------+
```

### What makes a rectangle special?

- It has **4 sides**.
- It has **4 corners** (all are **square corners** -- like the corner of your book).
- The **opposite sides are equal** in length. The two long sides are equal. The two short sides are equal.

![A rectangle with four sides and four square corners labeled](rectangle-labeled.png)

**Where do we see rectangles?**
- A biscuit
- A door
- A matchbox face
- A notebook

> **Try this:** Trace all the faces of an eraser. You will get rectangles!

---

## Square

A **square** is a special kind of rectangle. It has **4 sides** and **4 corners**, but all **4 sides are equal** in length.

```
+------+
|      |
|      |
|      |
+------+
```

### How is a square different from a rectangle?

| | Rectangle | Square |
|---|---|---|
| Number of sides | 4 | 4 |
| Number of corners | 4 | 4 |
| Type of corners | Square corners | Square corners |
| Sides | Opposite sides are equal | **All 4 sides are equal** |

**Where do we see squares?**
- Chess board tiles
- Ludo board tiles
- Carrom board
- Floor tiles

> **Remember:** Every square is a rectangle, but not every rectangle is a square!

---

## Triangle

A **triangle** has **3 sides** and **3 corners**.

```
    /\
   /  \
  /    \
 /______\
```

The word "tri" means **three**. So a triangle has three angles (corners).

**Where do we see triangles?**
- A samosa shape
- A tent
- A hanger
- Rangoli designs
- A slice of pizza

> **Fun fact:** You can make a triangle by cutting a square along one corner to the opposite corner!

---

## Circle

A **circle** is a round shape with **no sides** and **no corners**. It is made of one smooth curved line.

Every circle has a **center** -- the point right in the middle.

**Where do we see circles?**
- A roti
- A bangle
- A coin
- A wheel
- A paper plate

### Finding the center of a circle

Take a round paper plate. Fold it in **half**. Then fold it in half again. Open it up. The two fold lines cross at one point -- that is the **center** of the circle!

---

## Corners: Square and Not Square

The corner of a square or rectangle is called a **square corner** (also known as a right angle). It looks like the corner of a book.

Not all corners are square corners:

| Type | What it looks like |
|------|-------------------|
| **Square corner** | Exactly like the corner of a book |
| **Less than a square corner** | A smaller, sharper corner |
| **More than a square corner** | A wider, more open corner |

> **Tip:** You can check if a corner is a square corner by placing the corner of a book or paper on it. If they match exactly, it is a square corner!

## Comparing Shapes

| Shape | Sides | Corners | Straight or Curved? |
|-------|:-----:|:-------:|---------------------|
| **Rectangle** | 4 | 4 (all square) | Straight |
| **Square** | 4 | 4 (all square) | Straight |
| **Triangle** | 3 | 3 | Straight |
| **Circle** | 0 | 0 | Curved |

## Joining Shapes to Make New Shapes

You can put shapes together to make new and bigger shapes!

- Join **2 squares** side by side to make a **rectangle**.
- Join **2 triangles** to make a **square** or a **rectangle**.
- Use many triangles and squares to make a beautiful **rangoli**.

```
Two triangles make a square:

 /|      |\        +------+
/ |  +   | \  =   |      |
/__|      |__\     +------+
```

A **tangram** is a puzzle made of 7 flat shapes. You can arrange them to make animals, houses, and many fun figures!

![A tangram puzzle showing 7 pieces arranged to form a square and an animal](tangram-puzzle.png)

## Lines and Patterns

Shapes are made of **lines**. Lines can be:

- **Straight** -- like the edge of a ruler
- **Curved** -- like part of a circle

Saree borders, flower decorations, and rangoli designs all use beautiful patterns of straight and curved lines.

> **Try this:** Draw a border pattern using triangles and circles in a row!

## Key Words to Remember

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Rectangle** | A flat shape with 4 sides and 4 square corners; opposite sides are equal |
| **Square** | A flat shape with 4 equal sides and 4 square corners |
| **Triangle** | A flat shape with 3 sides and 3 corners |
| **Circle** | A flat round shape with no sides and no corners |
| **Corner** | The point where two sides of a shape meet |
| **Square corner** | A corner shaped like the corner of a book (right angle) |
| **Side** | The straight line that forms part of a shape |
| **Center** | The exact middle point of a circle |
| **Tangram** | A puzzle made of 7 flat shapes that fit together |
