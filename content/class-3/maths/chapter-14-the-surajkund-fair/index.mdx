## Welcome to the Surajkund Fair!

The **Surajkund Fair** is a famous fair held every year in **Faridabad, Haryana**. People from all over India come to sell beautiful crafts, show art, and enjoy food. Soni and Avi are visiting the fair with their grandparents. Let us join them and learn about **symmetry**, **patterns**, and **maps**!

---

## What is Symmetry?

Look around you. Many things look the **same on both sides**. A butterfly's wings, your face, a flower -- if you draw a line down the middle, the left side looks just like the right side.

When something has **two equal halves** that are mirror images of each other, we say it is **symmetrical**.

```
Symmetrical:          Not Symmetrical:
    *                     *
   * *                   *  *
  *   *                 *    *
 *     *               **     *
*       *              *        *
 *     *                 *    *
  *   *                    *  *
   * *                      *
    *
(Both sides match)    (Sides are different)
```

> **Try this:** Fold a paper in half. If both halves match exactly when folded, the shape is symmetrical!

## Symmetry in Malas (Bead Necklaces)

Soni and Avi make malas with **8 beads** using 2 colours. Some malas are symmetrical and some are not.

**Symmetrical mala:**
```
🔴 🔵 🔵 🔴 | 🔴 🔵 🔵 🔴
  Left half    =    Right half
```

**Not symmetrical mala:**
```
🔴 🔴 🔵 🔵 | 🔴 🔵 🔴 🔵
  Left half    ≠    Right half
```

> **Tip:** To check if a mala is symmetrical, look at it from the middle. Does the left side look like a mirror of the right side?

## Symmetry in Rangolis

People across India make beautiful **rangoli** designs. Many of these are symmetrical!

- **Kolam** from Tamil Nadu
- **Pookalam** from Kerala
- **Aipan** from Uttarakhand

To check if a rangoli is symmetrical, draw a line through the middle. If both halves are the same, it is symmetrical.

> **Activity:** Draw half a rangoli on paper. Then complete the other half so that it looks like a mirror image!

## Mirror Images

When you stand in front of a **mirror**, you see your image. The mirror shows everything **flipped** from left to right.

### The Mirror Game

Soni places 4 counters on her side of a line. Avi must place his counters as the **mirror image** on the other side.

```
Soni's side:    |    Avi's side (mirror):
  X . . .       |       . . . X
  . . X .       |       . X . .
  . X . .       |       . . X .
  . . . X       |       X . . .
```

> **Tip:** In a mirror image, what is on the **left** becomes the **right**, and what is on the **right** becomes the **left**. Top and bottom stay the same!

## The Painter's Trick

A painter at the fair drew only **half** of Soni's picture and said: "Put a mirror in the middle to see the full picture!"

This works because a face is **symmetrical** -- the left half looks like the right half.

But this trick does NOT work for things that are **not symmetrical**. For example:
- A hand waving (left hand and right hand look different)
- A person standing sideways
- The number **6** (its mirror image looks like **d**, not **6**)

## Tiling: Covering a Path

At the fair, the paths are made of **tiles**. Good tiling means:
- **No gaps** between the tiles
- **No overlaps** (tiles should not sit on top of each other)

Different shapes can be used as tiles:

| Shape | Can it tile a path? |
|-------|:---:|
| **Squares** | Yes! They fit perfectly with no gaps |
| **Rectangles** | Yes! They fit perfectly too |
| **Triangles** | Yes! Two triangles make a rectangle |
| **Circles** | No! Circles always leave gaps |

```
Good tiling (squares):     Bad tiling (circles):
+--+--+--+--+              O  O  O
+--+--+--+--+              O  O  O
+--+--+--+--+              (gaps everywhere!)
```

> **Try this:** Cut out small squares and rectangles from paper. Try to cover a sheet of paper with them, leaving no gaps!

## Reading a Map

At the fair, Soni and Avi use a **map** to find their grandparents. A map shows where things are from above, like a bird looking down.

The Surajkund Fair map shows:
- **Parking** area
- **ATM** (for getting money)
- **Restaurant** (for eating)
- **Shops** and stalls
- **Giant wheel**
- **Pond**
- **Play area**
- **Chaupal** (meeting place)
- **Entry** and **Exit** gates

### Following directions on a map:

To find a place, you follow **directions** like:
- Walk **forward** (straight ahead)
- Turn **right**
- Turn **left**
- Go **back**

> **Example:** "Walk on the blue lane. Turn right on the green lane. You will see the restaurant on your right. Take a left towards the red lane."

---

## Maze Puzzles

A **maze** is a path with many turns and dead ends. To solve a maze, you must find the one path from the **start** to the **end** without hitting a wall.

> **Tip:** If you get stuck in a maze, try going back and picking a different path. Keep trying until you find the way out!

## Key Words to Remember

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Symmetry** | When two halves of something are exactly the same (mirror images) |
| **Symmetrical** | A shape or object that has symmetry |
| **Mirror image** | What you see when you flip something left-to-right (like in a mirror) |
| **Line of symmetry** | The line that divides a shape into two equal mirror halves |
| **Pattern** | Something that repeats in a regular way |
| **Tiling** | Covering a surface with shapes, leaving no gaps and no overlaps |
| **Map** | A drawing that shows where things are, seen from above |
| **Directions** | Words like left, right, forward, back that tell you which way to go |
| **Maze** | A puzzle with paths where you must find the way from start to end |
