## Sharing Equally

Shabnam and Mukta want to share a **paratha** equally. If one piece is bigger than the other, it is **not fair**!

To share **fairly**, both pieces must be the **same size**.

> **Tip:** To check if two pieces are equal, fold one piece over the other. If they match exactly, they are equal!

---

## What is a Half?

When you divide **1 whole** thing into **2 equal parts**, each part is called a **half**.

```
+----------+----------+
|          |          |
|  Half    |   Half   |
|          |          |
+----------+----------+
     One Whole
```

**2 halves make 1 whole.**

### Examples of halves:

- Cut a **roti** into 2 equal pieces -- each piece is a **half**.
- Share **1 chocolate** equally between 2 friends -- each gets a **half**.
- Fold a paper in the middle -- each side is a **half**.

> **Important:** The two parts must be **equal**. If one part is bigger, it is NOT a half!

---

## Different Ways to Make a Half

You can cut or fold a shape in half in **different ways**:

**A rectangle can be halved:**

```
Horizontal cut:      Vertical cut:       Diagonal cut:
+----------+        +-----+-----+        +----------+
|          |        |     |     |        |  \       |
+----------+        |     |     |        |     \    |
|          |        |     |     |        |        \ |
+----------+        +-----+-----+        +----------+
```

All three ways give you **two equal halves**!

## Half and Double

**Half** and **double** are opposites.

- **3** is **half** of **6**.
- **6** is **double** of **3**.

| Number | Half | Double |
|:------:|:----:|:------:|
| 4 | 2 | 8 |
| 6 | 3 | 12 |
| 8 | 4 | 16 |
| 10 | 5 | 20 |
| 12 | 6 | 24 |
| 14 | 7 | 28 |

> **Tip:** To find **half**, divide by 2. To find **double**, multiply by 2.

## What is a Quarter?

When you divide **1 whole** thing into **4 equal parts**, each part is called a **quarter**.

```
+------+------+
|      |      |
|  Q   |  Q   |
|      |      |
+------+------+
|      |      |
|  Q   |  Q   |
|      |      |
+------+------+

Q = Quarter
4 quarters = 1 whole
```

Think of a **chikki** (a sweet snack) broken into 4 equal pieces. Each piece is **one quarter** of the whole chikki.

> **Remember:** A quarter is also **half of a half**! Fold a paper in half, then fold it in half again -- you get 4 quarters.

## Halves and Quarters Together

| Parts | Name | How many make a whole? |
|:-----:|------|:----------------------:|
| 2 equal parts | **Half** | 2 halves = 1 whole |
| 4 equal parts | **Quarter** | 4 quarters = 1 whole |

We can also count quarters like this:

| Quarters | What it means |
|:--------:|---------------|
| 1 quarter | One out of four equal parts |
| 2 quarters | Half of the whole (because 2 quarters = 1 half) |
| 3 quarters | Three out of four equal parts |
| 4 quarters | The complete whole |

              (= one half)                 (= one whole)

## More Than Half or Less Than Half?

Look at a shape and think: is the coloured part **more than half**, **less than half**, or **exactly half**?

- If the coloured part is **bigger** than the uncoloured part, it is **more than half**.
- If the coloured part is **smaller**, it is **less than half**.
- If both parts are the **same size**, it is **exactly half**.

> **Example:** Shabnam ate some chikki from 3 sides. If only a small piece is left, she ate **more than half** the chikki!

---

## Fair Sharing in Real Life

Sharing things fairly means everyone gets an **equal part**.

- **2 friends** share a mango: each gets **half** (1 out of 2 equal parts).
- **4 friends** share a pizza: each gets a **quarter** (1 out of 4 equal parts).
- **8 mangoes** shared between 2 children: each gets **4 mangoes** (half of 8).

> **Remember:** Fair sharing means **equal sharing**. Nobody should get more or less than others!

---

## Key Words to Remember

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Whole** | The complete thing, not broken or divided |
| **Half** | One of 2 equal parts of a whole |
| **Quarter** | One of 4 equal parts of a whole |
| **Equal parts** | Parts that are exactly the same size |
| **Fair share** | When everyone gets the same amount |
| **Double** | Two times a number (double of 5 is 10) |
| **Half of a number** | A number divided by 2 (half of 10 is 5) |
