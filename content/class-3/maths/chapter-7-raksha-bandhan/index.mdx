## Repeated Addition

Gopal and Dhara are making **rakhis** for Raksha Bandhan. Each rakhi needs **1 flower**, **2 threads**, and **4 beads**.

If they make **5 rakhis**, how much material do they need?

**Flowers:** 1 + 1 + 1 + 1 + 1 = **5 flowers**

**Threads:** 2 + 2 + 2 + 2 + 2 = **10 threads**

**Beads:** 4 + 4 + 4 + 4 + 4 = **20 beads**

Adding the same number again and again is called **repeated addition**.

---

## What is Multiplication?

When you add the **same number many times**, there is a shorter way to write it. This is called **multiplication**.

Instead of writing 4 + 4 + 4 + 4 + 4, we write:

**5 times 4** = **5 x 4 = 20**

The sign **x** means "times" or "multiplied by".

| Repeated Addition | Multiplication | Answer |
|---|---|:---:|
| 2 + 2 + 2 | 3 x 2 | 6 |
| 3 + 3 + 3 | 3 x 3 | 9 |
| 4 + 4 + 4 + 4 + 4 | 5 x 4 | 20 |
| 5 + 5 + 5 + 5 | 4 x 5 | 20 |
| 6 + 6 + 6 | 3 x 6 | 18 |

> **Remember:** 5 x 4 means "5 groups of 4" or "add 4, five times".

## Counting in Groups

Counting in groups makes things easier!

**Laddoos in a box:** If a box has 3 rows and 3 laddoos in each row:

```
🟡 🟡 🟡
🟡 🟡 🟡
🟡 🟡 🟡

3 + 3 + 3 = 9 laddoos
3 x 3 = 9 laddoos
```

**Two boxes** of 9 laddoos each:
- 9 + 9 = 18, or **2 x 9 = 18 laddoos**

> **Tip:** When things are arranged in rows, count one row, then multiply by the number of rows!

---

## What is Division?

**Division** means sharing equally or splitting into equal groups.

If we have **18 laddoos** and **9 people**, how many does each person get?

We give 1 laddoo to each person. Then 1 more to each. After 2 rounds, all laddoos are finished!

**18 shared equally by 9 = 2 each**

We write: **18 / 9 = 2**

The sign **/** means "divided by".

| Total | Shared Among | Each Gets |
|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| 18 laddoos | 9 people | 2 each |
| 20 kaju katlis | 5 people | 4 each |
| 16 kaju katlis | 4 people | 4 each |
| 15 pedas | 5 people | 3 each |

> **Remember:** Division is the opposite of multiplication! If 5 x 4 = 20, then 20 / 5 = 4.

## Skip Counting and Times Tables

**Skip counting** means jumping by the same number again and again. This is the same as making a **times table**!

### Skip counting by 3:

```
0, 3, 6, 9, 12, 15, 18, 21, 24, 27, 30
```

This gives us the **times-3 table**:

| Times | Answer |
|:-----:|:------:|
| 1 x 3 | 3 |
| 2 x 3 | 6 |
| 3 x 3 | 9 |
| 4 x 3 | 12 |
| 5 x 3 | 15 |
| 6 x 3 | 18 |
| 7 x 3 | 21 |
| 8 x 3 | 24 |
| 9 x 3 | 27 |
| 10 x 3 | 30 |

## Patterns in Times Tables

### Times-5 table pattern:

```
5, 10, 15, 20, 25, 30, 35, 40, 45, 50
```

The last digit is always **0** or **5**!

- If you multiply 5 by an **even number** (2, 4, 6, 8, 10), the answer ends in **0**.
- If you multiply 5 by an **odd number** (1, 3, 5, 7, 9), the answer ends in **5**.

### A special pattern with tables:

The **times-2 table** and the **times-3 table** add up to the **times-5 table**!

| Times-2 | + | Times-3 | = | Times-5 |
|:---:|---|:---:|---|:---:|
| 1 x 2 = 2 | + | 1 x 3 = 3 | = | 1 x 5 = 5 |
| 2 x 2 = 4 | + | 2 x 3 = 6 | = | 2 x 5 = 10 |
| 3 x 2 = 6 | + | 3 x 3 = 9 | = | 3 x 5 = 15 |

## Multiplying Bigger Numbers

Bhim needs spokes for **20 wheels**. Each wheel needs **5 spokes**.

Break it down:
- First 10 wheels: 10 x 5 = **50 spokes**
- Next 10 wheels: 10 x 5 = **50 spokes**
- Total: 50 + 50 = **100 spokes**

So, **20 x 5 = 100**

| Wheels | Spokes (x 5) |
|:------:|:------------:|
| 10 | 50 |
| 20 | 100 |
| 30 | 150 |
| 40 | 200 |
| 50 | 250 |

## Division with Bigger Numbers

Dhruv has **112 sea-shells**. He wants to make necklaces of **28 shells** each. How many can he make?

We keep subtracting 28:
- 112 - 28 = **84** (1 necklace)
- 84 - 28 = **56** (2 necklaces)
- 56 - 28 = **28** (3 necklaces)
- 28 - 28 = **0** (4 necklaces)

**112 / 28 = 4 necklaces**

> **Tip:** For big numbers, you can keep subtracting the same number until you reach 0 (or close to 0). Count how many times you subtracted -- that is the answer!

## Key Words to Remember

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Multiplication** | A short way to do repeated addition (using the x sign) |
| **Times** | Another word for multiplication (5 times 3 = 5 x 3) |
| **Division** | Sharing equally or splitting into equal groups (using the / sign) |
| **Times table** | A list showing a number multiplied by 1, 2, 3, ... up to 10 |
| **Skip counting** | Counting by jumping the same number (like 3, 6, 9, 12...) |
| **Groups** | Equal sets of things put together |
| **Repeated addition** | Adding the same number over and over (like 4 + 4 + 4) |
| **Repeated subtraction** | Subtracting the same number over and over to divide |
