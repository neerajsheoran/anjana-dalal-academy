## Nani Maa's Magic Trick

Chirag and Nandini love visiting their **Nani Maa**. She knows a fun trick with marbles!

She says: "Hide some marbles under a cloth. I will tell you how many you hid!"

How does she know? If there are **15 marbles** in total and **12 are on the table**, then the hidden ones are:

**15 - 12 = 3 marbles hidden!**

> **Tip:** If you know the total and one part, you can always find the other part by **subtracting**.

| Total Seeds | Seeds on Table | Hidden Seeds |
|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| 15 | 12 | 3 |
| 20 | 9 | 11 |
| 17 | 10 | 7 |
| 23 | 7 | 16 |

## Adding Small Numbers

Nandini brought **7 balushahi** (a yummy sweet) and Chirag brought **5 balushahi** for Nani Maa.

How many sweets in total?

```
Nandini:  🟡🟡🟡🟡🟡🟡🟡       = 7
Chirag:   🟡🟡🟡🟡🟡             = 5
Together: 🟡🟡🟡🟡🟡🟡🟡🟡🟡🟡🟡🟡 = 12
```

**7 + 5 = 12 balushahi**

## Adding on the Number Line

The children had **15 stamps**. Nani Maa gave them **7 more**. How many now?

You can add by **jumping forward** on a number line:

```
Start at 15, jump forward 7:
        +1 +1 +1 +1 +1 +1 +1
  ---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---
    15  16  17  18  19  20  21  22
```

**15 + 7 = 22 stamps**

![Number line showing forward jumps from 15 to 22](number-line-addition.png)

You can also jump in bigger steps:

```
        +5          +2
  ---|---------|-----|---
    15         20    22
```

> **Tip:** It is easier to jump to a round number first (like 20), and then jump the rest!

## Subtracting on the Number Line

The children have **52 stamps**. **37** have faces of famous people. How many have pictures of monuments?

Jump **backward** on the number line:

```
     -2     -10        -3
  |---|-------|---------|---
  37  40      50        52
```

Count the jumps: 3 + 10 + 2 = **15 stamps** with monuments.

**52 - 37 = 15**

> **Another way to think:** 37 + ? = 52. So we need to find how many to add to 37 to reach 52.

## Skip Counting (Jumping by 10)

A frog starts at **7** and jumps **10 beads** each time:

```
7 → 17 → 27 → 37 → 47 → 57 → 67 → 77 → 87 → 97
```

Notice the pattern? The **ones digit stays the same** (7), and the **tens digit goes up by 1** each time!

Starting at **23**, jumping by 10:
```
23 → 33 → 43 → 53 → 63 → 73 → 83 → 93
```

**Jumping backward** by 10 from **76**:
```
76 → 66 → 56 → 46 → 36 → 26 → 16 → 6
```

## Adding Bigger Numbers

Nani Maa plucked **25 red radishes** and **36 white radishes**. How many in total?

### Method 1: Using bundles and sticks

```
  25  =  2 tens + 5 ones
+ 36  =  3 tens + 6 ones
------
         5 tens + 11 ones
       = 5 tens + 1 ten + 1 one
       = 6 tens + 1 one
       = 61
```

### Method 2: Number line jumps

```
Start at 25, add 36:
    +5       +10       +10       +10    +1
|---|---------|---------|---------|------|---
25  30        40        50        60     61
```

**25 + 36 = 61 radishes**

## Adding Numbers That Cross 100

Nandini collected **85 apples** and Chirag plucked **67 apples**.

```
Start at 85, add 67:
    +15            +52
|---|--------------|---
85  100            152
```

**85 + 67 = 152 apples**

> **Tip:** When adding, first jump to the nearest round number (like 100), then add the rest.

## Box Diagrams

A **box diagram** helps you see a problem clearly.

**Addition example:** 85 apples + 67 apples = ?

```
+---------------------------+
|    85     |      67       |
+---------------------------+
          ? (total)
```

**Subtraction example:** Who collected more, and by how much?

```
+---------------------------+
|    85     |  67  |   ?    |
+-----------+------+--------+
  Nandini    Chirag  difference
```

**85 - 67 = 18** (Nandini collected 18 more apples)

## More Than 100 or Less Than 100?

Before you solve, **estimate** first! Think: "Will the answer be more or less than 100?"

| Problem | Estimate | Answer |
|---------|----------|:------:|
| 70 + 30 + 1 | More than 100 | 101 |
| 20 + 41 | Less than 100 | 61 |
| 150 - 49 | More than 100 | 101 |
| 68 - 26 | Less than 100 | 42 |
| 95 + 10 + 5 | More than 100 | 110 |

> **Tip:** Round the numbers to the nearest ten to make a quick estimate!

## Key Words to Remember

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Addition** | Putting numbers together to find the total (using +) |
| **Subtraction** | Taking away one number from another (using -) |
| **Total / Sum** | The answer you get when you add |
| **Difference** | The answer you get when you subtract |
| **Estimate** | A smart guess about what the answer will be |
| **Number line** | A line with numbers in order, used for jumping forward or backward |
| **Box diagram** | A picture with boxes that shows the parts and the total |
| **Skip counting** | Counting by jumping the same number each time (like 10s) |
