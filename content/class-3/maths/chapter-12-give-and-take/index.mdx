## Adding 3-Digit Numbers

Kishan runs a plant nursery. He had **364 saplings** and brought **52 more**. How many does he have now?

We need to find: **364 + 52 = ?**

### Using a box diagram:

```
+------+------+
| 364  |  52  |
+------+------+
      ???
```

### Using Hundreds, Tens, and Ones:

| | Hundreds | Tens | Ones |
|---|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| 364 | 3 | 6 | 4 |
| + 52 | 0 | 5 | 2 |
| **Answer** | **4** | **1** | **6** |

**How?**
- Ones: 4 + 2 = 6 ones
- Tens: 6 + 5 = 11 tens = **1 hundred and 1 ten** (carry the 1 hundred over)
- Hundreds: 3 + 0 + 1 (carried) = 4 hundreds

**364 + 52 = 416 saplings**

> **Tip:** When the tens or ones add up to 10 or more, we regroup. 10 ones become 1 ten. 10 tens become 1 hundred.

## Subtracting 3-Digit Numbers

Kishan got an order for **230 saplings**. He packed **75** already. How many more does he need to pack?

We need to find: **230 - 75 = ?**

### Using a box diagram:

```
+----------+----------+
|    75    |    ???   |
+----------+----------+
         230
```

### Using Hundreds, Tens, and Ones:

We cannot take 5 from 0 ones! So we **borrow**:
- Change 1 ten into 10 ones: Now we have **2 tens** and **10 ones**.
- 10 - 5 = 5 ones.

Next, we cannot take 7 tens from 2 tens! So we **borrow** again:
- Change 1 hundred into 10 tens: Now we have **1 hundred** and **12 tens**.
- 12 - 7 = 5 tens.

| | Hundreds | Tens | Ones |
|---|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| 230 | 2 | 3 | 0 |
| - 75 | 0 | 7 | 5 |
| **Answer** | **1** | **5** | **5** |

**230 - 75 = 155 saplings**

> **Remember:** When you cannot subtract a digit, borrow from the place to the left. 1 hundred = 10 tens. 1 ten = 10 ones.

## Adding and Subtracting Mentally

Here are some tricks for quick mental maths:

### Adding 100, 10, or 1:

| Start | Add 100 | Add 10 | Add 1 |
|:-----:|:-------:|:------:|:-----:|
| 269 | 369 | 279 | 270 |
| 454 | 554 | 464 | 455 |

> **Pattern:** Adding 100 changes only the **hundreds** digit. Adding 10 changes only the **tens** digit. Adding 1 changes only the **ones** digit.

## Money: Notes and Coins

Long ago, people did not have money. They **exchanged things**. A farmer would give rice and get vegetables in return. This is called **barter**.

Today, we use **money** -- notes and coins -- to buy things.

### Indian Notes and Coins:

| Notes | Coins |
|-------|-------|
| ₹10 | ₹1 |
| ₹20 | ₹2 |
| ₹50 | ₹5 |
| ₹100 | ₹10 |
| ₹200 | ₹20 |
| ₹500 | |

### Important relationships:

```
₹500 = 5 notes of ₹100
₹100 = 2 notes of ₹50
₹100 = 5 notes of ₹20
₹100 = 10 notes of ₹10
```

> **Try this:** How many ₹100 notes make ₹500? Answer: **5 notes**!

## Solving Money Problems

### Example 1: How much was earned?

Peter uncle had **₹465** in the morning. By afternoon he had **₹756**. How much did he earn?

```
Box diagram:
+------+---------+
| 465  |   ???   |
+------+---------+
       756
```

**756 - 465 = ₹291** earned since morning.

### Example 2: Getting change

Reena bought groceries for **₹209**. She gave a **₹500** note. How much change should she get?

**500 - 209 = ₹291** change.

### Example 3: Saving over weeks

Shireen has **₹150** in her piggy bank. She puts **₹100** every week.

| After | Amount |
|:-----:|:------:|
| Start | ₹150 |
| Week 1 | ₹250 |
| Week 2 | ₹350 |
| Week 3 | ₹450 |
| Week 4 | ₹550 |

## Estimating Answers

Before solving a problem, make a **smart guess** (estimate). Round numbers to the nearest hundred.

| Problem | Estimate (nearest hundred) |
|---------|:-:|
| 156 + 34 | 200 (about 200 + 0) |
| 205 + 215 | 400 (about 200 + 200) |
| 500 - 395 | 100 (about 500 - 400) |
| 505 + 405 | 900 (about 500 + 400) |

> **Tip:** Estimating helps you check if your answer makes sense. If your answer is very different from your estimate, you may have made a mistake!

## Comparing Without Calculating

You can sometimes tell which answer is bigger **without actually solving**:

| Problem A | Problem B | Which is more? |
|-----------|-----------|:-:|
| 373 + 23 | 373 + 40 | B (because 40 > 23) |
| 345 - 45 | 345 - 54 | A (subtracting less means more is left) |
| 900 + 9 | 890 + 60 | B (890 + 60 = 950 is more than 909) |

> **Tip:** If you add **more** to the same number, the answer is **bigger**. If you subtract **more** from the same number, the answer is **smaller**.

## Key Words to Remember

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Addition** | Putting two or more numbers together to get a total |
| **Subtraction** | Taking one number away from another |
| **Regroup / Borrow** | Changing 1 ten into 10 ones (or 1 hundred into 10 tens) when subtracting |
| **Carry over** | Moving extra tens or hundreds to the next place when adding |
| **Box diagram** | A picture showing the parts and the whole of a problem |
| **Estimate** | A smart guess about the answer before solving |
| **Money** | Notes and coins used to buy things |
| **Barter** | Exchanging things without using money |
| **Change** | The money returned when you pay more than the cost |
