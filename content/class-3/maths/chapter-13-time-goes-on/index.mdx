## The Calendar

A **calendar** helps us know the **date**, the **day**, and the **month**.

### Months of the Year

There are **12 months** in a year:

| Month | Days |
|-------|:----:|
| January | 31 |
| February | 28 or 29 |
| March | 31 |
| April | 30 |
| May | 31 |
| June | 30 |
| July | 31 |
| August | 31 |
| September | 30 |
| October | 31 |
| November | 30 |
| December | 31 |

> **Tip:** To remember which months have 30 or 31 days, use the knuckle trick! Make a fist. The bumps are 31-day months, the dips are 30-day months (except February).

![A fist showing the knuckle trick for remembering days in each month](knuckle-trick-months.png)

### Days of the Week

There are **7 days** in a week:

```
Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday, Friday, Saturday, Sunday
```

### Important facts:

- **1 year** = **12 months** = **365 days** (or 366 in a leap year)
- **1 year** = about **52 weeks**
- **1 month** = about **4 weeks**
- **1 week** = **7 days**

## Reading Dates

Dates are written in a special way: **DD/MM/YYYY**

- **DD** = Day of the month (01 to 31)
- **MM** = Month (01 to 12)
- **YYYY** = Year

**Example:** 02/05/2015 means **2nd May, 2015**.

| Date written | What it means |
|---|---|
| 15/08/2024 | 15th August, 2024 (Independence Day!) |
| 26/01/2025 | 26th January, 2025 (Republic Day!) |
| 25/12/2024 | 25th December, 2024 (Christmas!) |

## How Old Are You?

Your **age** tells how many years have passed since you were born.

If you were born in **2015** and today is **2026**:
- 2026 - 2015 = **11 years old**

If your mother was **25 years old** when you were born, and you are **11 now**:
- Your mother is 25 + 11 = **36 years old**

> **Fun puzzle:** Hetal is **twice as old** as her brother. She is also **10 years older** than him. Can you guess their ages? Answer: Hetal is **20** and her brother is **10**!

## The Clock

A **clock** tells us the **time**. An analog clock (the one with hands) has:

- A **short hand** (hour hand) -- shows the **hour**
- A **long hand** (minute hand) -- shows the **minutes**
- The numbers **1 to 12** on its face

```
        12
    11      1
  10          2
  9            3
  8          4
    7      5
        6
```

---

## Reading Time

### O'Clock (Exact Hours)

When the **minute hand** points to **12**, and the **hour hand** points to a number, we say that number **o'clock**.

| Hour hand points to | We say |
|:---:|---|
| 7 | 7 o'clock |
| 9 | 9 o'clock |
| 12 | 12 o'clock |

### Quarter Past (15 minutes)

When the **minute hand** points to **3**, it has moved **15 minutes** past the hour.

- Minute hand on 3, hour hand on 8 = **8:15** or **quarter past 8**

### Half Past (30 minutes)

When the **minute hand** points to **6**, it has moved **30 minutes** past the hour.

- Minute hand on 6, hour hand between 8 and 9 = **8:30** or **half past 8**

| Time | What we say | Minute hand points to |
|------|-------------|:---:|
| 7:00 | 7 o'clock | 12 |
| 7:15 | Quarter past 7 | 3 |
| 7:30 | Half past 7 | 6 |
| 8:00 | 8 o'clock | 12 |

## A Day in Hetal's Life

A day has **24 hours**. Here is how Hetal spends her day:

| Time | Activity |
|:----:|----------|
| 7:00 AM | Wakes up |
| 7:15 AM | Finishes breakfast |
| 8:00 AM | Goes to school |
| 1:00 PM | Has lunch |
| 2:00 PM | Returns home |
| 4:00 PM | Plays |
| 5:00 PM | Studies |
| 9:00 PM | Goes to sleep |

**How long did Hetal take to eat breakfast?**
She started at 7:00 and finished at 7:15. That is **15 minutes**.

## How Long Does It Take?

Different things take different amounts of time:

| Takes minutes | Takes hours | Takes days | Takes weeks | Takes months |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Brushing teeth | A school day | A weekend trip | School exams | Growing a carrot |
| Taking a shower | Watching a movie | Knitting a sweater | A chapter in class | Changing seasons |
| Eating dinner | Stitching a shirt | A festival celebration | Growing radish | Building a house |

## Analog and Digital Clocks

There are two types of clocks:

| Analog Clock | Digital Clock |
|---|---|
| Has hands that move in a circle | Shows time with numbers on a screen |
| Has numbers 1-12 on the face | Shows hours and minutes like 07:15 |
| Found on walls, wristwatches | Found on phones, microwaves |

> **Fun activity:** You can make a **sand clock** (timer) using two small bottles, fine sand, and glue. Join the bottle caps together with a small hole. Fill one bottle with sand, attach the other, and flip it over. Time how long the sand takes to flow!

## Key Words to Remember

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Calendar** | A chart that shows days, weeks, and months of a year |
| **Date** | The day of the month (like 15th or 26th) |
| **Month** | One of the 12 parts of a year (like January or July) |
| **Year** | 12 months or about 365 days |
| **Week** | 7 days |
| **Hour hand** | The short hand on a clock that shows the hour |
| **Minute hand** | The long hand on a clock that shows the minutes |
| **O'clock** | When the minute hand is at 12 (an exact hour) |
| **Quarter past** | 15 minutes past the hour (minute hand at 3) |
| **Half past** | 30 minutes past the hour (minute hand at 6) |
| **Analog clock** | A clock with hands that move in a circle |
| **Digital clock** | A clock that shows time as numbers on a screen |
