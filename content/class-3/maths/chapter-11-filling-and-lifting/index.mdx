## How Much Can a Vessel Hold? (Capacity)

Different vessels hold different amounts of liquid. A big jug holds more water than a small glass.

The amount of liquid a vessel can hold is called its **capacity**.

| Vessel | Holds more or less? |
|--------|-------------------|
| Ladle (karchi) | Very little |
| Glass | A little |
| Bowl (katori) | A little more |
| Jug | More |
| Bucket | A lot! |

> **Tip:** To find out which vessel holds more, pour water from one into another. If the water overflows, the first one holds more!

## The Litre

Just like we use a **metre** to measure length, we use a **litre** to measure how much liquid a vessel holds.

A **litre** is a standard unit for measuring liquid.

**How much is 1 litre?**
- A medium-sized water bottle holds about 1 litre.
- A milk measuring cup that milkmen use holds 1 litre.
- A big jug holds about 1 litre.

| Vessel | Capacity |
|--------|----------|
| A glass | Less than 1 litre |
| A bowl (katori) | Less than 1 litre |
| A jug | About 1 litre |
| A mug | About half a litre |
| A bucket | More than 1 litre |

---

## Half Litre and Quarter Litre

Just like with a metre, we can divide a litre into smaller parts:

| Measurement | What it means |
|---|---|
| **1 litre** | A full litre |
| **Half litre** | Half of 1 litre (like a big mug) |
| **Quarter litre** | One-fourth of 1 litre (like a glass) |

**How they are related:**

```
1 litre = 2 half litres = 4 quarter litres
```

| Half litres | Make how many litres? |
|:---:|:---:|
| 2 half litres | 1 litre |
| 4 half litres | 2 litres |

| Quarter litres | Make how many litres? |
|:---:|:---:|
| 4 quarter litres | 1 litre |
| 2 quarter litres | Half a litre |

> **Did you know?** To make one single-use plastic bottle (1 litre), factories use about **5 litres** of water! Use your own reusable bottle to save water.

## Heavy or Light? (Weight)

Some things are **heavy** and some are **light**. If you hold a book in one hand and a pencil in the other, the book feels heavier. Your hand with the book goes **down**.

> **Try this:** Hold your lunch box in one hand and your pencil box in the other for 30 seconds. Which hand goes down? That one is heavier!

---

## Comparing Weights

We can compare weights using a **pan balance** (tarazu).

```
    ____
   /    \
  /      \
 |  Book  |     |  Eraser |
 |________|     |_________|
     ↓               ↑
  (heavier)       (lighter)
```

The **heavier** side goes **down**. The **lighter** side goes **up**.

If both sides are at the **same level**, the objects are **equal in weight**.

---

## Measuring Weight with Everyday Objects

We can use small objects as weights:

| Measuring tool | Good for weighing |
|---|---|
| **Coins** | Very light things (like a pencil or eraser) |
| **Matchbox full of sand** | Slightly heavier things (like a pencil box) |

**Example:**
- A pencil might balance with **5 coins**.
- A pencil box might balance with **3 matchboxes of sand**.

---

## The Kilogram

A **kilogram** (kg) is a standard unit for measuring weight.

**How heavy is 1 kilogram?**
- A packet of salt from the shop is usually **1 kg**.
- A packet of daal or rice from the shop is often **1 kg**.

| Object | Weight |
|--------|--------|
| An apple | Less than 1 kg |
| A pencil box | Less than 1 kg |
| A bag of rice | More than 1 kg |
| A watermelon | More than 1 kg |
| A packet of salt | About 1 kg |

---

## Half Kilogram and Quarter Kilogram

| Measurement | Example |
|---|---|
| **1 kilogram (1 kg)** | A salt packet |
| **Half kilogram (1/2 kg)** | A packet of daal |
| **Quarter kilogram (1/4 kg)** | A packet of tea |

**How they are related:**

```
1 kg = 2 half kg = 4 quarter kg
```

> **Tip:** Pick up a 1 kg salt packet and feel how heavy it is. Then try guessing which other things at home are about 1 kg!

## The Tricky Balls Puzzle

Here is a fun puzzle! You have **3 balls** that look the same. One is heavier. You have a **pan balance** but no weights.

**How to find the heavy ball in just 1 weighing:**

1. Put ball A on the left pan and ball B on the right pan.
2. If one side goes down, that ball is the **heavy** one.
3. If both sides are equal, then ball C (the one NOT on the balance) is the **heavy** one!
