## Counting Beyond 200

You already know numbers up to 200. Now let us go further!

When we count past 200, we keep going the same way:

```
200, 201, 202, 203, ... 210, 211, ... 250, ... 298, 299, 300!
```

- **298 + 1 = 299**
- **299 + 1 = 300** (Three Hundred!)

We can keep going: **300, 301, 302, ... 399, 400** (Four Hundred!)

> **Remember:** After every 99, the next number is a new hundred. 199 becomes 200, 299 becomes 300, 399 becomes 400, and so on.

## Hundreds, Tens, and Ones

Every 3-digit number is made up of **Hundreds (H)**, **Tens (T)**, and **Ones (O)**.

| Number | Hundreds (H) | Tens (T) | Ones (O) | Number Name |
|:------:|:---:|:---:|:---:|-------------|
| 235 | 2 | 3 | 5 | Two Hundred Thirty Five |
| 309 | 3 | 0 | 9 | Three Hundred Nine |
| 275 | 2 | 7 | 5 | Two Hundred Seventy Five |
| 423 | 4 | 2 | 3 | Four Hundred Twenty Three |
| 500 | 5 | 0 | 0 | Five Hundred |

We can also write number sentences:
- 235 = **200 + 30 + 5** (2 Hundreds + 3 Tens + 5 Ones)
- 309 = **300 + 0 + 9** (3 Hundreds + 0 Tens + 9 Ones)

> **Tip:** Think of it like sweet boxes! Big boxes hold 100, small boxes hold 10, and loose sweets are 1 each.

## Hop Hundreds Home (Number Grid)

Imagine a tall building where houses are numbered in rows of 10:

```
Row 3:  201  202  203  204  205  206  207  208  209  210
Row 2:  111  112  113  114  115  116  117  118  119  120  (not shown, just pattern)
Row 1:  101  102  103  104  105  106  107  108  109  110
Row 0:  001  002  003  004  005  006  007  008  009  010
```

**Patterns to notice:**
- Moving **right** by one house: the number goes up by **1**
- Moving **up** by one floor: the number goes up by **10**
- Moving **right** by one column and **up** one floor: the number goes up by **11**

> **Example:** One floor above house 105 is house 115. Ten floors above house 13 is house 113!

## Comparing Numbers: Greater Than and Less Than

To compare two numbers, we use two special signs:

- **&gt;** means **greater than** (more than)
- **&lt;** means **less than**

> **Tip:** The open mouth of the sign always points to the **bigger** number. Think of it like a hungry crocodile -- it always wants to eat the bigger number!

![A crocodile mouth opening towards the bigger number to show greater than and less than signs](crocodile-greater-less-than.png)

### How to compare 3-digit numbers:

**Step 1:** Compare the **hundreds** first.

| Compare | Hundreds | Result |
|---------|:--------:|--------|
| 321 vs 231 | 3 vs 2 | 321 > 231 (3 hundreds is more than 2 hundreds) |

**Step 2:** If hundreds are the same, compare the **tens**.

| Compare | Hundreds | Tens | Result |
|---------|:--------:|:----:|--------|
| 329 vs 392 | 3 = 3 | 2 vs 9 | 329 < 392 |

**Step 3:** If hundreds and tens are the same, compare the **ones**.

| Compare | Hundreds | Tens | Ones | Result |
|---------|:--------:|:----:|:----:|--------|
| 157 vs 153 | 1 = 1 | 5 = 5 | 7 vs 3 | 157 > 153 |

## Making Numbers with Digits

Using the digits **2**, **3**, and **4** (without repeating), you can make these 3-digit numbers:

| Number | Hundreds | Tens | Ones |
|:------:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| 234 | 2 | 3 | 4 |
| 243 | 2 | 4 | 3 |
| 324 | 3 | 2 | 4 |
| 342 | 3 | 4 | 2 |
| 423 | 4 | 2 | 3 |
| 432 | 4 | 3 | 2 |

- The **smallest** number is **234** (smallest digit in hundreds place).
- The **greatest** number is **432** (biggest digit in hundreds place).

> **Tip:** To make the smallest number, put the smallest digit first. To make the biggest number, put the biggest digit first!

## The Magical Count

Pick any number name. Count its letters. Write that number's name. Count again. Keep going. You will always reach **FOUR**!

```
235 → "Two Hundred and Thirty Five" → 23 letters
 23 → "Twenty Three" → 11 letters
 11 → "Eleven" → 6 letters
  6 → "Six" → 3 letters
  3 → "Three" → 5 letters
  5 → "Five" → 4 letters
  4 → "Four" → 4 letters ← It stays at FOUR!
```

> **Magic:** No matter what number you start with, you always end up at FOUR!

## Key Words to Remember

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Hundreds** | The place in a number that shows how many 100s (the leftmost digit in a 3-digit number) |
| **Tens** | The place that shows how many 10s (the middle digit) |
| **Ones** | The place that shows how many 1s (the last digit) |
| **Greater than (>)** | A sign that means the first number is bigger |
| **Less than (<)** | A sign that means the first number is smaller |
| **3-digit number** | A number with hundreds, tens, and ones (like 235 or 409) |
| **Number sentence** | Writing a number as a sum of its parts (like 235 = 200 + 30 + 5) |
| **Ascending** | Arranged from smallest to biggest |
| **Descending** | Arranged from biggest to smallest |
