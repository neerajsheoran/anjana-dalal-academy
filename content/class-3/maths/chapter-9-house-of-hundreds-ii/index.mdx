## Numbers Beyond 500

We already know numbers up to 500. Now let us explore bigger numbers -- all the way to **1000**!

```
500 --- 600 --- 700 --- 800 --- 900 --- 1000
```

These are the **hundreds** after 500:
- **600** = Six Hundred
- **700** = Seven Hundred
- **800** = Eight Hundred
- **900** = Nine Hundred
- **1000** = One Thousand

> **Fun fact:** In the story of Akbar and Birbal, Birbal said there were **963 crows** in the city. That is a big number -- 9 hundreds, 6 tens, and 3 ones!

## Numbers on the Number Line

We can place numbers on a **number line** to see where they live.

```
500---520---540---560---580---600---620---640---660---680---700
                                         |
                                        628
```

Every number fits between two other numbers. For example, **628** lives between **620** and **630**.

### Finding a number's neighbours:

| Number | Between which hundreds? | Between which tens? |
|:------:|:-----------------------:|:-------------------:|
| 468 | 400 and 500 | 460 and 470 |
| 693 | 600 and 700 | 690 and 700 |
| 734 | 700 and 800 | 730 and 740 |
| 899 | 800 and 900 | 890 and 900 |

> **Tip:** Look at the hundreds digit to find which hundreds a number is between. Look at the tens digit to find which tens it is between.

## Number Patterns

Numbers often follow **patterns**. Can you see the pattern and fill in what comes next?

**Counting by 2s backward:**
```
1000, 998, 996, 994, 992, 990, ...
```

**Counting by 10s forward from 720:**
```
720, 730, 740, 750, 760, 770, 780, ...
```

**Counting by 100s forward from 150:**
```
150, 250, 350, 450, 550, 650, 750, 850, ...
```

**Counting by 25s forward from 905:**
```
905, 930, 955, 980, ...
```

> **Tip:** Look at how much each number changes. Is it going up by 5? By 10? By 25? That is the pattern!

If you have **6 paper slips** and can write **100**, **10**, or **1** on each:

| Slips used | Number made |
|---|:---:|
| 6 slips of 100 | 600 (the biggest!) |
| 4 slips of 100, 2 slips of 10 | 420 |
| 2 slips of 100, 3 slips of 10, 1 slip of 1 | 231 |
| 1 slip of 100, 2 slips of 10, 3 slips of 1 | 123 |
| 6 slips of 1 | 6 (the smallest!) |

## Bhutasankhya: Word Numerals

Long ago in India, people used **words** to represent numbers! This system is called **Bhutasankhya**.

| Word | Number | Why? |
|------|:------:|------|
| Moon | 1 | There is 1 moon |
| Eyes | 2 | We have 2 eyes |
| Tri kala (Past, Present, Future) | 3 | There are 3 times |
| Directions (N, S, E, W) | 4 | There are 4 directions |
| Palm (fingers) | 5 | We have 5 fingers |
| Hexagon | 6 | A hexagon has 6 sides |
| Sapta svara (musical notes) | 7 | There are 7 musical notes: Sa Re Ga Ma Pa Dha Ni |

> **Fun fact:** Using this system, "Moon-Twins" means the number **12** (Moon = 1, Twins = 2). "Qutub Minar-Char Minar" means **14**!

## Key Words to Remember

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Thousand** | 1000, equal to 10 hundreds |
| **Number line** | A line where numbers are placed in order |
| **Number pattern** | Numbers that follow a rule (like counting by 5s) |
| **Number sentence** | A way to write a number using + or - (like 300 + 68) |
| **Palindrome number** | A number that reads the same forwards and backwards (like 353) |
| **Expanded form** | Writing a number showing hundreds, tens, and ones (like 300 + 60 + 8) |
| **Bhutasankhya** | An ancient Indian way of using words to say numbers |
