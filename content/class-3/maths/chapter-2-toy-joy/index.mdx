## 3D Shapes Around Us

Look around you! Many things have shapes. A box, a ball, a can, an ice-cream cone -- they are all **3D shapes**. "3D" means they are not flat. You can hold them in your hand.

Here are the five main 3D shapes:

| Shape | What it looks like | Examples |
|-------|-------------------|----------|
| **Cube** | A box with all sides equal | Dice, sugar cube, Rubik's cube |
| **Cuboid** | A box shape (like a brick) | Book, matchbox, brick, eraser |
| **Cylinder** | A round tube shape | Water bottle, bangle, pipe |
| **Cone** | Pointed at one end, round at the other | Ice-cream cone, birthday cap, funnel |
| **Sphere** | A round ball shape | Cricket ball, orange, marble |

> **Remember:** A **cube** is a special type of **cuboid** where all sides are the same size!

---

## Faces, Edges, and Corners

Every 3D shape has **faces**, **edges**, and **corners**.

- A **face** is the flat or curved surface of a shape.
- An **edge** is the line where two faces meet.
- A **corner** is the point where edges meet.

| Shape | Faces | Edges | Corners |
|-------|:-----:|:-----:|:-------:|
| **Cube** | 6 flat | 12 | 8 |
| **Cuboid** | 6 flat | 12 | 8 |
| **Cylinder** | 2 flat + 1 curved | 2 curved | 0 |
| **Cone** | 1 flat + 1 curved | 1 curved | 1 (the tip) |
| **Sphere** | 1 curved | 0 | 0 |

> **Tip:** A **sphere** has no edges and no corners. That is why a ball rolls so easily!

---

## Flat Faces and Curved Faces

Some shapes have only **flat faces**. Some have only **curved faces**. Some have **both**!

| Type | Shapes |
|------|--------|
| Only flat faces | Cube, Cuboid |
| Only curved faces | Sphere |
| Both flat and curved faces | Cylinder, Cone |

## Making Things with Shapes

You can join shapes to make new things!

**A toy engine** can be made with:
- Cylinders for the wheels and chimney
- A cuboid for the body
- A cone for the funnel on top

**A house** can be made with:
- A cuboid for the walls
- A cone or cuboid for the roof
- Cylinders for pillars

```
  /\          <-- Cone (roof)
 /  \
|    |        <-- Cuboid (walls)
|    |
------
```

> **Try this:** Collect old boxes, bottles, and cans. Use them to build a rocket, a house, or a train!

## The Dice

A **dice** (or die) is a **cube**. It has 6 faces with dots numbered 1 to 6.

There is a special pattern on a dice:
- The face opposite to **1** has **6** (1 + 6 = 7)
- The face opposite to **2** has **5** (2 + 5 = 7)
- The face opposite to **3** has **4** (3 + 4 = 7)

> **Pattern:** On a dice, opposite faces always add up to **7**!

---

## Joining Cubes

When you join cubes together, you can make new shapes.

- Join **2 cubes** side by side and you get a **cuboid**.
- Join **3 cubes** and you can make different shapes depending on how you arrange them.

```
Join 2 cubes:
+---+---+
|   |   |   <-- This looks like a cuboid!
+---+---+

Join 3 cubes in a line:
+---+---+---+
|   |   |   |
+---+---+---+

Join 3 cubes in an L-shape:
+---+---+
|   |   |
+---+---+
|   |
+---+
```

## Key Words to Remember

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **3D shape** | A shape that is not flat -- it has length, width, and height |
| **Cube** | A 3D shape with 6 equal square faces (like a dice) |
| **Cuboid** | A 3D shape with 6 rectangular faces (like a brick) |
| **Cylinder** | A 3D shape with 2 flat round faces and 1 curved face (like a can) |
| **Cone** | A 3D shape with 1 flat round face and 1 curved face, pointed at one end |
| **Sphere** | A 3D shape that is perfectly round (like a ball) |
| **Face** | The flat or curved surface of a 3D shape |
| **Edge** | The line where two faces of a shape meet |
| **Corner** | The point where edges meet |
