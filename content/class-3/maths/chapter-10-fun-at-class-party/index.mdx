## Measuring Length

When you want to know how long, how tall, or how wide something is, you are **measuring its length**.

There are many ways to measure length:
- Using your **hand span** (how wide your open hand is)
- Using your **footsteps**
- Using a **pencil** or **paper strip**
- Using a **metre rod** or **measuring tape**

> **Important:** When two people measure the same table with their hand spans, they may get **different answers** because their hands are different sizes!

---

## Comparing Lengths

To find out which thing is **longer** or **shorter**, we compare them.

### Ways to compare:

1. **Place side by side:** Put two things next to each other. The one that sticks out more is **longer**.

2. **Use a string:** If you cannot move the things, use a string. Cut a string as long as one thing. Then hold the string against the other thing to compare.

```
Shelly's pencil:   |---------|
Adi's pencil:      |-------------|

Adi's pencil is LONGER.
```

> **Example:** To check if a table can fit through a door, measure the table's width with a string. Then hold the string against the door. If the string is shorter than the door, the table will fit!

---

## Measuring Without a Ruler

Before we use proper tools, we can measure with everyday things:

| What to use | Good for measuring |
|---|---|
| **Hand span** | Small things like books, desks |
| **Footsteps** | Distance across a room |
| **Pencil lengths** | Medium-sized things |
| **Paper strips** | Walls, tables, boards |

But there is a problem! Everyone's hand, foot, and pencil are **different sizes**. So we need something that is **always the same**.

## The Metre

A **metre** is a standard unit of length. It is the same everywhere in the world!

**How long is one metre?**
- It is about the height of a 3-year-old child.
- It is about the length of a big step for an adult.
- A metre rod or inch tape can show you exactly one metre.

### Making a metre rope:

1. Take a **metre rod** and a piece of **rope**.
2. Make a knot at one end.
3. Stretch the rope along the metre rod.
4. Make a knot at the other end, at exactly one metre.

Now you have a **metre rope** to measure things!

---

## Things Around Us: More or Less Than a Metre?

| Less than one metre | About one metre | More than one metre |
|---|---|---|
| A pencil | Width of a door | A classroom wall |
| A school bag | Height of a young child | A cricket pitch |
| A notebook | A big umbrella | A tall tree |
| A water bottle | A walking stick | A bus |

---

## Half a Metre and Quarter of a Metre

We can make smaller measurements from one metre:

```
One metre rope:
|---------------------------------------------------|

Fold in half = Half a metre:
|-------------------------|

Fold in half again = Quarter of a metre:
|------------|
```

- **Half a metre** = the metre rope folded in 2 equal parts
- **Quarter of a metre** = the metre rope folded in 4 equal parts

![A metre rope folded into half and quarter showing the relative sizes](metre-half-quarter.png)

| Measurement | How long? |
|---|---|
| Quarter of a metre | About the length of a large pencil |
| Half a metre | About the length of your arm from elbow to hand |
| One metre | About the height of a 3-year-old child |

## Interesting Body Measurements

Some fun things that are often true about our bodies:

| Statement | Often true! |
|---|---|
| Your head is about **3 hand spans** around | Check with a string! |
| Your **forearm** is about as long as your **foot** | Measure and see! |
| Your **height** is about the same as your **arms stretched wide** | Try it out! |

> **Tip:** These are not exact for everyone, but they are close for most people. Try measuring yourself and your friends!

---

## Estimating Lengths

**Estimating** means making a smart guess before you actually measure.

Try these:
1. Guess how long your desk is. Then measure it with your hand span.
2. Guess how tall your teacher is. Is it more or less than one metre?
3. Cut a piece of wool that you think is one metre long. Then check with a metre rope!

> **Remember:** The more you practise estimating, the better you get at it!

## Key Words to Remember

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Length** | How long or tall something is |
| **Measure** | To find out the size or length of something |
| **Hand span** | The distance from your thumb tip to your little finger tip when your hand is open |
| **Metre** | A standard unit for measuring length (the same everywhere) |
| **Half metre** | Half of one metre |
| **Quarter metre** | One-fourth of a metre |
| **Estimate** | A smart guess about the measurement before actually measuring |
| **Compare** | Looking at two things to see which is longer or shorter |
