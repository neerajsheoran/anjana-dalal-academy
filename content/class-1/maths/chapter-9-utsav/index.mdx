## Festivals of India

India is a land of many festivals! When winter ends and the sunny days begin, people celebrate with joy.

Different parts of India have different names for this celebration:

| Festival | Where It is Celebrated |
|----------|----------------------|
| **Makar Sankranti** | Maharashtra, Karnataka, and other states |
| **Pongal** | Tamil Nadu |
| **Bihu** | Assam |
| **Lohri** | Punjab |
| **Uttarayan** | Gujarat |

People fly kites, make sweets, dance, and sing during these festivals. They wear colourful clothes and decorate their homes with beautiful **patterns**.

---

## What is a Pattern?

A **pattern** is something that repeats in the same order, again and again.

Patterns are everywhere! Look around you:

- The **stripes** on a zebra repeat: black, white, black, white.
- The **petals** of a flower go round and round.
- The **tiles** on a floor repeat their design.
- The **beads** on a necklace follow a pattern of colours.

## Colour Patterns

Munna loves painting. He made a pattern using thumb prints:

**Red, Blue, Red, Blue, Red, Blue...**

What colour comes next? **Red!** Because the pattern repeats: Red, Blue, Red, Blue...

Here are more colour patterns:

- **Green, Yellow, Green, Yellow, Green, Yellow...** — Next is **Green**.
- **Red, Red, Blue, Red, Red, Blue...** — Next is **Red**.
- **Pink, Orange, Orange, Pink, Orange, Orange...** — Next is **Pink**.

> **The trick:** Look at what group repeats. Then continue the group!

## Number Patterns

Numbers can also make patterns!

| Pattern | What Comes Next? |
|---------|-----------------|
| 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, **?** | **6** — Each number is 1 more |
| 2, 4, 6, 8, **?** | **10** — Each number is 2 more |
| 5, 10, 15, 20, **?** | **25** — Each number is 5 more |
| 10, 20, 30, 40, **?** | **50** — Each number is 10 more |
| 1, 1, 2, 2, 3, 3, **?** | **4** — Each number is repeated twice |

> **Think about it:** In the pattern 3, 6, 9, 12, what comes next? The answer is **15** because each number is 3 more than the one before it!

## Patterns in Nature

The world around us is full of beautiful patterns!

- **Butterfly wings** — both sides look the same.
- **Flower petals** — arranged in a circle.
- **Honeycomb** — hexagons (six-sided shapes) packed together.
- **Zebra stripes** — black and white lines.
- **Peacock feathers** — spots that repeat.
- **Leaves** — veins spreading out from the middle.

> **Activity:** Go outside and look at leaves, flowers, and animals. Can you find any patterns?

## Patterns in Indian Art

India has many beautiful art forms that use patterns.

### Rangoli and Kolam

In South India, people draw **kolam** patterns at the entrance of their homes every morning. In other parts of India, we call it **rangoli**.

Kolam uses dots and curved lines to make lovely designs. The lines loop around the dots in a repeating pattern.

Rangoli is made during festivals like **Diwali** and **Pongal** using coloured powder, flower petals, or rice.

![A colourful rangoli pattern with repeating shapes and symmetrical design](rangoli-kolam-pattern.png)

### Patterns in Temples and Monuments

If you visit old temples, mosques, churches, or gurudwaras, you will see amazing patterns carved in stone. These patterns repeat shapes like flowers, leaves, and geometric designs.

## Making Your Own Patterns

You can make patterns using things around you:

- **Clapping pattern:** Clap, snap, clap, snap, clap, snap...
- **Pebble pattern:** Big stone, small stone, big stone, small stone...
- **Leaf pattern:** Neem leaf, mango leaf, neem leaf, mango leaf...
- **Bangle pattern:** Red bangle, blue bangle, red bangle, blue bangle...

> **Activity:** Collect pebbles, flowers, and leaves. Arrange them in a pattern. Show it to your friend and ask them to continue the pattern!

## Key Words to Remember

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Pattern** | Something that repeats in the same order |
| **Repeat** | To happen again and again in the same way |
| **Rangoli** | A colourful pattern made on the floor during festivals |
| **Kolam** | A pattern drawn with dots and lines, popular in South India |
| **Sequence** | Things arranged in a particular order |
