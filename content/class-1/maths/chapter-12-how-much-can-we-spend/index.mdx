## Indian Money — Coins and Notes

In India, we use **Rupees** to buy things. The symbol for Rupees is **Rs** or the sign **₹**.

We have **coins** and **notes** (paper money).

### Coins

| Coin | How It Looks |
|------|-------------|
| **₹1** | Small silver coin |
| **₹2** | Slightly bigger coin |
| **₹5** | A coin with a round edge |
| **₹10** | A coin with two colours (silver and gold) |

### Notes

| Note | Colour |
|------|--------|
| **₹10** | Chocolate brown |
| **₹20** | Greenish-yellow |
| **₹50** | Fluorescent blue |
| **₹100** | Lavender (light purple) |

> Coins are round and made of metal. Notes are flat and made of paper or special material.

## Buying Things with Coins

We can pay for things using different coins.

**Example:** A ball costs **₹10**. Riya can pay in different ways:

- **10** coins of ₹1
- **5** coins of ₹2
- **2** coins of ₹5
- **1** coin of ₹10

All of these add up to ₹10!

**Example:** A toy car costs **₹14**.
- Riya has ₹10. She needs **₹4 more** to buy the car.
- She can give a ₹10 note and 4 coins of ₹1.
- Or she can give a ₹10 note and 2 coins of ₹2.

---

## Adding Up Money

When we have many coins, we add them up to find the **total amount**.

**Example:** You have three ₹5 coins. How much money do you have?
5 + 5 + 5 = **₹15**

**Example:** You have one ₹10 note and one ₹5 coin. How much do you have?
10 + 5 = **₹15**

**Example:** You have two ₹10 notes. How much money is that?
10 + 10 = **₹20**

## Paying in Different Ways

The same amount can be paid using different combinations of coins and notes.

### How to make ₹20:

| Way | Coins or Notes Used |
|-----|-------------------|
| Way 1 | One ₹20 note |
| Way 2 | Two ₹10 coins |
| Way 3 | Four ₹5 coins |
| Way 4 | One ₹10 coin + two ₹5 coins |
| Way 5 | One ₹10 coin + five ₹2 coins |

### How to make ₹13:

| Way | Coins Used |
|-----|-----------|
| Way 1 | One ₹10 coin + one ₹2 coin + one ₹1 coin |
| Way 2 | One ₹10 coin + three ₹1 coins |
| Way 3 | Two ₹5 coins + one ₹2 coin + one ₹1 coin |

> There are many ways to make the same amount!

## Matching Same Amounts

Sometimes different sets of coins have the **same value**.

- Five ₹2 coins = One ₹10 coin (both are ₹10)
- Two ₹5 coins = One ₹10 coin (both are ₹10)
- Two ₹10 notes = One ₹20 note (both are ₹20)

## Saving Money — The Gullak

A **gullak** is a piggy bank or money box. It is a box with a small hole on top where you drop coins.

Saving money is a very good habit! Even small coins add up over time.

- If you save **₹2 every day**, in 5 days you will have ₹10.
- If you save **₹5 every day**, in 10 days you will have ₹50!

> **Activity:** You can make your own gullak from an old box! Cut a small slit on the top. Decorate it with colours. Start saving your coins in it!
