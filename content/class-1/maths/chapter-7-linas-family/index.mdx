## Meet Lina's Family

Lina lives in a hilly village with her family. She stays with her **grandparents**, **parents**, and her brother **Shanbor**.

Her uncles, aunts, and cousins live nearby. They all come together for a family get-together!

Lina is standing on a stool because she wants to look tall. But her grandfather is the **tallest** member of the family, and little Shanbor is the **shortest**.

> **Think about it:** Who is the tallest person in your family? Who is the shortest?

---

## Tall and Short

We can compare how tall people and things are.

- **Tall** means high up. A tree is tall. A giraffe is tall.
- **Short** means not so high. A cat is short. A stool is short.
- **Taller** means more tall than something else.
- **Shorter** means less tall than something else.

**Example:** Lina's father is **taller** than her mother. But her grandfather is the **tallest** of all.

> **Did you know?** The **Statue of Unity** in Gujarat, India, is the tallest statue in the whole world! It is a statue of Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel.

---

## Long and Short

Things also have **length**. Some things are **long** and some are **short**.

- A rope can be **long** or **short**.
- A pencil is **shorter** than a stick.
- A snake is **longer** than a worm.

| Longer | Shorter |
|--------|---------|
| A bus | A car |
| A river | A stream |
| A train | An auto |
| A broom | A toothbrush |

---

## Thick and Thin

Some things are **thick** and some are **thin**.

- A tree trunk is **thick**. A twig is **thin**.
- A dictionary is **thick**. A notebook is **thin**.
- A rope is **thick**. A thread is **thin**.

---

## Near and Far

Lina's house is near the shop but far from the school.

- **Near** means close by. The things you can reach easily are near.
- **Far** means a long way away. The things you need to walk a lot to reach are far.

> **Think about it:** Is your home near or far from your school?

---

## Measuring Length with Handspans

How can we find out how long something is? One way is to use our **handspan**.

A **handspan** is the distance from the tip of your thumb to the tip of your little finger when your hand is spread wide open.

**Example:** Dadi measured Chitra's sweater using her handspan. The sweater is about **5 handspans** long.

You can measure many things with your handspan:

| Object | About How Many Handspans? |
|--------|--------------------------|
| Your table | Try it! |
| Your Maths book | Try it! |
| Your school bag | Try it! |
| Your arm | Try it! |

> **Note:** For very small things (like an eraser or a key), a handspan is too big. Use **finger widths** instead!

---

## Measuring with Footspans

For bigger distances, we can use our **footspan**. A footspan is the length of your foot, from heel to toe.

You can measure:
- The distance from your seat to the classroom door.
- The length of one side of your classroom.
- The distance from your bed to the washroom.

> **Fun fact:** Your footspan and your friend's footspan may be different sizes. So you may get different answers for the same distance!

---

## Heavy and Light

Some things are **heavy** and some are **light**.

- A **watermelon** is heavy. A **grape** is light.
- A **school bag full of books** is heavy. An **empty bag** is light.
- A **brick** is heavy. A **feather** is light.

| Heavy Things | Light Things |
|-------------|-------------|
| Bag of rice | Leaf |
| Stone | Paper |
| Chair | Pencil |
| Bucket of water | Empty cup |

> **Think about it:** Between you and your friend, who is heavier? You can find out by sitting on a **see-saw** (jhula) or using a **weighing scale**.

---

## How Much Water?

Different things hold different amounts of water. This is called **capacity**.

- A **bucket** holds more water than a **mug**.
- A **mug** holds more water than a **glass**.
- A **glass** holds more water than a **spoon**.

You can fill a bucket using glasses of water:

| Container | How Many Glasses to Fill It? |
|-----------|----------------------------|
| A mug | About 2-3 glasses |
| A bottle | About 3-4 glasses |
| A bucket | Many, many glasses! |

> **Important:** Water is precious! We should not waste water.
> - Use a **bucket** for bathing, not a running shower.
> - Close the **tap** when not using water.
> - Give leftover water to **plants**.

## Key Words to Remember

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Tall / Short** | How high something stands |
| **Long / Short** | How stretched out something is |
| **Thick / Thin** | How wide something is from side to side |
| **Near / Far** | How close or distant something is |
| **Heavy / Light** | How much something weighs — heavy weighs more, light weighs less |
| **Handspan** | The distance across your open hand, from thumb tip to little finger tip |
| **Footspan** | The length of your foot, used to measure bigger distances |
| **Capacity** | How much a container can hold |
