## Where Are Things? — A Chat with Papa

**Kiran:** Papa, today teacher asked us — "Where is the cat?" But why does it matter where the cat is?

**Ravi:** Good question, beta! Knowing where things are helps us in everyday life. Think about it — when Mummy asks you, "Where are your shoes?", what do you say?

**Kiran:** I say they are near the door!

**Ravi:** Exactly! You just used a **position word** — "near". These words help us tell people exactly where something is. Let's play a game. Look around our room — where is the TV remote?

**Kiran:** It is **on** the table!

**Ravi:** And where is Chintu the dog sleeping?

**Kiran:** He is **under** the bed! He always sleeps there when it's hot.

**Ravi:** See? You already know position words! **On** means something is touching the top of another thing. **Under** means something is below it. Simple!

---

## Inside and Outside

**Kiran:** Papa, what about inside and outside? Dadi always says "Don't go **outside** after dark!"

**Ravi:** Ha ha, yes! **Inside** means within something — like the sweets are **inside** the dabba. **Outside** means not within — like the cows are **outside** our house on the gali.

**Kiran:** Oh! So my pencil is **inside** my pencil box, and the tree is **outside** our window?

**Ravi:** Perfect! Here's a fun one — when you open your tiffin box at school, what is **inside**?

**Kiran:** Parantha and achar! And sometimes Mummy keeps a chocolate **inside** too!

**Ravi:** And what is **outside** the tiffin box?

**Kiran:** The water bottle and my napkin!

**Ravi:** See, you're already using these words like a pro.

---

## Above and Below

**Kiran:** Papa, what is the difference between "above" and "on"? Teacher said both mean up.

**Ravi:** Great question! **On** means something is touching — like the glass is **on** the table, it touches the table. But **above** means something is higher but not touching. The fan is **above** our heads — it's up there but not touching us!

**Kiran:** Ohhhh! So the kite in the sky is **above** us, but the cap on my head is **on** my head?

**Ravi:** Exactly right! And **below** means lower than something. The floor is **below** our feet. When we stand on the balcony, the garden is **below** us.

**Kiran:** Like when we go to the temple and climb the stairs, the road is **below** us!

**Ravi:** Very good example, beta!

---

## Our Tiranga — Position in the Flag

**Kiran:** Papa, teacher also showed us the Indian flag. She said the colours have positions too!

**Ravi:** Yes! Our flag is called the **Tiranga** — it has three stripes. The saffron colour is at the **top**, white is in the **middle**, and green is at the **bottom**.

**Kiran:** And the blue chakra is in the white part!

**Ravi:** Right — the **Ashoka Chakra** is in the **middle** of the white stripe. See, even in our flag, we need position words to describe it properly. Without position words, how would you explain where the chakra is?

**Kiran:** I would just say "it's there!" and point!

**Ravi:** But what if you're talking on the phone and can't point? That's why we need words like top, middle, bottom, inside, above!

---

## Before and After

**Kiran:** Papa, what about "before" and "after"? Like I brush my teeth **before** school?

**Ravi:** That's "before" in time, but we also use it for position! Think of a train — you love trains, right?

**Kiran:** Yes! The Rajdhani Express!

**Ravi:** So the engine is **before** all the bogies — it comes first. And the last bogie is **after** all the others. When you stand in the assembly line at school, Priya is **before** you and Amit is **after** you.

**Kiran:** Oh, so "before" means in front and "after" means behind!

**Ravi:** Exactly!

---

## Why Position Words Matter

**Kiran:** Papa, but why do I need to learn all these words? Can't I just point?

**Ravi:** Think about this, Kiran. If there's a fire and you need to tell the fireman where someone is — "The aunty is **inside** the room **above** the shop" — these words can save a life! Or when the postman asks, "Where is flat number 5?", you say, "It is **on** the second floor, **after** the stairs." These words help everyone understand exactly where things are.

**Kiran:** Wow, I didn't think of it like that!

**Ravi:** And when you play hide-and-seek with your friends? You use all these words! "She's **behind** the tree!" or "Check **under** the table!" or "Look **inside** the cupboard!"

**Kiran:** Ha ha, yes! Position words make everything more fun!

**Ravi:** That's right, beta. Now — can you tell me, where is your Maths book right now?

**Kiran:** It is **on** my desk, **inside** my room, **near** the window!

**Ravi:** Three position words in one sentence! Well done!
