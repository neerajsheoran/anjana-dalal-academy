## Where Is the Cat?

Meena has a furry cat. But her cat loves to hide! Let us help Meena find her Cat.

The cat is **on** the window. Now the cat is **inside** the backpack. Oh look, it is **under** the bed! Wait, it ran **outside** the red rack!!

The cat climbed to the **top** of the car. Then it jumped **above** the hat. Finally, it hid **below** the mat.

We found the furry cat!

## Position Words

We use special words to tell **where** something is. These words tell us the **position** of things.

| Word        | What It Means                                                |
| ----------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **On**      | Touching the top of something. The book is **on** the table. |
| **Under**   | Below something. The dog is **under** the chair.             |
| **Inside**  | Within something. The toys are **inside** the box.           |
| **Outside** | Not within something. The cow is **outside** the house.      |
| **Above**   | Higher than something. The fan is **above** our head.        |
| **Below**   | Lower than something. The floor is **below** our feet.       |

## Inside and Outside

Look around your room. Some things are **inside** and some things are **outside**.

* We keep our shoes **inside** the shoe rack.
* We throw garbage **inside** the dustbin.
* The birds fly **outside** the window.
* We play **outside** the classroom.

> **Try this:** Look at your school bag. What is **inside** it? What is **outside** it?

## Near and Far

Some things are **near** us and some things are **far** away.

* Your desk is **near** you.
* The playground may be **far** from your classroom.
* The blackboard is **near** the teacher.

## Our National Flag — Tiranga

The Indian flag is called **Tiranga**. It has three colours.

| Position   | Colour           |
| ---------- | ---------------- |
| **Top**    | Saffron (orange) |
| **Middle** | White            |
| **Bottom** | Green            |

The **Ashoka Chakra** is **in the middle** of the white stripe. It is blue in colour.

!\[Indian Tiranga flag with labels showing top saffron, middle white with Ashoka Chakra, and bottom green]\(tiranga-flag-positions.png)

We hoist our flag on **Republic Day** (26 January) and **Independence Day** (15 August).

## Before and After

Imagine a train going **chhuk chhuk** on the tracks!

A train has an **engine** and many **bogies** (coaches).

* The engine is **before** all the bogies.
* Some bogies come **before** the red bogie.
* Some bogies come **after** the red bogie.

> **Think about it:** When you stand in a line, who is **before** you? Who is **after** you?

## Sorting and Grouping

We can put things that are **alike** together in a group.

* Put all the **pebbles** in one group.
* Put all the **leaves** in another group.
* Put all the **chalk pieces** in yet another group.

Buttons can be sorted in many ways:

* By **colour** — all red buttons together, all blue buttons together.
* By **size** — big buttons in one group, small buttons in another.
* By **shape** — round buttons together, square buttons together.

!\[Buttons sorted into groups by colour, size, and shape]\(sorting-buttons-groups.png)

> **Activity:** Collect some leaves, seeds, and stones. Can you sort them into groups?

## Key Words to Remember

| Word        | Meaning                                   |
| ----------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| **On**      | Resting on the surface of something       |
| **Under**   | Below or beneath something                |
| **Inside**  | Within a space or container               |
| **Outside** | Not within; in the open                   |
| **Above**   | Higher up than something                  |
| **Below**   | Lower down than something                 |
| **Near**    | Close to something                        |
| **Far**     | A long way from something                 |
| **Before**  | In front of something in a line           |
| **After**   | Behind something in a line                |
| **Sorting** | Putting things that are alike into groups |
