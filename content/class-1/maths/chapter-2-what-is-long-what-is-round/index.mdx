## Shapes of Things We See

Things around us look like different shapes. Let us learn about some shapes.

### Ball Shape (Sphere)

A **ball**, an **orange**, and a **laddoo** are all round like a ball. This shape is called a **sphere**.

Other examples: marble, globe, rasgulla

### Box Shape (Cuboid)

A **matchbox**, a **book**, and a **brick** look like a box. This shape is called a **cuboid**.

Other examples: eraser, lunch box, almirah

### Tube Shape (Cylinder)

A **glass**, a **pipe**, and a **candle** look like a tube. This shape is called a **cylinder**.

Other examples: water bottle, bangle box, drum

### Cap Shape (Cone)

A **birthday cap**, an **ice-cream cone**, and a **funnel** look like a pointed cap. This shape is called a **cone**.

Other examples: joker's hat, carrot tip

## Rolling and Sliding

Have you noticed that some things **roll** and some things **slide**?

### What is Rolling?

When something turns over and over and moves forward, it is **rolling**. Round things roll easily.

- A **ball** rolls.
- A **marble** rolls.
- An **orange** rolls.

### What is Sliding?

When something moves smoothly without turning over, it is **sliding**. Flat things slide easily.

- A **book** slides on a table.
- An **eraser** slides.
- A **box** slides.

### The Wise Grandmother's Story

Once, a little lamb was going to meet her grandmother. A big wolf wanted to catch her! The clever grandmother put the lamb inside a **dholak** (drum) and rolled it down the hill. The round dholak rolled very fast and the wolf could not catch the lamb.

> **Why did the dholak roll?** Because it is **round**! Round things roll easily, especially on a slope.

## Roll, Slide, or Both?

Some objects can do **both** — roll and slide!

| Object | Rolls? | Slides? |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Ball | Yes | No |
| Book | No | Yes |
| Coin | Yes | Yes |
| Pencil | Yes | Yes |
| Eraser | No | Yes |
| Bangle | Yes | No |
| Glass (tumbler) | Yes | Yes |

A **coin** can roll on its edge, but it can also slide on its flat side. A **pencil** can roll sideways, but it can also slide.

> **Activity:** Collect a ball, a box, a coin, and a pencil. Try rolling and sliding each one. What did you notice?

## Building with Shapes

You can use different shaped things to **build** structures.

- Stack **boxes** to make a tower or a building.
- Use a **cone** on top as a roof.
- Place **cylinders** (tubes) to make pillars.

> **Try this:** Use your books, pencil box, water bottle, and other things to build a house or a robot!

**Which shapes make the best towers?** Box shapes! They are flat on top, so you can stack them easily. Round shapes like balls roll away and cannot be stacked.

## Key Words to Remember

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Long** | Stretched out in one direction, like a pencil |
| **Round** | Shaped like a circle or ball |
| **Sphere** | A ball shape — round on all sides |
| **Cuboid** | A box shape — has flat sides |
| **Cylinder** | A tube shape — round and long |
| **Cone** | A cap shape — round at bottom, pointed at top |
| **Roll** | To turn over and over while moving |
| **Slide** | To move smoothly without turning over |
