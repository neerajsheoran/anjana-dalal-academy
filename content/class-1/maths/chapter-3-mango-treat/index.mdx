## The Mango Story

Some birds are sitting on a tree. They see a squirrel eating a big, juicy mango below.

One bird flies down to join. Now **2** of them are eating the mango.

One more bird joins. Now **3** of them are eating the mango.

One more joins. Now **4**. Then **5**, then **6**, then **7**, then **8**!

Finally, **9** birds and animals shared the mango together.

> **Notice:** Every time one more joined, the number went up by one. This is called **counting**!

---

## Counting from 1 to 9

When we count, we say the numbers in order. Each number is **one more** than the number before it.

| Number | Word | One More Makes |
|--------|------|---------------|
| 1 | One | 2 |
| 2 | Two | 3 |
| 3 | Three | 4 |
| 4 | Four | 5 |
| 5 | Five | 6 |
| 6 | Six | 7 |
| 7 | Seven | 8 |
| 8 | Eight | 9 |
| 9 | Nine | — |

## Counting with Fingers

We can use our fingers to show numbers!

- Hold up **1** finger — that is one.
- Hold up **2** fingers — that is two.
- Hold up **5** fingers — that is one full hand!

There are many ways to show the same number on your fingers. You can show **3** using your thumb and two fingers, or three fingers without the thumb.

![Hands showing numbers 1 to 5 using fingers in different ways](counting-with-fingers.png)

> **Activity:** Show 4 fingers to your friend. Ask them to show 4 fingers in a different way!

## More, Less, or Equal

When we compare two groups of things, we can tell which group has **more**, which has **less**, or if they are **equal** (the same).

**Example:** Riya has 5 crayons. Aman has 3 crayons.
- Riya has **more** crayons.
- Aman has **less** crayons.

**Example:** Priya has 4 flowers. Meena also has 4 flowers.
- They have an **equal** number of flowers.

> **How to compare:** Put things side by side, one next to the other. The group that has leftover things is **more**. The group that runs out first is **less**.

## Let Us Count Things

Look around you and count!

- **One** sun in the sky
- **Two** eyes on your face
- **Three** wheels on an auto-rickshaw
- **Four** legs on a table
- **Five** fingers on one hand
- **Six** legs on an ant
- **Seven** days in a week
- **Eight** legs on a spider
- **Nine** planets around the sun

> **Did you know?** A group of seven stars that you can see in the night sky is called **Saptarshi** (Big Dipper).

## Number Order

Numbers have a special order. We always count: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9.

If some numbers are missing, we can fill them in:

- 1, 2, **3**, 4, 5
- 3, 4, **5**, **6**, 7
- 5, **6**, **7**, **8**, 9

The number order goes from the **smallest** to the **largest**.

- **1** is the smallest number we learned.
- **9** is the largest number we learned so far.

> **Activity:** Can you count backwards? Try saying: 9, 8, 7, 6, 5, 4, 3, 2, 1!

## Putting Groups Together

When we put two groups of things together, we get a bigger group.

- A group of **3** mangoes and a group of **5** mangoes together make **8** mangoes.
- A group of **2** bananas and a group of **6** bananas together make **8** bananas.

## Key Words to Remember

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Counting** | Saying numbers in order: 1, 2, 3, 4... |
| **Number** | A word or symbol that tells how many (like 1, 2, 3) |
| **More** | A bigger amount — 5 is more than 3 |
| **Less** | A smaller amount — 2 is less than 7 |
| **Equal** | The same amount — 4 and 4 are equal |
| **Order** | Numbers arranged from smallest to largest or largest to smallest |
