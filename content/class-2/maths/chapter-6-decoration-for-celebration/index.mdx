## Getting Ready for a Festival

Rohan and his friends are making flower garlands to decorate their homes for a festival. Each garland has **10 flowers**, and some extra loose flowers are left over.

This is just like how numbers work! A garland of 10 flowers is like the **tens place**, and the loose flowers are like the **ones place**.

| Child | Garlands (Tens) | Loose Flowers (Ones) | Total Flowers |
|-------|----------------|---------------------|---------------|
| Rohan | 1 | 2 | 12 |
| Simran | 3 | 7 | 37 |
| Akash | 4 | 8 | 48 |
| Suwali | 5 | 3 | 53 |

## What is Addition?

When we put two groups **together**, we are doing **addition**. We use the **plus sign (+)** to show addition.

If Rohan has **12 flowers** and Suwali has **53 flowers**, how many do they have together?

```
  12  +  53  =  65
```

They have **65 flowers** in all!

## Adding Two-Digit Numbers (Without Carrying)

When the ones digits add up to **less than 10**, the addition is simple.

**Step 1:** Add the **ones** first.
**Step 2:** Add the **tens** next.

### Example: 43 + 20

```
   T  O
   4  3
 + 2  0
 ------
   6  3
```

- Ones: 3 + 0 = 3
- Tens: 4 + 2 = 6
- Answer: **63**

### Example: 12 + 53

```
   T  O
   1  2
 + 5  3
 ------
   6  5
```

- Ones: 2 + 3 = 5
- Tens: 1 + 5 = 6
- Answer: **65**

## Adding on the Ginladi (Bead String)

You can add numbers on a ginladi too. To find **25 + 12**:

1. Start at bead **25**.
2. Move **12 beads forward** (count 12 more).
3. You land on bead **37**.

So, 25 + 12 = **37**.

## Adding on the Number Line

A number line is another great tool for addition.

To find **23 + 14** on a number line:

1. Start at **23**.
2. Jump **10 steps** forward to reach **33**.
3. Jump **4 more steps** to reach **37**.

So, 23 + 14 = **37**.

> **Tip:** When adding on a number line, first jump by tens, then jump by ones. It is much easier!

## Adding Two-Digit Numbers (With Carrying)

Sometimes, the ones digits add up to **10 or more**. Then we need to **carry** 1 ten to the tens place.

### Example: 36 + 47

```
     T  O
     3  6
  +  4  7
  -------
     8  3
```

**Step 1:** Add the ones: 6 + 7 = **13**. That is 1 ten and 3 ones. Write **3** in the ones place. **Carry 1** to the tens place.

**Step 2:** Add the tens: 3 + 4 + 1 (carried) = **8**. Write **8** in the tens place.

**Answer: 83**

![Step-by-step visual showing carrying in addition - ones column adding to more than 10](addition-with-carrying-visual.png)

### Example: 56 + 19

```
     T  O
     5  6
  +  1  9
  -------
     7  5
```

- Ones: 6 + 9 = 15. Write 5, carry 1.
- Tens: 5 + 1 + 1 = 7.
- Answer: **75**

### Example: 45 + 35

```
     T  O
     4  5
  +  3  5
  -------
     8  0
```

- Ones: 5 + 5 = 10. Write 0, carry 1.
- Tens: 4 + 3 + 1 = 8.
- Answer: **80**

> **Remember:** When the ones add up to 10 or more, we "carry" the extra ten to the tens column. Think of it like making a new garland when you have 10 loose flowers!

## Addition with Blocks

Mahi has **23 blocks** (2 tens and 3 ones).
Isha has **38 blocks** (3 tens and 8 ones).

Together:
- Ones: 3 + 8 = 11 ones = 1 ten and 1 one
- Tens: 2 + 3 + 1 = 6 tens

Total: **61 blocks**

## Addition in Real Life

Addition helps us every day! Here are some examples:

- Rahim scored **43 runs** in one cricket match and **58 runs** in another. Total runs = 43 + 58 = **101 runs**.
- Simarpreet had **12 colour pencils** and got **36 more**. Total = 12 + 36 = **48 colour pencils**.
- Sarika spent **56 rupees** and Manish spent **35 rupees** at the fair. Total = 56 + 35 = **91 rupees**.

## Subtraction -- Taking Away

**Subtraction** means taking something away. We use the **minus sign (-)** to show subtraction.

A diya maker made **75 diyas** and sold **35** of them. How many are left?

```
  75 - 35 = 40
```

She has **40 diyas** left.

## Subtracting on the Ginladi

To find **32 - 12** on the ginladi:

1. Start at bead **32**.
2. Move **12 beads backward**.
3. You land on bead **20**.

So, 32 - 12 = **20**.

## Subtracting on the Number Line

To find **19 - 15** on the number line:

1. Start at **19**.
2. Jump **10 steps backward** to reach **9**.
3. Jump **5 more steps backward** to reach **4**.

So, 19 - 15 = **4**.

> **Tip:** When subtracting on a number line, first jump back by tens, then jump back by ones.

## Subtracting Without Borrowing

When the ones digit on top is **bigger** than the ones digit below, subtraction is simple.

### Example: 45 - 12

```
   T  O
   4  5
 - 1  2
 ------
   3  3
```

- Ones: 5 - 2 = 3
- Tens: 4 - 1 = 3
- Answer: **33**

## Subtracting With Borrowing

Sometimes, the ones digit on top is **smaller** than the ones digit below. Then we need to **borrow** 1 ten from the tens place.

### Example: 46 - 18

```
     T  O
     4  6    -->   3  16  (borrow 1 ten = 10 ones)
  -  1  8         -1   8
  -------         ------
     2  8          2   8
```

**Step 1:** We cannot take 8 away from 6. So we borrow 1 ten from the tens place. Now we have **3 tens** and **16 ones**.

**Step 2:** Ones: 16 - 8 = **8**.

**Step 3:** Tens: 3 - 1 = **2**.

**Answer: 28**

![Step-by-step visual showing borrowing in subtraction - opening a garland of 10 to get more ones](subtraction-with-borrowing-visual.png)

> **Think of it this way:** Jyoti has garlands of flowers. She needs to give 8 loose flowers, but she only has 6. So she opens 1 garland (1 ten) to get 10 more loose flowers. Now she has 16 loose flowers and can give away 8.

### Example: 54 - 28

```
     T  O
     5  4    -->   4  14
  -  2  8         -2   8
  -------         ------
     2  6          2   6
```

- Borrow: 5 tens becomes 4 tens, 4 ones becomes 14 ones.
- Ones: 14 - 8 = 6
- Tens: 4 - 2 = 2
- Answer: **26**

## Special Subtraction Facts

- When you subtract a number from **itself**, the answer is always **0**.
  - Example: 22 - 22 = **0**

- When you subtract **0** from a number, the number stays the same.
  - Example: 14 - 0 = **14**

## Addition and Subtraction Are Related

Addition and subtraction are like a **family**! They are related to each other.

If we know that **20 + 30 = 50**, then we also know:
- 50 - 30 = 20
- 50 - 20 = 30

This is called a **fact family**.

| Fact Family for 20, 30, 50 |
|----------------------------|
| 20 + 30 = 50 |
| 30 + 20 = 50 |
| 50 - 30 = 20 |
| 50 - 20 = 30 |

## Key Words to Remember

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Addition** | Putting two or more numbers together to find the total |
| **Plus (+)** | The sign used for addition |
| **Sum / Total** | The answer you get after adding |
| **Subtraction** | Taking one number away from another |
| **Minus (-)** | The sign used for subtraction |
| **Difference** | The answer you get after subtracting |
| **Carry** | Moving extra ones to the tens place when ones add up to 10 or more |
| **Borrow** | Taking 1 ten from the tens place to make more ones when subtracting |
| **Fact family** | A group of related addition and subtraction facts using the same three numbers |
