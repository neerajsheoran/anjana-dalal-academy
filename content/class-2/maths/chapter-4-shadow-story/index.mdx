## The Magic of Shadows

Have you ever seen a shadow play? In Karnataka, there is a famous puppet art called **Togalu Gombeyaata**. Artists use flat puppets to make shadows on a white screen. The shadows tell stories from Indian epics like the Ramayana and Mahabharata.

You can also make shadows! Hold your hand in front of a torch or a lamp near a wall. Move your fingers to make shapes of a **dog**, a **bird**, a **deer**, or a **rabbit**.

> **Fun Fact:** Shadows form when light falls on an object. The object blocks the light, and a dark shape appears on the other side -- that is the shadow!

![Hand shadows on a wall showing shapes of a dog, bird, and rabbit](hand-shadow-puppets.png)

## Shadows Change Size

Your shadow is not always the same size!

- In the **morning**, when the sun is low, your shadow is very **long**.
- At **noon**, when the sun is right above you, your shadow is very **short**.
- In the **evening**, your shadow becomes **long** again.

If you move a torch closer to an object, the shadow gets **bigger**. If you move the torch farther away, the shadow gets **smaller**.

## Tracing Shapes

When you place an object on paper and draw around its edges, you are **tracing** its shape.

| Object | Shape You Get After Tracing |
|--------|-----------------------------|
| Bottle cap | Circle |
| Eraser | Rectangle |
| Coin | Circle |
| Matchbox face | Rectangle |
| Leaf | Curved shape |

> **Try this:** Collect small objects like an eraser, a coin, a leaf, and a pencil sharpener. Put them on paper and trace around them. What shapes do you see?

## Flat Shapes -- Circle, Rectangle, Triangle, Square

When we trace objects or look at shadows, we see flat shapes. These are the four main flat shapes:

### Circle
- A circle is perfectly **round**.
- It has **no corners** and **no straight edges**.
- You can see circles in clocks, coins, bangles, plates, and wheels.

### Rectangle
- A rectangle has **4 sides** and **4 corners**.
- Two sides are **long** and two sides are **short**.
- You can see rectangles in doors, books, TV screens, and photo frames.

### Square
- A square has **4 sides** and **4 corners**.
- All four sides are **equal** in length.
- You can see squares in chessboard tiles, biscuits, and handkerchiefs.

### Triangle
- A triangle has **3 sides** and **3 corners**.
- You can see triangles in samosas, rooftops, and traffic signs.

| Shape | Sides | Corners |
|-------|-------|---------|
| **Circle** | 0 (it is curved) | 0 |
| **Triangle** | 3 | 3 |
| **Square** | 4 | 4 |
| **Rectangle** | 4 | 4 |

![Four flat shapes - circle, triangle, square, and rectangle - with sides and corners labelled](flat-shapes-sides-corners.png)

## Shapes Hide Everywhere!

Shapes like to play hide and seek! Here is where they hide:

- **Circle** hides behind a clock, a ball, a wheel, and a plate.
- **Rectangle** hides behind a shoe box, a photo frame, a door, and a TV.
- **Triangle** hides in a samosa, a roof, and a sliced sandwich.
- **Square** hides in a chessboard tile, a window pane, and a napkin.

## Corners and Edges in Flat Shapes

Some shapes have **straight edges** and some have **curved edges**.

- Objects with **curved edges** (like a circle) have **no corners**.
- A **triangle** has 3 straight edges and 3 corners.
- A **square** and a **rectangle** both have 4 straight edges and 4 corners.

Some objects have **both** straight and curved edges. For example, a semicircle has one straight edge and one curved edge.

## Patterns with Shapes

You can make beautiful **patterns** using shapes. A pattern is something that repeats in a regular way.

### Shape Patterns

```
Circle, Square, Circle, Square, Circle, Square ...
Triangle, Triangle, Circle, Triangle, Triangle, Circle ...
```

### Colour Patterns

You can colour shapes to make patterns too:
```
Red triangle, Blue triangle, Red triangle, Blue triangle ...
```

### Mehandi Patterns

When someone applies **mehandi** (henna) on their hands, they draw patterns using circles, curves, leaves, and dots that repeat again and again. This is a beautiful example of patterns from Indian culture!

![Shape and colour patterns repeating in a sequence - circles and squares alternating](repeating-shape-colour-patterns.png)

## Number Patterns

Patterns are not just about shapes. Numbers can also make patterns!

| Pattern | Rule |
|---------|------|
| 5, 10, 15, 20, 25 | Add 5 each time |
| 7, 14, 21, 28, 35 | Add 7 each time |
| 1, 7, 13, 19, 25 | Add 6 each time |
| 1, 4, 7, 10, 13 | Add 3 each time |

### Jumping Patterns

Think of Kikku the rabbit. He starts at tile 1 and jumps to tile 4. That means he jumps 3 tiles at a time.

```
1, 4, 7, 10, 13, 16, 19, 22, 25, 28
```

His mother jumps 5 tiles at a time starting from tile 1:

```
1, 6, 11, 16, 21, 26
```

### Letter Patterns

Letters can form patterns too:

```
AB, BC, CD, DE, EF ...
AA, B, C, D, E ...
```

> **Tip:** To find a pattern, look at what changes each time. Ask yourself: "What is being added? What is being repeated?"

## Key Words to Remember

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Shadow** | A dark shape made when an object blocks light |
| **Tracing** | Drawing around the edge of an object to get its shape |
| **Circle** | A round flat shape with no corners |
| **Rectangle** | A flat shape with 4 sides and 4 corners, two sides longer than the other two |
| **Square** | A flat shape with 4 equal sides and 4 corners |
| **Triangle** | A flat shape with 3 sides and 3 corners |
| **Pattern** | Something that repeats in a regular and predictable way |
| **Corner** | The point where two edges of a shape meet |
| **Edge** | The side or boundary line of a shape |
| **Curved edge** | An edge that is not straight, like the edge of a circle |
