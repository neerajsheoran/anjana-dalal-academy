## The Story of the Carpenter's Bed

Long ago, Raja Jagdeep wanted a beautiful carved bed for his queen. He called his best carpenter and said, "Make a bed that is **10 handspans long**."

The carpenter made the bed. But when the queen tried to lie on it, she did not fit! The bed was too small.

The raja measured the bed with his own hands. He counted **8 handspans**. But when a guard measured it, it was **14 handspans**!

How is this possible? Because everyone's hands are a **different size**! The carpenter had big hands, the raja had medium hands, and the guard had small hands.

> **This teaches us:** We need **standard units** (like centimetres and metres) so that everyone gets the same measurement. But first, let us learn to measure using everyday objects!

## Measuring Length

**Length** tells us how long or short something is.

We can measure length using things around us:

| Measuring Tool | What It Measures |
|----------------|-----------------|
| **Handspan** | The width of your stretched hand, from thumb tip to little finger tip |
| **Footstep** | The length of one step |
| **Eraser** | Short objects on your desk |
| **Pencil** | Medium-length objects |
| **Textbook** | The length of larger objects like a desk |

> **Remember:** When you measure using your hand or your foot, your answer might be different from your friend's answer. That is okay! It is because your hands and feet are different sizes.

## Long and Short Paths

Kavita needs to go to school from her home. There are many paths she can take. Some paths are **long** and some are **short**.

The **shortest path** takes the least time. The **longest path** takes the most time.

> **Try this:** You can compare the length of curved paths by placing a thread along each path. Then stretch the threads out straight and see which one is longer!

## Measuring with Blocks

We can stack small blocks next to objects to find their height.

Imagine stacking blocks next to toy animals:
- A giraffe toy is about **10 blocks** tall.
- An elephant toy is about **7 blocks** tall.
- A monkey toy is about **5 blocks** tall.

The giraffe is the **tallest** and the monkey is the **shortest**.

## Heavier and Lighter

Some things are **heavy** and some things are **light**. We find out by **weighing** them.

### Comparing Vegetables

Imagine all the vegetables sitting together at a chaupal (village meeting place):

- A **pumpkin** is very heavy. It is the **heaviest** of all.
- A **watermelon** is also heavy.
- A **cauliflower** is lighter than a pumpkin but heavier than a carrot.
- A **capsicum** is lighter than a cauliflower.
- A **carrot** is light.
- A **green chilli** is very light. It is the **lightest** of all.

| Vegetable | Weight |
|-----------|--------|
| Pumpkin | Heaviest |
| Watermelon | Very heavy |
| Cauliflower | Medium heavy |
| Capsicum | Medium |
| Carrot | Light |
| Green chilli | Lightest |

## How to Compare Weight

You can compare the weight of two things by holding one in each hand. The hand that goes **down** is holding the **heavier** object. The hand that goes **up** is holding the **lighter** object.

### Making a Simple Balance

You can make your own balance at home!

**What you need:** 2 paper cups, some thread, and a clothes hanger.

1. Tie a thread to each paper cup.
2. Hang both cups from the two ends of the hanger.
3. Put one object in each cup.
4. The cup that goes **down** has the **heavier** object.

## Understanding a Balance

A balance has two sides. When we put objects on both sides:

- If one side goes **down**, that side is **heavier**.
- If both sides are at the **same level**, both objects have **equal weight** -- they are **balanced**.

Think of a **see-saw** in a park! When a watermelon sits on one side and one mango on the other, the watermelon side goes down because it is heavier. To balance the watermelon, you need **many mangoes** on the other side.

## Measuring Liquids -- Capacity

**Capacity** tells us how much liquid a container can hold.

### Making Nimbu Pani (Lemonade)

Raghu makes nimbu pani for his grandmother. For **1 glass** of nimbu pani, he needs:

| Ingredient | Quantity |
|------------|----------|
| Water | 1 glass |
| Sugar | 1 spoon |
| Lemon juice | 2 spoons |
| Salt | A pinch |

If Raghu wants to make **3 glasses** of nimbu pani (for himself and 2 friends), he needs:

| Ingredient | Quantity |
|------------|----------|
| Water | 3 glasses |
| Sugar | 3 spoons |
| Lemon juice | 6 spoons |
| Salt | 3 pinches |

> **Tip:** When we make more servings, we multiply each ingredient by the number of servings.

### Comparing Bottles

Different bottles hold **different amounts** of water.

- A big bottle might hold **4 glasses** of water.
- A small bottle might hold only **2 glasses** of water.
- The big bottle has **more capacity** than the small bottle.

You can compare the capacity of two bottles by filling them with glasses of water and counting how many glasses each one holds.

### Jal Tarang -- Musical Water

You can make a musical instrument with water! Take 5 to 7 similar glasses and fill them with **different levels** of water. Tap each glass gently with a spoon. Each glass makes a **different sound**!

- A glass with **less water** makes a **higher** sound.
- A glass with **more water** makes a **lower** sound.

This instrument is called **Jal Tarang** and it is used in Indian classical music.

> **Remember:** Water is very precious. We should never waste water. Always turn off the tap after use!

## Key Words to Remember

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Length** | How long or short something is |
| **Handspan** | The distance from the tip of the thumb to the tip of the little finger when the hand is stretched |
| **Measure** | To find out how long, heavy, or full something is |
| **Heavier** | Weighing more than something else |
| **Lighter** | Weighing less than something else |
| **Balance** | A tool used to compare the weight of two objects |
| **Capacity** | How much liquid a container can hold |
| **Taller** | Having more height than something else |
| **Shorter** | Having less height than something else |
