## A Visit to the Fair

Rupal loves fairs! Her mother gave her **Rs 50** to spend. At the fair, there are rides, games, balloons, snacks, and lots of fun.

But Rupal has to be careful. She needs to **count her money** so she knows how much she has spent and how much is left.

## Indian Coins and Notes

We use **rupees** and **paise** in India. The sign for rupees is **Rs**.

### Coins We Use

| Coin | Value |
|------|-------|
| Rs 1 coin | 1 rupee |
| Rs 2 coin | 2 rupees |
| Rs 5 coin | 5 rupees |
| Rs 10 coin | 10 rupees |
| Rs 20 coin | 20 rupees |

### Notes We Use

| Note | Value |
|------|-------|
| Rs 10 note | 10 rupees |
| Rs 20 note | 20 rupees |
| Rs 50 note | 50 rupees |
| Rs 100 note | 100 rupees |
| Rs 200 note | 200 rupees |
| Rs 500 note | 500 rupees |

> **Fun Fact:** Look at a Rs 500 note carefully. The number "500" and the words "Five Hundred Rupees" are written many times on it in different Indian languages! The **Red Fort** of Delhi is printed on this note.

### Old Coins -- Paise

Long ago, people used **paise** along with rupees. Tejal's grandmother showed her some old coins:

- **25 paise** coin
- **50 paise** coin
- **10 paise** coin
- **20 paise** coin

```
100 paise = 1 rupee
```

So:
- 2 coins of 50 paise = Rs 1
- 4 coins of 25 paise = Rs 1
- 5 coins of 20 paise = Rs 1

> Today, paise coins are no longer used. The smallest coin we use now is **Rs 1**.

## Counting Money

To count money, we add up the value of each coin and note.

**Example:** If Rupal has these coins: Rs 10 + Rs 10 + Rs 5 + Rs 2 + Rs 1

Total = **Rs 28**

## Spending Money -- Subtraction

When we **buy** something, we give money. The money we give is **spent**. The money left is what we **save**.

```
Money we had  -  Money we spent  =  Money left
```

**Example:** Rupal had Rs 50. She spent Rs 10 on a ride and Rs 15 on snacks.

- Money spent = Rs 10 + Rs 15 = Rs 25
- Money left = Rs 50 - Rs 25 = Rs 25

### Finding Money Spent

What if you know how much you had and how much is left?

```
Money we had  -  Money left  =  Money spent
```

Rupal's friend had Rs 50 and now has Rs 8 left.

Money spent = Rs 50 - Rs 8 = Rs 42

She can count it step by step:
- Rs 8 + Rs 20 = Rs 28
- Rs 28 + Rs 20 = Rs 48
- Rs 48 + Rs 2 = Rs 50
- Total spent: Rs 20 + Rs 20 + Rs 2 = **Rs 42**

> **Tip:** When you go to a shop, sometimes the shopkeeper gives you change. If something costs Rs 15 and you give Rs 20, the shopkeeper gives you back Rs 5 as change.

## Buying Things at the Fair

At the fair, different things have different prices.

| Item | Price |
|------|-------|
| Toy car | Rs 20 |
| Ice cream | Rs 15 |
| Balloon | Rs 5 |
| Cotton candy | Rs 10 |
| Bangles | Rs 30 |

If you want to buy an ice cream and a balloon:
- Cost = Rs 15 + Rs 5 = **Rs 20**

If you have Rs 50:
- Money left = Rs 50 - Rs 20 = **Rs 30**

## Adding Money

When we buy many things, we **add** the prices to find the total cost.

**Example 1:** Jayant spent Rs 25 on pens and Rs 40 on notebooks.
Total money spent = Rs 25 + Rs 40 = **Rs 65**

**Example 2:** A packet of bread costs Rs 30 and a chocolate costs Rs 60.
Total cost = Rs 30 + Rs 60 = **Rs 90**

## Subtracting Money

When we find how much is left or how much more we need, we **subtract**.

**Example:** Ajay has Rs 58. He spent Rs 48 in the market.
Money left = Rs 58 - Rs 48 = **Rs 10**

**Example:** Meera has Rs 43. Kanika has Rs 14 more than Meera.
Kanika has = Rs 43 + Rs 14 = **Rs 57**

## Digital Money

Today, people sometimes pay using **mobile phones** instead of cash. This is called **digital payment**. The money is sent from one phone to another. But whether we use cash or a phone, we still need to know how to **count and manage** our money!

> **Good habit:** It is important to **save money** and not spend it all at once. You can use a piggy bank to save your coins!

## Key Words to Remember

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Rupee (Rs)** | The money used in India |
| **Paise** | Smaller parts of a rupee (100 paise = 1 rupee), no longer used as coins |
| **Coin** | A small, round metal piece of money |
| **Note** | A paper piece of money |
| **Cost / Price** | How much money something is worth |
| **Spent** | The money given away to buy something |
| **Change** | The money a shopkeeper gives back when you pay more than the cost |
| **Total** | The sum of all the money or prices added together |
| **Save** | Keeping money for later instead of spending it |
