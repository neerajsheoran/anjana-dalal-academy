## Lines in Yoga

When we do **yoga**, our body makes different kinds of lines!

- In **Tadasana** (Mountain Pose), you stand tall and straight. Your body makes a **standing line**.
- In **Trikonasana** (Triangle Pose), your arms and legs make **slanting lines**.
- In **Chakrasana** (Wheel Pose), your back makes a **curved line**.
- When you lie flat in **Shavasana**, your body makes a **sleeping line**.

> **Fun Fact:** Yoga is an ancient Indian practice described in the **Yoga Sutras of Patanjali**. It helps keep our body and mind healthy!

## Types of Lines

There are four main types of lines:

### 1. Standing Line (Vertical Line)

A standing line goes **straight up and down**, like a tree trunk or a pole.

```
  |
  |
  |
  |
```

**Where you see it:** Pillars, flag poles, standing pencils, door frames (the sides).

### 2. Sleeping Line (Horizontal Line)

A sleeping line goes **straight from left to right**, like the ground or a table top.

```
  ___________
```

**Where you see it:** The horizon, a ruler lying flat, the top edge of a blackboard, railway tracks.

### 3. Slanting Line (Diagonal Line)

A slanting line goes at an **angle** -- it is not standing and not sleeping. It leans to one side.

```
    /        \
   /          \
  /            \
```

**Where you see it:** A ladder against a wall, the sides of a tent, the letter V, a kite string in the wind.

### 4. Curved Line

A curved line **bends smoothly**. It is not straight at all.

```
   ~~~~~
```

**Where you see it:** A rainbow, the edge of a bangle, a snake's body, a river from above.

## Straight or Not?

Hold a piece of thread tightly between your two hands. Pull it tight -- it makes a **straight line**.

Now bring your hands closer. The thread hangs down. It makes a **curved line**. It is no longer straight!

> **Remember:** Standing lines, sleeping lines, and slanting lines are all **straight lines**. Only curved lines are not straight.

| Line Type | Straight? | Direction |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Standing (Vertical) | Yes | Up and down |
| Sleeping (Horizontal) | Yes | Left and right |
| Slanting (Diagonal) | Yes | At an angle |
| Curved | No | Bends smoothly |

## Lines in the World Around Us

Look at any picture or object around you. You can find all types of lines!

**In a house:**
- The walls have **standing lines**.
- The floor has **sleeping lines**.
- The roof may have **slanting lines**.
- A doorknob or arch may have **curved lines**.

**In nature:**
- A tall tree trunk is a **standing line**.
- The ground is a **sleeping line**.
- A hill slope is a **slanting line**.
- A rainbow is a beautiful **curved line**.

**In letters:**
- The letter **L** has a standing line and a sleeping line.
- The letter **V** has two slanting lines.
- The letter **O** is made of curved lines.
- The letter **T** has a standing line and a sleeping line.

## Playing with Dots

You can connect dots to make shapes using different lines.

- Connect dots with **straight lines** (standing, sleeping, or slanting) to make shapes like triangles, squares, and rectangles.
- Connect dots with **curved lines** to draw clouds, waves, rainbows, and flowers.

## Lines in Indian Art

Many traditional Indian art forms use beautiful lines:

- **Madhubani painting** from Bihar uses bold straight and curved lines to draw fish, birds, and flowers.
- **Warli painting** from Maharashtra uses simple triangles and circles made of straight and curved lines.
- **Kalamkari art** from Andhra Pradesh uses fine curved lines to make detailed patterns.

## Fun with Paper Folding

When you fold a piece of paper, the fold creates a line called a **crease**.

- Fold a paper once -- you get **1 crease** (a straight line).
- Fold it again -- you get more creases.
- Different folds can give you **standing lines**, **sleeping lines**, or **slanting lines**.

> **Try this:** Take a sheet of paper and fold it many times. Open it up and trace the creases. Can you find standing, sleeping, and slanting lines?

## Key Words to Remember

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Standing line (Vertical)** | A straight line that goes up and down |
| **Sleeping line (Horizontal)** | A straight line that goes left and right |
| **Slanting line (Diagonal)** | A straight line that goes at an angle |
| **Curved line** | A line that bends smoothly, not straight |
| **Straight line** | A line that does not bend or curve |
| **Crease** | The line made when you fold a piece of paper |
