## Five Seasons of India

India has **five beautiful seasons** in a year. Each season brings different weather, food, and festivals.

### 1. Spring (Vasant)

Spring begins with a cheer! The weather is warm and pleasant. **Flowers bloom** and **birds sing**. Trees get new green leaves.

**Festivals in spring:** Holi, Baisakhi, Gudi Padwa, Shivratri, Ram Navami, Mahavir Jayanti

### 2. Summer (Grishma)

Summer comes with the **shining sun**. The days are **long and hot**. Children enjoy summer vacations! We eat mangoes, watermelons, and drink cold nimbu pani.

**Festivals in summer:** Guru Purnima, Buddha Purnima

### 3. Monsoon / Rainy Season (Varsha)

The rainy season brings **water drops** from the sky. The **peacock dances** and the **frog hops** with joy. Everything turns green. We carry umbrellas and raincoats.

**Festivals in monsoon:** Janmashtami, Raksha Bandhan, Independence Day (15th August)

### 4. Autumn (Sharad)

**Leaves fall** from the trees. The wind is **pleasant and cool**. The sky is clear and beautiful.

**Festivals in autumn:** Dussehra, Sharad Purnima

### 5. Winter (Shishir)

Winter is **cold**. We wear warm clothes like sweaters, jackets, and shawls. In some places, it even snows! We enjoy eating gajar ka halwa, gajak, and drinking hot chai.

**Festivals in winter:** Lohri, Christmas, Republic Day (26th January), Gurunanak Jayanti, Bihu

> **Fun Fact:** Some festivals like **Eid** are not linked to a particular season. They come at different times each year based on the lunar calendar.

## The Calendar

A **calendar** shows us all the days, weeks, and months of a year. It helps us know what day it is and plan ahead.

### Months of the Year

A year has **12 months**. Let us learn them in order:

| Month | Number of Days |
|-------|---------------|
| 1. January | 31 |
| 2. February | 28 (or 29 in a leap year) |
| 3. March | 31 |
| 4. April | 30 |
| 5. May | 31 |
| 6. June | 30 |
| 7. July | 31 |
| 8. August | 31 |
| 9. September | 30 |
| 10. October | 31 |
| 11. November | 30 |
| 12. December | 31 |

> **Easy way to remember:** Months with **31 days**: January, March, May, July, August, October, December. Months with **30 days**: April, June, September, November. February is special -- it has **28 or 29 days**.

### Days of the Week

A week has **7 days**:

1. **Sunday**
2. **Monday**
3. **Tuesday**
4. **Wednesday**
5. **Thursday**
6. **Friday**
7. **Saturday**

After Saturday, Sunday comes again! The days repeat in a **cycle**.

### Reading a Calendar

Look at a calendar. Each row shows one week. The columns show the days of the week. The numbers are the **dates**.

For example, in the month of August:
- **Independence Day** is on **15th August**.
- You can find the **day of the week** by looking at which column the 15th falls in.

## How Long Does It Take?

Different activities take different amounts of time.

| Activity | Time It Takes |
|----------|--------------|
| Doing yoga | A few **minutes** |
| Playing a game | About **1 hour** |
| Cooking a meal | A few **hours** |
| A school day | About **6 hours** |
| Summer vacation | About **2 months** |
| A seed growing into a plant | Several **weeks** |
| Knitting a sweater | Many **days** |
| Change of a season | A few **months** |

> **Remember:** Seconds are very short, minutes are a bit longer, hours are even longer, and days and months are the longest!

## Reading a Clock

A clock has **two hands**:

- The **short hand** is the **hour hand**. It moves slowly and tells us the **hour**.
- The **long hand** is the **minute hand**. It moves faster and tells us the **minutes**.

When the **long hand** points to **12** and the short hand points to a number, we say that time in **o'clock**.

```
Short hand at 6, Long hand at 12  -->  6 o'clock
Short hand at 9, Long hand at 12  -->  9 o'clock
Short hand at 3, Long hand at 12  -->  3 o'clock
```

## Directions

### Left and Right

Your body has a **left side** and a **right side**.

- Raise your **left hand** -- that is the left side.
- Raise your **right hand** -- that is the right side.

When someone gives you directions like "turn left" or "go right", they are using these words.

### North, South, East, and West

There are **four main directions**:

| Direction | How to Find It |
|-----------|---------------|
| **East** | Face the rising sun in the morning -- you are looking East |
| **West** | Your back faces West when you look East |
| **North** | Your left hand points North (when facing East) |
| **South** | Your right hand points South (when facing East) |

> **Easy way to remember:** Face the **sunrise** (East). Behind you is **West**. Left hand is **North**. Right hand is **South**.

![A compass rose showing North, South, East, and West directions with a child facing East](compass-directions-nsew.png)

### Using a Compass

A **compass** is a small tool that always points to the **North**. With a compass, you can find all four directions no matter where you are.

## Key Words to Remember

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Season** | A time of year with its own kind of weather (spring, summer, monsoon, autumn, winter) |
| **Calendar** | A chart that shows all the days, weeks, and months of a year |
| **Month** | One of the 12 parts of a year (January, February, March, etc.) |
| **Week** | A group of 7 days |
| **Hour hand** | The short hand on a clock that shows the hour |
| **Minute hand** | The long hand on a clock that shows the minutes |
| **O'clock** | The time when the minute hand points to 12 |
| **Direction** | The way something faces or moves (North, South, East, West) |
| **Compass** | A tool that shows directions, always pointing North |
