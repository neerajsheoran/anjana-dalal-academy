## Gravitation

Every object in the universe attracts every other object. When Newton saw an apple falling from a tree, he realised that the same force that pulls the apple towards the earth also keeps the moon in orbit around the earth, and the planets in orbit around the sun. This force is called the **gravitational force**.

---

## Universal Law of Gravitation

**Newton's Universal Law of Gravitation** states:

Every object in the universe attracts every other object with a force that is:
- **Directly proportional** to the product of their masses
- **Inversely proportional** to the square of the distance between them

![Two masses M and m separated by distance d with gravitational force arrows pointing towards each other](universal-law-of-gravitation-diagram.png)

**F = G x M x m / d squared**

Where:
- F = gravitational force between two objects
- G = universal gravitational constant = **6.673 x 10 to the power -11 N m squared / kg squared**
- M and m = masses of the two objects
- d = distance between the centres of the two objects

> **Key idea:** The gravitational force is always attractive and acts along the line joining the centres of the two objects. It acts on both objects -- the earth attracts the apple AND the apple attracts the earth (Newton's third law).

### Why Don't We See the Earth Move Towards the Apple?

Both the apple and the earth attract each other with equal force (Newton's third law). But the earth's mass is enormously large compared to the apple. Since acceleration = Force / mass, the earth's acceleration towards the apple is negligibly small, while the apple's acceleration towards the earth is significant (about 9.8 m/s squared).

### Importance of Gravitational Force

- Keeps planets in orbit around the sun
- Keeps the moon in orbit around the earth
- Causes tides in the oceans (due to gravitational pull of the moon and sun)
- Holds the atmosphere around the earth
- Causes rain to fall to the ground

## Free Fall and Acceleration Due to Gravity

### Free Fall

When an object falls towards the earth under the **gravitational force alone** (with no other forces like air resistance), it is said to be in **free fall**.

During free fall, the object **accelerates** -- its velocity increases as it falls. This acceleration is called the **acceleration due to gravity**, denoted by **g**.

### Value of g

**g = GM / R squared**

Where:
- G = gravitational constant
- M = mass of the earth = 6 x 10 to the power 24 kg
- R = radius of the earth = 6.4 x 10 to the power 6 m

**g = 9.8 m/s squared** (approximately 10 m/s squared for calculations)

> **Key idea:** The value of g is the same for all objects, regardless of their mass. A heavy object and a light object, when dropped from the same height in a vacuum, reach the ground at the same time.

### Equations of Motion for Free Fall

For an object falling vertically (taking downward as positive):

| General Equation | Free Fall (a = g) |
|---|---|
| v = u + at | v = u + gt |
| s = ut + (1/2)at squared | h = ut + (1/2)gt squared |
| v squared = u squared + 2as | v squared = u squared + 2gh |

**For an object dropped from rest:** u = 0

**For an object thrown upward:** At the highest point, v = 0, and g is negative (deceleration)

## Mass and Weight

### Mass

**Mass** is the amount of matter in an object. It is a **scalar quantity**.

- **SI unit:** kilogram (kg)
- Mass is **constant** everywhere (does not change with location)
- Mass is a measure of **inertia**

### Weight

**Weight** is the force with which the earth attracts an object. It is a **vector quantity**.

**Weight (W) = mass x g = mg**

- **SI unit:** Newton (N)
- Weight **changes** with location (because g changes)
- Weight is a force directed towards the centre of the earth

### Weight on the Moon

The moon has less mass and smaller radius than the earth. The acceleration due to gravity on the moon's surface is about **1/6th** of that on earth.

**g(moon) = g(earth) / 6**

So, weight on the moon = (1/6) x weight on earth

**Example:** If an object weighs 98 N on earth (mass = 10 kg):
- Weight on moon = 98/6 = **16.3 N**
- Mass remains 10 kg on both earth and moon

> **Key idea:** Mass is constant everywhere. Weight changes because it depends on g, which varies from place to place.

| Property | Mass | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| **Definition** | Amount of matter | Force of gravity on object |
| **SI Unit** | kg | N (Newton) |
| **Type** | Scalar | Vector |
| **Changes with location?** | No | Yes |
| **Measured by** | Beam balance | Spring balance |

## Thrust and Pressure

### Thrust

**Thrust** is the force acting **perpendicular** (at right angles) to a surface.

### Pressure

**Pressure** is the force per unit area.

**Pressure = Force / Area = Thrust / Area**

- **SI unit:** Pascal (Pa), where 1 Pa = 1 N/m squared

> **Key idea:** For the same force, pressure increases when the area decreases, and decreases when the area increases.

**Examples:**
- A sharp knife cuts better than a blunt one (same force, smaller area = more pressure)
- Wide tyres on tractors distribute weight over a larger area (less pressure on soft ground)
- Nails have pointed tips to concentrate force on a small area
- School bags have wide straps to reduce pressure on shoulders

### Pressure in Fluids

Fluids (liquids and gases) exert pressure in all directions. A fluid exerts pressure on the walls of its container, on the bottom, and on any object placed in it.

## Buoyancy and Archimedes' Principle

### Buoyant Force

When an object is placed in a fluid, the fluid exerts an **upward force** on the object. This upward force is called the **buoyant force** or **upthrust**.

![Object submerged in water with weight arrow pointing down and buoyant force arrow pointing up](buoyant-force-on-submerged-object.png)

The buoyant force depends on:
- The **density** of the fluid
- The **volume** of the object submerged in the fluid

### Why Do Objects Float or Sink?

- If the **density of the object is less** than the density of the fluid, the object **floats**
- If the **density of the object is greater** than the density of the fluid, the object **sinks**

**Example:** Wood floats on water (density of wood &lt; density of water), but iron sinks (density of iron > density of water).

### Archimedes' Principle

**Archimedes' Principle** states: When a body is immersed (fully or partially) in a fluid, it experiences a buoyant force equal to the **weight of the fluid displaced** by it.

**Buoyant force = Weight of fluid displaced = Volume displaced x density of fluid x g**

### Applications of Archimedes' Principle

- Designing **ships and submarines** -- ships are designed to displace enough water to support their weight
- **Lactometers** -- used to determine the purity of milk by measuring density
- **Hydrometers** -- used to measure the density of liquids

---

## Relative Density

**Relative density** of a substance is the ratio of its density to the density of water.

**Relative density = Density of substance / Density of water**

Relative density has **no unit** (it is a ratio).

- If relative density > 1, the substance is denser than water and will **sink**
- If relative density &lt; 1, the substance is less dense than water and will **float**

**Density of water = 1000 kg/m cubed = 1 g/cm cubed**

---

## Key Formulae

- **Gravitational force:** F = GMm / d squared
- **Acceleration due to gravity:** g = GM / R squared = 9.8 m/s squared
- **Weight:** W = mg
- **Pressure:** P = F / A
- **Buoyant force** = weight of fluid displaced
- **Relative density** = density of substance / density of water

## Key Terms to Remember

- **Gravitation** -- Attractive force between any two objects in the universe
- **Gravity** -- Gravitational force of the earth on an object
- **Free fall** -- Motion of an object under gravity alone
- **g (acceleration due to gravity)** -- 9.8 m/s squared on earth's surface
- **Mass** -- Amount of matter (constant everywhere)
- **Weight** -- Force of gravity (changes with g)
- **Thrust** -- Force perpendicular to a surface
- **Pressure** -- Force per unit area
- **Buoyant force** -- Upward force exerted by fluid on an immersed object
- **Archimedes' Principle** -- Buoyant force equals weight of fluid displaced
- **Relative density** -- Ratio of density of substance to density of water
