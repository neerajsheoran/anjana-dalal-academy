## Introduction

India has a population of over one billion people, and it is still growing. We need more than a quarter of a billion tonnes of grain every year to feed everyone. Since we cannot increase the land under cultivation significantly, we must **increase production efficiency** for both crops and livestock.

**Key revolutions in India:**
- **Green Revolution** -- increased food grain production
- **White Revolution** -- improved milk production and availability

> **Key idea:** We must increase food production without degrading the environment. Sustainable practices in agriculture and animal husbandry are essential.

**Food security** depends on both the availability of food and people's access to it. Scientific management and mixed farming practices (combining agriculture with livestock, poultry, fisheries, bee-keeping) help improve both food production and farmers' income.

## Improvement in Crop Yields

### Types of Crops and Their Contributions

| Type | Examples | Provides |
|---|---|---|
| **Cereals** | Wheat, rice, maize, millets, sorghum | Carbohydrates (energy) |
| **Pulses** | Gram, pea, black gram, green gram, lentil | Proteins |
| **Oilseeds** | Soyabean, groundnut, sesame, mustard, sunflower | Fats |
| **Vegetables and fruits** | Various | Vitamins, minerals, fibre |
| **Fodder crops** | Berseem, oats, sudan grass | Food for livestock |

### Crop Seasons in India

- **Kharif season** (June to October, rainy season): Paddy, soyabean, maize, cotton, green gram, black gram
- **Rabi season** (November to April, winter): Wheat, gram, peas, mustard, linseed

### Three Major Areas for Improvement

1. **Crop variety improvement** -- choosing the right seeds
2. **Crop production improvement** -- nurturing the crop
3. **Crop protection management** -- protecting from pests, diseases, and weeds

---

## Crop Variety Improvement

New crop varieties are developed through **hybridisation** (crossing genetically different plants) and **genetic modification** (introducing desired genes).

**Types of hybridisation:**
- **Intervarietal** -- crossing different varieties of the same species
- **Interspecific** -- crossing different species of the same genus
- **Intergeneric** -- crossing different genera

### Factors for Which Varieties Are Improved

- **Higher yield** -- more production per acre
- **Improved quality** -- better baking quality in wheat, protein quality in pulses, oil quality in oilseeds
- **Biotic resistance** -- resistance to diseases, insects, nematodes
- **Abiotic resistance** -- tolerance to drought, salinity, waterlogging, heat, cold, frost
- **Shorter maturity duration** -- allows growing multiple crops per year
- **Wider adaptability** -- grows well in different climatic conditions
- **Desirable agronomic characteristics** -- dwarfness in cereals (less nutrient consumption), tallness in fodder crops

> **Key idea:** Crop improvement aims to develop varieties that are high-yielding, disease-resistant, and adaptable to different conditions.

## Crop Production Improvement

### Nutrient Management

Plants need **16 essential nutrients** from air, water, and soil.

| Source | Nutrients |
|---|---|
| Air | Carbon, oxygen |
| Water | Hydrogen, oxygen |
| Soil -- Macronutrients | Nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium, calcium, magnesium, sulphur |
| Soil -- Micronutrients | Iron, manganese, boron, zinc, copper, molybdenum, chlorine |

**Macronutrients** are needed in large quantities; **micronutrients** are needed in small quantities.

#### Manure

**Manure** is an organic substance obtained by decomposition of plant and animal waste. It provides nutrients and improves **soil structure**.

**Types:**
- **Compost and vermi-compost** -- decomposed farm waste; vermi-compost uses earthworms
- **Green manure** -- plants like sun hemp or guar are grown and ploughed into the soil before sowing

#### Fertilisers

**Fertilisers** are commercially produced chemicals that supply specific nutrients (N, P, K) to crops.

**Examples:** Urea (nitrogen), superphosphate (phosphorus), potash (potassium)

### Manure vs Fertilisers

| Property | Manure | Fertiliser |
|---|---|---|
| Source | Natural (organic waste) | Manufactured (chemical) |
| Nutrients | All nutrients in small quantities | Specific nutrients in large quantities |
| Humus | Provides humus | Does not provide humus |
| Long-term effect | Improves soil structure | Can damage soil over time |
| Cost | Low | High |

> **Key idea:** Organic farming uses manure, bio-fertilisers, and natural pest control instead of chemical fertilisers and pesticides. It helps maintain soil health and environmental sustainability.

### Irrigation

Water is essential for plant growth, germination, and nutrient transport.

**Sources of irrigation:** Wells, canals, rivers, tanks, ponds

**Modern irrigation methods (reduce water wastage):**
- **Drip irrigation** -- water is delivered drop by drop at the roots; reduces water use significantly
- **Sprinkler system** -- water is sprayed like rain over the fields

### Cropping Patterns

- **Mixed cropping** -- growing two or more crops simultaneously on the same field (e.g., wheat + gram). Reduces risk of total crop failure.
- **Inter-cropping** -- growing two or more crops simultaneously in definite row patterns (e.g., soyabean + maize). Different crops have different nutrient needs.
- **Crop rotation** -- growing different crops on the same field in successive seasons (e.g., wheat in rabi, then groundnut in kharif). Replenishes soil nutrients.

## Crop Protection Management

Crops need to be protected from **weeds**, **insect pests**, and **diseases**.

### Weeds

**Weeds** are unwanted plants that grow alongside crops and compete for nutrients, water, light, and space.

**Control methods:** Manual removal, use of weedicides (chemicals), proper seed preparation, timely sowing, inter-cropping, crop rotation

### Insect Pests

Pests attack plants in three ways:
- **Cut** the root, stem, or leaf
- **Suck** cell sap from various plant parts
- **Bore** into stems and fruits

**Control methods:** Pesticides (chemicals), biological control (using natural predators), crop rotation, use of resistant varieties

### Diseases

Plant diseases are caused by **pathogens** -- bacteria, fungi, and viruses. They are spread through air, water, soil, and insects.

**Control methods:** Use of resistant varieties, proper treatment of seeds, crop rotation, fungicides/bactericides

### Storage of Grains

After harvesting, grains must be properly stored to prevent losses from:
- **Biotic factors** -- insects, rodents, fungi, mites, bacteria
- **Abiotic factors** -- moisture, temperature

**Preventive measures:** Proper drying (moisture below 14%), cleaning, fumigation, use of neem leaves, proper storage facilities (godowns, silos)

## Animal Husbandry

### Cattle Farming

Cattle are raised for **milk** (dairy cattle) and **draught work** (pulling carts, ploughing).

**Two main breeds:**
- **Indigenous breeds** (e.g., Red Sindhi, Sahiwal) -- disease-resistant
- **Exotic breeds** (e.g., Jersey, Holstein-Friesian) -- produce more milk

**Cross-breeding** is done to combine desirable traits of both breeds.

**Cattle feed:**
- **Roughage** -- fibre-rich food like hay, silage, green fodder
- **Concentrates** -- nutrient-rich food like grains, oilcakes, minerals

**Cattle diseases** can be caused by viruses, bacteria, or parasites (external like ticks, and internal like worms). Regular vaccination and veterinary care are essential.

### Poultry Farming

**Poultry** includes chickens raised for **eggs** (layers) and **meat** (broilers).

**Important practices:** Proper housing, temperature maintenance, hygiene, balanced feed, disease prevention through vaccination

**Indigenous breeds:** Aseel, Bushra
**Exotic breeds:** White Leghorn, Plymouth Rock, Rhode Island Red

Cross-breeding produces varieties with desirable traits like more eggs, better meat quality, and disease resistance.

### Fish Production

**Two main ways:**
- **Capture fishing** -- catching fish from natural water bodies
- **Culture fishing (aquaculture)** -- growing fish in controlled environments

**Mariculture** -- culture of marine fish (in sea water)
**Composite fish culture** -- growing 5-6 different species of fish together in the same pond, each feeding at different levels (surface, middle, bottom)

### Bee-Keeping (Apiculture)

**Bee-keeping** is done for obtaining **honey** and **beeswax**.

**Common bee varieties:**
- **Apis cerana indica** (Indian bee)
- **Apis dorsata** (rock bee)
- **Apis florea** (little bee)
- **Apis mellifera** (Italian bee -- preferred commercially)

**Factors for good honey production:**
- Location of bee hives near flowering plants
- Type of flowers available (determines the taste and quality of honey)
- Proper management of bee colonies

> **Key idea:** Improvement in food resources requires a combination of better crop varieties, efficient farming practices, sustainable resource management, and improved animal husbandry.

## Key Terms to Remember

- **Green Revolution** -- Increase in food grain production through modern techniques
- **White Revolution** -- Increase in milk production
- **Hybridisation** -- Crossing genetically dissimilar plants
- **Kharif crops** -- Summer/rainy season crops (June-October)
- **Rabi crops** -- Winter season crops (November-April)
- **Macronutrients** -- Nutrients needed in large quantities
- **Micronutrients** -- Nutrients needed in small quantities
- **Manure** -- Organic fertiliser from decomposed waste
- **Fertiliser** -- Manufactured chemical nutrients
- **Mixed cropping** -- Growing two or more crops together
- **Inter-cropping** -- Growing crops in definite row patterns
- **Crop rotation** -- Growing different crops in succession
- **Composite fish culture** -- Growing multiple fish species together
- **Apiculture** -- Bee-keeping for honey and beeswax
