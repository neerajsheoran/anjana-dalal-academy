## Introduction

A **quadrilateral** is a closed figure with four sides, four angles, and four vertices. In this chapter, you will study the properties of a special type of quadrilateral called a **parallelogram**, along with the conditions under which a quadrilateral becomes a parallelogram. You will also study the **mid-point theorem**.

---

## Properties of a Parallelogram

A **parallelogram** is a quadrilateral in which both pairs of opposite sides are parallel.

### Theorem 8.1: Diagonal divides a parallelogram into two congruent triangles

A diagonal of a parallelogram divides it into two congruent triangles.

**Proof:** In parallelogram ABCD with diagonal AC, using alternate interior angles (BC || AD and AB || DC with AC as transversal) and the common side AC, we get triangle ABC congruent to triangle CDA by ASA rule.

### Theorem 8.2: Opposite sides are equal

> **Key idea:** In a parallelogram, **opposite sides are equal**. (AB = CD and AD = BC)

### Theorem 8.3 (Converse): If opposite sides are equal, it is a parallelogram

If each pair of opposite sides of a quadrilateral is equal, then it is a parallelogram.

### Theorem 8.4: Opposite angles are equal

In a parallelogram, **opposite angles are equal**. (angle A = angle C and angle B = angle D)

### Theorem 8.5 (Converse): If opposite angles are equal, it is a parallelogram

### Theorem 8.6: Diagonals bisect each other

The diagonals of a parallelogram **bisect each other**. If diagonals AC and BD intersect at O, then OA = OC and OB = OD.

### Theorem 8.7 (Converse): If diagonals bisect each other, it is a parallelogram

> **Key idea:** A quadrilateral is a parallelogram if ANY ONE of the following conditions holds: (1) both pairs of opposite sides are parallel, (2) both pairs of opposite sides are equal, (3) both pairs of opposite angles are equal, (4) diagonals bisect each other, (5) one pair of opposite sides is both equal and parallel.

## Special Parallelograms

### Rectangle

A **rectangle** is a parallelogram in which one angle is a right angle (90 degrees). Since opposite angles are equal and adjacent angles are supplementary, **all angles of a rectangle are 90 degrees**.

The diagonals of a rectangle are **equal** in length.

### Rhombus

A **rhombus** is a parallelogram in which all four sides are equal. The diagonals of a rhombus are **perpendicular** to each other.

### Square

A **square** is a parallelogram that is both a rectangle and a rhombus. All sides are equal and all angles are 90 degrees. The diagonals are equal, bisect each other, and are perpendicular.

## The Mid-Point Theorem

> **Theorem 8.9 (Mid-Point Theorem):** The line segment joining the mid-points of two sides of a triangle is **parallel** to the third side and is **half** of it.

If D and E are mid-points of sides AB and AC of triangle ABC respectively, then:
- DE is parallel to BC
- DE = (1/2) BC

> **Theorem 8.10 (Converse):** The line drawn through the mid-point of one side of a triangle, parallel to another side, bisects the third side.

If D is the mid-point of AB and a line through D is parallel to BC, then it bisects AC.

**Example:** In triangle ABC, D and E are mid-points of AB and AC. If BC = 8 cm, then DE = 4 cm and DE is parallel to BC.

---

## Summary

1. A **parallelogram** has opposite sides parallel and equal, opposite angles equal, and diagonals that bisect each other.
2. A diagonal divides a parallelogram into two congruent triangles.
3. A quadrilateral is a parallelogram if: opposite sides are equal, or opposite angles are equal, or diagonals bisect each other, or one pair of opposite sides is equal and parallel.
4. **Rectangle:** All angles 90 degrees; diagonals are equal.
5. **Rhombus:** All sides equal; diagonals are perpendicular.
6. **Square:** All sides equal and all angles 90 degrees.
7. **Mid-Point Theorem:** The segment joining mid-points of two sides of a triangle is parallel to and half of the third side.
