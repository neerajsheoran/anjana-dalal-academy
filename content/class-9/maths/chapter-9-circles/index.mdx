## Introduction

A **circle** is the collection of all points in a plane that are at a fixed distance (called the **radius**) from a fixed point (called the **centre**). You have studied circles in earlier classes. In this chapter, you will study the relationship between chords, arcs, angles subtended by chords, and properties of cyclic quadrilaterals.

---

## Basic Terms

- **Centre:** The fixed point from which all points on the circle are equidistant.
- **Radius:** The fixed distance from the centre to any point on the circle.
- **Chord:** A line segment joining any two points on the circle.
- **Diameter:** A chord that passes through the centre. It is the **longest chord** and equals twice the radius.
- **Arc:** A continuous piece of a circle. A chord divides the circle into a **major arc** (larger) and a **minor arc** (smaller).
- **Semicircle:** Each of the two arcs formed when a diameter divides the circle.
- **Segment:** The region between a chord and the arc. A chord creates a **major segment** and a **minor segment**.
- **Sector:** The region between two radii and an arc.

---

## Angle Subtended by a Chord at a Point

The **angle subtended by a chord PQ** at a point R is the angle PRQ formed by joining P and Q to R.

- The angle subtended at the **centre** is called the **central angle**.
- The longer the chord, the bigger the angle subtended at the centre.

> **Theorem 9.1:** Equal chords of a circle subtend **equal angles** at the centre.

**Proof:** In triangles AOB and COD (where O is the centre, AB = CD), OA = OC and OB = OD (radii), and AB = CD (given). By SSS, the triangles are congruent, so angle AOB = angle COD.

> **Theorem 9.2 (Converse):** If the angles subtended by two chords at the centre are equal, then the chords are **equal**.

---

## Perpendicular from the Centre to a Chord

> **Theorem 9.3:** The perpendicular from the centre of a circle to a chord **bisects** the chord.

If OM is perpendicular to chord AB, then AM = MB.

> **Theorem 9.4 (Converse):** The line drawn through the centre of a circle to **bisect** a chord is **perpendicular** to the chord.

---

## Equal Chords and Distance from the Centre

The **distance** of a chord from the centre is the length of the perpendicular from the centre to the chord.

> **Theorem 9.5:** Equal chords of a circle are **equidistant** from the centre.

> **Theorem 9.6 (Converse):** Chords equidistant from the centre are **equal** in length.

> **Key idea:** The diameter, being the longest chord, has zero distance from the centre (since the centre lies on it).

---

## Angle Subtended by an Arc

> **Theorem 9.7:** The angle subtended by an arc at the centre is **double** the angle subtended by it at any point on the remaining part of the circle.

If arc PQ subtends angle POQ at centre O and angle PRQ at point R on the circle, then angle POQ = 2 times angle PRQ.

> **Theorem 9.8:** Angles in the **same segment** of a circle are **equal**.

All angles subtended by the same arc (or chord) at points on the same side of the chord are equal.

> **Theorem 9.9 (Converse):** If a line segment joining two points subtends equal angles at two other points lying on the **same side**, then the four points are **concyclic** (lie on a circle).

> **Key idea:** The angle in a **semicircle** is a **right angle** (90 degrees). This is because the diameter subtends 180 degrees at the centre, so the angle at the circumference is 180/2 = 90 degrees.

Conversely, if an angle in a segment is 90 degrees, the segment is a semicircle (and the chord is a diameter).

---

## Cyclic Quadrilaterals

A **cyclic quadrilateral** is a quadrilateral whose all four vertices lie on a circle.

> **Theorem 9.10:** The sum of either pair of **opposite angles** of a cyclic quadrilateral is **180 degrees** (supplementary).

In cyclic quadrilateral ABCD: angle A + angle C = 180 degrees, and angle B + angle D = 180 degrees.

> **Theorem 9.11 (Converse):** If the sum of a pair of opposite angles of a quadrilateral is 180 degrees, then the quadrilateral is **cyclic**.

---

## Summary

1. Equal chords subtend equal angles at the centre (and conversely).
2. The perpendicular from the centre to a chord bisects the chord (and conversely).
3. Equal chords are equidistant from the centre (and conversely).
4. The angle subtended by an arc at the centre is **double** the angle at the circumference.
5. Angles in the same segment are equal.
6. The angle in a semicircle is **90 degrees**.
7. Opposite angles of a cyclic quadrilateral sum to **180 degrees**.
