## Introduction

In earlier classes, you studied the surface areas of cubes, cuboids, and cylinders. In this chapter, you will extend your study to include the surface areas and volumes of **cones** and **spheres**, in addition to revising cylinders. These formulas are essential for solving real-world problems involving three-dimensional objects.

---

## Surface Area of a Right Circular Cone

A **right circular cone** has a circular base and a vertex. The key measurements are:

* **r** = radius of the base
* **h** = height (perpendicular distance from base to vertex)
* **l** = slant height (distance along the surface from base edge to vertex)

The relationship between these: **l^2 = r^2 + h^2** (by Pythagoras theorem), so **l = sqrt(r^2 + h^2)**.

**Curved (Lateral) Surface Area of a Cone = pi x r x l**

**Total Surface Area of a Cone = pi x r x l + pi x r^2 = pi x r(l + r)**

> **Key idea:** The curved surface area formula comes from "unrolling" the cone into a sector of a circle with radius l and arc length 2 x pi x r.

**Example:** Find the total surface area of a cone with height 16 cm and base radius 12 cm.

l = sqrt(16^2 + 12^2) = sqrt(256 + 144) = sqrt(400) = 20 cm

CSA = pi x 12 x 20 = 240pi = 753.6 cm^2

TSA = pi x 12 x 20 + pi x 12^2 = 240pi + 144pi = 384pi = 1205.76 cm^2

## Surface Area of a Sphere

A **sphere** is the set of all points in space at a fixed distance (radius) from a fixed point (centre).

**Surface Area of a Sphere = 4 x pi x r^2**

**Curved Surface Area of a Hemisphere = 2 x pi x r^2**

**Total Surface Area of a Hemisphere = 3 x pi x r^2** (curved surface + circular base)

> **Key idea:** The surface area of a sphere is exactly **4 times** the area of a circle with the same radius. Also, it equals the curved surface area of a cylinder that just encloses the sphere.

**Example:** Find the surface area of a sphere of radius 7 cm.

Surface area = 4 x (22/7) x 7^2 = 4 x 22 x 7 = 616 cm^2

---

## Volume of a Right Circular Cone

**Volume of a Cone = (1/3) x pi x r^2 x h**

The volume of a cone is exactly **one-third** the volume of a cylinder with the same base radius and height.

**Example:** Find the volume of a cone with radius 6 cm and height 7 cm.

Volume = (1/3) x (22/7) x 36 x 7 = (1/3) x 22 x 36 = 264 cm^3

---

## Volume of a Sphere

**Volume of a Sphere = (4/3) x pi x r^3**

**Volume of a Hemisphere = (2/3) x pi x r^3**

**Example:** Find the volume of a sphere of radius 21 cm.

Volume = (4/3) x (22/7) x 21^3 = (4/3) x (22/7) x 9261 = 38808 cm^3

---

## Volume of a Right Circular Cylinder (Revision)

**Curved Surface Area of a Cylinder = 2 x pi x r x h**

**Total Surface Area of a Cylinder = 2 x pi x r x h + 2 x pi x r^2 = 2 x pi x r(h + r)**

**Volume of a Cylinder = pi x r^2 x h**

---

## Summary of Formulas

| Solid          | Curved/Lateral Surface Area | Total Surface Area | Volume         |
| -------------- | --------------------------- | ------------------ | -------------- |
| **Cylinder**   | 2 pi r h                    | 2 pi r(h + r)      | pi r^2 h       |
| **Cone**       | pi r l                      | pi r(l + r)        | (1/3) pi r^2 h |
| **Sphere**     | 4 pi r^2                    | 4 pi r^2           | (4/3) pi r^3   |
| **Hemisphere** | 2 pi r^2                    | 3 pi r^2           | (2/3) pi r^3   |

Where l = sqrt(r^2 + h^2) for the cone.

---

## Summary

1. **Cone:** CSA = pi r l, TSA = pi r(l + r), Volume = (1/3) pi r^2 h, where l = sqrt(r^2 + h^2).
2. **Sphere:** Surface Area = 4 pi r^2, Volume = (4/3) pi r^3.
3. **Hemisphere:** CSA = 2 pi r^2, TSA = 3 pi r^2, Volume = (2/3) pi r^3.
4. The volume of a cone is one-third the volume of a cylinder with the same base and height.
