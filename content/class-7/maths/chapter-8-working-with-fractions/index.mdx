We already know what fractions are and how to add and subtract them. In this chapter, we take the next big step -- learning how to **multiply** and **divide** fractions. These operations are essential for solving real-life problems involving parts of quantities.

## Multiplication of Fractions

### Multiplying a Whole Number by a Fraction

Aaron walks 3 km in 1 hour. How far can he walk in 5 hours? Simple: 5 x 3 = 15 km.

But his pet tortoise walks only **1/4 km** in 1 hour. How far does it walk in 3 hours?

3 x 1/4 = 1/4 + 1/4 + 1/4 = **3/4 km**

> **Rule:** To multiply a whole number by a fraction, multiply the whole number by the numerator, and keep the denominator the same.
> **n x a/b = (n x a) / b**

### Multiplying a Fraction by a Whole Number

How far can Aaron (walking 3 km/hour) walk in **1/5** of an hour?

Distance = 1/5 x 3

Since 1/5 of an hour means dividing 1 hour into 5 equal parts, we divide the 3 km into 5 equal parts:
1/5 x 3 = 3/5 km

How far in **2/5** of an hour?

2/5 x 3 = (2 x 3) / 5 = **6/5 km** = 1 1/5 km

> **Rule:** To multiply a fraction by a whole number:
> 1. Divide the whole number by the denominator of the fraction
> 2. Multiply the result by the numerator
> **a/b x n = (a x n) / b**

### Practical Examples

**Example:** A farmer distributes 2/3 acre of land to each of her 5 grandchildren.
Total land = 5 x 2/3 = 10/3 = **3 1/3 acres**

**Example:** 1 hour of internet costs Rs 8. How much does 1 1/4 hours cost?
1 1/4 = 5/4 hours. Cost = 5/4 x 8 = (5 x 8) / 4 = 40 / 4 = **Rs 10**

## Multiplying Two Fractions

The tortoise walks 1/4 km in 1 hour. How far does it walk in **half** an hour?

Distance = 1/2 x 1/4

To find 1/2 of 1/4, we divide 1/4 into 2 equal parts. Using a unit square:
- First shade 1/4 of the square (divide into 4 columns, shade 1)
- Then take 1/2 of that shaded part (divide into 2 rows, shade 1)
- The result is **1/8** of the whole square

So: **1/2 x 1/4 = 1/8**

### The Rule for Multiplying Fractions

> **To multiply two fractions, multiply the numerators together and multiply the denominators together:**
>
> **a/b x c/d = (a x c) / (b x d)**

**Examples:**

| Multiplication | Working | Result |
|:-:|:-:|:-:|
| 2/3 x 4/5 | (2 x 4) / (3 x 5) | **8/15** |
| 3/7 x 2/3 | (3 x 2) / (7 x 3) | **6/21 = 2/7** |
| 5/6 x 3/10 | (5 x 3) / (6 x 10) | **15/60 = 1/4** |

> **Tip:** Simplify before multiplying when possible. In 5/6 x 3/10, we can cancel 5 with 10 (giving 1/2) and 3 with 6 (giving 1/2), so the answer is 1/2 x 1/2 = **1/4**.

### Visualising Fraction Multiplication

Using a unit square to visualise 2/3 x 4/5:
1. Divide the square into 5 vertical strips and shade 4 of them (representing 4/5)
2. Divide the square into 3 horizontal strips and shade 2 of them (representing 2/3)
3. The overlap region represents the product: 8 out of 15 small rectangles = **8/15**

### Mixed Numbers

To multiply mixed numbers, first convert them to **improper fractions**.

**Example:** 2 1/3 x 1 1/2

= 7/3 x 3/2 = (7 x 3) / (3 x 2) = 21/6 = **7/2 = 3 1/2**

## Properties of Fraction Multiplication

### Effect of Multiplying by a Fraction

When you multiply a number by a **proper fraction** (less than 1), the result is **smaller** than the original number.

- 6 x 1/2 = 3 (smaller than 6)
- 10 x 3/5 = 6 (smaller than 10)

When you multiply by a fraction **greater than 1** (an improper fraction), the result is **larger**.

- 4 x 3/2 = 6 (larger than 4)

### Fraction of a Quantity

"Fraction of" means multiplication:

- **1/4 of 20** = 1/4 x 20 = 5
- **2/3 of 60** = 2/3 x 60 = 40
- **3/5 of Rs 100** = 3/5 x 100 = **Rs 60**

**Example:** A school has 720 students. If 5/8 of them are girls, how many girls are there?
Number of girls = 5/8 x 720 = (5 x 720) / 8 = 3600 / 8 = **450**

## Division of Fractions

### Dividing a Whole Number by a Fraction

How many pieces of 1/2 metre length can be cut from a 3-metre rope?

We need to find: 3 / (1/2)

Since each metre contains 2 pieces of half a metre, 3 metres contain 3 x 2 = **6 pieces**.

So: **3 / (1/2) = 3 x 2 = 6**

> **Key Insight:** Dividing by a fraction is the same as **multiplying by its reciprocal** (the fraction flipped upside down).

### The Reciprocal

The **reciprocal** of a fraction a/b is **b/a** (flip the numerator and denominator).

| Fraction | Reciprocal |
|----------|-----------|
| 1/2 | 2/1 = 2 |
| 3/4 | 4/3 |
| 5/7 | 7/5 |
| 2 (= 2/1) | 1/2 |

A number multiplied by its reciprocal always equals **1**:
- 3/4 x 4/3 = 12/12 = 1

### The Division Rule

> **To divide by a fraction, multiply by its reciprocal:**
>
> **a/b / c/d = a/b x d/c**

**Examples:**

| Division | As Multiplication | Result |
|:-:|:-:|:-:|
| 5 / (2/3) | 5 x 3/2 | **15/2 = 7 1/2** |
| 3/4 / (1/2) | 3/4 x 2/1 | **6/4 = 3/2 = 1 1/2** |
| 2/5 / (3/7) | 2/5 x 7/3 | **14/15** |

### Why Does This Rule Work?

Consider 6 / (2/3). We are asking: how many groups of 2/3 fit into 6?

- Each unit contains 3/2 groups of 2/3 (since 1 / (2/3) = 3/2)
- So 6 units contain 6 x 3/2 = 9 groups

Therefore, 6 / (2/3) = 6 x 3/2 = **9**

### Practical Examples of Division

**Example:** A ribbon is 3/4 metre long. It is cut into pieces of 1/8 metre each. How many pieces are there?

3/4 / (1/8) = 3/4 x 8/1 = 24/4 = **6 pieces**

**Example:** A tank holds 15/2 litres of water. If each bucket holds 3/4 litres, how many buckets are needed to fill the tank?

15/2 / (3/4) = 15/2 x 4/3 = 60/6 = **10 buckets**

## Word Problems with Fractions

**Example:** Ravi earns Rs 2400 per month. He spends 3/4 of it and saves the rest. How much does he save?

Amount spent = 3/4 x 2400 = Rs 1800
Amount saved = 2400 - 1800 = **Rs 600**

Or: Fraction saved = 1 - 3/4 = 1/4. Savings = 1/4 x 2400 = **Rs 600**

**Example:** A car travels 24 1/2 km using 3 1/2 litres of petrol. How far does it travel per litre?

Distance per litre = 24 1/2 / 3 1/2 = 49/2 / 7/2 = 49/2 x 2/7 = 98/14 = **7 km per litre**

## Key Words to Remember

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Fraction** | A number of the form a/b where a is the numerator and b is the denominator |
| **Proper fraction** | A fraction where the numerator is less than the denominator (value < 1) |
| **Improper fraction** | A fraction where the numerator is greater than or equal to the denominator (value >= 1) |
| **Mixed number** | A number written as a whole number and a proper fraction (e.g., 2 1/3) |
| **Reciprocal** | The fraction obtained by swapping the numerator and denominator; a/b becomes b/a |
| **Multiplication of fractions** | Multiply numerators together, multiply denominators together: (a/b) x (c/d) = (ac)/(bd) |
| **Division of fractions** | Multiply by the reciprocal: (a/b) / (c/d) = (a/b) x (d/c) |
| **"Fraction of"** | Means multiplication: 2/3 of 15 = 2/3 x 15 = 10 |
