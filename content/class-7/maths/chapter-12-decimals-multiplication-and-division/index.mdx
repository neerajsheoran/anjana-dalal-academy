We learned about decimal numbers and how to add and subtract them in Chapter 3. Now, we take the next step -- **multiplying** and **dividing** decimals. These operations follow naturally from what we already know about fractions.

## A Quick Recap of Decimals

The decimal number **27.53** represents:
- 2 Tens + 7 Units + 5 Tenths + 3 Hundredths
- In fractions: 27 + 5/10 + 3/100

### Dividing by Powers of 10

To divide any number by 10, 100, 1000, etc., simply **move the decimal point to the left** by the number of zeroes.

| Division | Result | Rule |
|----------|--------|------|
| 123 / 10 | 12.3 | Move 1 place left |
| 24 / 100 | 0.24 | Move 2 places left |
| 678 / 1000 | 0.678 | Move 3 places left |
| 12345 / 1000 | 12.345 | Move 3 places left |
| 12 / 1000 | 0.012 | Move 3 places left (add leading zeros) |

## Decimal Multiplication

### Multiplying a Decimal by a Whole Number

**Example:** Arshad buys 5 pens at Rs 9.5 each.

Total cost = 5 x 9.5

Convert to fractions: 5/1 x 95/10 = 475/10 = **47.5**

Or simply: 5 x 95 = 475, then place the decimal point to get 1 digit after the point = **47.5**

### Multiplying Two Decimals

**Example:** A car travels 12.5 km per litre. Distance covered with 7.5 litres?

12.5 x 7.5 = 125/10 x 75/10 = 9375/100 = **93.75**

### The Quick Rule for Decimal Multiplication

> **Step 1:** Ignore the decimal points and multiply the numbers as whole numbers.
>
> **Step 2:** Count the total number of decimal places in both numbers being multiplied.
>
> **Step 3:** Place the decimal point in the product so it has that many decimal places.

**Example:** 5.96 x 24.8

- Multiply as whole numbers: 596 x 248 = 147,808
- Count decimal places: 5.96 has 2, and 24.8 has 1, total = 3
- Place decimal: **147.808**

**Example:** 5.8 x 1.24

- 58 x 124 = 7,192
- Decimal places: 1 + 2 = 3
- Result: **7.192**

### When is the Product Greater or Smaller?

Unlike whole numbers, multiplying decimals does not always make a number bigger:

| Situation | Example | Product | Comparison |
|-----------|---------|---------|------------|
| Both > 1 | 3.4 x 6.5 | 22.1 | Product > both numbers |
| Both between 0 and 1 | 0.75 x 0.4 | 0.3 | Product &lt; both numbers |
| One > 1, one &lt; 1 | 0.75 x 5 | 3.75 | Product is between the two numbers |

> **Key Insight:** Multiplying by a number less than 1 makes the result **smaller**, not larger.

## Decimal Division

### Dividing a Decimal by a Whole Number

**Example:** Share Rs 117.5 equally among 5 people.

117.5 / 5 = (1175/10) / 5 = 1175 / (10 x 5) = 1175/50

Using long division: 1175 / 50 = 23.5

Each person gets **Rs 23.5**

### Dividing a Whole Number by a Decimal

**Example:** How many pieces of 0.75 m can be cut from a 6 m ribbon?

6 / 0.75 = 6 / (75/100) = 6 x (100/75) = 600/75 = **8 pieces**

### Dividing a Decimal by a Decimal

**Example:** 7.5 / 2.5

Convert to fractions: (75/10) / (25/10) = 75/25 = **3**

Or: multiply both numbers by 10 to eliminate decimals, then divide:
75 / 25 = **3**

### The Quick Rule for Decimal Division

> **Method:** Multiply both the dividend and divisor by a power of 10 to make the **divisor** a whole number. Then perform normal division.

**Example:** 9.36 / 1.2

Multiply both by 10: 93.6 / 12

Now divide: 93.6 / 12 = **7.8**

**Example:** 0.4896 / 0.24

Multiply both by 100: 48.96 / 24

Now divide: 48.96 / 24 = **2.04**

## Practical Applications

**Shopping:** Meenu buys 4 notebooks at Rs 15.50 each and 3 erasers at Rs 2.75 each.
Total = 4 x 15.50 + 3 x 2.75 = 62.00 + 8.25 = **Rs 70.25**

**Measurement:** A rupee coin is 1.45 mm thick. A stack of 36 coins:
Height = 36 x 1.45 = 52.2 mm = **5.22 cm**

**Speed and Distance:** A person walks 0.827 km to school and back, 6 days a week:
Weekly distance = 0.827 x 2 x 6 = 0.827 x 12 = **9.924 km**

**Unit Price:** If 2.250 kg of oranges costs Rs 56.50 per kg:
Total cost = 2.25 x 56.5 = **Rs 127.125**

## Equivalent Decimals in Multiplication

Adding trailing zeros after the last decimal digit does not change the value:
- 56.50 = 56.5
- 2.250 = 2.25

So 56.50 x 2.250 = 56.5 x 2.25, and both give the same product.

## Using Known Products

If you know that 18 x 12 = 216, you can find many related products:

| Product | Working | Result |
|---------|---------|--------|
| 18 x 1.2 | 216 with 1 decimal place | **21.6** |
| 18 x 0.12 | 216 with 2 decimal places | **2.16** |
| 1.8 x 1.2 | 216 with 2 decimal places | **2.16** |
| 0.18 x 0.12 | 216 with 4 decimal places | **0.0216** |
| 1.8 x 12 | 216 with 1 decimal place | **21.6** |

## Key Words to Remember

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Decimal** | A number expressed using the place value system extended to tenths, hundredths, etc. |
| **Decimal places** | The number of digits after the decimal point |
| **Product of decimals** | Multiply as whole numbers, then place the decimal point so the total decimal places equals the sum of decimal places in the factors |
| **Division of decimals** | Make the divisor a whole number by multiplying both dividend and divisor by a power of 10, then divide normally |
| **Equivalent decimals** | Decimals that represent the same value, like 2.5 and 2.50 |
