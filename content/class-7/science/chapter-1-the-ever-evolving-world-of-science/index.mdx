## Welcome to Science in Class 7

Science is not just a subject in your textbook. It is a way of thinking that welcomes curiosity, asks questions, and is open to the unknown. In Class 7, we will ask deeper questions: How do things work? Why do events happen the way they do? And what can we learn from the patterns we see in nature?

Science covers everything -- from tiny cells inside a leaf to the movement of the sun and the stars. From testing materials at home to understanding how water flows underground. Every chapter in this book is an adventure that challenges your thinking and helps you become a young explorer.

> **Key idea:** Science is not just about discovering facts. It is a process -- a way of thinking that involves questioning, experimenting, and exploring the world around us.

---

## What Will You Learn This Year?

Class 7 Science takes you on an exciting journey through different fields -- physics, chemistry, biology, and earth sciences. While they appear as separate chapters, all these topics are deeply connected. A discovery in one area often inspires questions in another.

Here is a quick look at what lies ahead:

### Properties of Materials

- Why are some fruits sour and others sweet?
- What happens when you wash a **haldi** (turmeric) stain on your school uniform?
- You will explore how materials behave and learn to classify substances as **acidic**, **basic**, or **neutral**.

### Electricity and Circuits

- What happens when you connect batteries, lamps, and wires?
- What kind of materials make a lamp glow?
- You will build simple electric circuits and discover which materials conduct electricity.

### Metals and Non-Metals

- Why is iron used for making gates but not for making jewellery?
- You will classify materials based on their properties and enter the world of **metals** and **non-metals**.

### Changes Around Us

- A torch battery runs out and cannot be used again. Ice melts into water. Fruits ripen on the tree. Rocks break into pebbles.
- Some changes can be **reversed** (like melting ice back to water), while others **cannot be reversed** (like burning paper).

### Heat Transfer

- How does heat flow from one place to another?
- Whether it is the melting of an ice cube in a glass or the melting of a glacier, heat plays a key role.
- You will learn how the Sun heats water in the seas, causing it to evaporate and fall as rain far away.

### Growing Up -- Adolescence

- Especially around the middle-school years, your body changes rapidly.
- You will learn about the stage of **adolescence** and the changes that happen during this exciting time of growth.

### Life Processes in Animals and Plants

- To grow, animals need to eat, breathe, and circulate nutrients through their body.
- But what about plants? Do plants also need food? Do they breathe? How?
- Over millions of years, life on Earth has figured out these processes in a beautifully balanced way.

### Measuring Time and Motion

- Long before electric clocks and digital watches, early humans observed the **shadows** of objects in sunlight and used their position to tell the time.
- You will explore how we measure time and how fast things happen.

### Light, Shadows, and Reflections

- Light helps us see, and today we have many ways to generate light so we can read a book at night.
- Asking questions about the nature of light has given us a very deep understanding of the universe we live in.

### Earth, Moon, and the Sun

- Even the Earth and the Moon can cast shadows, leading to the fascinating events called **eclipses**.
- You will learn how the Earth rotates on its axis, how the Moon orbits the Earth, and how the Earth orbits the Sun.
- These movements create day and night, seasons, and many other wonders.

> **Key idea:** All branches of science are interconnected. Ideas in one area often lead to discoveries in another. As you study each chapter, look for connections between them!

## Science is About Asking Questions

Great scientists do not just answer questions -- they ask amazing ones! To think like a scientist, it is equally important to come up with curious, creative, and fun questions.

Remember: **"To be a wise person, you must be a whys person."**

### Activity: Question the Answer

Here is a fun exercise. Instead of finding answers to questions, try to come up with interesting questions for the following answers:

1. **Answer:** "Just add some milk."
   - What creative question can you think of that leads to this answer?

2. **Answer:** "Because the cat's teeth were crooked."
   - Can you come up with a funny or surprising question?

3. **Answer:** "Don't panic, I have my towel."
   - What situation could lead to this response?

4. **Answer:** "42"
   - Try to think of an interesting question (not just "What is 32 + 10?").

There are never any wrong questions, so let your imagination run wild!

## Science and Responsibility

As young science explorers, you will soon see how human activities affect the natural world. Science is not just about discovery -- it is also about **responsibility**.

- Understanding how our actions impact the environment helps us make better choices.
- Science plays an important role in addressing **environmental challenges**.
- It can help us create a more **sustainable** world for future generations.

> **Key idea:** Science gives us the power to understand the world, but with that power comes the responsibility to protect our planet and use our knowledge wisely.

## Happy Exploring!

In the chapters that follow, you will make simple observations, do fun experiments, and dive into topics that need careful thinking. Each chapter builds on what you already know and encourages you to:

- **Ask questions** about the world around you
- **Explore** through hands-on experiments
- **Think like a scientist** by observing, recording, and drawing conclusions

Even experiments that seem to confirm what you expected might lead to new questions -- and those new questions might need more experiments! That is the beauty of science. It never stops.

So turn the page, and let your curiosity take flight!
