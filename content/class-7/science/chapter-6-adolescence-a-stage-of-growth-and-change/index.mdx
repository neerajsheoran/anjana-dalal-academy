## Introduction

Just like a seed germinates, grows into a sapling, and matures into a plant that can produce flowers and fruits, humans also go through stages of growth and development. The journey of human life can be divided into different stages:

1. **Infancy** (birth to about 2 years)
2. **Childhood** (about 2 to 10 years)
3. **Adolescence** (about 10 to 19 years)
4. **Adulthood** (about 19 to 60 years)
5. **Old age** (60 years and above)

Each person experiences these stages at their own pace. The duration and timing of each stage may vary from one individual to another.

> **Key idea:** **Adolescence** is the period of change from childhood to adulthood. It generally begins around the age of 10 and usually lasts until about 19 years. During this time, the body prepares itself for adulthood.

---

## Physical Changes During Adolescence

When you look at students going from Class 5 to Class 8, you can notice several changes. These changes are a normal part of growing up. Let us understand the most common ones.

### Increase in Height

From birth, our body continuously grows and develops. However, the increase in height becomes **more prominent during adolescence**. You may notice that you or your classmates have grown significantly taller over just a year or two.

### Changes in Body Structure and Weight

- **Boys:** Along with growing taller, boys gain weight. Their shoulders may become broader, and their chests may get wider.
- **Girls:** Girls also undergo changes in height and weight. They may also experience **development of breasts**, which is a normal part of growing up.

### Changes in Voice

In adolescent boys, the **voice box** (a structure in the throat that helps us speak) grows larger. This may cause the voice to sound **hoarse or deeper**. The growth of the voice box can sometimes be seen as a bump in the throat region called the **Adam's apple**. However, it is not noticeable in every individual.

The voice box also grows in adolescent girls, but it is not as large as in boys, leading to only slight changes in voice.

### Growth of Hair

Both boys and girls experience the growth of hair in different parts of their bodies, such as the **armpits** and **pubic region**.

Boys often develop **facial hair**, which later grows into a moustache and beard as they move towards adulthood. Some boys may also develop chest hair. The amount and timing of hair growth varies from person to person, and these variations are completely normal.

### Pimples and Acne

A common occurrence during adolescence is **acne** -- a skin condition in which small, reddish **pimples** appear, usually on the face. Acne occurs because of an increase in oily secretions from the skin during adolescence, which can clog skin pores and lead to infections.

> **Key idea:** The timing, nature, and extent of physical changes during adolescence vary from person to person. These variations are completely normal. Each person experiences adolescence at their own pace.

## Secondary Sexual Characteristics and Puberty

Some changes during adolescence help distinguish males from females but are **not directly involved in reproduction**. These are called **secondary sexual characteristics**.

**In boys:**
- Deepening of voice
- Growth of facial hair (moustache and beard)
- Broadening of shoulders

**In girls:**
- Development of breasts
- Widening of hips

Secondary sexual characteristics are natural signs that the body is preparing for adulthood. They mark the onset of **puberty**.

> **Key idea:** **Puberty** is the stage during which the body of an adolescent undergoes external and internal changes to develop into an adult capable of reproduction.

---

## The Menstrual Cycle

An important internal change associated with adolescent girls is the onset of the **menstrual cycle**. Here are the key facts:

- The menstrual cycle generally recurs every **28 to 30 days** (though healthy girls may have cycles ranging from 21 to 35 days).
- The phase when **blood discharge** occurs from the body is called **menstruation** (commonly known as "the period").
- Menstruation may last for **3 to 7 days**.
- Some girls may feel **pain or discomfort** in the lower abdomen during these days. This is normal.
- Menstruation usually begins during puberty and stops naturally by the age of **45 to 55 years**.
- The menstrual cycle is a sign of **good reproductive health**.

### Breaking Myths About Menstruation

There are many wrong beliefs about menstruation that lead to unnecessary fear, shame, or guilt. Some of these myths include physically isolating menstruating girls or restricting their activities. These beliefs have **no scientific basis**.

Menstruation is a **completely natural process**. By encouraging a scientific outlook, we can help promote good reproductive health and contribute to a healthier lifestyle for women and girls.

> **Key idea:** Menstruation is a natural and healthy process in girls. Myths and taboos around menstruation have no scientific basis and should be dispelled through awareness and education.

## Emotional and Behavioural Changes

Adolescence is not just about physical changes. It also brings **emotional and behavioural changes** that can be exciting, confusing, or both.

### Common Emotional Changes

| Emotional Change | Effect on Behaviour | Positive Ways to Handle It |
|-----------------|--------------------|-----------------------------|
| **Mood swings** | Feeling very happy one moment and low the next | Engage in activities like music, dance, or sports |
| **Strong emotions** | Feeling things more deeply than before | Practice self-awareness and express yourself through creative activities |
| **Increased sensitivity** | Being more affected by what others say or think | Talk to a trusted adult; understand that these feelings are normal |
| **Compassion** | Feeling more empathy for others | Get involved in social work and community service |
| **Curiosity and excitement** | Wanting to explore new things and ideas | Channel this energy into learning, hobbies, and positive activities |

Understanding how our emotions influence our behaviour can help us make better choices and respond to situations thoughtfully.

> **Key idea:** Adolescence is marked by stronger emotions than childhood. Understanding and managing these emotions is an important part of growing up.

## Making Adolescence a Joyful Experience

### Nutrition

Since adolescence is a period of rapid growth and development, a **nutritious diet** is extremely important.

| Food Sources | Nutrients | Functions |
|-------------|-----------|-----------|
| Milk, millets, curd, cheese, paneer | Calcium, proteins, fats | Help in optimal bone growth |
| Dal, eggs, fish, soybean, nuts | Proteins | Help build and repair body tissues |
| Spinach, kidney beans (rajma), raisins, figs | Iron | Help in formation of blood and improve energy levels |
| Fruits, vegetables | Vitamins and minerals | Support overall growth and immunity |
| Rice, roti, potatoes | Carbohydrates | Provide energy for daily activities |

Adolescents, especially girls, may suffer from blood-related health problems due to a deficiency of **iron** or **vitamin B12**. Eating foods rich in these nutrients is very important.

### Personal Hygiene

Maintaining proper **body hygiene** is crucially important during adolescence:

- Keep the armpits and pubic region clean to prevent infections.
- Bathe regularly and wear clean clothes.
- For girls, maintaining proper **menstrual hygiene** is essential. Sanitary pads, reusable cloth pads, or other products designed for this purpose should be used.
- Used sanitary pads should be **properly disposed** of by wrapping them in newspaper and placing them in a dustbin.
- Biodegradable sanitary pads are also available and are more environment-friendly.

The government has introduced several initiatives to support menstrual hygiene, including:
- **Menstrual Hygiene Scheme (MHS)** -- provides free or low-cost sanitary pads to girls in rural areas
- **Rashtriya Kishor Swasthya Karyakram (RKSK)** -- aims to improve overall adolescent health
- **Suvidha Sanitary Napkin Initiative** -- provides affordable biodegradable pads through Jan Aushadhi Kendras

### Physical Activities

Regular exercise and physical activities are very important during adolescence. Activities like playing sports, running, yoga, swimming, or cycling help:

- Keep your body and mind fit and healthy
- Build stamina and strength
- Boost your mood and reduce stress

> **Key idea:** A balanced diet, good personal hygiene, and regular physical activity are the three pillars of a healthy adolescence.

## Balanced Social Life

We all live in a society and interact with each other daily. During adolescence, social interactions become more important. Here are some guidelines:

- Be **polite and respectful** towards everyone
- Be **thoughtful and responsible** while interacting with others, whether in person or online
- Use social media platforms **responsibly** for everyone's well-being
- Seek guidance from elders and teachers when in doubt

### Cyberbullying

**Cyberbullying** involves using digital devices to harass others by sending misleading messages, spreading false rumours, or sharing personal information without consent.

**What to do if someone tries to bully you online:**
- Do not feel scared or helpless
- Handle it wisely and seek help from parents and teachers
- Be cautious about uploading pictures online or sharing personal information with strangers

**Dos and Don'ts for Social Media:**

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Be respectful and kind | Do not share personal photos with strangers |
| Think before posting | Do not spread rumours or false information |
| Protect your privacy | Do not bully or harass others online |
| Report inappropriate behaviour | Do not share others' personal information |

## Say NO to Harmful Substances

Some people, including peers, may try to pressure you into consuming harmful substances such as **tobacco, gutka, cigarettes, beedi, alcohol**, or illegal drugs.

### Why These Substances Are Dangerous

- They are **harmful** to both physical and mental health
- They are **addictive** -- once people start consuming them, they develop a strong urge to take them again and again
- Over time, regular use develops into **substance abuse**
- They can cause serious health problems like breathing difficulties, memory loss, and **lung damage**

> **Key idea:** Someone who is addicted today might have started with "just one time." Say NO to addictive substances the first time and every time!

### Getting Help

If someone is struggling with addiction:
- Seek help and support from family and friends
- Talk to trusted people like parents or teachers
- Seek counselling and medical advice
- Call the **National De-addiction Helpline: 14446**

The **Nasha Mukt Bharat Abhiyaan** (Drug-Free India Campaign) was launched by the Government of India to spread awareness about avoiding substance abuse, with a special focus on prevention among young people.

## Why Do These Changes Happen?

Many changes during adolescence, including menstruation and other signs of puberty, are mainly due to **hormones** -- certain chemicals produced in our bodies.

- **Hormones** play a crucial role in regulating growth and development.
- They are produced in different parts of the body.
- They are released at the right time in response to signals from the **brain**.
- Some hormones also influence **mood and behaviour**.

> **Key idea:** **Hormones** are chemicals produced in the body that control and regulate the physical, emotional, and behavioural changes during adolescence.

## Summary

- **Adolescence** is the period of change from childhood to adulthood, generally from age 10 to 19.
- It is marked by significant physical, biological, and emotional changes.
- **Secondary sexual characteristics** (like voice change in boys, breast development in girls) help distinguish males from females but are not directly involved in reproduction.
- **Puberty** is the stage when the body develops into an adult capable of reproduction.
- The **menstrual cycle** in girls involves a discharge of blood generally every 28-30 days. Menstruation is a natural, healthy process.
- Adolescents face emotional and behavioural changes, including mood swings and stronger emotions.
- A **balanced diet**, **personal hygiene**, and **regular physical activity** are essential for a healthy adolescence.
- Addictive substances like tobacco, alcohol, and drugs have severe effects on health. It is wise to say NO to them.
- Changes during adolescence are primarily controlled by **hormones** -- chemicals produced in the body.
