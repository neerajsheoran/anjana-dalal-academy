## What Is a Turn?

Have you ever spun around on the spot? That's a **turn**! Turns are everywhere — a clock's hands turn, a giant wheel turns, a door opens with a turn, even a tap needs a turn to open.

- A **full turn** brings you back to exactly where you started — like a clock's minute hand going all the way around.
- A **half turn** takes you to the opposite direction — you're now facing backwards.
- A **quarter turn** takes you one-fourth of the way around — like turning from facing North to facing East.

---

## Turns and Angles

When you hold one straw still and rotate another straw around the same point, the **opening** between the two straws is called an **angle**. So:

> **An angle is the amount of turn** between two lines (or arms) that meet at a point.

---

## Types of Angles

### Right Angle (1/4 turn)

A **right angle** is exactly a **quarter turn**. It looks like the corner of a book or a square.

You see right angles in:
- Corners of windows, doors, and books
- The letter "L"
- Where walls meet the floor

### Acute Angle (less than 1/4 turn)

An **acute angle** is **less than a right angle** — it's a small, sharp angle.

*Think of:* A slightly open paper fan, or the beak of a bird.

### Obtuse Angle (between 1/4 and 1/2 turn)

An **obtuse angle** is **more than a right angle but less than a straight angle** — it's a wide, open angle.

*Think of:* A paper fan opened wide, or the angle at the top of a roof.

### Straight Angle (1/2 turn)

A **straight angle** is exactly a **half turn** — it looks like a straight line (180°).

*Think of:* Pointing forward, then turning to face completely backwards.

---

## How Turns Add Up

| Turn | What it looks like |
|------|-------------------|
| 1/8 turn | A small angle (half of a right angle) |
| 1/4 turn | A right angle |
| 1/2 turn | A straight angle (straight line) |
| 3/4 turn | Three right angles |
| Full turn | Back to the start — complete circle |

### Combining turns:
- **2 quarter turns** = 1 half turn
- **2 half turns** = 1 full turn
- **4 quarter turns** = 1 full turn
- **2 eighth turns** = 1 quarter turn

> **Try this:** Make two acute turns one after another. Can two acute turns together still be acute? Sometimes yes (if both are very small), and sometimes they add up to a right angle or even an obtuse angle!

---

## Making an Angle Measuring Tool

You can make a simple tool to measure angles:

1. **Cut out a circle** from tracing paper.
2. **Fold it in half** — this gives you a half-turn mark.
3. **Fold in half again** — now you have quarter-turn marks.
4. **Fold in half once more** — now you have eighth-turn marks (8 equal parts).
5. Attach a straw at the centre. The folds show you where 1/8, 1/4, 3/8, 1/2 turns are.

You can also fold a circle into **6 equal parts** (by folding in half, then into thirds) to measure 1/6 turns and 1/12 turns.

## Angles and the Clock

The minute hand of a clock is a perfect way to understand turns:

| Minute hand moves by | Turn completed |
|---------------------|---------------|
| 15 minutes | 1/4 turn (right angle) |
| 30 minutes | 1/2 turn (straight angle) |
| 45 minutes | 3/4 turn |
| 60 minutes | Full turn (complete circle) |
| 5 minutes | 1/12 turn |
| 10 minutes | 1/6 turn |

> **Think about it:** The minute hand of a clock completes a **full turn every 60 minutes** (1 hour).

---

## Clockwise and Anti-clockwise

- **Clockwise** = the direction in which clock hands move (right, then down, then left, then up).
- **Anti-clockwise** (or counter-clockwise) = the **opposite** direction.

*Real-life examples:*
- Opening a tap → usually **anti-clockwise**
- Closing a tap → usually **clockwise**
- Unscrewing a bottle cap → **anti-clockwise**
- Screwing it back on → **clockwise**

---

## Turns and Directions

If you're facing **North** and make a quarter turn **clockwise**, you face **East**.

| Starting | Turn | Direction | You now face |
|----------|------|-----------|-------------|
| North | 1/4 clockwise | → | East |
| North | 1/2 clockwise | → | South |
| North | 3/4 clockwise | → | West |
| North | Full turn | → | North (back to start) |
| East | 1/4 anti-clockwise | → | North |
| South | 1/4 clockwise | → | West |

> **Fun fact:** 1 right angle clockwise from North = East. 2 right angles = South. 3 right angles = West. 4 right angles = back to North!

## Everyday Objects and Turns

Think about how much these objects can turn:

| Object | Maximum turn |
|--------|-------------|
| Clothes clip | Less than 1/4 turn |
| Door with hinge | About 1/4 turn (some doors can open to 1/2 turn) |
| Tongs | Less than 1/4 turn |
| Scissors | About 1/4 turn |
| File cover | 1/2 turn |
| Tap/faucet | About 1/4 to 1/2 turn |
| Fan / Giant wheel | Full turn (and keeps going!) |

## Key Words to Remember

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Angle** | The amount of turn between two lines meeting at a point |
| **Right angle** | A quarter turn — like the corner of a square |
| **Acute angle** | Less than a right angle — a small, sharp angle |
| **Obtuse angle** | More than a right angle but less than a straight angle — a wide angle |
| **Straight angle** | A half turn — looks like a straight line |
| **Clockwise** | The direction clock hands move |
| **Anti-clockwise** | The opposite direction to clock hands |
| **Full turn** | A complete rotation — 360° — back to the start |
