## What Is Symmetry?

A shape or design has **symmetry** when one part is a mirror image or rotation of another part. You see symmetry everywhere — in butterfly wings, flowers, rangoli, and even letters of the alphabet!

There are two main types of symmetry:
1. **Reflection symmetry** (mirror symmetry / line symmetry)
2. **Rotational symmetry** (turn symmetry)

---

## Reflection Symmetry (Line Symmetry)

![Butterfly with a vertical line of symmetry showing both halves as mirror images](reflection-symmetry-butterfly.png)

A shape has **reflection symmetry** if you can draw a line through it so that one half is the **mirror image** of the other half. This line is called the **line of symmetry**.

### Lines of Symmetry in Letters

| Letter | Vertical line | Horizontal line | Both? |
|--------|:------------:|:--------------:|:-----:|
| A | Yes | No | No |
| E | No | Yes | No |
| H | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| X | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| T | Yes | No | No |
| K | No | Yes | No |
| O | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| V | Yes | No | No |
| N | No | No | No |

### Making Cutouts Using Symmetry

**For a letter with 1 line of symmetry** (like A):
1. Fold paper in half
2. Draw **half** the letter along the fold
3. Cut and unfold — you get the full letter!

**For a letter with 2 lines of symmetry** (like H):
1. Fold paper into quarters (fold twice)
2. Draw **one-quarter** of the letter
3. Cut and unfold — you get the full letter!

---

## Rotational Symmetry

A shape has **rotational symmetry** if it looks **exactly the same** after being turned (rotated) by less than a full turn.

### The Windmill (Firki)

![A paper windmill (firki) shown in four positions after each quarter turn, looking identical each time](rotational-symmetry-firki.png)

A firki (windmill) looks the same after every **1/4 turn**. That means during one full turn, it looks the same **4 times**.

| Turn | Looks the same? |
|------|:--------------:|
| 1/4 turn | Yes |
| 1/2 turn | Yes |
| 3/4 turn | Yes |
| Full turn | Yes (always) |

### Rotational Symmetry in Letters

| Letter | Rotational symmetry? | At which turn? |
|--------|:-------------------:|:--------------:|
| H | Yes | 1/2 turn |
| N | Yes | 1/2 turn |
| S | Yes | 1/2 turn |
| O | Yes | every turn |
| A | No | — |
| E | No | — |

> **Key idea:** Some shapes have only reflection symmetry, some have only rotational symmetry, and some have **both**!

---

## Symmetry in Digits

| Digit | Reflection symmetry? | Rotational symmetry? |
|-------|:-------------------:|:-------------------:|
| 0 | Yes (vertical and horizontal) | Yes (1/2 turn) |
| 1 | Yes (vertical) | No |
| 8 | Yes (vertical and horizontal) | Yes (1/2 turn) |
| 3 | Yes (horizontal) | No |

> **Fun challenge:** The number **1001** has both reflection symmetry (horizontal line) and rotational symmetry (1/2 turn). Can you find other such numbers?

## Making Symmetrical Designs

You can create beautiful designs by combining shapes:

1. **Start with a simple design** in one quarter of a square.
2. **Reflect it** across the vertical line of symmetry.
3. **Reflect the top half** down to fill the bottom.
4. Now you have a design with **both horizontal and vertical** reflection symmetry!

To add **rotational symmetry**, make sure the design looks the same when you rotate it by 1/4 turn.

## Block Printing — Traditional Indian Art

**Block printing** is a traditional craft of Rajasthan. Artisans carve designs onto wooden blocks and stamp them onto fabric.

- A single block print can be **repeated** across fabric in patterns.
- Many traditional prints use **symmetry** — rotating the same block creates designs that look the same after 1/4 or 1/2 turns.
- Some designs have **reflection symmetry**, and some have **rotational symmetry**, and some have **both**!

## Comparing the Two Types of Symmetry

![Side by side comparison: reflection symmetry in letter A vs rotational symmetry in letter S](reflection-vs-rotational-symmetry.png)

| Feature | Reflection Symmetry | Rotational Symmetry |
|---------|:------------------:|:------------------:|
| **Test** | Fold along a line — do halves match? | Rotate — does it look the same? |
| **Example** | Butterfly, letter A | Windmill, letter S |
| **Line of symmetry** | Yes | Not needed |
| **Can have both?** | Yes! (like letter H, digit 8, many rangoli designs) | Yes! |

## Key Words to Remember

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Reflection symmetry** | One half is the mirror image of the other half |
| **Line of symmetry** | The line that divides a shape into two mirror-image halves |
| **Rotational symmetry** | A shape looks the same after being rotated by less than a full turn |
| **Vertical line** | A line going up and down (like the letter I) |
| **Horizontal line** | A line going left and right (like a horizon) |
