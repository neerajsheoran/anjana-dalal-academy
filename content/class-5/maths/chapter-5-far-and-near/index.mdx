## Units of Measuring Length

We use different units to measure different lengths:

| Unit | Short form | Used for |
|------|-----------|----------|
| Millimetre | mm | Very small things — thickness of a coin, length of an ant |
| Centimetre | cm | Small things — length of a pencil, width of a book |
| Metre | m | Medium things — height of a door, length of a room |
| Kilometre | km | Long distances — distance between cities |

---

## Relationships Between Units

```
10 mm  = 1 cm
100 cm = 1 m
1,000 m = 1 km
```

> **"Kilo" means thousand.** So 1 kilometre = 1,000 metres.

---

## Millimetres — Measuring Tiny Things

Look closely at your ruler. Between each centimetre mark, there are **10 small lines**. Each small gap is **1 millimetre (mm)**.

**1 cm = 10 mm**

Millimetres help us measure small things precisely:
- A sprout growing from a seed
- Small screws and nails
- The width of a pencil lead

### Converting between mm and cm:
- 45 mm = 4 cm 5 mm (because 45 = 40 + 5, and 40 mm = 4 cm)
- 3 cm 6 mm = 36 mm (because 3 cm = 30 mm, plus 6 mm)

---

## Centimetres and Metres

**100 cm = 1 m**

### Converting between cm and m:
- 234 cm = 2 m 34 cm (because 234 = 200 + 34, and 200 cm = 2 m)
- 5,792 cm = 57 m 92 cm
- 6 m 34 cm = 634 cm

---

## Metres and Kilometres

**1,000 m = 1 km**

When we walk **1,000 metres**, we say we have walked **1 kilometre**.

### Converting between m and km:
- 34 km = 34,000 m
- 6,870 m = 6 km 870 m
- 29 km 30 m = 29,030 m

## Choosing the Right Unit

| What you're measuring | Best unit |
|----------------------|-----------|
| Height of India Gate | 42 **metres** |
| Length of a handkerchief | 40 **centimetres** |
| Depth of a well | 50 **metres** |
| Length of a mobile phone | 13 **centimetres** |
| Distance between two cities | **kilometres** |
| Thickness of a coin | **millimetres** |

## Comparing Lengths

To compare lengths, first convert them to the **same unit**.

**Example:** Which is longer — 456 cm or 5 m?
- 5 m = 500 cm
- 456 cm &lt; 500 cm
- So 456 cm **&lt;** 5 m

**Example:** Which is longer — 6 m 5 cm or 6 m 50 cm?
- 6 m 5 cm = 605 cm
- 6 m 50 cm = 650 cm
- So 6 m 5 cm **&lt;** 6 m 50 cm

## Adding and Subtracting Lengths

### Method 1: Add like units separately

**Example:** 3 km 450 m + 4 km 650 m
- km: 3 + 4 = 7 km
- m: 450 + 650 = 1,100 m = 1 km 100 m
- Total: 7 km + 1 km 100 m = **8 km 100 m**

### Method 2: Convert everything to one unit, then calculate

**Example:** 63 m − 16 m 75 cm
- 63 m = 6,300 cm
- 16 m 75 cm = 1,675 cm
- 6,300 − 1,675 = 4,625 cm = **46 m 25 cm**

---

## Multiplying and Dividing Lengths

**Example:** A shirt needs 1 m 80 cm of cloth. How much for 20 shirts?
- 20 × 1 m 80 cm = 20 × 1 m + 20 × 80 cm
- = 20 m + 1,600 cm = 20 m + 16 m = **36 m**

**Example:** A 12 km 600 m road is laid equally over 6 days. How much per day?
- 12 km 600 m = 12,600 m
- 12,600 ÷ 6 = **2,100 m = 2 km 100 m** per day

The **Gulmarg Gondola** in Kashmir is one of the world's longest and highest cable cars:

## Feet and Inches

In daily life, height is often measured in **feet and inches**:
- **1 inch ≈ 2 cm 5 mm** (2.54 cm)
- **1 foot = 12 inches ≈ 30 cm**

> Stand against a wall, mark your height, and measure it in feet and inches!
