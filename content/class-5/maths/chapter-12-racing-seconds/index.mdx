## Measuring Time

We use different units to measure time depending on how long something takes:

| Unit | Used for |
|------|----------|
| **Seconds** | Very short durations — blinking, snapping fingers |
| **Minutes** | Short activities — brushing teeth, boiling an egg |
| **Hours** | Longer activities — a school period, cooking a meal |

---

## Relationships Between Time Units

```
60 seconds = 1 minute
60 minutes = 1 hour
24 hours   = 1 day
```

> The **seconds hand** on a clock goes around once every minute (60 seconds). The **minute hand** goes around once every hour (60 minutes).

---

## Why Do We Need Seconds?

In a 200m walking race, three children finished in:
- Akira: **1 minute 55 seconds**
- Sunita: **1 minute 56 seconds**
- Mary: **1 minute 57 seconds**

All three took about 1 minute — but **Akira won** because she was 1 second faster than Sunita! Without seconds, we couldn't tell who won.

> Seconds help us measure **small differences** in time precisely.

## 12-Hour and 24-Hour Time

### 12-Hour Format
Uses **AM** (midnight to noon) and **PM** (noon to midnight):
- 5:30 AM = early morning
- 2:30 PM = afternoon
- 9:35 PM = night

### 24-Hour Format
Counts hours from 0 to 23 (no AM/PM needed):
- **05:30 hours** = 5:30 AM
- **14:30 hours** = 2:30 PM (12 + 2 = 14)
- **21:35 hours** = 9:35 PM (12 + 9 = 21)

### Quick conversion:
- **AM times** stay the same: 8:35 AM = 08:35 hours
- **PM times** add 12: 5:30 PM = 17:30 hours (5 + 12 = 17)
- **12:00 PM** (noon) = 12:00 hours
- **12:00 AM** (midnight) = 00:00 hours

| 12-Hour | 24-Hour |
|---------|---------|
| 6:30 AM | 06:30 |
| 11:55 AM | 11:55 |
| 2:30 PM | 14:30 |
| 8:45 PM | 20:45 |
| 11:45 PM | 23:45 |

## Converting Hours and Minutes

### Hours to Minutes:
Multiply by 60.
- 2 hours = 2 × 60 = **120 minutes**
- 3 hours = **180 minutes**
- 4 hours 20 minutes = (4 × 60) + 20 = 240 + 20 = **260 minutes**

### Minutes to Hours:
Divide by 60.
- 75 minutes = 1 hour 15 minutes (75 ÷ 60 = 1 remainder 15)
- 150 minutes = 2 hours 30 minutes
- 220 minutes = 3 hours 40 minutes

---

## Converting Minutes and Seconds

### Minutes to Seconds:
Multiply by 60.
- 5 minutes = 5 × 60 = **300 seconds**
- 10 minutes 13 seconds = (10 × 60) + 13 = **613 seconds**

### Seconds to Minutes:
Divide by 60.
- 320 seconds = 5 minutes 20 seconds (320 ÷ 60 = 5 remainder 20)
- 700 seconds = 11 minutes 40 seconds

## Finding Time Duration

### How long did something take?

**Method: Count forward from start time.**

**Example:** Raghu left at 08:00 and arrived at 09:05.
- 08:00 → 09:00 = 1 hour
- 09:00 → 09:05 = 5 minutes
- Total: **1 hour 5 minutes**

### What time did it end?

**Example:** Jyoti went to play at 6:15 PM and played for 1 hour 45 minutes.
- 6:15 + 1 hour = 7:15 PM
- 7:15 + 45 minutes = **8:00 PM**

### What time did it start?

**Example:** Ragini finished homework at 9:40 PM. It took 1 hour 10 minutes.
- 9:40 − 1 hour = 8:40
- 8:40 − 10 minutes = **8:30 PM**

## Quick Time Activities

Things you can do in **seconds**:
- Blink your eyes
- Snap your fingers
- Switch a light on and off

Things that take **minutes**:
- Fill a glass of water
- Wash your hands
- Drink a glass of water
- Count from 1 to 20

> **Try this:** How many times can you skip rope in 10 seconds? How many push-ups can you do in 1 minute?

## Reading Clocks

![Analog clock with hour hand, minute hand, and seconds hand labeled](reading-a-clock.png)

A clock has:
- **Hour hand** (short) — shows the hour
- **Minute hand** (long) — shows the minutes
- **Seconds hand** (thin, fast) — shows the seconds

The seconds hand completes one full circle in **60 seconds** (1 minute).

Each small mark on the clock = **1 second** (or 1 minute, depending on which hand you're reading).

## Key Words to Remember

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Second** | A very short unit of time; 60 seconds = 1 minute |
| **Minute** | A short unit of time; 60 minutes = 1 hour |
| **Hour** | A unit of time; 24 hours = 1 day |
| **AM** | Ante Meridiem — time from midnight to noon |
| **PM** | Post Meridiem — time from noon to midnight |
| **24-hour format** | Time shown as 00:00 to 23:59, used in railways and airports |
| **Duration** | How long something takes from start to finish |
| **Elapsed time** | The time that has passed between two events |
