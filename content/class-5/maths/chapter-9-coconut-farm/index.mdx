## Division — The Reverse of Multiplication

**Division** means splitting a number into equal groups. It is the **reverse** of multiplication.

If **5 × 7 = 35**, then:
- **35 ÷ 7 = 5** (35 split into 7 groups → 5 in each group)
- **35 ÷ 5 = 7** (35 split into 5 groups → 7 in each group)

> **Key relationship:** Dividend (N) = Divisor (D) × Quotient (Q)

---

## Division Facts from Multiplication Facts

Every multiplication fact gives you **two division facts**:

| Multiplication | Division fact 1 | Division fact 2 |
|---------------|----------------|----------------|
| 5 × 7 = 35 | 35 ÷ 5 = 7 | 35 ÷ 7 = 5 |
| 30 × 30 = 900 | 900 ÷ 30 = 30 | — |
| 15 × 60 = 900 | 900 ÷ 15 = 60 | 900 ÷ 60 = 15 |
| 400 × 8 = 3,200 | 3,200 ÷ 400 = 8 | 3,200 ÷ 8 = 400 |

## Mental Strategies for Division

### Strategy 1: Split into Convenient Parts

**Example:** 1,248 ÷ 6
- Split: 1,248 = 1,200 + 48
- 1,200 ÷ 6 = 200
- 48 ÷ 6 = 8
- **Answer: 208**

### Strategy 2: Use Nearest Multiple, Then Adjust

**Example:** 1,992 ÷ 4
- 1,992 is close to 2,000
- 2,000 ÷ 4 = 500
- 2,000 − 1,992 = 8, and 8 ÷ 4 = 2
- 500 − 2 = **498**

### Strategy 3: Repeated Halving (for dividing by 4 or 8)

**Example:** 128 ÷ 4
- Half of 128 = 64
- Half of 64 = 32
- **Answer: 32**

**Example:** 168 ÷ 8
- Half of 168 = 84
- Half of 84 = 42
- Half of 42 = 21
- **Answer: 21**

> **When does this work?** Repeated halving works when the divisor is **4** (halve twice) or **8** (halve three times).

## Division with Remainders

Sometimes a number doesn't divide evenly. The leftover is called the **remainder**.

**Example:** 535 ÷ 25
- 25 × 21 = 525
- 535 − 525 = 10
- **Quotient: 21, Remainder: 10**

### The Division Formula:
> **N = D × Q + R**
>
> Dividend = Divisor × Quotient + Remainder

**Example:** 726 ÷ 4
- 4 × 181 = 724
- 726 = 4 × 181 + 2
- **Quotient: 181, Remainder: 2**

Always **check** your division using this formula!

## Long Division — Partial Quotients Method

![Step-by-step partial quotients division showing 582 divided by 6 with groups being subtracted](partial-quotients-method.png)

This method takes away groups of the divisor step by step.

### Example: 582 ÷ 6

**Susie's way (many small steps):**
- 582 − 120 (6 × 20) = 462
- 462 − 120 (6 × 20) = 342
- 342 − 120 (6 × 20) = 222
- 222 − 120 (6 × 20) = 102
- 102 − 60 (6 × 10) = 42
- 42 − 42 (6 × 7) = 0
- Total: 20 + 20 + 20 + 20 + 10 + 7 = **97**

**Sunitha's way (fewer, bigger steps):**
- 582 − 540 (6 × 90) = 42
- 42 − 42 (6 × 7) = 0
- Total: 90 + 7 = **97**

> Both methods give the same answer! Sunitha's method is faster because she chose bigger multiples.

## Division Using Place Value

This is the standard long division method.

### Example: 324 ÷ 3

```
        1 0 8
    ┌────────
3   │ 3 2 4
    │−3        (3 × 1 Hundred)
    │  0 2     (can't divide 2 Tens by 3 → 0 Tens)
    │    2 4   (regroup: 24 Ones)
    │   −2 4   (3 × 8 Ones)
    │     0
```
**Answer: 108**

### Example: 136 ÷ 6

```
        0 2 2
    ┌────────
6   │ 1 3 6
    │    1 3   (can't divide 1 Hundred by 6 → regroup to 13 Tens)
    │   −1 2   (6 × 2 Tens)
    │     1 6  (1 Ten + 6 Ones = 16 Ones)
    │    −1 2  (6 × 2 Ones)
    │      4   (Remainder)
```
**Answer: 22, Remainder 4**

> **Watch out for zeros!** If a digit is too small to divide, put **0** in the quotient and regroup with the next digit.

## Real-Life Problems — Susie's Coconut Farm

### Extracting Coconut Oil
Susie and Sunitha used **4,376 coconuts** for oil. They get 1 litre from every 8 coconuts.
- 4,376 ÷ 8 = **547 litres** of coconut oil!

### Selling Coconut Husk
They earned ₹9,913 from selling husk at ₹23 per kg.
- 9,913 ÷ 23 = **431 kg** of husk sold.

### Tender Coconuts
Ibrahim sold tender coconuts at ₹35 each and earned ₹8,890.
- 8,890 ÷ 35 = **254 tender coconuts**
- He bought them at ₹20 each: 254 × 20 = ₹5,080
- Extra money earned: 8,890 − 5,080 = **₹3,810 profit**

## Mathematical Statements — True or False?

Test if both sides of the "=" sign have the same value:

- 8 × 9 = 70 + 2 → 72 = 72 ✓ **True**
- 20 ÷ 6 = 7 − 3 → This doesn't work evenly — **False**
- 25 + 10 = 45 − 10 → 35 = 35 ✓ **True**

### Important patterns:
- **Adding two odd numbers always gives an even number** → Always true
- **Multiplying a number by 2 can give an odd number** → Never true
- **Halving a number always gives an even number** → Sometimes true (half of 6 = 3, which is odd!)

## Key Words to Remember

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Dividend** | The number being divided |
| **Divisor** | The number you divide by |
| **Quotient** | The result of division |
| **Remainder** | The amount left over after division |
| **Partial quotients** | A division method where you subtract groups step by step |
| **Regroup** | Combine with the next place value when a digit is too small to divide |
