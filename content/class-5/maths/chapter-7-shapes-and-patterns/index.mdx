## Weaving Mats — Patterns in Art

Have you seen woven baskets? If you look closely, you'll notice different weaving patterns on each one. Weaving follows a **rule** — just like patterns in maths!

A simple weave follows this rule:
- **Row 1:** 1 under, 1 over, 1 under, 1 over… (repeat)
- **Row 2:** 1 over, 1 under, 1 over, 1 under… (repeat)

Each row does the **opposite** of the row before it. You can create beautiful designs by changing the pattern — like "2 over, 1 under" or "3 over, 3 under".

## Tiling and Tessellation

**Tessellation** means covering a flat surface with shapes that fit together **without any gaps or overlaps**.

### Which shapes tessellate?

![Tessellation examples showing triangles, squares, and hexagons covering a surface without gaps](tessellation-examples.png)

| Shape | Tessellates? | How many fit at a point? |
|-------|-------------|------------------------|
| Equilateral Triangle | Yes | 6 triangles meet at each point |
| Square | Yes | 4 squares meet at each point |
| Regular Hexagon | Yes | 3 hexagons meet at each point |
| Regular Pentagon | No | They leave gaps! |
| Regular Octagon | No | They leave gaps (but octagons + squares together can tile!) |

> **Key idea:** Only three regular shapes can tessellate on their own — **triangles, squares, and hexagons**. But you can combine different shapes (like octagons with squares, or triangles with squares) to create beautiful tessellations!

## Types of Triangles

![Three triangles labeled equilateral, isosceles, and scalene with side lengths marked](types-of-triangles.png)

### By sides:

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Equilateral** | All 3 sides are equal. All 3 angles are also equal. |
| **Isosceles** | 2 sides are equal. The 2 angles opposite the equal sides are also equal. |
| **Scalene** | No sides are equal. No angles are equal. |

> **How to check:** Fold an isosceles triangle in half along its line of symmetry. The two halves match perfectly — proving two sides and two angles are equal!

## Types of Quadrilaterals (4-sided shapes)

![Five quadrilaterals shown side by side: square, rectangle, rhombus, parallelogram, and kite with properties labeled](types-of-quadrilaterals.png)

### Special quadrilaterals:

| Shape | Properties |
|-------|-----------|
| **Square** | All 4 sides equal. All 4 angles are right angles. |
| **Rectangle** | Opposite sides equal. All 4 angles are right angles. |
| **Rhombus** | All 4 sides equal. Opposite angles are equal. Angles are NOT right angles. |
| **Parallelogram** | Opposite sides equal and parallel. Opposite angles equal. |
| **Kite** | Two pairs of adjacent (neighbouring) sides are equal. |

### Relationships:
- A **square** is a special rectangle (with all sides equal).
- A **square** is also a special rhombus (with all right angles).
- A **rectangle** is a special parallelogram (with right angles).

---

## Which Shape Am I?

- "All my angles are right angles, but not all sides are equal" → **Rectangle**
- "All my sides are equal, but not all angles are right angles" → **Rhombus**
- "All my sides are equal AND all angles are right angles" → **Square**
- "Opposite sides and angles are equal" → **Parallelogram**
- "Two pairs of adjacent sides are equal" → **Kite**

## Making Shapes from Triangles

When you cut a rhombus along its diagonals, you get **4 triangles**. With these triangles you can make:

- **Using 2 triangles:** Different triangles (isosceles), parallelograms, kites
- **Using 3 triangles:** Triangles, quadrilaterals, pentagons (5-sided shapes)
- **Using all 4 triangles:** Squares, rectangles, triangles, pentagons, hexagons

## Tangram

![Tangram puzzle showing the seven pieces arranged as a square and rearranged into an animal shape](tangram-pieces.png)

A **tangram** is an ancient Chinese puzzle made of 7 pieces cut from a square:
- 2 large triangles
- 1 medium triangle
- 2 small triangles
- 1 square
- 1 parallelogram

With these 7 pieces, you can make hundreds of different shapes — animals, people, houses, letters, and more!

## Circles and Shapes

Here's a fun discovery:

1. Draw a **circle** with a compass.
2. Draw **two diameters** (lines through the centre).
3. Connect the 4 endpoints where the diameters meet the circle.
4. You always get a **rectangle**!

If the two diameters are perpendicular (at right angles), you get a **square**.

## 3D Shapes — Cubes and Beyond

![3D cube with faces, edges, and vertices labeled, and a 3x3x3 painted cube puzzle](cube-3d-shape.png)

### Cube:
- 6 faces (all squares)
- 12 edges
- 8 vertices (corners)

**Painted cube puzzle:** If you glue 27 small cubes into a big 3×3×3 cube and paint the outside:
- **8 cubes** have 3 faces painted (the corners)
- **12 cubes** have 2 faces painted (the edges)
- **6 cubes** have 1 face painted (the centres of each face)
- **1 cube** has 0 faces painted (the one hidden in the middle!)

### Icosahedron:
A 3D shape with **20 triangular faces** — "icosa" means twenty.

### Dodecahedron:
A 3D shape with **12 pentagonal faces** — "dodeca" means twelve.

## Key Words to Remember

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Tessellation** | Covering a flat surface with shapes, leaving no gaps or overlaps |
| **Equilateral triangle** | A triangle with all 3 sides (and angles) equal |
| **Isosceles triangle** | A triangle with 2 equal sides (and 2 equal angles) |
| **Scalene triangle** | A triangle with no equal sides |
| **Parallelogram** | A quadrilateral with opposite sides equal and parallel |
| **Rhombus** | A parallelogram with all 4 sides equal |
| **Kite** | A quadrilateral with 2 pairs of adjacent equal sides |
| **Tangram** | A puzzle of 7 shapes that fit together to make a square |
