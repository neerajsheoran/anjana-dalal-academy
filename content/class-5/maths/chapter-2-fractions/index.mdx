## What Are Fractions?

A **fraction** tells us about a **part of a whole**. When you cut a roti into 2 equal pieces, each piece is **one-half** or **1/2** of the roti. When you cut it into 4 equal pieces, each piece is **one-fourth** or **1/4**.

> **Important:** To compare fractions of two different things, the **wholes must be the same size**. Half of a big chocolate can be bigger than one-third of a small chocolate!

---

## Equivalent Fractions

**Equivalent fractions** are different fractions that represent the **same part** of a whole.

Look at this:
- Shade **1/3** of a rectangle.
- Now draw a horizontal line to split the rectangle into 6 equal parts.
- The shaded part is now **2/6** — but it's the **same amount**!

So: **1/3 = 2/6**

### How to Find Equivalent Fractions

Multiply (or divide) both the **numerator** and the **denominator** by the **same number**.

```
1/3 = 2/6 = 3/9 = 4/12 = 5/15 = ...
(×2)  (×3)  (×4)   (×5)
```

**Another example:**
```
2/5 = 4/10 = 6/15 = 8/20 = ... = 20/50 = 40/100
```

> **Think of it this way:** When you cut each piece into smaller equal parts, the number of pieces changes but the amount stays the same.

### Using a Fraction Kit

A fraction kit has strips of paper cut into halves, thirds, fourths, fifths, sixths, eighths, and twelfths. You can place them next to each other to discover equivalent fractions:

- 1 piece of **1/2** = 2 pieces of **1/4** → so **1/2 = 2/4**
- 1 piece of **1/2** = 3 pieces of **1/6** → so **1/2 = 3/6**
- 1 piece of **1/2** = 4 pieces of **1/8** → so **1/2 = 4/8**
- 1 piece of **1/3** = 2 pieces of **1/6** → so **1/3 = 2/6**
- 1 piece of **1/4** = 3 pieces of **1/12** → so **1/4 = 3/12**

## Comparing Fractions — Same Denominator

When fractions have the **same denominator** (same-sized pieces), just compare the **numerators** (number of pieces).

**Example:** Sevi ate 1/3 of a chikki. Shami ate 2/3 of the same chikki. Who ate more?

2 pieces of 1/3 > 1 piece of 1/3 → **2/3 > 1/3**

More examples:
- **3/4 > 1/4** (3 quarter-pieces is more than 1 quarter-piece)
- **5/7 > 2/7**
- **7/8 > 3/8**

> **Rule:** When denominators are the same, the fraction with the **bigger numerator** is greater.

---

## Comparing Fractions — Same Numerator

When fractions have the **same numerator** (same number of pieces), the one with the **smaller denominator** is bigger!

**Why?** A smaller denominator means each piece is **bigger**.

**Example:** Sevi ate 4/6 paratha. Shami ate 4/5 paratha. Who ate more?

- A **1/5** piece is bigger than a **1/6** piece (fewer cuts = bigger pieces).
- So **4 pieces of 1/5** > **4 pieces of 1/6**.
- Therefore: **4/5 > 4/6**

More examples:
- **3/7 > 3/8** (sevenths are bigger than eighths)
- **2/5 > 2/7** (fifths are bigger than sevenths)
- **5/6 > 5/7** (sixths are bigger than sevenths)

> **Rule:** When numerators are the same, the fraction with the **smaller denominator** is greater.

## Fractions Greater Than 1

What happens when you eat **more** pieces than what makes one whole?

### Example with Halves

Raman's father cuts parathas into halves (1/2 each).

- **2 halves** = 2/2 = **1 whole paratha**
- **3 halves** = 3/2 = **1 and 1/2 parathas** (written as 1½)
- **5 halves** = 5/2 = **2 and 1/2 parathas** (written as 2½)
- **6 halves** = 6/2 = **3 whole parathas**

On a number line:
```
0    1/2    2/2=1    3/2    4/2=2    5/2    6/2=3
|-----|------|--------|------|--------|------|
```

### Example with Fourths

Dadaji took 9 pieces of 1/4 paratha:
- 9/4 = 2 whole parathas + 1/4 paratha = **2¼ parathas**

```
0   1/4  2/4  3/4  4/4=1  5/4  6/4  7/4  8/4=2  9/4
|----|----|----|----|------|----|----|----|------|----|
```

> **How to tell:** A fraction is **greater than 1** when the numerator is **bigger** than the denominator. For example, 5/4 > 1 because 5 > 4.

### Mixed Numbers

A **mixed number** has a whole number part and a fraction part:
- 5/2 = **2½** (two and a half)
- 9/4 = **2¼** (two and a quarter)
- 4/3 = **1⅓** (one and a third)

---

## Comparing Fractions Using 1 as a Reference

A quick way to compare some fractions: check if each is **less than, equal to, or greater than 1**.

- If the **numerator &lt; denominator** → fraction is **less than 1**
- If the **numerator = denominator** → fraction **equals 1**
- If the **numerator > denominator** → fraction is **greater than 1**

**Example:** Compare 7/8 and 8/6.
- 7/8 &lt; 1 (because 7 &lt; 8)
- 8/6 > 1 (because 8 > 6)
- So **7/8 &lt; 8/6**

---

## Comparing Fractions Using 1/2 as a Reference

Another useful trick: check if each fraction is **less than, equal to, or greater than 1/2**.

**How to tell if a fraction equals 1/2:**
The numerator is exactly half of the denominator.
- 3/6 = 1/2, 4/8 = 1/2, 5/10 = 1/2, 7/14 = 1/2

**How to tell if a fraction is more than 1/2:**
The numerator is **more than** half of the denominator.
- 5/8 > 1/2 (because 5 > 4, and half of 8 is 4)

**How to tell if a fraction is less than 1/2:**
The numerator is **less than** half of the denominator.
- 3/9 &lt; 1/2 (because 3 &lt; 4.5, and half of 9 is 4.5)

**Example:** Compare 5/8 and 3/6.
- 3/6 = 1/2 exactly
- 5/8 > 1/2
- So **5/8 > 3/6**

## Fun Fraction Challenge

> If the length of an ant is **1/4 cm**, what is the total length of **16 ants** walking in a line?
>
> 16 × 1/4 = 16/4 = **4 cm**

## Key Words to Remember

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Fraction** | A number that shows a part of a whole (like 1/2, 3/4) |
| **Numerator** | The top number — tells how many parts you have |
| **Denominator** | The bottom number — tells how many equal parts the whole is divided into |
| **Equivalent fractions** | Different fractions that show the same amount (1/2 = 2/4 = 3/6) |
| **Mixed number** | A number with a whole part and a fraction part (like 2½) |
| **Unit fraction** | A fraction with 1 as the numerator (like 1/3, 1/5, 1/8) |
