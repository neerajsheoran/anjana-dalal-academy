## Perimeter — The Boundary Length

The **perimeter** of a shape is the **total length of its boundary** — how far you'd walk if you went all the way around the shape.

![Rectangle with all four sides labeled showing how perimeter is the total boundary length](perimeter-rectangle.png)

### Finding Perimeter:
Add up all the side lengths!

**Example:** A rectangle with length 15 and breadth 10:
- Perimeter = 15 + 10 + 15 + 10 = **50 units**

### Formulas:

**Rectangle:**
> Perimeter = 2 × Length + 2 × Breadth

**Square** (all sides equal):
> Perimeter = 4 × Side length

**Example:** A square with side 5 cm:
- Perimeter = 4 × 5 = **20 cm**

---

## Area — The Space Inside

The **area** of a shape is the amount of **flat space it covers** — like how much paint you'd need to cover it.

### Finding Area by Counting Squares

![A rectangle on a square grid with unit squares counted inside to show area](area-counting-squares.png)

Place the shape on a **square grid** and count how many unit squares it covers.

- A square with side 1 cm has area = **1 square cm** (also written as 1 cm²)
- Count full squares, and estimate half squares

### Comparing Areas

To compare which shape is bigger, put them on the same grid and count. You can also **cut and overlap** shapes to compare.

---

## Which Shapes Tile (Cover Without Gaps)?

When covering a surface (like a table or floor), some shapes work and some don't:

| Shape | Tiles without gaps? |
|-------|:------------------:|
| Squares | Yes |
| Rectangles | Yes |
| Triangles | Yes |
| Circles | No (they leave gaps!) |

> The area of a table = the number of square tiles needed to cover it completely (without gaps or overlaps).

---

## Area and Perimeter of Rectangles

### Area of a Rectangle

Look at a rectangle made of square patches — 6 rows of 4 patches each:
- Area = 6 × 4 = **24 square units**

The number of rows = the **length**, and the number of columns = the **breadth**.

> **Area of Rectangle = Length × Breadth**

**Example:** Length = 42 m, Breadth = 34 m
- Area = 42 × 34 = **1,428 square metres**

### Perimeter of a Rectangle

> **Perimeter of Rectangle = 2 × Length + 2 × Breadth**

**Example:** Length = 6 cm, Breadth = 4 cm
- Perimeter = 2 × 6 + 2 × 4 = 12 + 8 = **20 cm**

---

## Area and Perimeter of Squares

For a square, all sides are equal, so:

> **Area of Square = Side × Side**

> **Perimeter of Square = 4 × Side**

**Example:** Side = 5 cm
- Area = 5 × 5 = **25 square cm**
- Perimeter = 4 × 5 = **20 cm**

---

## Important Discovery: Same Area ≠ Same Perimeter!

![Three different rectangles all with area 12 square units but different perimeters shown on a grid](same-area-different-perimeter.png)

**Shapes with the same area can have different perimeters**, and shapes with the same perimeter can have different areas!

### Example — Same area (12 square units), different perimeters:

| Shape | Dimensions | Area | Perimeter |
|-------|-----------|------|-----------|
| Rectangle A | 6 × 2 | 12 | 16 |
| Rectangle B | 4 × 3 | 12 | 14 |
| Rectangle C | 12 × 1 | 12 | 26 |

All have the same area (12), but very different perimeters!

### Example — Same perimeter (16 units), different areas:

| Shape | Dimensions | Perimeter | Area |
|-------|-----------|-----------|------|
| Rectangle A | 7 × 1 | 16 | 7 |
| Rectangle B | 6 × 2 | 16 | 12 |
| Rectangle C | 4 × 4 | 16 | 16 |

All have the same perimeter (16), but different areas. The **square** (4 × 4) has the **largest area** for this perimeter.

> **Key insight:** If you increase the area, the perimeter doesn't necessarily increase — and vice versa!

---

## Finding Missing Dimensions

If you know the area and one side, you can find the other:

**Example:** Area = 64 square m, Length = 16 m. What is the breadth?
- Breadth = Area ÷ Length = 64 ÷ 16 = **4 m**

---

## Estimating Area of Irregular Shapes

For shapes that aren't perfect rectangles (like your palm or a leaf):
1. Place the shape on a **square grid**
2. Count the full squares inside
3. For partial squares: if more than half is covered, count it as 1; if less than half, count it as 0
4. Add up for an **approximate area**

## Key Words to Remember

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Perimeter** | The total length of the boundary of a shape |
| **Area** | The amount of flat space a shape covers |
| **Square unit** | The area of a square with side 1 unit — used to measure area |
| **Length** | The longer side of a rectangle |
| **Breadth** | The shorter side of a rectangle (also called width) |
| **Tiling** | Covering a surface with shapes without gaps or overlaps |
