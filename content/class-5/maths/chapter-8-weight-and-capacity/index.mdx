## Measuring Weight

We measure how heavy things are using **weight** (mass). Different units are used depending on how heavy the object is:

| Unit | Short form | Used for |
|------|-----------|----------|
| Milligram | mg | Very light things — medicines, gold |
| Gram | g | Small things — a pencil, a coin, an apple |
| Kilogram | kg | Medium things — a bag of rice, a person |
| Quintal | q | Heavy things — a large sack of grain |
| Tonne | t | Very heavy things — a truck, an elephant |

---

## Relationships Between Units

```
1,000 mg = 1 g
1,000 g  = 1 kg
100 kg   = 1 quintal
10 quintals = 1 tonne
1,000 kg = 1 tonne
```

> **From tiny to big:** mg → g → kg → quintal → tonne

---

## Converting Between Grams and Kilograms

**1 kg = 1,000 g**

### Examples:
- 3 kg 500 g = 3,000 g + 500 g = **3,500 g**
- 5 kg 50 g = 5,000 g + 50 g = **5,050 g** (not 5,500 g!)
- 7 kg 67 g = **7,067 g**
- 10,760 g = 10 kg 760 g
- 4,080 g = 4 kg 80 g

> **Common mistake:** 5 kg 50 g ≠ 5,500 g. Be careful! 50 g is 50 g, not 500 g. Write it as 5,050 g.

---

## Milligrams — For Very Light Things

**1 g = 1,000 mg**

Milligrams are used for:
- Weight of an **ant** (1–5 mg)
- **Medicine tablets** (like 500 mg paracetamol)
- **Gold ornaments** (4 g 100 mg = 4,100 mg)

### Converting:
- 4 g 100 mg = 4,000 mg + 100 mg = **4,100 mg**
- A 5 g sugar sachet = **5,000 mg**

---

## Quintals and Tonnes — For Very Heavy Things

| Unit conversion | Example |
|----------------|---------|
| 100 kg = 1 quintal | A large sack of grain |
| 1,000 kg = 1 tonne | A small car |
| 5,000 kg = 5 tonnes | A loaded truck |

---

## Comparing Weights

To compare weights, convert them to the **same unit** first.

**Example:** Which is heavier — 1 kg 600 g or 1,700 g?
- 1 kg 600 g = 1,600 g
- 1,600 g < 1,700 g
- So 1 kg 600 g **<** 1,700 g

**Example:** Compare 10 kg 35 g and 10,035 g.
- 10 kg 35 g = 10,035 g
- They are **equal**!

---

## Reading a Weighing Scale

Weighing scales come in different types:
- **Spring scales** with a dial and pointer
- **Digital scales** that show the number directly
- **Balance scales** where you compare with known weights

Always check:
1. What is the **maximum** the scale can measure?
2. What is each **small mark** worth (the graduation)?
3. Make sure the scale shows **zero** before you start!

---

## Adding and Subtracting Weights

### Method 1: Add like units separately

**Example:** 2 kg 500 g + 1 kg 750 g
- kg: 2 + 1 = 3 kg
- g: 500 + 750 = 1,250 g = 1 kg 250 g
- Total: 3 + 1 kg and 250 g = **4 kg 250 g**

### Method 2: Convert everything to grams

**Example:** 2 kg 500 g − 1 kg 750 g
- 2,500 g − 1,750 g = **750 g**

---

## Multiplying and Dividing Weights

### Multiplying:
**Example:** A sack of potatoes weighs 10 kg 500 g. The farmer has 4 sacks.
- 4 × 10 kg = 40 kg
- 4 × 500 g = 2,000 g = 2 kg
- Total: **42 kg**

### Dividing:
**Example:** 4 kg 800 g of nuts shared equally into 4 boxes.
- 4 kg ÷ 4 = 1 kg per box
- 800 g ÷ 4 = 200 g per box
- Each box: **1 kg 200 g**

## Measuring Capacity

**Capacity** tells us how much liquid a container can hold.

| Unit | Short form | Used for |
|------|-----------|----------|
| Millilitre | ml | Small amounts — a spoon of medicine, a cup |
| Litre | l | Larger amounts — a bottle of water, a bucket |

**1 litre = 1,000 ml**

### Converting:
- 2 l = 2,000 ml
- 1 l 400 ml = 1,400 ml (not 1,040 ml!)
- 3 l 8 ml = 3,008 ml
- 14,075 ml = 14 l 75 ml

> **Common mistake:** 1 l 400 ml = 1,400 ml, not 1,040 ml. Remember: 1 l = 1,000 ml, so add 1,000 + 400 = 1,400 ml.

---

## Adding and Subtracting Capacities

Works the same way as weight!

**Example:** 2 l 500 ml + 2 l 800 ml
- l: 2 + 2 = 4 l
- ml: 500 + 800 = 1,300 ml = 1 l 300 ml
- Total: **5 l 300 ml**

**Example:** 9 l − 5 l 300 ml
- Convert: 9,000 ml − 5,300 ml = **3,700 ml = 3 l 700 ml**

## Real-Life Problems

### Grocery Shopping:
Rathna bought 2 kg 500 g rice for daily use and 1 kg 750 g extra for Pongal.
Total: 2 kg 500 g + 1 kg 750 g = **4 kg 250 g**

### Water Bottles:
Ramiz brings a 500 ml bottle to school and drinks 2 full bottles.
Total: 500 ml + 500 ml = 1,000 ml = **1 litre**

### Petrol Pump:
- A truck fills 500 litres × 3 trucks = 1,500 litres
- A bus fills 300 litres × 6 buses = 1,800 litres
- A car fills 50 litres × 10 cars = 500 litres

### The King's Weight:
A king donates wheat equal to **10 times his weight** on his birthday. If he donated 800 kg, his weight = 800 ÷ 10 = **80 kg**.

## Key Words to Remember

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Milligram (mg)** | A very tiny unit of weight; 1,000 mg = 1 g |
| **Gram (g)** | A small unit of weight; 1,000 g = 1 kg |
| **Kilogram (kg)** | A standard unit of weight; 100 kg = 1 quintal |
| **Quintal** | A large unit of weight; 10 quintals = 1 tonne |
| **Tonne** | The largest common unit of weight; 1 tonne = 1,000 kg |
| **Millilitre (ml)** | A small unit of capacity; 1,000 ml = 1 litre |
| **Litre (l)** | A standard unit of capacity for liquids |
| **Capacity** | The maximum amount of liquid a container can hold |
