## A Special Day -- Republic Day

Every year on **26 January**, schools across India celebrate **Republic Day**. Students gather around the flagpole, the tricolour unfurls with the sound of drums and claps, and everyone sings the **National Anthem** with pride.

But why is 26 January so special?

- India became **independent on 15 August 1947**. Many brave freedom fighters struggled hard to make India free.
- After independence, the country needed a set of rules to run smoothly and keep everyone safe and happy.
- On **26 January 1950**, India adopted its **Constitution** -- a special book of rules for the country.
- That is why we celebrate **Republic Day** every year on this date.

> The Republic Day parade in New Delhi is a grand event with fighter jets, state tableaux (floats), cultural programmes, and the Indian Defence Forces -- the Army, Navy, Air Force, and paramilitary forces.

## Our National Flag

The Indian National Flag -- our **tricolour** -- has three horizontal bands and a special symbol in the centre:

| **Part of Flag** | **Colour** | **Meaning** |
|---|---|---|
| Top band | **Saffron** | Strength and courage |
| Middle band | **White** | Peace and truth |
| Bottom band | **Green** | Growth and prosperity |
| Centre symbol | **Blue Ashoka Chakra** | Duty (dharma) -- has 24 spokes |

## Finding India in Currency Notes

Have you ever looked closely at an Indian currency note? There is so much to discover!

**What you can find on a currency note:**
- The image of **Mahatma Gandhi** (and a second hidden image visible when held up to light!)
- The **value** of the note and its **colour**
- Text written in **multiple languages** -- India has many languages!
- Images of famous **monuments** from different states
- The **currency symbol** of India
- Small **raised prints and symbols** that help visually impaired people identify the note
- Tiny round spectacles -- the symbol of the **Swachh Bharat Abhiyan** (Clean India Mission), inspired by Gandhi ji's spectacles

> **Did you know?** The **MANI app** helps people with visual impairment identify different currency notes by listening to sounds and feeling vibrations.

**Monuments on currency notes:**

| **Note Value** | **Monument** |
|---|---|
| 10 rupees | Sun Temple, Konark (Odisha) |
| 20 rupees | Ellora Caves |
| 50 rupees | Hampi with Chariot |
| 100 rupees | Rani ki Vav |
| 200 rupees | Sanchi Stupa |
| 500 rupees | Red Fort |

## The National Emblem

The **National Emblem of India** shows **three lions** standing on a circular platform. It represents **strength, courage, and confidence**. Below the lions, you can see the **Ashoka Chakra**. You will find this emblem on many official documents and even on your **Aadhaar card**.

> **Did you know?** Lions once roamed freely across vast areas of India. Today, they are found only in the **Gir forests of Gujarat**. About 150 years ago, only a few dozen lions were left. But thanks to great conservation efforts, India now has **several hundred lions** in the wild!

## India's Many Languages

India is one of the most **multilingual countries** in the world. More than **a thousand languages** are spoken across our country! Each language has its own rich history, songs, and stories.

The same word can sound very different in different languages. For example, the word for "mango":

| **Language** | **Word for Mango** |
|---|---|
| Tamil | Maampalam |
| Telugu | Mamidi |
| Hindi | Aam |
| Marathi | Amba |
| Bengali | Aam |

> **Did you know?** There are mobile apps that can translate one language to another. We can use them to learn and understand many languages!

## Diversity in Clothing and Headgear

People across India wear different types of clothing and headgear, each telling a story about the region they come from:

- In **Rajasthan**, men wear a colourful **saafa** or **pagri** (turban)
- In **Himachal Pradesh**, people wear a traditional **topi** (cap)
- Headgear can show where someone is from, what they believe in, or what they do for a living

> **Safety headgear:** Helmets worn by soldiers, construction workers, and motorcyclists protect them from injuries. Wearing a helmet on a two-wheeler is **compulsory** in India!

**Some traditional Indian drums:**
- Tabla, Mridangam, Pakhawaj, Dholak, Dhol, Khol, Idakka, Nagara, Thavil, Khanjira, Dhama

**Classical dance forms from different states:**

- Kathak (Uttar Pradesh)
- Odissi (Odisha)
- Kathakali (Kerala)
- Manipuri (Manipur)
- Kuchipudi (Andhra Pradesh)

> India is like a forest -- just as a forest is strong because it has many different types of plants and trees that support each other, India is strong because of its **diversity**!

## The Spirit of Togetherness

Despite all our differences in languages, clothing, food, religions, and traditions, India is a shining example of **togetherness**. We live together, helping and supporting each other.

India has a rich history and culture, including **Ayurveda, yoga, and meditation**. At the same time, India is building an exciting future:
- **Scientists** launching rockets (like Chandrayaan missions)
- **Farmers** using technology for better crops
- **UPI** -- the world's largest digital payment system
- **Fast trains** connecting cities
- **Aadhaar** -- the world's largest digital ID system

> India has the **longest written Constitution** of any country in the world. It is also the world's **largest democracy**!

> India runs the **world's largest postal network** and has the **second largest road network** in the world.
