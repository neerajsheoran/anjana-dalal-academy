## Why Water Matters

Water is one of the most important things on Earth. Every living being -- people, animals, birds, and plants -- needs water to survive. We use water for drinking, cooking, bathing, growing crops, and so many daily activities. Without water, life would not be possible!

> Think of it this way: if all the water on Earth could fit inside a glass (200 ml), the freshwater we can actually use would be only about a teaspoon (5 ml)!

Most of the Earth's surface is covered with water, but the **majority of it is salty ocean water**. We cannot drink salty water directly. Only a very small part of Earth's water is **freshwater** -- the kind we need for drinking and farming.

> **Did you know?** Wular Lake in Jammu and Kashmir is one of the largest freshwater lakes in Asia. It helps control river flow and prevents floods.

**Ocean water** is too salty to drink, but it is useful in other ways. For example, in Gujarat, there are vast **salt pans** where seawater is dried under the sun to collect salt. Gujarat is one of the largest salt-producing areas in India!

## Three Forms of Water

Water is special because it can exist in **three different forms**:

| **Form** | **State** | **Example** |
|---|---|---|
| Liquid | Flows freely | Rain, river water, tap water |
| Solid | Hard and cold | Ice, snow, glaciers |
| Gas (Vapour) | Invisible in air | Steam from boiling water |

Water can change from one form to another:
- **Ice melts** and becomes liquid water when it gets warm.
- **Water boils** and becomes steam (water vapour) when heated.
- **Water vapour cools** and turns back into tiny water droplets.

## The Water Cycle

Have you ever wondered where rain comes from? Water keeps moving in a big circle called the **water cycle**. Here is how it works:

1. The **Sun heats up** water in rivers, lakes, and oceans.
2. This water turns into **water vapour** and rises into the air.
3. High up in the sky, the vapour **cools down** and forms tiny droplets that make **clouds**.
4. When the clouds get heavy, the water falls back as **rain, snow, or hail**.
5. This water flows into rivers, lakes, and oceans -- and the cycle starts again!

> The water cycle is a **never-ending journey**. The same water keeps going around and around in nature.

## Groundwater

When rain falls on the ground, some of it **seeps through the soil** and flows down through layers of soil and rock. This water gets stored deep underground and is called **groundwater**.

We use **wells, borewells, tube wells, and handpumps** to bring this water up for our use.

**How does groundwater get recharged?**
- Open, uncovered land allows rainwater to soak into the ground.
- **Soak pits, ponds, and planting trees** help rainwater go back underground.

> **Problem in cities:** In cities with lots of concrete roads and buildings, rainwater cannot seep into the ground easily. This stops groundwater from being recharged.

> **Did you know?** In Rajasthan, people build traditional small earthen dams called **Johads** to collect rainwater and recharge groundwater. Hiware Bazar in Maharashtra is a famous water-positive village thanks to community-led rainwater harvesting!

## Surface Water and How Rivers Flow

Rivers, ponds, and lakes are natural sources of freshwater on the surface. A river usually begins its journey **high up in the mountains** and flows downward across the land.

Water always flows from **higher ground to lower ground**. It follows the shape of the land -- curving around hills and collecting in low areas to form lakes.

> **Amazing fact:** The Luni river in Rajasthan is the only major Indian river that does not drain into any sea. It ends up in the marshy lands of the Rann of Kutch in Gujarat!

Some of India's rivers flow towards the **Bay of Bengal** (on the east) and some towards the **Arabian Sea** (on the west). The direction depends on the shape of the land and the mountain ranges.

## Life in Water

Freshwater bodies like ponds, lakes, and rivers are home to a wonderful variety of **plants and animals**.

**Animals found in and around water:**

| **Creature** | **Special Feature** |
|---|---|
| Fish | Have fins to swim |
| Frog | Can live on land and in water |
| Freshwater turtle | Soft shell, lives in ponds and rivers |
| Dragonfly | Flies fast, lays eggs near water, eats mosquitoes |
| Pond heron | Long legs, stands still to catch fish |
| Water snake | Harmless, eats fish and frogs |

**Plants found in water:**

| **Plant Type** | **Example** |
|---|---|
| Free-floating | Water hyacinth |
| Rooted floating (flowers on surface) | Lotus, water lily |
| Edge plants (grow along pond edges) | Reeds |

> **Fun fact:** The Fishing Cat, found in India's wetlands, has partly webbed paws that make it an excellent swimmer. It dives right into the water to catch fish!

> **Did you know?** The Keibul Lamjao National Park in Manipur is the **only floating national park** in the world. It has unique floating islands called **phumdis** and is home to the endangered Sangai deer.

## Water Plants Have Special Features

Plants that live in water have special features to survive:
- **Lotus and water lily** have broad, flat leaves that float on the surface.
- Many water plants have a **waxy coating** on their leaves. This coating is waterproof and keeps too much water from getting inside the plant.

## Food Chains in Water

Living things in water depend on each other for food. Small fish eat tiny plants and insects. Bigger fish eat the smaller fish. Birds like herons catch fish. If one type of animal disappears, it affects all the others. This chain of who-eats-whom is called a **food chain**.

## Why We Must Save Water

Freshwater is **limited and precious**. Here are some simple things you can do every day:

- **Turn off the tap** while brushing your teeth.
- **Do not waste** drinking water.
- **Fix leaking taps** with the help of adults.
- **Reuse water** -- water left from washing fruits or vegetables can be used for watering plants.
- **Remind your family and friends** to save water too.

> **Remember: Every drop counts!**

> India's first water museum -- the **Jal Shakti Museum** in New Delhi -- celebrates India's water heritage and inspires everyone to conserve water.
