## What is Energy?

Look around you. A fan spins, a bulb glows, a car moves, food gets cooked, and birds fly through the sky. Something makes all of this happen -- that something is **energy**.

> **Energy** is what makes things move, light up, produce sound, do work, and change temperature.

We use energy every single day in so many ways, sometimes without even noticing it! Let us explore how energy works.

## Energy is Everywhere

Energy makes many things possible around us:

| **What Happens** | **Type of Energy Used** |
|---|---|
| A ball rolls across the ground | Movement energy |
| A bulb lights up a room | Light energy |
| A drum makes a sound | Sound energy |
| A stove heats up food | Heat energy |
| A fridge keeps food cold | Electrical energy |

## Sources of Energy

Everything that moves, lights up, or makes a sound needs energy from somewhere. Let us look at where energy comes from.

### Energy from Food

When you eat breakfast, your body gets **energy from food**. This energy lets you run, play, think, and do all your activities throughout the day.

- When you **skip a meal**, you might feel tired and weak.
- When you **eat properly**, you feel active and ready to play.

Animals get their energy from food too -- a dog running, a bird flying, a fish swimming -- all of them need food to get energy.

> **Did you know?** Our brain uses energy even when we are just sitting, sleeping, or thinking! Our body also **stores energy** for future use, which is why we can still walk or run even if we skip one meal.

### Energy from Fuel

Vehicles like cars, scooters, buses, and trucks run on **fuel** -- petrol, diesel, or CNG. Fuel is a source of energy, just like food is for us.

We use fuel at home too:
- **Cooking gas (LPG)** is used in most kitchens
- In some villages, people use **wood or coal** to cook food

> **Be careful!** Burning wood or coal creates **smoke and pollution**. This is not good for our health or the environment. Cleaner fuels like LPG or electric stoves are better options.

### Energy from Electricity

Think about all the things in your home that need electricity to work:

| **Device** | **What It Does** | **Type of Energy** |
|---|---|---|
| Fan | Blows air | Movement |
| Bulb / Tube light | Gives light | Light |
| Radio / Speaker | Makes sound | Sound |
| Iron / Heater | Makes things hot | Heat |
| Refrigerator | Keeps things cold | Cooling |
| Washing machine | Washes clothes | Movement |

Electricity is **very useful**, but it can also be **dangerous** if not used carefully.

**Safety rules for electricity:**
- **Never touch** wires or plugs with wet hands
- **Do not put** fingers, pens, or sticks into electrical sockets
- **Stay away** from broken wires or fallen electric poles and tell an adult immediately
- **Do not play** near electric boxes or transformers
- If something looks unsafe, **do not try to fix it yourself** -- always tell an adult

> **What is energy efficiency?** It means using **less energy** to do the same work. For example, **LED bulbs** give the same light as old bulbs but use much less electricity. Switching to LEDs helps save energy!

## Where Does Electricity Come From?

Most of the electricity we use comes from **burning fuels like coal** in power stations. But burning coal creates a lot of **smoke and harmful gases** that pollute the air.

Fortunately, there are **cleaner ways** to make electricity!

## Clean Energy -- From Sun, Wind, and Water

Nature gives us wonderful sources of energy that **do not cause pollution**:

### Solar Energy (From the Sun)

The Sun gives us **light and heat energy** every day. We use solar energy in many ways:
- **Drying clothes** in the sunlight
- **Drying papads and chillies** under the sun
- **Solar panels** on rooftops capture sunlight and turn it into electricity
- **Solar water heaters** warm water without using gas or electricity

> **Did you know?** The **Bhadla Solar Park** in Rajasthan is one of the largest solar power plants in the world! The **Cochin International Airport** in Kerala is the world's first airport to run entirely on solar energy.

### Wind Energy (From the Wind)

Moving air (wind) has energy. We use wind energy in these ways:
- **Windmills** spin with the wind to make electricity
- Long ago, ships used large **sails** to travel across oceans using wind power
- Ancient traders from Gujarat and Tamil Nadu sailed to Africa, Arabia, and Southeast Asia using wind

### Water Energy (From Flowing Water)

Fast-moving rivers or waterfalls have a lot of energy. We use this energy to:
- Generate **hydroelectricity** by building dams
- Run **watermills** (called **gharaats** in hilly areas) to grind grains

> Electricity made from the Sun, wind, and water is called **clean energy** because it does not pollute the planet with smoke or waste.

> **Amazing fact:** Even vehicles can run on electricity! Electric cars and buses are becoming more common in India.

## Energy All Around Us -- A Summary

| **Source of Energy** | **Type of Energy** | **Example** |
|---|---|---|
| Sun | Heat and Light | Drying clothes, solar panels |
| Wind | Movement | Windmills, sailing ships |
| Water | Movement | Hydroelectric dams, watermills |
| Food | Chemical energy | Our body running and playing |
| Fuel (petrol, diesel, gas) | Heat and movement | Vehicles, cooking |
| Electricity | Light, heat, sound, movement | Fans, bulbs, fridges |

## Smart Energy Use in India

People in India have been using energy wisely for thousands of years:

- **Vaastu Shastra**, the ancient Indian system of design, guides the placement of rooms, doors, and windows to make the best use of sunlight and wind. Courtyards and open spaces help homes stay bright and airy.
- In **Himachal Pradesh, Uttarakhand, and Ladakh**, people sometimes keep cattle on the ground floor. The heat from the animals helps keep the rooms above warm during winter -- a clever way to stay warm without extra firewood!
- **Traditional houses** in India had small windows and thick walls. This kept the house cool in summer and warm in winter.

> **Did you know?** The Sun gives more energy to Earth in **one hour** than all humans on Earth use in a **whole year**!

## Storing Energy

Energy can also be **stored** for use later:
- **Batteries** store energy for torches, toys, and remote controls
- Our **body stores energy** from food for future use
- **Dams** store water that can be used to make electricity when needed

## Save Energy Every Day

Here are simple ways you can save energy at home and school:

- **Switch off** lights and fans when leaving a room
- Use **LED bulbs** instead of old bulbs
- Use **natural light** during the day -- open windows and curtains
- **Walk or cycle** for short distances instead of using vehicles
- Choose **clean energy** sources whenever possible

> Energy is what makes our world active and alive. Let us use it wisely and choose clean sources of energy to protect our beautiful planet!
