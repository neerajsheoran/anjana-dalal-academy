## Meet the Godavari

Imagine a river that could tell its own story! The **Godavari** is one of India's greatest rivers. It begins its journey high up in the **Western Ghats** at a place called **Trimbakeshwar in Maharashtra**.

At first, the Godavari is just a small stream. But as it flows across the land, rainwater and other smaller streams join it, making it wider and stronger. These smaller streams that join a river are called **tributaries**.

The Godavari travels a massive **1,465 kilometres** before finally meeting the **Bay of Bengal**. It is the **second longest river in India**, after the Ganga.

> The Godavari is also called **"Dakshina Ganga"** (Ganga of the South) because it flows through southern India and is considered sacred.

## The Godavari's Journey on the Map

The Godavari flows through several states and many important places:

| **Feature** | **Details** |
|---|---|
| **Origin** | Brahmagiri Hills, Trimbakeshwar, Maharashtra |
| **States it flows through** | Maharashtra, Telangana, Chhattisgarh, Odisha, Andhra Pradesh |
| **Major tributaries** | Manjira, Indravati, Sabari |
| **Dams** | Sriram Sagar Project (SRSP), Polavaram Dam |
| **Wildlife areas** | Kawal Tiger Reserve, Pench National Park, Coringa Wildlife Sanctuary |
| **Ends at** | Bay of Bengal (Andhra Pradesh) |

## Perennial and Seasonal Rivers

The Godavari flows **all year round**, which makes it a **perennial river**. Some other rivers flow only during the rainy season when they get water from the monsoon. These are called **seasonal rivers**.

## Life Along the Godavari

The Godavari passes through rich forests full of amazing wildlife including **tigers, barking deer, the Indian golden gecko**, and the famous **red sandalwood trees**.

When the Godavari reaches the sea, it spreads out into many small streams that form a **delta**. Here, freshwater meets salty seawater. This area is called the **Coringa Mangrove Forests** -- a very special habitat where the river's journey ends.

## How Rivers Support People

Since ancient times, people have built their homes near rivers. Can you guess why? Rivers help us in so many ways:

- **Drinking water** for towns and villages
- **Farming** -- water from rivers is used to irrigate fields and grow crops like rice
- **Cooking and washing** in daily life
- **Factories** use river water to make many products
- **Fishing** provides food and livelihood
- **Transport** -- boats carry people and goods
- **Ecotourism** -- visiting beautiful natural places along rivers

> Water from rivers is carried through **pipes and canals** and shared by many people.

## Dams -- Storing Water, Changing Flow

There are more than **900 dams** built on the Godavari! Dams are like giant walls that block the river's flow and store water in a large area called a **reservoir**.

**Why are dams useful?**
- They store water so people can use it during **dry seasons**
- They help **grow crops** by providing water for irrigation
- They help make **electricity** (hydropower)

**But dams also cause problems:**
- They **flood large areas** of land that were once home to animals and people
- Many families have to **leave their homes** and move elsewhere
- Forests and wildlife habitats can be destroyed

> **Think about it:** When you open a tap at home, it is hard to imagine the long journey that water has taken. That is why we must use water wisely and never waste it!

> **Did you know?** The Hirakud Dam in Odisha is one of the longest earthen dams in the world, stretching over 25 kilometres!

## River Pollution

As more people and factories come up near rivers, the water often gets polluted. Here is what harms our rivers:

- **Plastic waste** and garbage thrown into the water
- **Dirty water from factories** (chemical waste)
- **Soapy water** from homes
- **Fertilisers** from farms that wash into the river

When fertilisers enter the water, they make tiny water plants grow very fast. These plants cover the river's surface like a **"green blanket"**. This blocks sunlight and makes it hard for fish to breathe. The water becomes unsafe for everyone.

> Even when river water **looks clean**, it might still carry hidden harmful chemicals.

> **Good news:** The **Namami Gange programme** is helping clean the Ganga river. Along the Godavari too, people are working hard to stop waste from entering the water.

## When a River Floods

Most of the time, rivers flow gently. But when there is **too much rain**, a river can overflow its banks and flood the land around it.

**Floods can cause:**

| **Problem** | **What It Affects** |
|---|---|
| Dirty water | Safe drinking |
| Broken roads | Travel and transport |
| Lost crops | Food supply |
| No electricity | Lights, fans, and phones |
| Spread of diseases | People's health |
| Damaged homes | Shelter for people |
| Schools shut | Learning |

**Trees help prevent floods!** Trees slow down rainwater and help the soil soak it up. When forests are cut down, water runs off the land faster and causes worse flooding.

**Flood safety tips:**
- Stay informed through local news
- Prepare an emergency bag with food, medicines, and a flashlight
- Move to higher ground when floods come
- Never walk or drive through flood water
- Follow evacuation orders from authorities

## When a River Runs Dry

Too little water is also a serious problem. When there is no rain for a long time, rivers can dry up, and people face a **water crisis**.

> **Real example:** In 2019, Chennai had to run **special water trains** from other districts because its reservoirs dried up. In 2022, heavy rains in Bengaluru flooded homes and roads. Both too much and too little water can cause big problems!

## Saving Our Rivers

Every small action can make a big difference:

- **Use water wisely** -- do not waste it
- **Turn off the tap** while brushing your teeth
- **Fix leaking taps** with help from adults
- **Reuse clean water** (like water from washing vegetables) for watering plants
- **Do not throw waste** into rivers or water bodies
- **Plant trees** near water bodies
- **Remind your family and friends** to save water

> **Every drop counts!** The **National Water Mission** works to help people across India manage and use water wisely.

> **Amazing fact:** The Ganges river dolphin, India's **national aquatic animal**, is blind and uses sound to find its way through the water!
