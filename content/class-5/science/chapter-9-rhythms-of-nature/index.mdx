## Everything Changes

Have you noticed how much you have changed since you were in Grade 3? Maybe you are taller now, your hair is different, you have new friends, and you have learned so many new things!

Changes are happening **all the time** -- to us and to the world around us. Some changes take years, some happen in days, and some take just minutes or seconds!

**Examples of change:**
- You **grow taller** each year
- Leaves **fall off trees** and grow again
- Flowers **bloom**, dry up, and bloom again
- The sky **changes colour** from dawn to dusk
- The sun appears to **rise and set** every day

## Day and Night

One of the most basic rhythms of nature is the change from **day to night** and back to day again.

### What Causes Day and Night?

It may seem like the Sun moves across the sky -- rising in the east and setting in the west. But actually, **the Sun stays in one place**. It is the **Earth that rotates** (spins like a top).

Here is how it works:
1. The Earth is shaped like a ball (a sphere).
2. It keeps **spinning on its axis** -- one full spin takes about **24 hours** (one day).
3. The side of the Earth facing the Sun has **day**.
4. The side facing away from the Sun has **night**.
5. As the Earth keeps spinning, different parts take turns facing the Sun.

> You can understand this with a simple experiment: shine a torch (representing the Sun) on a globe. The lit side has day, and the dark side has night. Slowly rotate the globe, and you will see how day and night happen!

> **Did you know?** A **globe** is a model that represents our Earth. The blue parts are seas and oceans -- they cover about **three-fourths** of the Earth. The remaining parts are land masses where all the countries are.

> When it is **daytime in India**, countries on the opposite side of the Earth have nighttime!

> **Dong** in Arunachal Pradesh is known as India's **"First Village of the Sunrise"** -- it is the very first place where the sun's rays touch Indian land each morning.

## Changes Across the Year -- Seasons

Just as day changes into night, nature also changes **across the year** in a repeating pattern. The weather warms up, gets hot, then it rains, then it gets cooler, and then it warms up again. This repeating pattern is called **seasons**.

### India's Six Seasons

In India, we experience **six seasons** based on how nature changes every couple of months:

| **Season** | **Sanskrit Name** | **Approximate Months** | **What Happens** |
|---|---|---|---|
| Spring | **Vasanta** | February - March | Flowers bloom, pleasant weather |
| Summer | **Grishma** | April - June | Hot and dry, mangoes ripen |
| Monsoon | **Varsha** | July - August | Heavy rains, everything turns green |
| Autumn | **Sharad** | September - October | Clear skies, mild weather |
| Pre-winter | **Hemant** | November - December | Cool mornings and evenings |
| Winter | **Shishir** | January - February | Cold weather, foggy mornings |

> **Important:** Seasons are experienced differently in different parts of India. Winter in **Kerala** feels mild and pleasant, while winter in the **Kashmir Valley** brings heavy snowfall. Monsoon in **Assam** brings flooding rains, while **Rajasthan** gets much less rain.

## How Seasons Affect Life Around Us

Seasons shape almost everything in our lives:

### Plants Change with Seasons

- In **spring**, new leaves and flowers appear on trees
- In **summer**, many fruits like mango and lychee ripen
- In **monsoon**, the rain makes everything lush and green
- In **autumn**, leaves change colour and start falling
- In **winter**, many trees shed their leaves and look bare

### Animals Respond to Seasons

- In the **monsoon**, frogs come out and croak loudly
- **Snakes** come out of flooded burrows during the rains -- this is why the festival of **Nag Panchami** is celebrated to show respect to nature and reptiles
- In **winter**, many birds migrate to warmer places
- When **ants carry their eggs to higher ground**, it is a natural sign that rain is coming soon!

> **Did you know?** In Odisha, when the **koel (cuckoo)** bird sings, farmers know the rains are coming. That is when they start planting seeds!

### Farming and Seasons

Farmers grow **different crops in different seasons** because some crops need heat, while others need cold or rain:

| **Season** | **Types of Crops** | **Examples** |
|---|---|---|
| Summer (Kharif preparation) | Need rain to grow | Rice, maize, cotton |
| Winter (Rabi) | Need cooler weather | Wheat, mustard, peas |
| Monsoon (Kharif) | Need lots of water | Rice, sugarcane, jute |

### Festivals and Seasons

Many of India's festivals are connected to seasons and harvests:

| **Festival** | **Season / Connection** |
|---|---|
| **Pongal / Makar Sankranti** | Winter -- harvest festival, celebrates the new crop |
| **Holi** | Spring -- celebrates the arrival of spring and blooming flowers |
| **Baisakhi / Gudi Padwa / Vishu / Rongali Bihu** | New year and spring harvest festivals |
| **Diwali** | Autumn -- celebrated after crops are harvested |

> India celebrates over **1,000 festivals** throughout the year, making it one of the most festive countries in the world!

### What We Eat and Wear Changes Too

- In **summer**, we eat cooling foods like watermelon, curd, and buttermilk, and wear light cotton clothes
- In **monsoon**, we enjoy hot pakoras and chai, and carry umbrellas and raincoats
- In **winter**, we eat warming foods like gajar ka halwa and dry fruits, and wear sweaters and shawls

## Keeping a Nature Journal

One of the best ways to notice and understand these patterns is to keep a **Seasons Journal** -- a notebook where you record what you observe in nature throughout the year.

**What to observe and record:**

| **Theme** | **What to Look For** |
|---|---|
| **Plant life** | Are trees flowering or fruiting? Are leaves changing colour or falling? |
| **Birds and animals** | What birds and insects do you see? Are they building nests? Are any new animals around? |
| **Air, heat, and light** | What is the weather like? Is it sunny, cloudy, rainy, or foggy? What time does the sun rise and set? |
| **Water** | Is there rain? Are puddles forming? Are ponds full or drying up? |
| **Human activities** | What are people wearing? What food are they eating? Which festivals are being celebrated? |

## The Rhythm of Life

Seasons are not just changes in weather -- they shape **everything around us**. From the crops we harvest to the festivals we celebrate, seasons give **rhythm to our lives**.

> Seasons teach us that **change is natural and necessary**. Just like day follows night, summer follows spring, and rain follows the heat. These patterns repeat year after year.

By observing and understanding seasons, we understand the **cycles of life**. As you step out each day, you are witnessing nature's rhythm unfolding -- **one season at a time**.

> India hosts both the **wettest place on Earth** -- Mawsynram in Meghalaya -- and one of the **driest** -- the Thar Desert. This shows the amazing diversity of our country's climate!
