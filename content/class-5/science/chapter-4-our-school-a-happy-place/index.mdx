## What Makes School a Happy Place?

School is a place where we learn new things, make friends, play games, and grow together. But a truly happy school is also a place that is **clean, safe, green, and caring** -- for students, teachers, and the environment.

Two students named Aasha and Mohit dreamed of turning their school into a **"green school."** But what does that mean?

> A **green school** is a school where everyone works together to **manage waste, save water, use electricity carefully**, and make the campus greener by **planting more trees** and taking care of them.

## The Swachh Bharat Swachh Vidyalaya Mission

The Government of India started the **Swachh Bharat Swachh Vidyalaya** (Clean India Clean Schools) programme. It focuses on:
- Clean toilets in every school
- Safe drinking water
- Proper waste management
- Teaching students about hygiene

> Every child has the right to study in a **clean and safe environment**!

> **Did you know?** Mawlynnong in Meghalaya is celebrated as **"Asia's Cleanest Village."** The people there keep their village spotlessly clean!

## Waste Management

We create waste every day. The smart way to handle it is by **separating waste** into different types:

| **Type** | **Bin Colour** | **Examples** |
|---|---|---|
| **Wet waste** | Green bin | Food peels, leftovers, tea leaves |
| **Dry waste** | Blue bin | Paper, plastic bottles, wrappers |

**What about recycling?**

Many things we throw away can be **reused or recycled**. Old newspapers, metal cans, glass bottles, and plastic items are often collected by **recyclers** (sometimes called kabadiwalas). These items are taken to special places where they are turned into new things.

> **Important:** We should always respect the hard work of sanitation workers and recyclers. Their work keeps our surroundings clean and healthy.

**What happens to wet waste?**

Wet waste like vegetable peels and leftover food can be turned into **compost** -- a natural fertiliser for plants. Many schools and homes have a **compost pit** where wet waste is collected and allowed to break down naturally.

## Keeping Cool Without Wasting Energy

Have you noticed that some rooms feel **warmer** than others? Here are some smart ways to keep a place cool:

- **Trees** provide shade and keep the air cool around them
- **Windows** let in natural light and fresh breeze
- **White roofs** reflect the sun's heat and keep buildings cooler
- **Dark surfaces** absorb heat and make things warmer

> **Try this:** Paint one tile white and another black. Keep both under the sun. After some time, touch them. The white one will feel **much cooler** than the black one!

> **Did you know?** Clay pots (**matkas**) are used to cool drinking water in many parts of India. They do not need any electricity -- a truly smart and eco-friendly design!

> In Ladakh, students help make **"ice stupas"** -- tall ice towers that slowly melt in summer to water school gardens!

## Saving Water at School

Water is a precious treasure, and even a small dripping tap can waste a huge amount of water over time.

**Simple ways to save water at school:**
- Report **leaking taps** immediately
- Turn off taps properly after use
- Keep **water tanks clean and covered**
- Collect **rainwater** for use in gardens
- Make sure there are no wet places that could **breed mosquitoes**

> The **Jal Shakti Abhiyan** is a government campaign to save and protect water. It encourages everyone to plant trees, harvest rainwater, and use water wisely.

## Greenery Around Us

Trees are extremely important for our school and surroundings:

- They keep the **air clean** by taking in carbon dioxide and giving out oxygen
- They provide **shade** and keep the area cool
- They give **food** to birds, insects, and animals
- They are **homes** for many living beings

> Water kept under a tree stays **cooler** than water kept under the sun. Trees make a real difference to temperature!

## Traffic Safety Near School

Many accidents happen near schools because people do not follow traffic rules. Here are some important rules:

- Always use the **pedestrian crossing** to cross the road
- **Look both ways** before crossing
- Never run across the road
- Drivers should **slow down** near schools
- Wearing a **helmet** is compulsory on two-wheelers

## Fire Safety at School

Knowing what to do during a fire is very important:

1. **Move quickly but calmly** to the nearest exit -- do not panic or run
2. **Crawl low** under smoke and cover your mouth to breathe cleaner air
3. **Do not hide** in closets or bathrooms
4. **Help others** if it is safe, but never go back inside once you are out
5. Go to the **assembly point** and stay with your group
6. **Tell a teacher** immediately if anyone is missing or injured

> Every school should have a **fire extinguisher** and a clear **emergency exit plan**. Practice fire drills regularly!

## Being Kind and Respectful

A happy school is not just about clean buildings and green gardens. It is also about how we **treat each other**.

**What makes a school truly happy:**
- **Waiting for your turn** in queues
- Using **kind words** with everyone
- **Helping** someone who is new or shy
- **Not teasing or bullying** others
- Keeping **shared spaces clean** -- classrooms, playgrounds, and washrooms
- **Respecting** everyone -- classmates, teachers, and staff

> The way we behave shows the kind of person we are. When we are kind and polite, **everyone feels happy**.
