## The Blue Planet

![Earth as seen from space showing blue oceans, green land masses, and white clouds](earth-from-space.png)

When astronauts look at the Earth from space, they see a beautiful blue planet with no borders, no lines between countries -- just land, water, and clouds. The Earth looks like **one home** shared by all of us.

> **Group Captain Shubhanshu Shukla**, the first Indian to reach the International Space Station, said: "The Earth looks completely one, no border is visible from outside. We all are part of humanity and the Earth is our one home."

> **Wing Commander Rakesh Sharma**, the first Indian to see Earth from space, was asked how India looked from above. He replied: **"Saare Jahaan Se Achcha"** -- the best in the entire world!

## We Are All Connected

From high up in space, we cannot see cities, villages, or country borders. Nature has no boundaries -- air, water, clouds, seeds, and even animals move freely across the world.

People around the world are connected in many ways too:
- The **clothes** we wear may come from different countries
- The **food** we eat may have travelled from far away
- **Ideas, music, stories, and inventions** are shared across borders

> Everything is linked through nature, trade, travel, and the ways we care for our planet together.

## Story 1: The Travelling Birds

Have you seen **pink and black birds** flying in big groups? Those are **Rosy Starlings**!

Every **winter**, they fly thousands of kilometres from **southern Russia and Mongolia** all the way to India. Here is why they are so special:

- They enjoy India's **warm weather** during the cold months
- They help Indian **farmers** by eating pests like locusts and grasshoppers from crops
- They show us how **nature connects different countries** -- a tiny bird travelling such a huge distance!

> When animals move freely and safely across the world, it shows how deeply nature is connected across the globe.

## Story 2: Yoga -- India's Gift to the World

**Yoga** has been practised in India for more than **3,000 years**. It was a way to keep the body healthy and the mind peaceful. Over time, travellers, scholars, and teachers from India shared yoga with the rest of the world.

Today, yoga is practised in **almost every country** on Earth!

> In 2014, the **United Nations** declared **21 June** as the **International Day of Yoga**, with millions of people around the world doing yoga together!

| **What Yoga Came From** | **India** |
|---|---|
| **Where it is practised now** | Almost every country in the world |
| **International Day of Yoga** | 21 June |
| **How old** | More than 3,000 years |

> It is amazing that a practice that began in India is now loved all over the world!

## Story 3: The Chilli -- A Spice That Changed Our Food

Can you imagine Indian food without chillies? It is hard to believe, but **chillies did not originally grow in India**!

- Chilli plants were first found only in **South America** (near Mexico)
- About **400 to 500 years ago**, Portuguese travellers brought chillies to India
- Indians loved chillies so much that today, they are an essential part of our cooking!
- Before chillies arrived, Indians used **black pepper** to make food spicy

> Many other foods also travelled to India from far away: **potatoes, tomatoes, peanuts, and cashews** all came from the Americas!

## Story 4: Sugar -- India's Gift to the World

Just as chillies came to India, **sugar went from India to the world**!

- Long ago, people around the world used only **honey** and other natural things for sweetness
- India was the first place where people discovered how to make **jaggery** (gur) from sugarcane juice
- Over time, India also figured out how to turn jaggery into **sugar**
- This knowledge spread to other countries through **trade and travel**

> Even **rice, mangoes, and bananas** from India found their way to other lands. These gifts from India are enjoyed worldwide!

## Story 5: The Mexican Marigold in India

The bright orange and yellow **marigold** flowers that we see at every Indian wedding, temple, and Diwali celebration originally came from **Mexico**!

- In Mexico, marigolds are used during their festivals too
- The flower travelled across the world and reached India
- Indians loved its warm, bright colours so much that it became part of our culture

> It is amazing how a flower from a faraway land became such a big part of Indian celebrations!

## Story 6: Indian Cows in Brazil

Long ago, **Portuguese traders** took some Indian cows to **Brazil** in South America. These cows were strong, gave good milk, and adapted well to the Brazilian climate.

Today, more than **three-fourths of Brazil's milk** comes from three Indian cow breeds:

| **Indian Cow Breed** | **Origin in India** |
|---|---|
| **Gir** | Gujarat |
| **Kankrej** | Gujarat |
| **Ongole** | Andhra Pradesh |

These cows became so important in Brazil that their pictures appear on Brazilian **stamps and coins**!

## Vasudhaiva Kutumbakam -- The World is One Family

There is a beautiful saying from ancient India:

> **"Vasudhaiva Kutumbakam"** -- meaning **"The whole world is one family."**

All people, animals, trees, rivers, and even the air and sky belong to this family. We live together, depend on each other, and share the same home -- our Earth.

**What this means for us:**
- When we see the world as one family, we learn to live with **respect, care, and love** for one another
- We must care for the Earth because it supports **all life**
- Protecting nature means protecting ourselves -- we are all connected

## Your Address on Planet Earth

Every one of us has an address that connects us to this big planet:

- **Your name**
- **House / Building**
- **Street**
- **Village / Town / City**
- **District**
- **State / Union Territory**
- **Country: India**
- **Planet: Earth**

> **Did you know?** **DIGIPIN** is like a digital address! It gives every small place in India its own special 10-character code. It helps the postman, ambulance, or delivery person find you faster, even in small villages.

## Our Earth Needs Care

Earth is the **only planet** we know that has life. For thousands of years, it has supported people, animals, and plants. But Earth needs our care too.

**Simple ways to care for our planet:**
- **Save water** -- every drop matters
- **Plant trees** -- they clean the air and provide homes for animals
- **Reduce waste** -- reuse and recycle whenever possible
- **Use clean energy** -- solar, wind, and water power instead of coal
- **Respect all living things** -- animals, birds, plants, and insects

> Earth is not just our home -- it is a **gift** we must protect, for ourselves, for others, and for the future.

> When we care for the Earth, we are caring for each other, because we are **all part of the same family**.
