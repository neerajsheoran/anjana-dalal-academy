## 3.1 Proportionality -- A Quick Recap

When two or more related quantities change by the **same factor**, we call that a **proportional relationship**.

**Example:** Idli batter is made by mixing rice and urad dal in the ratio **2 : 1**. Viswanath uses 6 cups of rice and 3 cups of dal (6 : 3), while Puneet uses 4 cups of rice and 2 cups of dal (4 : 2). Will their idlis taste the same?

Using **cross multiplication**: 6 x 2 = 12 and 3 x 4 = 12. Since both products are equal, the ratios are proportional. The idlis will taste the same!

Two ratios a : b and c : d are proportional if:
- **a x d = b x c** (cross multiplication), or equivalently
- **a/b = c/d**

## 3.2 Ratios in Maps

Maps use a **Representative Fraction (RF)** to show the scale. For example, **1 : 60,00,000** means 1 cm on the map represents 60,00,000 cm (= 60 km) on the ground.

**How to use a map scale:**
1. Measure the distance between two places on the map using a ruler (in cm).
2. Multiply by the scale factor to get the actual geographical distance.

**Example:** If Bengaluru and Chennai are 5.5 cm apart on a map with scale 1 : 60,00,000:
- Actual distance = 5.5 x 60 = **330 km** (approximately)

### Map Making Activity

You can make a sketch of your classroom using a scale of 1 : 50 (1 cm on paper = 50 cm in real life). Mark the positions of the teacher's desk, blackboard, fans, and other objects.

## 3.3 Ratios with More than 2 Terms

Ratios can have **multiple terms** when comparing several quantities at once.

**Example:** Viswanath's spice mix uses:
- 8 spoons coriander : 4 red chillies : 2 spoons toor dal : 1 spoon fenugreek
- Ratio: **8 : 4 : 2 : 1**

If Puneet has only 2 red chillies (half of Viswanath's 4), he must halve everything:
- **4 : 2 : 1 : 0.5**

The ratios are proportional: 8 : 4 : 2 : 1 :: 4 : 2 : 1 : 0.5

When two multi-term ratios are proportional, a : b : c : d :: p : q : r : s, then:

**a/p = b/q = c/r = d/s**

### Example: Mixing Paint

To make purple paint, mix Red : Blue : White = **2 : 3 : 5**.

If you have 10 litres of white paint:
- White = 5 parts = 10 litres, so 1 part = 2 litres
- Red = 2 parts = **4 litres**
- Blue = 3 parts = **6 litres**
- Total purple paint = 4 + 6 + 10 = **20 litres**

### Example: Cement Concrete

For strong structures (pillars, beams, roofs), cement : sand : gravel = **1 : 1.5 : 3**.

With 3 bags of cement, multiply each term by 3:
- Cement : Sand : Gravel = 3 : 4.5 : 9
- Total concrete = 3 + 4.5 + 9 = **16.5 bags**

## 3.4 Dividing a Whole in a Given Ratio

To divide a quantity **x** in the ratio **a : b : c**:

1. Add the terms: a + b + c
2. Each part = x divided by (a + b + c)
3. The portions are: x x a/(a+b+c), x x b/(a+b+c), x x c/(a+b+c)

### Example: Concrete Mixture

Mix cement, sand, and gravel in 1 : 1.5 : 3. Need 110 units of concrete.

- Total parts = 1 + 1.5 + 3 = 5.5
- Multiplier = 110 / 5.5 = 20
- Cement = 1 x 20 = **20 units**
- Sand = 1.5 x 20 = **30 units**
- Gravel = 3 x 20 = **60 units**

### Example: Purple Paint

Red : Blue : White = 2 : 3 : 5. Need 50 mL of purple paint.

- Total parts = 2 + 3 + 5 = 10
- Red = 50 x 2/10 = **10 mL**
- Blue = 50 x 3/10 = **15 mL**
- White = 50 x 5/10 = **25 mL**

### Example: Triangle Angles

Construct a triangle with angles in the ratio 1 : 3 : 5.

Sum of angles = 180 degrees. Total parts = 1 + 3 + 5 = 9.
- Angle A = 180 x 1/9 = **20 degrees**
- Angle B = 180 x 3/9 = **60 degrees**
- Angle C = 180 x 5/9 = **100 degrees**

> **Key idea:** To divide any quantity in a given ratio, first find the total parts, then distribute proportionally. This works for any number of terms in the ratio.

## 3.5 Pie Charts

A **pie chart** shows different proportions of a whole as slices of a circle. The full circle represents 360 degrees and the whole quantity (100%).

### Creating a Pie Chart

**Step 1:** Find each quantity as a fraction of the total.

**Step 2:** Convert each fraction to degrees (multiply by 360).

**Step 3:** Draw the corresponding angles.

**Example:** In a class of 40 students, the grade distribution is:
- A: 12 students, B: 8 students, C: 10 students, D: 6 students, E: 4 students

The angles for the pie chart:
- A: (12/40) x 360 = **108 degrees**
- B: (8/40) x 360 = **72 degrees**
- C: (10/40) x 360 = **90 degrees**
- D: (6/40) x 360 = **54 degrees**
- E: (4/40) x 360 = **36 degrees**

Total: 108 + 72 + 90 + 54 + 36 = 360 degrees (check!)

> **Key idea:** In a pie chart, the angle of each slice is proportional to the quantity it represents. The total of all angles is always 360 degrees.

## 3.6 Direct and Inverse Proportion

### Direct Proportion

Two quantities are in **direct proportion** if when one increases, the other increases by the same factor (and vice versa).

**Examples:**
- More cups of rice means more cups of dal (in the same ratio)
- More distance means more fuel (at the same rate)
- More items bought means more money spent (at the same price)

If x and y are directly proportional: **x/y = constant (k)**

### Inverse Proportion

Two quantities are in **inverse proportion** if when one increases, the other decreases by the same factor.

**Examples:**
- More workers means less time to finish a job
- Higher speed means less time to cover a distance
- More pipes filling a tank means less time to fill it

If x and y are inversely proportional: **x x y = constant (k)**

### Example: Workers and Time

If 6 workers can build a wall in 10 days, how many days will 15 workers take?

Workers and time are inversely proportional:
- 6 x 10 = 15 x d
- d = 60/15 = **4 days**

### Example: Speed and Time

A car takes 4 hours at 60 km/h to reach a city. How long at 80 km/h?

Speed and time are inversely proportional:
- 60 x 4 = 80 x t
- t = 240/80 = **3 hours**

> **Key idea:** In **direct proportion**, both quantities increase or decrease together (ratio is constant). In **inverse proportion**, one increases while the other decreases (product is constant).

## Summary

- **Proportional ratios** have the same simplest form. Cross multiplication can verify proportionality.
- **Map scales** use ratios to represent real distances on paper.
- Ratios can have **multiple terms** (a : b : c : d) for comparing several quantities.
- To **divide a quantity** in a ratio, find the total parts and distribute proportionally.
- **Pie charts** represent proportions as angles of a circle (total = 360 degrees).
- **Direct proportion:** x/y = constant (both increase or decrease together).
- **Inverse proportion:** x x y = constant (one increases as the other decreases).
